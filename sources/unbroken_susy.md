# Unbroken supersymmetry without new particles

**Author:** Alejandro Rivero (BIFI, Universidad de Zaragoza)
**arXiv:** 0910.4793 [hep-ph]
**Submitted:** October 26, 2009

## Abstract

We consider Koide's equation for charged leptons jointly with hypotheses implied in the ancient Dual Quark-Gluon model. The focus is to motivate the possibility of a supersymmetric framework akin to the one of D=11 maximal supergravity.

---

## Section I: Koide's Model of Subquarks

Y. Koide's 1981 proposal involves composite quarks and leptons from elementary bosons and fermions with SU(3) color and generational symmetry. Koide's symmetry-breaking scheme constrains the square root of the lepton mass matrix $M_l$:

$$(U + V)^2 = M_l$$

where $U$ is the central component and $V$ the traceless part, satisfying:

$$\mathrm{Tr}[U^2] = \mathrm{Tr}[V^2]$$

With PDG 2009 data the quotient $\mathrm{Tr}[U^2]/\mathrm{Tr}[V^2] = 1.00002 \pm 0.00008$.

Two key observations:
1. The condition on mass's square root "smells to a condition on supersymmetry generators."
2. If supersymmetry were unbroken this constraint should appear in scalar partners of charged fermions.

---

## Section II: sBootstrap

The central proposal: **sfermion particles are really quarks themselves.** With five light quarks and one massive quark, the flavour global symmetry is SU(5), decomposable as $SU(3) \times SU(2)$.

For three generations with one massive quark: "the system closes on itself: the degrees of freedom generated in the product are the ones needed for the sfermions of a supersymmetric standard model."

**"It is not possible to do the same trick with any other number of generations, so in some sense the sBootstrap fixes the number of generations."**

SU(5) decomposition:
- Sleptons: $5 \otimes \bar{5} = 24 \oplus 1$ (mesons)
- Squarks: $\bar{5} \otimes \bar{5} = 15 \oplus 10$ (diquarks)

The Koide condition $\mathrm{Tr}[U^2] = \mathrm{Tr}[V^2]$ is interpreted as the SUSY condition.

---

## Section III: D=11 Supergravity Multiplet

Seven extra dimensions are identified as the minimum for $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ symmetry. D=11 supergravity's minimal multiplet contains 128-component fermions, requiring additional fields to accommodate 84 bosonic degrees of freedom.

### Numerological observations:
- "Charged fermions" of the SM = **84 degrees of freedom** (plus 12 neutrinos = 96)
- "Light fermions" = **84 degrees of freedom** (plus 3 colors of top quark = 96)

Type IIA strings recover 11D supergravity in strong coupling limits, with decompositions showing 28 in NS-NS and 56 in R-R sectors.

---

## Section IV: Koide's Relationship for Mesons

Examining meson mass sectors, the author finds Koide's relationship "fares better" when setting bottom and strange quark masses equal to down quark mass. The quotient $\mathrm{Tr}[U^2]/\mathrm{Tr}[V^2]$ achieves approximately 1.005 in this configuration.

"It seems that the breaking of SUSY aims to preserve Koide's, contrary to our initial expectation."

---

## Section V: Puzzling Chirality

"There are no chiral fermions in D=11, thus no way to implement SU(2) in the way it works in the standard model."

The electroweak $SU(2) \times U(1)$ "interpolates, as we move the mass of W from zero to infinity, between two non-chiral theories in D=9 and D=11."

The $(3,1)$ triplet representation from SU(5) decomposition produces particles that "should have electric charge 4/3."

---

## Main Results

- The sBootstrap (sfermions = quarks) closes **uniquely for 3 generations** with 5 light + 1 heavy quark.
- This matches the D=11 supergravity degree-of-freedom count.
- Koide's equation holds with precision $10^{-5}$ for charged leptons.
- The framework suggests **unbroken SUSY is hiding in plain sight** within known particles, without new superpartners.
