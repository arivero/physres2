# A possible origin of the q=4/3 diquark

**Author:** Alejandro Rivero (BIFI, Universidad de Zaragoza)
**arXiv:** 1111.7230 [hep-ph]
**Submitted:** November 14, 2011

## Abstract

Between the new physics candidates proposed to explain the $t\bar{t}$ asymmetry measured in the Tevatron, there are some scalar diquarks with electric charge +4/3. These are also needed to classify all scalars of the supersymmetric standard model with three generations under global flavour symmetry.

---

## Introduction

The forward-backward $t\bar{t}$ asymmetry at the Tevatron motivates new physics. Among candidates, scalar diquarks with charge +4/3 are favored. This paper proposes these emerge **uniquely** from a flavour-symmetric construction of SUSY scalars with exactly 3 generations and 5 light quarks.

---

## Flavour in SUSY Scalars

A flavour SU(5) symmetry classifies the 24 sleptons in the SSM (with right-handed neutrinos):

$$5 \otimes \bar{5} = 24 \oplus 1$$

Branching to $SU(3) \times SU(2)$:

$$24 = (1,1) + (3,1) + (2,3) + (2,\bar{3}) + (1,8)$$

Assigning electric charge +2/3 to the SU(3) piece and -1/3 to SU(2) gives sextets of charge +1, -1, and twelve charge-zero states.

For squarks:

$$\bar{5} \otimes \bar{5} = 15 \oplus 10$$

with:

$$15 = (3,1) + (2,3) + (1,6)$$

This gives one sextet of charge $-1/3$ and one of charge $+2/3$. The $(3,1)$ triplet consists of **three scalars with charge +4/3** — the same kind of particle expected to solve the Tevatron asymmetry.

The SU(3) piece of the 24 comes from quarks $(d, s, b)$ and the SU(2) piece from $(u, c)$. The construction is **unique** and exhausts exactly for three generations.

The 15 multiplet contains three extra scalars not in the standard model. Being odd in number they cannot be arranged in Dirac supermultiplets.

## A Mass Spectrum

The $m_e = m_u = 0$ spectrum might serve as a starting point for supersymmetric spectrum searches. Left-right models suggest superpartners might be positioned at the $b$ quark's energy scale.

---

## Towards Technicolor

The diquarks have:
- $Q_v = \frac{1}{2}(B-L) = \frac{1}{3}$ from non-chiral B-L interaction
- $Q_{ch} = +1$ chiral component

Condensation might eliminate the vector component, making diquarks resemble gauge supermultiplet scalars (colour-neutral, integer electric charge). Treating B-L differently suggests skepticism toward R-symmetry. With colour neutrality, three chiral supermultiplets could be formed where one scalar becomes the W/Z extra degree of freedom and another surfaces as Higgs.

---

## Main Results

- The charge +4/3 diquark required by Tevatron anomaly explanations arises **naturally and uniquely** from flavour SU(5) symmetry applied to SUSY scalars.
- The construction works **only for 3 generations** with 5 light quarks (u, c, d, s, b).
- There is a potential Technicolor interpretation where these diquarks condense.
- The J=0 colour-$\bar{3}$ diquark theorem: **flavour must be antisymmetric $10$ regardless of L,S values** (consequence of Fermi statistics applied to colour antisymmetric pair).
