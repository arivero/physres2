# No Elementary Scalars in Experimental Supersymmetry

**Author:** Alejandro Rivero
**viXra:** 1102.0034
**Submitted:** February 20, 2011

## Abstract

This sheet presents an extreme interpretation of the global SU(5) symmetry that has been gradually discovered in the spectrum of scalar particles of the Supersymmetric Standard Model. It postulates that such scalars are actually the different aspects of the QCD string. If so, only the gauginos and perhaps two neutral Higgs particles are candidates for discovery in the LHC.

---

## Main Claim

The scalars of the MSSM (squarks and sleptons) are **not elementary** but are identified with different configurations of the QCD string. The SU(5) flavour symmetry observed in their quantum numbers is explained because they are composites of the five light quarks (u, c, d, s, b).

---

## Key Prediction (February 2011)

Under this interpretation:
- **No elementary scalar superpartners exist or will be found at the LHC.**
- Only **gauginos** (fermionic partners of gauge bosons) and possibly **two neutral Higgs bosons** are genuine new particles discoverable at the LHC.
- All other "SUSY scalars" in the spectrum are reinterpreted as QCD string states organized by global SU(5).

Written in February 2011, shortly before LHC exclusions of simple MSSM began to mount.

## sBootstrap Conditions

With $N$ generations and $k_u$ up-type, $k_d$ down-type light quarks:

$$2N = k_u k_d \quad \text{(squarks +1/3)}$$
$$2N = \frac{k_d(k_d+1)}{2} \quad \text{(squarks -2/3)}$$
$$4N = k_u^2 + k_d^2 - 1 \quad \text{(sleptons with R-handed neutrino)}$$

Unique solution: $N=3$, $k_u=2$, $k_d=3$.

## Historical Significance

This is the most compact (2-page) early statement of the sBootstrap program, and the most radical: it is the earliest written prediction that the LHC would not find squarks or sleptons as new particles. The prediction proved correct as of the time of writing.
