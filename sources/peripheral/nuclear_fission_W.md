# Possible Sensibility of Nuclear Fragmentation to the Mass of W

**Author:** Alejandro Rivero
**viXra:** 1509.0090
**Submitted:** September 7, 2015

## Abstract

The possibility of an enhancement of the yields of asymmetric fission when the mass of one of the fragments has the same size as the electroweak W mass, causing some kind of recoil resonance.

---

## Main Idea

The paper examines whether nuclear fission yield distributions show an enhancement when one fragment's mass (in atomic mass units, near ~80 u) coincides with the W boson mass (~80.4 GeV/c²).

The proposal is a resonance-type effect: a "recoil resonance" when the fragment mass numerically matches the W boson mass scale.

### Key Observation

For U-235 thermal fission:
$$235 - 86.29 = 148.71 \text{ MeV}$$

where $86.29 = 0.9315 \text{ GeV} / 10.77$ ratio.

The twin yield peaks are at $A \sim 90$ and $A \sim 140$.

---

## Hypothesis

**Fission yield A-dependence is sensitive to the electroweak scale $M_W \approx 80.4$ GeV.**

This could be tested by:
1. Obtaining fission yield data for U-235, Pu-239 (thermal and fast neutrons)
2. Parametrizing yield $Y(A)$ vs diquark mass $M_{diq} \in [200, 500]$ MeV
3. Testing whether varying $M_{diq}$ by ±10% shifts yield peak positions by observable amounts

The diquark mass scale relevant here is either ~313.85 MeV (from preon model) or ~939.65 MeV (constituent quark scale).
