SECTION 3: FERMIONIC MASS MATRIX -- explicit construction
==============================================================================

The superpotential W = h Tr(q Phi q_tilde) - h mu^2 Tr(Phi)
expanded around the vacuum q = q_tilde^T = (mu I_2, 0):

Write q = q_0 + delta_q, q_tilde = q_tilde_0 + delta_q_tilde
where q_0 = (mu I_2 | 0), q_tilde_0^T = (mu I_2 | 0)

Second derivatives of W = h q_ia Phi_ab q_tilde_jb:

  d^2W / (dPhi_ab dq_ic) = h delta_ac q_tilde_0_ib = h mu delta_ac delta_ib
    (nonzero only for a=c, i=b, and b in {1,2})

  d^2W / (dPhi_ab d(q_tilde)_jc) = h q_0_ja delta_bc = h mu delta_ja delta_bc
    (nonzero only for j=a, b=c, and a in {1,2})

  d^2W / (dq_ia d(q_tilde)_jb) = h Phi_0_ab delta_ij
    (Phi_0 = 0 at the ISS vacuum, so this is ZERO)

  d^2W / (dPhi_ab dPhi_cd) = 0  (no Phi^2 term in W)

RESULT: The fermionic mass terms couple Phi to (q, q_tilde) but NOT
        Phi to Phi or q to q_tilde (at tree level in the ISS vacuum).

Specifically, the massive fermions at tree level are:
  - The Y-sector: Phi_{ab} with a,b in {1,2} pairs with q, q_tilde
  - The Z-sector: Phi_{a,alpha} with a in {1,2}, alpha in {3,4,5}
    pairs with the corresponding q components

The X-sector: Phi_{alpha,beta} with alpha,beta in {3,4,5}
  has NO tree-level fermionic mass terms!
  (because q_0 and q_tilde_0 are zero in directions 3,4,5)

SECTION 9: ISS AS GENERALIZED O'RAIFEARTAIGH
==============================================================================

In the ISS model with non-degenerate quark masses, the effective
theory for the 3 diagonal pseudo-moduli Phi_33, Phi_44, Phi_55
looks like a GENERALIZED O'Raifeartaigh model with 3 independent
F-terms instead of 1.

The effective superpotential (schematically):
  W_eff = sum_{alpha=3}^5 [ -h mu_alpha^2 Phi_{alpha,alpha}
          + (loop corrections involving q_{i,alpha}) ]

For the mapping to work, we need to identify which ISS fields
play the role of Phi_0, Phi_1, Phi_2 in the minimal O'R.

Natural identification:
  - Phi_55 (b-direction) = Phi_0 (pseudo-modulus, largest F-term)
  - Phi_33 (s-direction) ~ Phi_1 (light)
  - Phi_44 (c-direction) ~ Phi_2 (heavy)

But the ISS model does NOT have a tree-level coupling like
g Phi_0 Phi_1^2 between these diagonal elements.
The O'R cubic coupling must emerge from integrating out Z-sector fields.

The effective cubic coupling: After integrating out q_{i,alpha},
the leading interaction between X-sector fields comes at one loop:

  delta_W_eff ~ (h^3/M) Phi_{alpha,beta} Phi_{beta,gamma} Phi_{gamma,alpha}
  (trace over flavor indices, with M = h*mu the Z-sector mass)

For the diagonal components, this gives:
  delta_W_eff ~ (h^3/M) sum_alpha Phi_{alpha,alpha}^3
             + (h^3/M) sum_{alpha!=beta} Phi_{alpha,alpha} Phi_{alpha,beta} Phi_{beta,alpha}

The second term couples diagonal to off-diagonal: this IS the O'R
cubic coupling! Phi_{alpha,alpha} plays Phi_0, and Phi_{alpha,beta}
with alpha!=beta plays Phi_1.

SECTION 12: CW SPECTRUM AND KOIDE CHECK
==============================================================================

The CW effective potential for pseudo-modulus X_alpha receives
contributions from the massive Z-sector fields that couple to it.

For each X_alpha = Phi_{alpha,alpha}, the one-loop CW potential is:

  V_CW(X_alpha) = (1/64pi^2) STr[M^4(X_alpha) ln(M^2(X_alpha)/Lambda_UV^2)]

where M(X_alpha) is the field-dependent mass matrix of the Z-sector
fields that couple to X_alpha.

In the ISS model, the Z-sector boson masses (squared) at
X_alpha = x_alpha are:

  m_B^2 = |h mu|^2 +/- |h^2 mu_alpha^2|  (SUSY split by F-term)

  = (h mu)^2 (1 +/- (mu_alpha/mu)^2)

The CW mass for X_alpha is then:

  m_CW^2(alpha) = (h^4/16pi^2) |mu_alpha^2|^2 / |mu|^2 * f(mu_alpha^2/mu^2)

where f(x) is a slowly varying function (logarithmic).

In the limit F_alpha << M^2 (i.e., mu_alpha << mu):

  m_CW^2(alpha) ~ (h^4 / 8 pi^2) * (mu_alpha^4 / mu^2) * log(2)

  m_CW(alpha) / m_CW(beta) = mu_alpha^2 / mu_beta^2 = m_alpha / m_beta

So the CW mass RATIOS for the pseudo-moduli are:
  m_CW(s) : m_CW(c) : m_CW(b) = m_s : m_c : m_b
  = 93.4 : 1270.0 : 4180.0
  = 1 : 13.60 : 44.75

IMPORTANT: These are SCALAR CW masses for the pseudo-moduli bosons.
The FERMIONIC spectrum is different:
  - One linear combination of psi_{X_3}, psi_{X_4}, psi_{X_5}
    is the true goldstino (remains massless, eaten by gravitino)
  - The other two get masses from higher-order CW corrections

The goldstino direction is parallel to F_alpha:
  |G> ~ F_3 |psi_3> + F_4 |psi_4> + F_5 |psi_5>
       ~ m_s |psi_3> + m_c |psi_4> + m_b |psi_5>

Normalized goldstino:
  |G> = (0.021375) |psi_3> + (0.290640) |psi_4> + (0.956594) |psi_5>
       (dominated by b-direction: 0.9566)

SECTION 16: O'RAIFEARTAIGH KOIDE CONDITION IN ISS PARAMETERS
==============================================================================

Recall the O'R result: Koide seed requires gv/m = sqrt(3).

In the ISS model, the effective O'R parameters are:
  f_eff = h mu_alpha^2  (the F-term, plays role of 'f' in O'R)
  m_eff ~ h mu  (the Z-sector mass, plays role of 'm' in O'R)
  g_eff ~ h^2/mu  (effective cubic from integrating out Z)
  v_eff = <X_alpha>  (the pseudo-modulus VEV)

The O'R Koide condition g_eff * v_eff / m_eff = sqrt(3) becomes:
  (h^2/mu) * v / (h mu) = sqrt(3)
  h v / mu^2 = sqrt(3)

This fixes the pseudo-modulus VEV in terms of mu:
  v = sqrt(3) mu^2 / h

The CW potential determines v dynamically. The question is whether
the CW-determined VEV satisfies this constraint.

In the ISS model, the pseudo-modulus VEV is determined by:
  V_CW'(v) = 0

The CW potential for X_alpha at one loop gives (schematically):
  V_CW(x) = (h^4/64pi^2) sum_Z [ M_Z^4(x) log(M_Z^2(x)/Lambda^2) ]

The minimum is at x = 0 for the ISS model (the origin is the
metastable vacuum). So v_eff = 0, not sqrt(3) mu^2/h.

CONCLUSION: The ISS vacuum sits at v = 0 (origin in field space),
which corresponds to gv/m = 0, NOT sqrt(3).
The Koide seed condition is NOT naturally satisfied in ISS.

==============================================================================
SECTION 17: DEFORMING ISS TOWARD THE KOIDE SEED
==============================================================================

The ISS vacuum is at the origin in pseudo-moduli space (v = 0).
For a Koide seed, we need v != 0 with gv/m = sqrt(3).

Deformations that move the ISS vacuum away from origin:

1. LINEAR DEFORMATION (ISS + tree-level mass for X):
   Add delta_W = epsilon Tr(Phi_X^2)
   This shifts the minimum to v ~ epsilon/h, but breaks
   the R-symmetry and typically destabilizes the metastable vacuum.

2. CUBIC DEFORMATION (direct O'R coupling):
   Add delta_W = g' X_alpha (Phi_{alpha,beta})^2
   This is a relevant deformation. With g' ~ h^2/mu (natural scale),
   the O'R Koide condition becomes g' v / m_eff = sqrt(3),
   which determines v ~ sqrt(3) m_eff / g' ~ sqrt(3) mu^3 / h.

3. FLAVOR-DEPENDENT DEFORMATION:
   Use different mu_alpha to create a hierarchy in the CW masses.
   Even with v = 0, the CW mass RATIOS can satisfy Koide if
   the quark mass ratios do.

Option 3 is the most promising and connects to the sBootstrap:

The CW mass ratios ARE the quark mass ratios.
So the ISS Koide condition reduces to:
  sqrt(m_s) / sqrt(m_c) = 2 - sqrt(3)  (the sBootstrap seed condition!)

  Observed: sqrt(m_s)/sqrt(m_c) = 0.271189
  Required: 2 - sqrt(3) = 0.267949
  Deviation: 1.2%

SECTION 18: FULL CW MASS WITH LOG FACTORS
==============================================================================

The exact one-loop CW mass for pseudo-modulus X_alpha in ISS:

  m_CW^2(alpha) = (N_m * h^4 / 8 pi^2) * mu_alpha^4 / mu^2
                   * [1 + (mu_alpha^2/mu^2) log(1 - mu_alpha^4/mu^4)]

where N_m = 2 (magnetic colors), and we assumed mu_alpha < mu.

In the limit mu_alpha << mu:
  m_CW^2(alpha) ~ (N_m h^4 / 8 pi^2) * mu_alpha^4 / mu^2 * log(2)

In the general case, mu_alpha^2 = m_alpha * Lambda and mu^2 = m_q Lambda
(where m_q is the mass of the 'canceled' flavors 1,2).

Using m_u = 2.16 MeV, m_d = 4.67 MeV for canceled flavors.

mu_alpha^2 / mu_d^2 = m_alpha / m_d:
  x_s = m_s/m_d = 20.00
  x_c = m_c/m_d = 271.95
  x_b = m_b/m_d = 895.07

NOTE: x_c and x_b >> 1, so the limit mu_alpha << mu is VIOLATED!
The ISS metastable vacuum requires mu_alpha < mu (i.e., m_alpha < m_q),
but m_c >> m_d and m_b >> m_d.

This means the ISS framework with u,d as 'canceled' flavors and s,c,b
as 'broken' flavors is NOT self-consistent for the physical quark masses!

Self-consistent ISS assignments (mu_alpha < mu for broken flavors):
Need the 'canceled' flavors to be HEAVIER than the 'broken' ones.

Possibility 1: canceled = {c, b}, broken = {u, d, s}
  mu_u/mu_c = sqrt(m_u/m_c) = 0.0412 < 1  OK
  mu_d/mu_c = sqrt(m_d/m_c) = 0.0606 < 1  OK
  mu_s/mu_c = sqrt(m_s/m_c) = 0.2712 < 1  OK

Possibility 2: canceled = {b, t}, broken = {u, d, s} or {s, c, ...}
  (But t is above the ISS range)

IMPORTANT REALIZATION: For the ISS metastable vacuum to exist,
the 'canceled' (rank-condition) directions need the LARGEST mu_i^2.
Since mu_i^2 ~ m_i Lambda, this means the canceled flavors should
be the HEAVIEST quarks.

For N_c=3, N_f=5 with quarks {u, d, s, c, b}:
  Canceled: {c, b} (heaviest, rank 2)
  Broken: {u, d, s} (lightest, rank condition)

With correct assignment: canceled = {c, b}, broken = {u, d, s}

  F-terms: F_u ~ m_u, F_d ~ m_d, F_s ~ m_s
  CW masses: m_CW(u) ~ m_u, m_CW(d) ~ m_d, m_CW(s) ~ m_s

  Koide Q(m_u, m_d, m_s) = 0.567043
  (not close to 2/3 = 0.666667)

SECTION 22: SUMMARY AND CONCLUSIONS
==============================================================================

1. ISS STRUCTURE:
   The ISS model (N_c=3, N_f=5) produces SUSY breaking by the rank
   condition. The X-sector {3,4,5} has 9 pseudo-moduli with zero
   tree-level fermionic masses. This is a 'degenerate' O'Raifeartaigh
   model where ALL masses are loop-generated.

2. O'RAIFEARTAIGH MAPPING:
   The ISS X-sector maps to a GENERALIZED O'R model, but the mapping
   is NOT to the minimal 3-field O'R. The key difference is that the
   minimal O'R has tree-level masses (0, m_-, m_+) while ISS has
   (0, 0, ..., 0) at tree level. The CW potential generates all masses.

3. CW MASS SPECTRUM:
   With non-degenerate quark masses (flavor-dependent mu_i),
   the CW masses for the diagonal pseudo-moduli are proportional
   to the quark masses: m_CW(alpha) ~ m_alpha.
   Including the goldstino, the spectrum is (0, ~m_s, ~m_c) or
   (0, ~m_s, ~m_c, ~m_b, ...) depending on the assignment.

4. KOIDE SEED:
   The spectrum (0, m_s, m_c) IS approximately a Koide seed:
   sqrt(m_s)/sqrt(m_c) = 0.2712 vs 2-sqrt(3) = 0.2679
   (deviation: 1.2%)

   The Koide seed condition in the ISS framework reduces to a
   condition on the ELECTRIC quark mass ratio m_s/m_c.
   This is EXACTLY the sBootstrap seed condition!

5. ISS PARAMETERS vs O'R gv/m = sqrt(3):
   The O'R Koide condition gv/m = sqrt(3) translates in ISS to a
   condition on the pseudo-modulus VEV, which is dynamically determined
   by the CW potential. The ISS vacuum sits at v=0 (origin),
   so the O'R condition is NOT directly satisfied.
   However, the CW mass RATIOS can still produce a Koide seed
   if the quark masses satisfy the seed ratio.

6. THE ISS PROVIDES A UV COMPLETION:
   The minimal O'R model has the Koide seed as a BOUNDARY CONDITION
   (gv/m = sqrt(3) must be imposed). In the ISS model, this condition
   becomes a condition on the quark mass spectrum -- which is an
   INPUT from the electric theory. The ISS does not PREDICT the Koide
   seed but rather TRANSMITS it from the electric quark mass ratios
   to the magnetic pseudo-moduli spectrum.

7. CONSISTENCY:
   For the ISS vacuum to exist, the 'canceled' flavors must be the
   heaviest. The natural assignment for the sBootstrap is:
   - Canceled: c, b (or b, t) -- heaviest, stabilize the vacuum
   - Broken: u, d, s (or u, d, s) -- lightest, F-terms nonzero
   This does NOT directly produce the (0, s, c) seed, because both
   s and c would need to be in the 'broken' sector, but c is too heavy.

   Alternative: N_c=2, N_f=3 with flavors (X, s, c) where X is heavy.
   Cancel X, break s and c. This gives spectrum (0, m_s, m_c) as a
   Koide seed. The canceled flavor X could be b or t.
