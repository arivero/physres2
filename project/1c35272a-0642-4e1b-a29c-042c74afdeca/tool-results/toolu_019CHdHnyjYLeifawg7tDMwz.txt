    29â†’\begin{abstract}
    30â†’We reformulate the sBootstrap in terms of signed mass charges 
    31â†’$\zch = z_0 + z_i$ with $m = \zch^2$, and show that the Koide ratio 
    32â†’$Q = 2/3$ is the algebraic consequence of two conditions: 
    33â†’$\sum z_i = 0$ and $\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$ (perturbation 
    34â†’energies average to the vacuum energy). Allowing negative charges, 
    35â†’we find that pseudoscalar mesons satisfy a Koide triple 
    36â†’$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to 0.1\%, and identify \emph{seed triples} with 
    37â†’one massless member---$(0, s, c)$ for quarks, $(0, \pi, D_s)$ for 
    38â†’mesons---as the pre-breaking configurations from which the full 
    39â†’spectrum emerges. These seed triples share the same analytical 
    40â†’structure (charge ratio $2-\sqrt{3}$) and, strikingly, the meson 
    41â†’seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ has vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 351\MeV$, 
    42â†’close to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 \approx 314\MeV$. A third 
    43â†’tuple $(c,b,t)$ stands apart, having no meson counterpart because 
    44â†’top diquarks do not hadronize. We discuss the diquark sector---which 
    45â†’fills the symmetric $\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ of $SU(5)$ 
    46â†’flavor, in conflict with Pauli statistics for point-like diquarks, 
    47â†’proving that the diquark cannot be a $qq$ composite---and outline 
    48â†’how SUSY breaking proceeds through two mechanisms---F-term (chiral 
    49â†’condensate, with Volkov--Akulov as its low-energy realization) and 
    50â†’D-term (electromagnetic Fayet--Iliopoulos)---plus a separate 
    51â†’heavy-sector origin for the $(c,b,t)$ triple.
    52â†’\end{abstract}

    57â†’\section{Introduction}
    58â†’\label{sec:intro}
    59â†’%======================================================================
    60â†’
    61â†’The sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005,Rivero2012} proposes that the Standard 
    62â†’Model's pseudoscalar mesons and leptons fill common $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
    63â†’supermultiplets. Its foundational relation is
    64â†’\begin{equation}
    65â†’\label{eq:pimu}
    66â†’m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2\,,
    67â†’\end{equation}
    68â†’identifying the pion decay constant $f_\pi \approx 92.2\MeV$ as the 
    69â†’SUSY-breaking order parameter. Numerically, $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = 
    70â†’8{,}316\MeV^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 = 8{,}501\MeV^2$, a $2.2\%$ 
    71â†’discrepancy---comparable to the precision of the quark Koide triples 
    72â†’discussed below. In this paper we develop two themes 
    73â†’that change the structure of the program.
    74â†’
    75â†’First, \textbf{mass is the square of a signed charge}: $m = \zch^2$ 
    76â†’where $\zch = z_0 + z_i$ can be negative. The Koide ratio $Q = 2/3$ 
    77â†’is then not numerology but the energy-balance condition 
    78â†’$\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$. With signed charges, pseudoscalar 
    79â†’mesons satisfy Koide, and the spectrum organizes into \emph{seed 
    80â†’triples} with a massless member that grow into full triples upon 
    81â†’symmetry breaking.
    82â†’
    83â†’Second, \textbf{the full sBootstrap has three Koide tuples, not one}, 
    84â†’and the breaking structure must generate all three: a light sector 
    85â†’$(0,s,c) \to (-s,c,b)$ seen in both quarks and mesons, a lepton 
    86â†’sector $(e\!\approx\!0, \mu, \tau)$, and a heavy sector $(c,b,t)$ 
    87â†’that decouples from meson physics because the top quark does not 
    88â†’hadronize. The diquark partners of quarks, needed to complete the 
    89â†’SUSY multiplets, fill the symmetric $\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ 
    90â†’of $SU(5)$ flavor---the representation produced naturally by the 
    91â†’SUSY generator and by the $SO(32)$ decomposition. A kinematic 
    92â†’theorem shows that no $J=0$, color-$\bar{3}$ diquark can have 
    93â†’symmetric flavor---regardless of orbital angular momentum---so the 
    94â†’sBootstrap diquark cannot be a $qq$ composite but must be an 
    95â†’algebraically or string-theoretically generated object.

    97â†’%======================================================================
    98â†’\section{The Koide Formula as Energy Balance}
    99â†’\label{sec:koide_algebra}
   100â†’%======================================================================
   101â†’
   102â†’\subsection{Charges, not masses}
   103â†’
   104â†’The fundamental variable is the signed mass charge $\zch = z_0 + z_i 
   105â†’\in \mathbb{R}$, with the physical mass $m = \zch^2 \geq 0$.
   106â†’
   107â†’\subsection{Derivation of $Q = 2/3$}
   108â†’
   109â†’For three charges $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ with $z_0$ the mean 
   110â†’($\sum z_k = 0$):
   111â†’\begin{equation}
   112â†’Q = \frac{\sum \zch_k^2}{(\sum \zch_k)^2} 
   113â†’= \frac{3z_0^2 + \sum z_k^2}{9z_0^2}
   114â†’= \frac{1}{3} + \frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{3z_0^2}\,.
   115â†’\end{equation}
   116â†’Therefore:
   117â†’\begin{equation}
   118â†’\boxed{Q = \frac{2}{3} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad 
   119â†’\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2\,.}
   120â†’\end{equation}
   121â†’
   122â†’The two conditions are:
   123â†’\begin{enumerate}
   124â†’\item \textbf{Zero-sum perturbations:} $z_1 + z_2 + z_3 = 0$ 
   125â†’(definition of $z_0$ as the mean charge).
   126â†’\item \textbf{Energy balance:} The mean energy of the perturbations 
   127â†’equals the energy of the unperturbed charge: 
   128â†’$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
   129â†’\end{enumerate}
   130â†’
   131â†’Condition~2 is the physics. It says the splitting scale matches the 
   132â†’base scale---the coefficient of variation equals unity: 
   133â†’$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$. This is neither perturbative 
   134â†’($\sigma \ll \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1/3$, all equal) nor 
   135â†’dominant ($\sigma \gg \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1$, one mass 
   136â†’dominates). The Koide value $2/3$ is the equipartition point.
   137â†’
   138â†’\subsection{Why one mass can vanish}
   139â†’
   140â†’If $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$, a perturbation $z_k$ can reach 
   141â†’$|z_k| \sim z_0$, making $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k \approx 0$. A vanishing 
   142â†’mass is the \emph{natural extreme} of energy balance, not fine-tuning.
   143â†’
   144â†’%======================================================================
   145â†’\section{Seed Triples: The $(0, a, b)$ Structure}
   146â†’\label{sec:seeds}
   147â†’%======================================================================
   148â†’
   149â†’\subsection{Analytical condition}
   150â†’
   151â†’For a triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ with one massless member, the 
   152â†’Koide ratio becomes:
   153â†’\begin{equation}
   154â†’Q = \frac{\zch_a^2 + \zch_b^2}{(\zch_a + \zch_b)^2}\,.
   155â†’\end{equation}
   156â†’Setting $Q = 2/3$ and writing $r = \zch_a/\zch_b$ (with $r < 1$):
   157â†’\begin{equation}
   158â†’3(r^2 + 1) = 2(r + 1)^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad r^2 - 4r + 1 = 0\,,
   159â†’\end{equation}
   160â†’giving:
   161â†’\begin{equation}
   162â†’\boxed{r = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.}
   163â†’\end{equation}
   164â†’
   165â†’Equivalently, in mass space: 
   166â†’$m_{\text{light}}/m_{\text{heavy}} = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 = 7 - 4\sqrt{3} 
   167â†’\approx 0.07180$.
   168â†’
   169â†’This is a \emph{universal} prediction: any Koide triple with one 
   170â†’massless member has the same charge ratio between the two massive 
   171â†’members, regardless of their absolute scale.
   172â†’
   173â†’\subsection{The quark seed: $(0, s, c)$}
   174â†’
   175â†’The strange and charm quark charges give:\footnote{Throughout 
   176â†’this paper, quark masses follow PDG conventions: 
   177â†’$m_s(2\GeV) = 93.4\MeV$, $m_c(m_c) = 1.27\GeV$, 
   178â†’$m_b(m_b) = 4.18\GeV$, and the top pole mass 
   179â†’$m_t = 172.76\GeV$. This is a mixed convention: the light 
   180â†’quark is evaluated at 2 GeV while heavier quarks are 
   181â†’evaluated at their own scale. Since QCD mass ratios are 
   182â†’RG invariants (the anomalous dimension $\gamma_m$ is 
   183â†’flavor-universal at leading order), the Koide ratio $Q$ is 
   184â†’scheme-independent. However, the seed ratio 
   185â†’$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ as computed here uses masses at 
   186â†’\emph{different} scales, giving $0.271$; at a common scale 
   187â†’the ratio is $\sim 0.29$, further from $2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.268$. 
   188â†’The meson seed, using unambiguous physical (pole) masses, 
   189â†’is free of this scheme issue and provides the cleaner test.}
   190â†’\begin{equation}
   191â†’\frac{\sqrt{m_s}}{\sqrt{m_c}} = \frac{9.67}{35.64} = 0.2712\,, 
   192â†’\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 1.2\%\,.
   193â†’\end{equation}
   194â†’
   195â†’This triple has $Q = 0.6644$, deviating from $2/3$ by only $0.35\%$. 
   196â†’Its vacuum energy is $v_0^2 = (\zch_s + \zch_c)^2/9 \approx 228\MeV$.
   197â†’
   198â†’A historical note: Harari, Haut, and Weyers \cite{Harari} observed 
   199â†’a quark mass ratio pattern that, in the language of the present paper, 
   200â†’corresponds to an approximate seed triple---$(0, d, s)$---at a time 
   201â†’when the up quark mass was 
   202â†’still thought to be possibly zero \cite{Harari}. With current masses, 
   203â†’$\sqrt{m_d}/\sqrt{m_s} = 0.224$, deviating from $2 - \sqrt{3}$ by 
   204â†’$16\%$---too large for a good Koide triple. But if $m_s$ were 
   205â†’$\sim 150\MeV$ and $m_d \sim 7\MeV$ (values common in that era), the 
   206â†’ratio improves considerably. The seed idea was already present, ahead 
   207â†’of its time.
   208â†’
   209â†’\subsection{The meson seed: $(0, \pi, D_s)$}
   210â†’
   211â†’The pion and $D_s$ meson charges give:
   212â†’\begin{equation}
   213â†’\frac{\sqrt{m_\pi}}{\sqrt{m_{D_s}}} = \frac{11.81}{44.37} = 0.2663\,, 
   214â†’\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 0.6\%\,.
   215â†’\end{equation}
   216â†’
   217â†’This gives $Q = 0.6679$, deviating by $0.18\%$. It is the 
   218â†’\textbf{meson counterpart} of the quark seed $(0, s, c)$---both 
   219â†’involve the strange-charm sector. Indeed, $D_s = c\bar{s}$ is the 
   220â†’meson that directly contains the $s$ and $c$ quarks.
   221â†’
   222â†’\subsection{The lepton triple as an ``almost-seed''}
   223â†’
   224â†’The charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ has $\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$, 
   225â†’which is small but nonzero. If $m_e$ were zero, we would need 
   226â†’$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.2679$; the actual value 
   227â†’is $0.2439$, deviating by $9\%$. So the leptons are \emph{not} an 
   228â†’exact seed---the electron, while very light, is not massless. But the 
   229â†’triple is clearly the same family: the near-critical Koide phase puts 
   230â†’one member close to zero.
   231â†’
   232â†’\subsection{Vacuum energy coincidence}
   233â†’
   234â†’The $v_0^2$ values of the seed triples cluster remarkably:
   235â†’
   236â†’\begin{center}
   237â†’\begin{tabular}{lcc}
   238â†’\toprule
   239â†’Triple & $v_0^2$ (MeV) & $v_0$ ($\MeV^{1/2}$) \\
   240â†’\midrule
   241â†’$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 & 17.72 \\
   242â†’$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 351 & 18.73 \\
   243â†’$(0, s, c)$ & 228 & 15.10 \\
   244â†’\bottomrule
   245â†’\end{tabular}
   246â†’\end{center}
   247â†’
   248â†’The lepton and meson seed triples share a vacuum energy around 
   249â†’310--350~MeV, squarely in the QCD scale range. This is the 
   250â†’sBootstrap connection made quantitative: \textbf{the lepton triple 
   251â†’and the meson seed triple operate at the same scale.}

   258â†’\subsection{From seed to full triple}
   259â†’
   260â†’The seed triples grow into full triples upon symmetry breaking:
   261â†’
   262â†’\begin{center}
   263â†’\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   264â†’\toprule
   265â†’Sector & Seed triple & Full triple & $v_0^2$ (full) \\
   266â†’\midrule
   267â†’Quarks & $(0, s, c)$ & $(-s, c, b)$ & 913 MeV \\
   268â†’Mesons & $(0, \pi, D_s)$ & $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 1230 MeV \\
   269â†’Leptons & $(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & $(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 MeV \\
   270â†’\bottomrule
   271â†’\end{tabular}
   272â†’\end{center}
   273â†’
   274â†’In each case:
   275â†’\begin{itemize}
   276â†’\item The seed has a massless (or near-massless) member, two massive 
   277â†’members in the ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$, and $Q \approx 2/3$.
   278â†’\item The full triple has \textbf{no} massless member: the zero has 
   279â†’been lifted (to $m_e$, or the sign-flipped strange/pion mass). The 
   280â†’$b$ quark and $B$ meson are always present as flavor degrees of 
   281â†’freedom; what the breaking does is give them their \emph{distinctive 
   282â†’masses}---the specific values that complete the Koide triple. Before 
   283â†’breaking, the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$); 
   284â†’after, it acquires $\zch_b = 64.65\MeV^{1/2}$, the value selected 
   285â†’by the energy balance with $c$ and the sign-flipped $s$. The bloom 
   286â†’is not particle creation but \emph{mass generation}.
   287â†’\end{itemize}
   288â†’
   289â†’The ``tale of two tuples'' is this: \textbf{each sector presents 
   290â†’first as a seed with a massless member, then ``blooms'' into a 
   291â†’full triple when SUSY breaks.} The massless member is the necessary 
   292â†’initial condition; the breaking lifts it and generates the full spectrum.

   349â†’%======================================================================
   350â†’\section{The Meson Koide Triple: $(-\pi, D_s, B)$}
   351â†’\label{sec:mesonkoide}
   352â†’%======================================================================
   353â†’
   354â†’With the pion charge negative, the triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ satisfies:
   355â†’\begin{equation}
   356â†’Q(-\pi, D_s, B) = 
   357â†’\frac{m_\pi + m_{D_s} + m_B}{(-\sqrt{m_\pi} + \sqrt{m_{D_s}} + \sqrt{m_B})^2}
   358â†’= 0.6674\,,
   359â†’\end{equation}
   360â†’deviating from $2/3$ by $0.10\%$---the second most precise Koide 
   361â†’triple known. The energy balance:
   362â†’\begin{equation}
   363â†’\frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{z_0^2} = 1.002\,.
   364â†’\end{equation}
   365â†’
   366â†’For comparison, the ``standard'' all-positive quark triples $(d,s,b)$ 
   367â†’and $(u,c,t)$ deviate from $Q = 2/3$ by 9.7\% and 27\% respectively 
   368â†’at $\overline{\text{MS}}$ masses. \textbf{The meson triple is more 
   369â†’precise than any quark triple} except $(c,b,t)$.
   370â†’
   371â†’The pion's negative charge has physical meaning: as a pseudo-Goldstone 
   372â†’boson, the pion would be massless ($\zch = 0$) if chiral symmetry were 
   373â†’exact. It sits just below zero ($\zch_\pi = -11.81$), approaching zero 
   374â†’from the negative side as $m_q \to 0$. The sign of $\zch$ encodes 
   375â†’Goldstone nature: pseudo-Goldstone bosons sit on the negative side 
   376â†’of the critical point, having crossed through zero from positive 
   377â†’territory during chiral symmetry breaking.
   378â†’
   379â†’%======================================================================
   380â†’\section{The Third Tuple: $(c, b, t)$}
   381â†’\label{sec:cbt}
   382â†’%======================================================================
   383â†’
   384â†’\subsection{A tuple without mesons}
   385â†’
   386â†’The triple $(c, b, t)$ with all positive signs gives $Q = 0.6695$, 
   387â†’deviating by $0.42\%$. Its vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ 
   388â†’is far above the QCD scale.
   389â†’
   390â†’This triple has \textbf{no meson counterpart}: the top quark decays 
   391â†’weakly ($t \to Wb$, lifetime $\sim 5 \times 10^{-25}$~s) before the 
   392â†’strong interaction can bind it ($\tau_{\text{QCD}} \sim 1/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} 
   393â†’\sim 10^{-24}$~s). No $T$-meson ($t\bar{q}$) exists. The meson matrix 
   394â†’$M^i{}_j$ extends only to $b$-flavored states; the top sector lives 
   395â†’purely at the quark level.
   396â†’
   397â†’\subsection{Diquarks and the top}
   398â†’
   399â†’In the sBootstrap, diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ are the scalar partners of 
   400â†’antiquarks $\bar{q}_k$, with the baryon acting as the SUSY generator 
   401â†’\cite{CattoGursey, Miyazawa}. For five active flavors 
   402â†’$(u, d, s, c, b)$, the diquarks form a representation of $SU(5)_f$.
   403â†’
   404â†’The top diquarks $[tq]$ are kinematic objects only---they do not 
   405â†’hadronize. Yet their masses should follow the same pattern as other 
   406â†’diquarks: \textbf{there is no dynamical reason for top diquark masses 
   407â†’to differ from the others}. The $(c,b,t)$ Koide triple governs the 
   408â†’quark masses that enter the diquark sector, and its energy balance 
   409â†’$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ constrains the possible diquark 
   410â†’spectrum even though the top diquarks are never observed as bound 
   411â†’states.
   412â†’
   413â†’\subsection{The $(c,b,t)$ triple has no seed}
   414â†’
   415â†’Unlike the $(0,s,c)$ and $(0,\pi,D_s)$ seeds, there is no obvious 
   416â†’$(0, x, y)$ seed for the $(c,b,t)$ triple. No pair of quarks in this 
   417â†’sector satisfies $\sqrt{m_{\text{light}}}/\sqrt{m_{\text{heavy}}} 
   418â†’\approx 2 - \sqrt{3}$: one has $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_t} = 0.086$ 
   419â†’(too small) and $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_b} = 0.551$ (too large).
   420â†’
   421â†’This means $(c,b,t)$ is \emph{not} a ``bloomed seed'' in the same 
   422â†’sense as $(-s,c,b)$. It may represent a fundamentally different 
   423â†’structure---a triple that was never seeded by a massless member but 
   424â†’instead emerged fully formed at a higher breaking scale, perhaps 
   425â†’connected to electroweak symmetry breaking rather than to chiral 
   426â†’symmetry breaking.

   428â†’%======================================================================
   429â†’\section{The Diquark Sector and SU(5) Flavor}
   430â†’\label{sec:diquarks}
   431â†’%======================================================================
   432â†’
   433â†’\subsection{Counting partners}
   434â†’
   435â†’In the full sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005}, the scalar partners of 
   436â†’the Standard Model fermions include both mesons ($q\bar{q}$) and 
   437â†’diquarks ($qq$). For five active flavors, the meson matrix gives 
   438â†’$5 \times 5 = 25$ scalars. The diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ form a 
   439â†’representation of $SU(5)_f$:
   440â†’\begin{equation}
   441â†’\mathbf{5} \otimes \mathbf{5} = \overline{\mathbf{10}}_A 
   442â†’\oplus \mathbf{15}_S\,.
   443â†’\end{equation}
   444â†’
   445â†’The symmetric $\mathbf{15}_S$ gives 15 diquarks including 
   446â†’same-flavor states $[uu]$, $[dd]$, \ldots, while the antisymmetric 
   447â†’$\overline{\mathbf{10}}_A$ gives 10 diquarks with $i \neq j$ only.
   448â†’
   449â†’\subsection{The symmetric representation}
   450â†’\label{subsec:sym15}
   451â†’
   452â†’The sBootstrap SUSY generator acts asymmetrically on the two 
   453â†’fermion sectors. When it acts on a \emph{lepton}, it produces a 
   454â†’meson: $Q \cdot \ell \sim q\bar{q}$. Since the quark and antiquark 
   455â†’are distinguishable, the meson lies in the full 
   456â†’$\mathbf{5} \otimes \bar{\mathbf{5}} = \mathbf{24} + \mathbf{1}$. 
   457â†’There is no Pauli constraint, and the meson spectrum is perfectly 
   458â†’reproduced.
   459â†’
   460â†’When it acts on a \emph{quark}, it produces a diquark: 
   461â†’$Q \cdot q_i \sim q_i q_j$. The bosonic SUSY generator applied to 
   462â†’a single flavor index $i$ naturally produces the \textbf{symmetric} 
   463â†’product---the $\mathbf{15}_S$ representation, not the antisymmetric 
   464â†’$\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. This is the representation identified 
   465â†’in the $SO(32)$ decomposition \cite{Rivero2005}: the adjoint of 
   466â†’$SO(32)$ under $SU(5)_f$ decomposes to include the 
   467â†’$\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ (diquarks) alongside the 
   468â†’$\mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ (mesons).
   469â†’
   470â†’The 15 includes 5 same-flavor states ($[uu]$, $[dd]$, $[ss]$, 
   471â†’$[cc]$, $[bb]$) absent from the $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. Among 
   472â†’these are exotic $+4/3$-charged diquarks such as $[uu]$. The cost: 
   473â†’an additional chiral $+4/3$ quark per generation, as identified 
   474â†’in \cite{Rivero2005}. Whether these exotic states appear in nature 
   475â†’as resonances, or whether they decouple at some high scale, is an 
   476â†’open question.
   477â†’
   478â†’\subsection{The Pauli theorem for scalar diquarks}
   479â†’\label{subsec:pauli}
   480â†’
   481â†’The requirement of the symmetric $\mathbf{15}$ conflicts with 
   482â†’Fermi statistics---not merely for $L=0$ diquarks, but for 
   483â†’\emph{all} scalar diquarks in the color $\bar{3}$ channel, 
   484â†’regardless of internal orbital structure.
   485â†’
   486â†’\medskip
   487â†’\noindent\textbf{Theorem.}
   488â†’\textit{For a spin-0 ($J=0$) diquark in the color $\bar{3}$ 
   489â†’(antisymmetric) representation, the flavor wave function is 
   490â†’antisymmetric for \textbf{all} values of the orbital angular 
   491â†’momentum $L$ and spin $S$.}
   492â†’
   493â†’\medskip
   494â†’\noindent\textit{Proof.}
   495â†’$J = 0$ requires $L = S$ (since $|L - S| \leq J \leq L + S$). 
   496â†’The exchange symmetry of the spin-orbital wave function is 
   497â†’$(-1)^{L+S+1} = (-1)^{2L+1} = -1$ for all $L$. Combined with 
   498â†’the color $\bar{3}$ factor $(-1)$, the non-flavor product is 
   499â†’$(-1)(-1) = +1$ (symmetric). Total antisymmetry then requires 
   500â†’flavor to be antisymmetric: $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. \hfill$\square$
   501â†’
   502â†’\medskip
   503â†’This result is purely kinematic---independent of dynamics, 
   504â†’binding mechanisms, or spatial extent. No wave function, however 
   505â†’extended, can produce a symmetric-flavor scalar diquark in color 
   506â†’$\bar{3}$ while respecting Fermi statistics. The key cases:
   507â†’
   508â†’\begin{center}
   509â†’\begin{tabular}{ccccc}
   510â†’\toprule
   511â†’$L$ & $S$ & Spin-orbital $(-1)^{2L+1}$ & $\times$ Color & Flavor \\
   512â†’\midrule
   513â†’0 & 0 & $-1$ & $\times\,(-1) = +1$ & must be A \\
   514â†’1 & 1 & $-1$ & $\times\,(-1) = +1$ & must be A \\
   515â†’2 & 2 & $-1$ & $\times\,(-1) = +1$ & must be A \\
   516â†’\bottomrule
   517â†’\end{tabular}
   518â†’\end{center}
   519â†’
   520â†’The $L=1$, $S=1$ row closes the ``P-wave diquark'' escape route 
   521â†’that might otherwise seem available.
   522â†’
   523â†’The theorem has a sharp consequence: \textbf{the sBootstrap 
   524â†’diquark in the $\mathbf{15}$ cannot be a $qq$ composite.} It 
   525â†’must be either:
   526â†’\begin{enumerate}
   527â†’\item An algebraically generated object---the image of the 
   528â†’Miyazawa supercharge \cite{Miyazawa, CattoGursey} acting on 
   529â†’an antiquark, whose symmetry is determined by the superalgebra 
   530â†’rather than by the statistics of constituent quarks; or
   531â†’\item A fundamental string-theoretic object, as in the $SO(32)$ 
   532â†’decomposition \cite{Rivero2005}, where the $\mathbf{15}$ 
   533â†’arises from the adjoint representation of the gauge group and 
   534â†’the open-string diquark is not built from point-like quarks.
   535â†’\end{enumerate}
   536â†’
   537â†’The contrast with the meson sector is instructive. When the 
   538â†’SUSY generator acts on a lepton, it produces a meson 
   539â†’($q\bar{q}$): the quark and antiquark are distinguishable, 
   540â†’no Pauli constraint applies, and the meson spectrum is 
   541â†’reproduced without tension. When it acts on a quark, it 
   542â†’produces a diquark in the ``wrong'' representation---and the 
   543â†’theorem shows this cannot be fixed by any internal structure 
   544â†’of a $qq$ pair. The asymmetry between the two SUSY directions 
   545â†’is absolute: mesons work as composites; diquarks do not. 
   546â†’This is not a weakness of the sBootstrap but a 
   547â†’\emph{prediction}: the diquark sector requires new physics 
   548â†’beyond the $qq$ picture.
   549â†’
   550â†’\subsection{The SO(32) connection}
   551â†’
   552â†’The sBootstrap counting matches a decomposition of the $SO(32)$ gauge 
   553â†’group familiar from Type~I/heterotic string theory. Under 
   554â†’$SO(32) \supset SU(5) \times \ldots$, the 496-dimensional adjoint 
   555â†’decomposes to include the 
   556â†’$\mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ (mesons/sleptons) and the 
   557â†’$\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ (diquarks/squarks).
   558â†’
   559â†’This is suggestive: the same group structure that gives anomaly-free 
   560â†’string compactifications produces the symmetric diquark 
   561â†’representation demanded by the SUSY generator. The $SO(32)$ embedding 
   562â†’may provide the UV completion in which the compositeness of diquarks 
   563â†’(and hence the resolution of the Pauli sign) is made explicit: in 
   564â†’the string picture, diquarks are open-string states whose symmetry 
   565â†’is determined by the gauge group, not by point-particle statistics.
   566â†’
   567â†’%======================================================================
   568â†’\section{SUSY Breaking: From Seeds to Spectrum}
   569â†’\label{sec:breaking}
   570â†’%======================================================================
   571â†’
   572â†’The SUSY-breaking story begins with the foundational observation 
   573â†’\eqref{eq:pimu}: $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2$. This is the 
   574â†’Volkov--Akulov mass formula for a pseudo-Goldstino---the muon---whose 
   575â†’superpartner---the pion---is a pseudo-Goldstone boson. The SUSY-breaking 
   576â†’scale is $f_\pi \approx 92\MeV$, and the breaking mechanism is chiral: 
   577â†’$\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   578â†’simultaneously. The Volkov--Akulov realization is the \emph{physical 
   579â†’starting point}; what follows in this section is the SQCD machinery 
   580â†’that makes it precise.
   581â†’
   582â†’The dynamics must accomplish three things: generate the seed-to-bloom 
   583â†’transition for each tuple, preserve the energy-balance condition 
   584â†’$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ through the breaking, and produce 
   585â†’the correct splitting between meson and lepton masses at scale $f_\pi$.
   586â†’
   587â†’\subsection{The Seiberg framework}
   588â†’
   589â†’The natural arena is $\mathcal{N}=1$ SQCD with gauge group $SU(3)_c$ 
   590â†’and $N_f$ flavors of chiral superfields $Q^i$, $\bar{Q}_j$ in the 
   591â†’fundamental and antifundamental representations. For $N_f = N_c = 3$ 
   592â†’(the case relevant to light QCD), Seiberg's exact results 
   593â†’\cite{Seiberg1995} establish that the theory confines, with the 
   594â†’low-energy degrees of freedom being the composite meson superfield
   595â†’\begin{equation}
   596â†’M^i{}_j = Q^i \cdot \bar{Q}_j\,,
   597â†’\end{equation}
   598â†’a $3\times 3$ matrix of chiral superfields, together with baryons 
   599â†’$B = \epsilon_{ijk}Q^iQ^jQ^k$ and $\bar{B}$. These satisfy the 
   600â†’quantum-modified constraint
   601â†’\begin{equation}
   602â†’\label{eq:seiberg}
   603â†’\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^{2N_c}\,,
   604â†’\end{equation}
   605â†’where $\Lambda$ is the dynamical scale.
   606â†’
   607â†’Each entry $M^i{}_j$ is a chiral superfield containing a complex 
   608â†’scalar (the meson) and a Weyl fermion (the lepton, in the sBootstrap 
   609â†’identification). The scalar and fermion are degenerate in the SUSY 
   610â†’limit; SUSY breaking splits them.
   611â†’
   612â†’\subsection{The three chiral superfields of each seed}
   613â†’
   614â†’The seed triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ maps naturally onto three 
   615â†’chiral superfields with the following mass structure before breaking:
   616â†’
   617â†’\begin{center}
   618â†’\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   619â†’\toprule
   620â†’Superfield & Mass & Role \\
   621â†’\midrule
   622â†’$\Phi_0$ & $m_0 = 0$ & Massless: flat direction \\
   623â†’$\Phi_a$ & $m_a = \zch_a^2$ & Light massive member \\
   624â†’$\Phi_b$ & $m_b = \zch_b^2$ & Heavy massive member \\
   625â†’\bottomrule
   626â†’\end{tabular}
   627â†’\end{center}
   628â†’
   629â†’The ratio $\zch_a/\zch_b = 2 - \sqrt{3}$ is enforced by the 
   630â†’Koide energy-balance condition applied to the seed.
   631â†’
   632â†’In the Seiberg description, these three superfields are entries of 
   633â†’the meson matrix $M^i{}_j$. For the $(0, s, c)$ seed, the relevant 
   634â†’entries involve the strange and charm quarks; $\Phi_0$ is the entry 
   635â†’whose quark-mass combination vanishes in the seed limit (i.e., the 
   636â†’direction in flavor space where $m_i + m_j \to 0$ through GOR).
   637â†’
   638â†’\subsection{F-term breaking: the O'Raifeartaigh mechanism}
   639â†’\label{subsec:fterm}
   640â†’
   641â†’The VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} tells us \emph{that} SUSY is broken at 
   642â†’scale $f_\pi$. To understand \emph{how}, we need the microscopic 
   643â†’mechanism. The O'Raifeartaigh model \cite{ORaifeartaigh} is the simplest 
   644â†’realization of spontaneous $F$-term SUSY breaking. With three chiral 
   645â†’superfields, the superpotential
   646â†’\begin{equation}
   647â†’W = f\,\Phi_0 + m\,\Phi_1\Phi_2 + g\,\Phi_0\Phi_1^2
   648â†’\end{equation}
   649â†’gives $F$-terms:
   650â†’\begin{align}
   651â†’F_{\Phi_0}^* &= f + g\,\phi_1^2\,, \\
   652â†’F_{\Phi_1}^* &= m\,\phi_2 + 2g\,\phi_0\phi_1\,, \\
   653â†’F_{\Phi_2}^* &= m\,\phi_1\,.
   654â†’\end{align}
   655â†’If $f \neq 0$ and $m \neq 0$, then $F_{\Phi_2}^* = 0$ requires 
   656â†’$\phi_1 = 0$, but then $F_{\Phi_0}^* = f \neq 0$: SUSY is 
   657â†’\textbf{necessarily broken}. The key features are:
   658â†’\begin{enumerate}
   659â†’\item A massless field ($\Phi_0$) exists at tree level---it is the 
   660â†’pseudo-modulus.
   661â†’\item The breaking is triggered because $F_{\Phi_0}$ cannot vanish.
   662â†’\item After including loop corrections, $\Phi_0$ acquires a mass 
   663â†’(the Coleman--Weinberg potential lifts the flat direction).
   664â†’\end{enumerate}
   665â†’
   666â†’The structural parallel with the seed triples is:
   667â†’
   668â†’\begin{center}
   669â†’\begin{tabular}{llll}
   670â†’\toprule
   671â†’O'Raifeartaigh & Flavor & Seed & After breaking \\
   672â†’\midrule
   673â†’$\Phi_0$ (massless) & $b$ / $B$ & $\zch = 0$ (flat direction) & $\zch_b = 64.65$ (mass generated) \\
   674â†’$\Phi_1$ (light) & $s$ / $\pi$ & $\zch_s = 9.67$ & $\zch \to -9.67$ (sign-flipped) \\
   675â†’$\Phi_2$ (heavy) & $c$ / $D_s$ & $\zch_c = 35.64$ & $\zch_c \approx 35.64$ (shifted) \\
   676â†’\midrule
   677â†’ & & $f = 0$ (SUSY) & $f = f_\pi$ (broken) \\
   678â†’\bottomrule
   679â†’\end{tabular}
   680â†’\end{center}
   681â†’
   682â†’This parallel is \emph{structural}, not quantitative: the minimal 
   683â†’O'Raifeartaigh model gives $\Phi_1$ and $\Phi_2$ the same mass 
   684â†’parameter $m$, whereas the seed triple has a mass ratio 
   685â†’$m_s/m_c = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 \approx 0.07$---far from degenerate. 
   686â†’The hierarchy between the two massive members must come from 
   687â†’additional dynamics beyond the minimal O'Raifeartaigh superpotential. 
   688â†’What the O'Raifeartaigh structure \emph{does} provide is the essential 
   689â†’feature: a massless flat direction ($\Phi_0 = $ the $b$-direction) 
   690â†’whose $F$-term is forced nonzero. The $b$ quark and $B$ meson are 
   691â†’always present as flavor degrees of freedom; the seed configuration 
   692â†’$(0, s, c)$ means the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction before 
   693â†’breaking. The bloom is not particle creation but mass generation: 
   694â†’breaking lifts $\Phi_0$ from zero to $\zch_b \neq 0$, simultaneously 
   695â†’flipping the sign of $\zch_s$, so that the same three flavors 
   696â†’rearrange from $(0, s, c)$ into $(-s, c, b)$.
   697â†’
   698â†’A striking difference from standard O'Raifeartaigh: the 
   699â†’pseudo-modulus $\Phi_0$ normally acquires the \emph{smallest} mass 
   700â†’in the spectrum (a loop-suppressed Coleman--Weinberg mass, 
   701â†’parametrically $\sim g^2 f/16\pi^2 m$). In the sBootstrap bloom, 
   702â†’the $b$-direction acquires the \emph{largest} mass 
   703â†’($m_b \gg m_s, m_c$). The bloom is far more dramatic than a 
   704â†’perturbative CW lifting---it is a nonperturbative, strong-dynamics 
   705â†’effect in which confinement generates a mass of order $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2/m_q$, 
   706â†’not $g^2/(16\pi^2)$. This inversion is a signature of the 
   707â†’dynamical (SQCD) realization versus the perturbative 
   708â†’(superpotential) one.
   709â†’
   710â†’The identification $f = \langle F_{\Phi_0}\rangle \sim f_\pi$ is the 
   711â†’sBootstrap's central claim: the SUSY-breaking $F$-term is the chiral 
   712â†’condensate parameter.
   713â†’
   714â†’In SQCD, this is realized dynamically rather than through a 
   715â†’superpotential: the chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ acts as the 
   716â†’nonzero $F$-term, the impossibility of restoring chiral symmetry 
   717â†’in the QCD vacuum is the dynamical analogue of $f \neq 0$, and the 
   718â†’pion---the would-be Goldstone boson that acquires mass from explicit 
   719â†’chiral breaking---plays the role of $\Phi_0$ whose mass is lifted 
   720â†’by loop corrections (here, by quark masses).
   721â†’
   722â†’\subsection{The Goldstino direction and the Volkov--Akulov realization}
   723â†’\label{subsec:va}
   724â†’
   725â†’When SUSY is spontaneously broken, a massless fermion appears: the 
   726â†’Goldstino, the fermionic analogue of the Goldstone boson. Its decay 
   727â†’constant $f_{\text{VA}}$ sets the SUSY-breaking scale through the 
   728â†’Volkov--Akulov Lagrangian \cite{VolkovAkulov1973}:
   729â†’\begin{equation}
   730â†’\mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}} = -f_{\text{VA}}^2 \det\!\left(
   731â†’\delta_\mu^\nu + \frac{i}{f_{\text{VA}}^2}\,\bar{\lambda}\bar{\sigma}^\nu
   732â†’\partial_\mu\lambda\right),
   733â†’\end{equation}
   734â†’where $\lambda$ is the Goldstino field. At low energies, the Goldstino 
   735â†’couples derivatively with strength $\sim 1/f_{\text{VA}}^2$, exactly 
   736â†’as the pion couples with strength $\sim 1/f_\pi^2$ in the chiral 
   737â†’Lagrangian.
   738â†’
   739â†’The sBootstrap identification is:
   740â†’\begin{equation}
   741â†’\label{eq:va_id}
   742â†’f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi\,, \qquad 
   743â†’\text{Goldstino} = \text{muon (partner of }\pi\text{)}\,.
   744â†’\end{equation}
   745â†’More precisely, the muon is a \emph{pseudo-Goldstino}: in the 
   746â†’limit $m_q \to 0$ where the pion becomes exactly massless (a true 
   747â†’Goldstone boson), its fermionic partner also becomes massless (a 
   748â†’true Goldstino). The finite quark masses explicitly break both 
   749â†’chiral symmetry (giving $m_\pi \neq 0$) and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   750â†’(giving $m_\mu \neq m_\pi$), with the splitting controlled by 
   751â†’$f_\pi$:
   752â†’\begin{equation}
   753â†’m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2\,.
   754â†’\end{equation}
   755â†’
   756â†’This is the Volkov--Akulov relation for the pseudo-Goldstino: the 
   757â†’mass splitting between the scalar (pion) and its fermionic partner 
   758â†’(muon) is set by the SUSY-breaking scale squared.
   759â†’
   760â†’The VA mechanism is not a separate breaking from the F-term: it is 
   761â†’the \emph{low-energy, composite-level description} of the same 
   762â†’breaking. At the microscopic level, $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ 
   763â†’in the meson superfield; at the composite level, this manifests 
   764â†’as the Goldstino coupling with $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$. These 
   765â†’two quantities---the Goldstino decay constant and the pion decay 
   766â†’constant---are \emph{identified} in the sBootstrap; this is the 
   767â†’central claim, not a derivation.
   768â†’
   769â†’The identification should not be confused with the standard relation 
   770â†’between the $F$-term VEV and the chiral condensate. In standard 
   771â†’SQCD, $\langle F\rangle \sim |\qqbar|/f_\pi \sim (250\MeV)^3/92\MeV 
   772â†’\approx 170{,}000\MeV^2$, while $f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2$---a 
   773â†’factor of $\sim 20$ discrepancy. The GOR relation 
   774â†’$m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$ connects these through the 
   775â†’small quark masses, but does not equate them. The sBootstrap 
   776â†’identification $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is therefore a 
   777â†’\emph{low-energy effective} statement about the composite sector, 
   778â†’not a microscopic identity. It says: the scale at which SUSY 
   779â†’appears broken in the meson--lepton spectrum is $f_\pi$, regardless 
   780â†’of the microscopic $F$-term that produces this breaking.
   781â†’
   782â†’The VA realization gives more than a mass formula---it organizes the 
   783â†’entire breaking narrative. In the chiral limit ($m_q \to 0$), both 
   784â†’pion and muon are exactly massless: the pion as the Goldstone boson 
   785â†’of broken chiral symmetry, the muon as the Goldstino of broken 
   786â†’sBootstrap SUSY. This is the \emph{exact seed}: the $B$-meson 
   787â†’direction sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$), and the 
   788â†’$\pi$--$\mu$ pair is massless. Turning on quark masses ($m_q \neq 0$) 
   789â†’does three things simultaneously:
   790â†’\begin{enumerate}
   791â†’\item Gives the pion its GOR mass (lifting the flat direction for $\pi$).
   792â†’\item Gives the muon its mass (the pseudo-Goldstino acquires mass 
   793â†’when the explicit breaking parameter $m_q$ is nonzero).
   794â†’\item Generates the $B$-meson mass (the flat direction for $\Phi_0$ 
   795â†’is lifted by the Coleman--Weinberg potential, i.e., by the same 
   796â†’quark masses that feed the GOR formula).
   797â†’\end{enumerate}
   798â†’The splitting between (1) and (2) is $f_\pi^2$---the VA relation. 
   799â†’The seed-to-bloom transition of \S\ref{sec:two_tuples} is the same 
   800â†’physics seen from the charge-space perspective: $m_q \neq 0$ lifts 
   801â†’the zero, generates the $b$/$B$ mass, flips the sign of $\zch_s$ 
   802â†’and $\zch_\pi$, and produces the full triple. The VA equation 
   803â†’\eqref{eq:pimu} is not one fact among many; it is the generating 
   804â†’relation from which the bloom follows.
   805â†’
   806â†’A note on quantum numbers: the Goldstino of spontaneously broken 
   807â†’spacetime SUSY is a neutral fermion (the fermionic component of the 
   808â†’superfield whose $F$-term breaks SUSY). The muon carries electric 
   809â†’charge and lepton number. The sBootstrap SUSY, however, is not a 
   810â†’spacetime symmetry but an emergent hadronic symmetry---a 
   811â†’Miyazawa-type superalgebra \cite{Miyazawa} acting on composite 
   812â†’states. In this context, the ``Goldstino'' need not carry the 
   813â†’quantum numbers of a spacetime supercharge; it carries the quantum 
   814â†’numbers appropriate to the broken hadronic generator. The muon's 
   815â†’charge is inherited from the pion multiplet it partners.
   816â†’
   817â†’\subsection{D-term breaking: electromagnetic splittings}
   818â†’\label{subsec:dterm}
   819â†’
   820â†’The unbroken $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ gauge symmetry allows a 
   821â†’Fayet--Iliopoulos (FI) term \cite{FayetIliopoulos}:
   822â†’\begin{equation}
   823â†’\mathcal{L}_{\text{FI}} = \xi\,D_{\text{em}}\,,
   824â†’\end{equation}
   825â†’which shifts scalar masses by $\delta m^2 = Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi$, 
   826â†’where $Q_{\text{em}}$ is the electric charge. This is the 
   827â†’supersymmetric version of the electromagnetic mass difference.
   828â†’
   829â†’In QCD without SUSY, the electromagnetic mass splitting of the 
   830â†’pion is given by the Das--Guralnik--Mathur--Low--Young (DGMLY) 
   831â†’formula \cite{DGMLY1967}:
   832â†’\begin{equation}
   833â†’m_{\pi^\pm}^2 - m_{\pi^0}^2 = \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,
   834â†’\frac{m_\rho^2}{f_\pi^2}\,f_\pi^2 
   835â†’\approx (35.5\MeV)^2\,,
   836â†’\end{equation}
   837â†’which in the SUSY framework becomes a D-term contribution:
   838â†’\begin{equation}
   839â†’\xi \sim \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,m_\rho^2 
   840â†’\sim (35\MeV)^2\,.
   841â†’\end{equation}
   842â†’
   843â†’In charge space, the D-term shifts $\zch$ by an amount proportional 
   844â†’to $Q_{\text{em}}$:
   845â†’\begin{equation}
   846â†’\zch \to \zch + \delta\zch\,, \qquad 
   847â†’\delta\zch \approx \frac{Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi}{2\zch}\,.
   848â†’\end{equation}
   849â†’For the pion ($Q_{\text{em}} = \pm 1$, $\zch \approx 11.8$): 
   850â†’$\delta\zch \approx 0.2\MeV^{1/2}$, matching the observed 
   851â†’$\zch_{\pi^\pm} - \zch_{\pi^0} = 11.82 - 11.62 = 0.20\MeV^{1/2}$.
   852â†’
   853â†’The D-term effect is subleading to the F-term: 
   854â†’$\xi \sim (35\MeV)^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 \sim (92\MeV)^2$. It 
   855â†’operates \emph{within} each supermultiplet entry, splitting charged 
   856â†’from neutral mesons, while the F-term splits bosons from fermions 
   857â†’across the supermultiplet.
   858â†’
   859â†’Witten's inequality \cite{Witten1983} guarantees 
   860â†’$m_{\pi^\pm} > m_{\pi^0}$ in QCD with electromagnetism---the 
   861â†’charged pion is always heavier. In the SUSY framework this 
   862â†’becomes the statement that the D-term contribution is positive 
   863â†’for $Q_{\text{em}} \neq 0$, consistent with the FI sign.
   864â†’
   865â†’\subsection{Mapping the breaking to the tuples}
   866â†’\label{subsec:mapping}
   867â†’
   868â†’The tuple structure maps onto a layered breaking with two independent 
   869â†’mechanisms (F-term and D-term) plus a separate heavy-sector origin. 
   870â†’The Volkov--Akulov realization of \S\ref{subsec:va} is not a third 
   871â†’mechanism: it is the low-energy, composite-level description of the 
   872â†’same F-term breaking. The VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} \emph{sets the 
   873â†’scale} ($f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$); the O'Raifeartaigh/SQCD dynamics 
   874â†’of \S\ref{subsec:fterm} \emph{provides the mechanism} 
   875â†’($\langle F\rangle \neq 0$).
   876â†’
   877â†’\paragraph{Layer 1: F-term/VA (chiral condensate, scale $\sim f_\pi$).}
   878â†’The chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry 
   879â†’and the sBootstrap SUSY. This is the primary breaking, affecting 
   880â†’the light sector. The seed triples $(0, s, c)$ and $(0, \pi, D_s)$ 
   881â†’are the pre-breaking configurations; the massless member is the flat 
   882â†’direction whose $F$-term is forced nonzero by confinement. After 
   883â†’breaking:
   884â†’\begin{itemize}
   885â†’\item The zero member is lifted and acquires negative charge 
   886â†’($\zch < 0$), becoming the sign-flipped member of the full triple 
   887â†’($-s$, $-\pi$).
   888â†’\item The full triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ emerge, 
   889â†’incorporating the $b$-quark and $B$-meson sectors.
   890â†’\item The lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the fermionic shadow: 
   891â†’the leptons acquire masses through the same condensate, with the 
   892â†’muon as pseudo-Goldstino.
   893â†’\end{itemize}
   894â†’
   895â†’\paragraph{Layer 2: D-term (electromagnetism, scale $\sim \alpha_{\text{em}} m_\rho$).}
   896â†’The $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI term generates electromagnetic mass 
   897â†’splittings within each multiplet. This is a perturbative correction 
   898â†’on top of the F-term breaking, affecting only charged particles. 
   899â†’It does not create new Koide triples but shifts existing ones by 
   900â†’$O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$.
   901â†’
   902â†’\paragraph{Layer 3: The heavy sector (scale $\sim v_0^{(cbt)}$).}
   903â†’The $(c,b,t)$ triple at $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ operates far 
   904â†’above the chiral scale. It has no seed (no massless member), no 
   905â†’meson shadow (top does not hadronize), and its breaking may be 
   906â†’connected to a different sector of the theory---possibly the 
   907â†’electroweak Higgs mechanism.
   908â†’
   909â†’This layered structure involves three distinct scales, each with 
   910â†’different physical meaning:
   911â†’\begin{align}
   912â†’\text{F-term (boson--fermion splitting):} &\quad 
   913â†’f_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \quad f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2\,; \\
   914â†’\text{D-term (EM fine structure):} &\quad 
   915â†’\xi^{1/2} \approx 35\MeV\,, \quad \xi \approx 1{,}200\MeV^2\,; \\
   916â†’\text{Heavy sector (Koide scale):} &\quad 
   917â†’v_0^{(cbt)} \approx 172\MeV^{1/2}\,, \quad (v_0^{(cbt)})^2 \approx 29{,}600\MeV\,.
   918â†’\end{align}
   919â†’Comparing the squared scales: $f_\pi^2 \gg \xi$ (F-term dominates 
   920â†’over D-term by a factor $\sim 7$, consistent with the D-term being 
   921â†’a perturbative $O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$ correction). The heavy-sector 
   922â†’scale $(v_0^{(cbt)})^2$ carries dimensions of $\MeV$ (not $\MeV^2$) 
   923â†’because $v_0$ is a charge ($\MeV^{1/2}$), not an energy; direct 
   924â†’comparison with $f_\pi$ requires specifying which physical quantity 
   925â†’is being compared.
   926â†’
   927â†’\subsection{The O'Raifeartaigh structure in SQCD}
   928â†’
   929â†’In Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, the quantum constraint 
   930â†’\eqref{eq:seiberg} provides a natural setting for the 
   931â†’O'Raifeartaigh mechanism. The constraint $\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ 
   932â†’means the moduli space has a specific geometry: not all field directions 
   933â†’are flat. When quark masses are added via a tree-level superpotential 
   934â†’$W_{\text{tree}} = m_i M^i{}_i$ (sum over flavors), the $F$-terms become:
   935â†’\begin{equation}
   936â†’F_{M^i{}_j} = m_i\,\delta^i_j + \Lambda^6\,(\text{constraint terms})\,.
   937â†’\end{equation}
   938â†’
   939â†’For the seed triple $(0, s, c)$, the ``$0$'' member corresponds to a 
   940â†’direction in the meson matrix where the tree-level mass vanishes---the 
   941â†’entry involving the lightest quark combination. In the exact chiral 
   942â†’limit ($m_u = m_d = 0$), this entry is truly massless, and the 
   943â†’constraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} forces $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ for 
   944â†’that entry. This is the dynamical O'Raifeartaigh mechanism: 
   945â†’confinement (encoded in $\Lambda^6$) plays the role of the parameter 
   946â†’$f$ in the superpotential, and the impossibility of setting all 
   947â†’$F$-terms to zero is a consequence of the strong dynamics rather 
   948â†’than a choice of superpotential.
   949â†’
   950â†’The ISS mechanism (Intriligator--Seiberg--Shih \cite{ISS2006}) 
   951â†’establishes metastable SUSY-breaking vacua for $N_c < N_f < 3N_c/2$, 
   952â†’where the rank condition in the magnetic dual prevents all $F$-terms 
   953â†’from vanishing. The sBootstrap case $N_f = N_c = 3$ is strictly 
   954â†’\emph{below} the ISS window---at $N_f = N_c$ the low-energy 
   955â†’description is qualitatively different, governed by the 
   956â†’quantum-modified constraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} on the moduli space 
   957â†’rather than by a free magnetic dual. However, the physical QCD has 
   958â†’$N_f = 3$ light flavors and $N_f = 4$--$5$ if charm and bottom are 
   959â†’included at their respective thresholds, which does fall within the 
   960â†’ISS window for $N_c = 3$. The sBootstrap's multi-scale structure---with 
   961â†’the seed triples involving $s$, $c$, and $b$ quarks at different 
   962â†’scales---may require treating the effective $N_f$ as 
   963â†’scale-dependent, entering the ISS regime above the strange threshold.
   964â†’
   965â†’\subsection{How the three supermultiplets join}
   966â†’
   967â†’The seed-to-bloom transition generates three separate Koide triples. 
   968â†’But the full sBootstrap requires these triples to be \emph{embedded 
   969â†’in a single meson matrix}. The $3\times 3$ light-flavor matrix is:
   970â†’
   971â†’\begin{equation}
   972â†’M^{(3)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   973â†’u\bar{u} & u\bar{d} & u\bar{s} \\
   974â†’d\bar{u} & d\bar{d} & d\bar{s} \\
   975â†’s\bar{u} & s\bar{d} & s\bar{s}
   976â†’\end{pmatrix}
   977â†’\;\longleftrightarrow\;
   978â†’\begin{pmatrix}
   979â†’\pi^0/\eta & \pi^+ & K^+ \\
   980â†’\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 \\
   981â†’K^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta'
   982â†’\end{pmatrix}.
   983â†’\end{equation}
   984â†’
   985â†’When charm and bottom are included, the matrix extends to $5\times 5$. 
   986â†’We display it schematically, with the members of the meson Koide 
   987â†’triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ marked by boxes:
   988â†’
   989â†’\begin{equation}
   990â†’M^{(5)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   991â†’\pi^0/\eta & \boxed{\pi^+} & K^+ & D^0 & \boxed{B^+} \\
   992â†’\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 & D^- & B^0 \\
   993â†’K^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta' & \boxed{D_s^-} & B_s \\
   994â†’\bar{D}^0 & D^+ & D_s^+ & \eta_c & B_c \\
   995â†’B^- & \bar{B}^0 & \bar{B}_s & \bar{B}_c & \eta_b
   996â†’\end{pmatrix},
   997â†’\end{equation}
   998â†’where rows are indexed by $(u, d, s, c, b)$ and columns by 
   999â†’$(\bar{u}, \bar{d}, \bar{s}, \bar{c}, \bar{b})$.
  1000â†’
  1001â†’The three members of the meson Koide triple occupy different 
  1002â†’off-diagonal blocks of this matrix: $\pi^+ = u\bar{d}$ is in the 
  1003â†’light $(u,d)$ block, $D_s^- = s\bar{c}$ bridges the light-charm 
  1004â†’sector, and $B^+ = u\bar{b}$ bridges the light-bottom sector. They 
  1005â†’do not form a ``slice'' in any simple linear-algebraic sense---they 
  1006â†’are scattered entries connected by the Koide condition on their 
  1007â†’\emph{masses}, not by their positions in the matrix. The meson seed 
  1008â†’$(0, \pi, D_s)$ drops the $B^+$ and replaces it with zero (the 
  1009â†’entry that would involve the top quark, absent from the matrix).
  1010â†’
  1011â†’This scattered structure is the central puzzle: the Koide triples 
  1012â†’are \emph{mass relations} among matrix entries, not submatrices. 
  1013â†’The question is whether the Seiberg constraint 
  1014â†’$\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ (appropriately generalized to 
  1015â†’$5 \times 5$), combined with the SUSY-breaking dynamics, produces 
  1016â†’correlations among these scattered entries that enforce the 
  1017â†’energy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
  1018â†’
  1019â†’\subsection{The energy balance as a fixed point}
  1020â†’\label{subsec:fixedpoint}
  1021â†’One possibility is that the energy-balance condition 
  1022â†’$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a \emph{dynamical attractor}: 
  1023â†’any set of three masses evolving under the SUSY-breaking dynamics 
  1024â†’flows toward the Koide surface $Q = 2/3$, as a fixed point of the 
  1025â†’RG flow for the soft SUSY-breaking parameters. This is speculative, 
  1026â†’but the observation that the condition holds for fundamental fermions 
  1027â†’(leptons, quarks) \emph{and} composite bosons (mesons) across very 
  1028â†’different scales lends it plausibility. A concrete test would be to 
  1029â†’show that the one-loop soft-mass RG equations in $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
  1030â†’SQCD with appropriate boundary conditions flow toward 
  1031â†’$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$; this calculation has not yet been 
  1032â†’performed.
  1033â†’
  1034â†’\subsection{The exact seed and what breaks it}
  1035â†’\label{subsec:exactseed}
  1036â†’
  1037â†’The seed configuration $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ requires \emph{two} 
  1038â†’independent conditions:
  1039â†’\begin{enumerate}
  1040â†’\item One member at $\zch = 0$ exactly (the flat direction).
  1041â†’\item The ratio of the massive members equals $2 - \sqrt{3}$:
  1042â†’\begin{equation}
  1043â†’\label{eq:seedratio}
  1044â†’\frac{\sqrt{m_a}}{\sqrt{m_b}} = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.
  1045â†’\end{equation}
  1046â†’\end{enumerate}
  1047â†’These are logically distinct. The chiral limit ($m_u = m_d = 0$, 
  1048â†’$\qqbar \neq 0$) enforces condition (1) for the meson sector: the 
  1049â†’pion is an exact Goldstone boson, $m_\pi = 0$, and in the 
  1050â†’picture of \S\ref{subsec:fterm} the $B$-meson direction sits at 
  1051â†’the flat direction before acquiring mass. But the chiral limit says 
  1052â†’\emph{nothing} about condition (2)---it does not constrain 
  1053â†’$\sqrt{m_{D_s}}/\sqrt{m_B}$ or $\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ 
  1054â†’to equal $2 - \sqrt{3}$.
  1055â†’
  1056â†’The physical deviations from the exact ratio are small but 
  1057â†’measurable:
  1058â†’
  1059â†’\begin{center}
  1060â†’\begin{tabular}{lccc}
  1061â†’\toprule
  1062â†’Seed & Exact ratio & Physical ratio & Deviation \\
  1063â†’\midrule
  1064â†’$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1065â†’$\sqrt{m_\pi}/\sqrt{m_{D_s}} = 0.2663$ & $-0.6\%$ \\
  1066â†’$(0, s, c)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1067â†’$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c} = 0.2712$ & $+1.2\%$ \\
  1068â†’$(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1069â†’$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 0.2439$ & $-9.0\%$ \\
  1070â†’\bottomrule
  1071â†’\end{tabular}
  1072â†’\end{center}
  1073â†’
  1074â†’The meson seed is closest to the exact ratio (0.6\% deviation). 
  1075â†’The quark seed deviates by 1.2\%. A natural thought is that QCD 
  1076â†’running might shift the quark ratio: perhaps at some scale $\mu^*$ 
  1077â†’the ratio $\sqrt{m_s(\mu^*)}/\sqrt{m_c(\mu^*)}$ hits $2 - \sqrt{3}$ 
  1078â†’exactly. But this is \emph{not possible within QCD alone}. At leading 
  1079â†’order, all quark masses run with the same anomalous dimension 
  1080â†’$\gamma_m$:
  1081â†’\begin{equation}
  1082â†’m_i(\mu) = m_i(\mu_0)\left(\frac{\alpha_s(\mu)}{\alpha_s(\mu_0)}\right)^{\gamma_m/\beta_0},
  1083â†’\end{equation}
  1084â†’so the ratio $m_s(\mu)/m_c(\mu)$---and therefore 
  1085â†’$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$---is an RG invariant of QCD. Higher-order 
  1086â†’corrections break this invariance, but only at the $O(\alpha_s^2)$ 
  1087â†’level, far too small to account for 1.2\%.
  1088â†’
  1089â†’The quark seed deviation is therefore \emph{intrinsic}: the 
  1090â†’$\overline{\text{MS}}$ mass ratio $m_s/m_c$ is a fixed number 
  1091â†’(at any QCD scale), and it misses the Koide target by 1.2\%. 
  1092â†’Three possible explanations present themselves:
  1093â†’
  1094â†’\paragraph{Electroweak running.} Above the electroweak scale, 
  1095â†’quark masses are controlled by Yukawa couplings $y_i$ to the 
  1096â†’Higgs, which \emph{do} run differently for different flavors: 
  1097â†’the $\beta$-functions for $y_s$ and $y_c$ involve different 
  1098â†’electroweak quantum numbers ($SU(2)_L$ doublet structure, 
  1099â†’hypercharge). The ratio $y_s/y_c$ evolves with scale, and 
  1100â†’there may exist a scale $\mu^*$ above the electroweak 
  1101â†’threshold where $\sqrt{y_s(\mu^*)}/\sqrt{y_c(\mu^*)} = 2 - \sqrt{3}$. 
  1102â†’This would identify the ``seed scale'' as an electroweak-sector 
  1103â†’phenomenon, not a QCD one---consistent with the sBootstrap's 
  1104â†’identification of the breaking with $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ 
  1105â†’rather than $SU(3)_c$.
  1106â†’
  1107â†’\paragraph{Charge running.} Even if mass ratios are QCD-invariant, 
  1108â†’the Koide decomposition $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ may have its own 
  1109â†’flow. The vacuum energy $v_0 = z_0^2$ sets the common scale, 
  1110â†’and the fluctuations $z_k$ encode the deviations. If the 
  1111â†’energy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a 
  1112â†’dynamical attractor (as suggested in \S\ref{subsec:fixedpoint}), 
  1113â†’the $z_k$ may evolve under a flow in which the \emph{ratio} 
  1114â†’$z_a/z_b$ approaches $2 - \sqrt{3}$ at the fixed point while 
  1115â†’the individual masses (and their QCD-invariant ratios) remain 
  1116â†’unchanged. In this picture, the 1.2\% deviation measures how 
  1117â†’far the quark sector is from the attractor.
  1118â†’
  1119â†’\paragraph{Meson vs.\ quark deviations.} The meson deviation 
  1120â†’(0.6\%) is smaller than the quark deviation (1.2\%), and for 
  1121â†’a different reason: meson masses are \emph{not} simple functions 
  1122â†’of quark mass ratios. The pion mass is given by the GOR 
  1123â†’relation $m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$; the $D_s$ 
  1124â†’mass involves heavy-quark effective theory; and both receive 
  1125â†’chiral perturbation theory corrections. These nonperturbative 
  1126â†’QCD effects are the dominant source of the meson seed deviation, 
  1127â†’and are in principle calculable at NLO in ChPT.
  1128â†’
  1129â†’\paragraph{A remarkable feature of the VA relation.} The 
  1130â†’foundational equation $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2$ contains 
  1131â†’no QCD coupling constant: no $\alpha_s$, no $N_c$, no 
  1132â†’$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$. It is a pure mass relation among three 
  1133â†’experimentally measured quantities---two masses and a decay 
  1134â†’constant---holding to 2.2\%. In the sBootstrap, this is natural: 
  1135â†’the relation is a SUSY mass splitting, set by the breaking 
  1136â†’scale $f_\pi$, and SUSY mass relations do not involve the gauge 
  1137â†’coupling of the confining theory. Standard QCD \emph{explains} 
  1138â†’$f_\pi$ in terms of $\qqbar$ and $\alpha_s$; the sBootstrap 
  1139â†’\emph{uses} $f_\pi$ as the fundamental parameter, with QCD 
  1140â†’providing the dynamical origin but not appearing in the mass 
  1141â†’formula. The absence of QCD factors in \eqref{eq:pimu} is a 
  1142â†’hint that the relevant symmetry is not $SU(3)_c$ but the 
  1143â†’hadronic superalgebra that connects pions to leptons.
  1144â†’
  1145â†’The lepton ``seed'' has the worst ratio match ($-9\%$), which 
  1146â†’is consistent with the observation that the lepton triple is 
  1147â†’\emph{not} a true seed: $m_e \neq 0$, and the electron was never 
  1148â†’at the flat direction. The leptons are an ``almost-seed'' whose 
  1149â†’excellent $Q = 0.66666$ arises not from the seed structure but 
  1150â†’from the smallness of $m_e$, which makes the two conditions 
  1151â†’approximately degenerate. For the leptons, condition (1) is 
  1152â†’approximate rather than exact, and condition (2) is correspondingly 
  1153â†’relaxed.
  1154â†’
  1155â†’\subsection{What the $(c,b,t)$ triple tells us}
  1156â†’
  1157â†’The $(c,b,t)$ triple ($Q = 0.6695$, $v_0^2 = 29.6\GeV$) has 
  1158â†’neither a seed nor a meson shadow. It operates at a scale far 
  1159â†’above $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, suggesting its origin is different:
  1160â†’
  1161â†’\begin{enumerate}
  1162â†’\item It may be governed by the electroweak Higgs mechanism rather 
  1163â†’than the chiral condensate. The top Yukawa $y_t \approx 1$ is 
  1164â†’``natural'' in the sense that $m_t \approx v_{\text{EW}}/\sqrt{2}$; 
  1165â†’the Koide condition on $(c,b,t)$ would then constrain the Yukawa 
  1166â†’coupling matrix.
  1167â†’\item It may represent a Volkov--Akulov realization at the 
  1168â†’electroweak scale: if there is a second SUSY breaking at 
  1169â†’$f' \sim v_{\text{EW}}$, the $(c,b,t)$ triple would be its 
  1170â†’analog of the $(\pi, \mu)$ system, but with $f'$ replacing $f_\pi$.
  1171â†’\item Or it may be a purely kinematic accident---the quark masses 
  1172â†’happen to satisfy the Koide condition without a dynamical reason. 
  1173â†’The 0.42\% precision makes this unlikely but not impossible.
  1174â†’\end{enumerate}
  1175â†’
  1176â†’Interpretation (1) is most natural within the sBootstrap: the 
  1177â†’$(c,b,t)$ triple involves quarks whose masses are dominated by 
  1178â†’Yukawa couplings to the Higgs, not by the chiral condensate, and the 
  1179â†’Koide condition would then be a constraint on the Yukawa matrix 
  1180â†’rather than on the SQCD dynamics. The chiral sector and the 
  1181â†’electroweak sector would each independently produce a Koide-type 
  1182â†’energy balance, with the charm and bottom quarks serving as the 
  1183â†’bridge between the two.
  1184â†’
  1185â†’If interpretation (2) is also invoked, the sBootstrap has two breaking 
  1186â†’scales:
  1187â†’\begin{equation}
  1188â†’f_{\text{chiral}} = f_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \qquad
  1189â†’f_{\text{EW}} \sim v_{\text{EW}} \approx 246\GeV\,,
  1190â†’\end{equation}
  1191â†’and the full spectrum requires both. The light sector (leptons, 
  1192â†’light mesons) is governed by $f_\pi$; the heavy sector (top, and 
  1193â†’by extension charm and bottom through the Koide network) is 
  1194â†’governed by $v_{\text{EW}}$. The charm and bottom quarks, 
  1195â†’appearing in both the $(0,s,c)/(-s,c,b)$ light-sector triples 
  1196â†’and the $(c,b,t)$ heavy-sector triple, are the ``bridges'' 
  1197â†’between the two scales.

  1200â†’\section{The Electron Mass and the Lepton Seed}
  1201â†’\label{sec:electron}
  1202â†’%======================================================================
  1203â†’
  1204â†’The charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the ``almost-seed'': 
  1205â†’$\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$ is small but nonzero. The electron's 
  1206â†’charge is near zero because of cancellation in $\zch_e = z_0 + z_e$:
  1207â†’\begin{equation}
  1208â†’v_0 = 17.716, \quad z_e = -17.001, \quad 
  1209â†’\zch_e = 17.716 - 17.001 = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}\,.
  1210â†’\end{equation}
  1211â†’A 96\% cancellation. The mass $m_e = 0.715^2 = 0.511\MeV$ is the 
  1212â†’square of something already small.
  1213â†’

  1215â†’embedding, not fine-tuning: the energy balance allows perturbations 
  1216â†’as large as $z_0$. Every Koide triple has one member with 
  1217â†’$|\zch|/v_0$ small:

  1232â†’The electron is by far the most extreme case, consistent with the

  1236â†’\section{Symmetry Restoration}
  1237â†’\label{sec:restoration}
  1238â†’%======================================================================
  1239â†’
  1240â†’The broken spectrum reassembles as symmetry-breaking effects are 
  1241â†’removed:
  1242â†’
  1243â†’\begin{enumerate}
  1244â†’\item \textbf{Turn off QED:} Electromagnetic splittings vanish 
  1245â†’($\pi^\pm = \pi^0$, $K^\pm = K^0$). D-term effects disappear.
  1246â†’\item \textbf{Set $m_u = m_d$:} Exact isospin.
  1247â†’\item \textbf{Set $m_s = m_d$:} $SU(3)_V$ restored, 
  1248â†’$\pi = K = \eta$. Koide splitting $v_1 \to 0$; lepton partners 
  1249â†’degenerate.
  1250â†’\item \textbf{Send $m_q \to 0$:} Chiral limit, all meson charges 
  1251â†’$\to 0$. If sBootstrap SUSY restores, all light lepton masses 
  1252â†’vanish too. This is the seed configuration: $(0, 0, 0)$.
  1253â†’\end{enumerate}
  1254â†’
  1255â†’The seed triples are the ``penultimate'' stage: one member has 
  1256â†’reached zero, but the other two retain the $2 - \sqrt{3}$ ratio. 
  1257â†’Full triples are the final broken state. The lepton triple, 
  1258â†’trapped between seed and bloom, reveals the hierarchy: 
  1259â†’chiral breaking $\to$ seed $\to$ bloom $\to$ full spectrum.
  1260â†’
  1261â†’The direction of the flow also matters: approaching the chiral 
  1262â†’limit, the pion charge rises from $\zch = -11.81$ toward zero 
  1263â†’from below. It crosses zero exactly in the chiral limit. This 
  1264â†’means the pion was ``born negative''---its charge sign is set by 
  1265â†’the direction of chiral symmetry breaking, and the seed 
  1266â†’$(0, \pi, D_s)$ is the configuration at the moment of crossing.

  1290â†’The sBootstrap adds: superspace chirality (boson vs.\ fermion 
  1291â†’distinction within a multiplet) is broken by the same condensate 
  1292â†’that breaks flavor chirality, with $f_{\text{SUSY}} = f_\pi$. The 
  1293â†’energy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ may be 
  1294â†’related: the breaking is ``as large as the background 
  1295â†’allows''---maximal, not perturbative.

  1298â†’\section{Open Problems}
  1299â†’\label{sec:open}
  1300â†’%======================================================================
  1301â†’
  1302â†’\subsection{The diquark sector}
  1303â†’The Pauli theorem (\S\ref{subsec:pauli}) proves that no $J=0$, 
  1304â†’color-$\bar{3}$ diquark can have symmetric flavor, closing all 
  1305â†’field-theoretic escape routes ($L=0$, P-wave, or higher). The 
  1306â†’sBootstrap's $\mathbf{15}$ diquark must therefore be non-composite: 
  1307â†’either an algebraic image of the Miyazawa supercharge or a 
  1308â†’fundamental $SO(32)$ string state. The exotic $+4/3$-charged 
  1309â†’diquarks predicted by the $\mathbf{15}$ are a testable consequence.
  1310â†’
  1311â†’\subsection{What determines the Koide phase?}
  1312â†’The energy balance constrains but does not uniquely fix $\delta$. 
  1313â†’The value $\delta_\ell \approx 2/9$ for leptons must come from 
  1314â†’dynamics. Is it a fixed point?
  1315â†’
  1316â†’\subsection{Connecting the three tuples}
  1317â†’The light sector (seed $\to$ bloom, scale $\sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$) 
  1318â†’and heavy sector ($c,b,t$, scale $\sim v_{\text{EW}}$) must connect. 
  1319â†’The charm and bottom quarks appear in both, providing the links.
  1320â†’
  1321â†’\subsection{The baryon sector}
  1322â†’In Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, baryons are part of the 
  1323â†’low-energy spectrum. Their SUSY partners are ``baryonic fermions'' 
  1324â†’with no known candidates among observed particles.
  1325â†’
  1326â†’\subsection{The strong CP problem}
  1327â†’The principle ``Nature abhors chirality'' predicts $\bar{\theta} = 0$ 
  1328â†’but provides no mechanism.
  1329â†’
  1330â†’\subsection{The kaon--muon puzzle}
  1331â†’The SUSY splitting $m_K^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx (482\MeV)^2$ is much 
  1332â†’larger than $f_K^2 \approx (110\MeV)^2$, showing that the breaking 
  1333â†’is strongly flavor-dependent.

  1335â†’%======================================================================
  1336â†’\section{Conclusions}
  1337â†’\label{sec:conclusions}
  1338â†’%======================================================================
  1339â†’
  1340â†’Three principal results:
  1341â†’
  1342â†’\paragraph{1.\ The Koide formula is energy equipartition.}
  1343â†’$Q = 2/3$ is $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$: perturbation energies 
  1344â†’match the vacuum energy. The signed-charge framework resolves the 
  1345â†’electron mass puzzle (near-cancellation, not fine-tuning) and reveals 
  1346â†’that mesons satisfy Koide with $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to $0.1\%$.
  1347â†’
  1348â†’\paragraph{2.\ Seed triples set the stage for SUSY breaking.}
  1349â†’The quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ share the 
  1350â†’universal ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$ and operate at $v_0^2 \sim 
  1351â†’230$--$350\MeV$, close to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 = 314\MeV$. 
  1352â†’The massless member is the necessary condition for F-term 
  1353â†’(O'Raifeartaigh) SUSY breaking. Upon breaking, seeds bloom into 
  1354â†’full triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi,D_s,B)$. The lepton triple 
  1355â†’is the ``almost-seed'' that has barely bloomed.
  1356â†’
  1357â†’\paragraph{3.\ Two breaking mechanisms plus a heavy-sector origin.}
  1358â†’F-term breaking (via the massless seed member) generates the light 
  1359â†’sector; the Volkov--Akulov relation $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is its 
  1360â†’low-energy realization, not a separate mechanism. D-term breaking 
  1361â†’($U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI parameter) provides electromagnetic 
  1362â†’splittings as a perturbative correction. The heavy triple 
  1363â†’$(c,b,t)$ stands apart: no seed, no meson shadow, scale far above 
  1364â†’$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, possibly connected to electroweak breaking.
  1365â†’
  1366â†’The diquark sector, required to complete the SUSY multiplet count, 
  1367â†’fills the symmetric $\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ of 
  1368â†’$SU(5)_f$---the representation produced naturally by the SUSY 
  1369â†’generator acting on quarks and by the $SO(32)$ decomposition. A 
  1370â†’kinematic theorem (\S\ref{subsec:pauli}) shows that \emph{no} 
  1371â†’$J=0$ color-$\bar{3}$ diquark can have symmetric flavor, regardless 
  1372â†’of orbital angular momentum: the sBootstrap diquark in the 
  1373â†’$\mathbf{15}$ cannot be a $qq$ composite. It must be an 
  1374â†’algebraically generated object (Miyazawa supercharge) or a 
  1375â†’fundamental string-theoretic state ($SO(32)$ adjoint).
  1376â†’
  1377â†’The organizing principle is that each sector of the spectrum begins 
  1378â†’with a massless member (a seed), which is the necessary condition 
  1379â†’for spontaneous SUSY breaking. The breaking lifts the zero, 
  1380â†’generates the full triple, and the energy-balance condition 
  1381â†’$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is preserved as a fixed point 
  1382â†’of the dynamics. The entire charged fermion and pseudoscalar meson 
  1383â†’spectrum is then determined by a small number of parameters: the 
  1384â†’vacuum charges $v_0$ of the three tuples, the Koide phases 
  1385â†’$\delta$ that control the bloom, and the SUSY-breaking scale 
  1386â†’$f_\pi$ that separates bosons from fermions.
