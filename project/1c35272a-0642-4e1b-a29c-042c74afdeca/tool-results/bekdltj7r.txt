  SECTION 1: INSTANTON EFFECTS IN N=1 SQCD
==============================================================================

  For SU(N_c) SQCD with N_f flavors:

  Case N_f < N_c (ADS superpotential):
    W_ADS = (N_c - N_f) * (Lambda^(3N_c - N_f) / det(M))^(1/(N_c - N_f))

  Case N_f = N_c (our case, N_c = N_f = 3):
    No instanton-generated superpotential.
    Instead: quantum deformation of the moduli space:
      det(M) - B*Btilde = Lambda^(2*N_c)

    This is the Seiberg constraint. It modifies the classical moduli space
    but does NOT generate a W_inst.

  Case N_f = N_c + 1:
    W = (M_ij * B^i * Btilde^j - det(M)) / Lambda^(2*N_c - 1)

  One-loop beta function coefficient: b_0 = 3*N_c - N_f = 6
  Lambda^(2*N_c) = Lambda^6 = 2.4414e+14 MeV^6

  The meson vev matrix M_ij ~ <Q_i Qtilde_j>
  For diagonal M with quark masses m_i, at tree level: M_ii ~ Lambda^2/m_i
  (This is the ISS/Seiberg mass relation: meson mass ~ Lambda^2/quark_mass)
    M_uu ~ Lambda^2/m_u = 62500/2.16 = 28935.2 MeV
    M_dd ~ Lambda^2/m_d = 62500/4.67 = 13383.3 MeV
    M_ss ~ Lambda^2/m_s = 62500/93.40 = 669.2 MeV

  Check quantum constraint: det(M_tree) = Lambda^6/(m_u*m_d*m_s)
    det(M_tree) = 2.5913e+11 MeV^3
    Lambda^6 = 2.4414e+14 MeV^6
    Ratio det(M_tree)/Lambda^6 = 1.0614e-03
    This ratio = 1/(m_u*m_d*m_s) [in MeV^-3] = 1.0614e-03

  KEY OBSERVATION: The instanton-generated quantum constraint for N_f=N_c=3
  involves det(M), which depends on products of meson fields M_ij.
  These relate to PRODUCTS of quark masses, not SUMS of sqrt(masses).

  The v0-doubling requires: sum_i sqrt(m_i) = const.
  The quantum moduli space constrains: product_i M_ii = const.

  These are fundamentally different algebraic structures:
  - Instanton vertex: multiplicative (products of zero modes)
  - v0-doubling: additive (sum of square roots)

  For the superpotential (holomorphic), the constraint is det(M) = Lambda^6.
  This is M_1 * M_2 * M_3 = Lambda^6, i.e., product structure.
  There is no way to get sum(sqrt(m_i)) from a holomorphic W.

  However, the KAHLER potential K is non-holomorphic and could in principle
  contain |Tr(sqrt(M))|^2 type terms.

  Multi-instanton Kahler corrections:
  The instanton-anti-instanton contribution to the Kahler potential is:
    delta_K ~ |Lambda|^(2*b0) * F(M, M^dagger)
    |Lambda|^(2*b0) = |Lambda|^12 = 5.9605e+28 MeV^12

  The function F(M, M^dagger) from the 't Hooft vertex involves:
    F ~ product_i |M_ii|^2 * (instanton moduli integral)
  This still has PRODUCT structure, not SUM structure.

==============================================================================
  SECTION 3: FRACTIONAL INSTANTONS AND sqrt(m)
==============================================================================

  On R^3 x S^1 with circumference L, or with center-stabilized holonomy,
  the instanton fractionalizes into N_c = 3 monopole-instantons (BPS and KK).

  Each monopole-instanton carries:
    Action: S_mon = S_inst/N_c = 8*pi^2/(N_c*g^2)
    Fugacity: ~ exp(-S_mon) = exp(-8*pi^2/(N_c*g^2))
    Magnetic charge: under the unbroken U(1)^(N_c-1)
    Fermionic zero modes: 1 per flavor (vs N_f per full instanton)

  CRITICAL: When a quark of mass m_i has its zero mode lifted by the mass,
  the contribution to the monopole-instanton amplitude picks up a factor:

    m_i^(1/2)  (not m_i!)

  This is because the zero mode wavefunction normalization goes as sqrt(m)
  when the zero mode is lifted. Specifically:

    Monopole-instanton amplitude with mass lifting:
      A_mon ~ exp(-S_mon) * product_i (m_i)^(n_i/2)

  where n_i is the number of zero modes for flavor i on that monopole.
  For a SINGLE zero mode per flavor: n_i = 1, so we get sqrt(m_i).

  For SU(3) on R^3 x S^1, there are 3 types of monopole-instantons:
  M_1 (BPS_1), M_2 (BPS_2), M_3 (KK/affine)

  Each M_a carries zero modes for specific flavors.
  In the center-symmetric vacuum, the monopole fugacities are:

  Full instanton = M_1 * M_2 * M_3 (composite)
  Full instanton zero modes: N_f = 3 per chirality (total 2*N_f = 6)
  Split among monopoles: 2 zero modes per monopole (1 per chirality)

  For the 't Hooft vertex with mass lifting:
    Full instanton: product_i m_i  (N_f factors of m_i)
    Single monopole: sqrt(m_i) for the flavor whose zero mode it carries

  KEY: The monopole-instanton effective potential involves a SUM over
  monopole types, each contributing sqrt(m_i) for its flavor.

  The monopole-instanton generated potential on R^3 x S^1:
    W_mon ~ sum_{a=1}^{N_c} exp(-S_a) * sqrt(m_{i(a)})

  If the monopoles M_1, M_2, M_3 couple to flavors 1, 2, 3 respectively:
    W_mon ~ zeta * (sqrt(m_1) + sqrt(m_2) + sqrt(m_3)) + ...

  where zeta = exp(-8*pi^2/(N_c*g^2)) is the monopole fugacity
  and the equality of coefficients holds in the center-symmetric vacuum.

  THIS IS THE SUM OF SQUARE ROOTS STRUCTURE!

  Numerical estimates (alpha_s = 0.3 at ~1 GeV):
    g^2 = 4*pi*alpha_s = 3.7699
    S_mon = 8*pi^2/(N_c*g^2) = 6.9813
    zeta = exp(-S_mon) = 9.290788e-04
    (Very small -- monopole effects are exponentially suppressed)

  Monopole sum: W_mon ~ zeta * sum_i sqrt(m_i)
    sum sqrt(m_i) for (u,d,s) = 13.2951 MeV^(1/2)
    sum sqrt(m_i) for (s,c,b) unsigned = 109.9543 MeV^(1/2)
    sum sqrt(m_i) for (-s,c,b) signed = 90.6256 MeV^(1/2)

    v0_seed = (0 + sqrt(m_s) + sqrt(m_c))/3 = 15.1005
    v0_full = (-sqrt(m_s) + sqrt(m_c) + sqrt(m_b))/3 = 30.2085
    3*v0_seed = 45.3014 = sqrt(m_s) + sqrt(m_c)
    3*v0_full = 90.6256 = -sqrt(m_s) + sqrt(m_c) + sqrt(m_b)
    v0_full/v0_seed = 2.000502

  ANALYSIS: Fractional instantons (monopole-instantons) on R^3 x S^1 produce
  exactly the right algebraic structure: a SUM of sqrt(m_i) terms.

  However, several caveats:
  1. This is on R^3 x S^1, not R^4. The decompactification limit L->infinity
     must be taken carefully (Unsal's continuity conjecture).
  2. The monopole potential enters the SUPERPOTENTIAL, which is holomorphic.
     But sqrt(m) is not holomorphic in the quark mass m.
  3. The sqrt(m) arises from zero-mode lifting, which involves the absolute
     value of the mass: |m|^(1/2). This is non-holomorphic.
  4. In the Kahler potential, the instanton-anti-instanton contribution CAN
     be non-holomorphic, and DOES involve |sqrt(m)|^2 = |m|.

  The resolution may be:
  - The monopole-instanton MEASURE on the monopole moduli space contains
    sqrt(m_i) factors from zero-mode normalization
  - In the Kahler potential (inst-anti-inst), this becomes |sqrt(m_i)|^2 = m_i
  - But CROSS TERMS in the Kahler potential can produce:
    |sum_i sqrt(m_i)|^2 = sum_i m_i + sum_{i<j} 2*sqrt(m_i*m_j)

  This last expression IS non-holomorphic and involves sqrt(m_i*m_j).

  SECTION 4: 'T HOOFT DETERMINANT AND KAHLER CORRECTIONS
==============================================================================

  The 't Hooft instanton vertex for SU(N_c) with N_f flavors:

    V_tH = C * Lambda^(b_0) * det_{ij}(psi_R^i psi_L^j) / rho^{something}

  When quarks have masses m_i, the zero modes are lifted and the vertex becomes:

    V_tH(m) = C * Lambda^(b_0) * product_i m_i * f(m_i * rho)

  The instanton measure includes integration over the instanton size rho:
    d_mu = d_rho/rho^5 * rho^(b_0) * exp(-8*pi^2/g^2(1/rho)) * product_i h(m_i*rho)

  where h(m_i*rho) ~ 1 for m_i*rho << 1 and h(m_i*rho) ~ m_i*rho for m_i*rho >> 1.

  Instanton-anti-instanton contribution to the Kahler potential:
    delta_K ~ integral d_rho1 d_rho2 |V_tH|^2 * G(rho1, rho2, R)

  where G is the instanton-anti-instanton interaction potential.

  |V_tH|^2 ~ |Lambda|^(2*b_0) * |product_i m_i|^2
           = |Lambda|^12 * (product |m_i|)^2

  |product m_i|^2 for (u,d,s) = 887636.2212 MeV^6
  |sum sqrt(m_i)|^2 for (u,d,s) = 176.7591 MeV
  These are not the same (different dimensions even).

  However, the FRACTIONAL instanton picture changes this.
  In the monopole-instanton decomposition:
    V_full = V_M1 * V_M2 * V_M3  (instanton = composite of 3 monopoles)

  The INDIVIDUAL monopole vertices V_Ma each carry sqrt(m_{i(a)}):
    V_Ma ~ exp(-S_mon) * sqrt(m_{i(a)})

  The Kahler potential from monopole-anti-monopole pairs:
    delta_K_mon ~ sum_a |V_Ma|^2 ~ sum_a m_{i(a)}
                = m_1 + m_2 + m_3  (for diagonal pairing)

  Cross terms (monopole_a paired with anti-monopole_b, a != b):
    delta_K_cross ~ sum_{a!=b} V_Ma * V*_Mb ~ sum_{a!=b} sqrt(m_a) * sqrt(m_b)

  The TOTAL monopole contribution to the Kahler potential:
    delta_K_total ~ |sum_a sqrt(m_a)|^2
                  = sum_a m_a + 2*sum_{a<b} sqrt(m_a*m_b)

  Numerical evaluation of |sum sqrt(m_i)|^2:
    (u,d,s): |sum sqrt(m_i)|^2 = 176.7591 MeV
    (s,c,b) unsigned: |sum sqrt(m_i)|^2 = 30729283.5600 MeV
    (-s,c,b) signed: |sum sqrt(m_i)|^2 = 8213.0014 MeV

  CRITICAL QUESTION: Can v0-doubling emerge as a stationary point of delta_K?

  If the effective Kahler potential has the form:
    K_eff = sum_i |Phi_i|^2 + c * |sum_i sqrt(m_i(Phi))|^2 / Lambda^2 + ...

  then the vacuum condition dK/dPhi_i = 0 could constrain the sqrt(m_i) sum.

  The v0-doubling relates TWO triples:
    seed: (0, m_s, m_c)   -> 3*v0_seed = sqrt(m_s) + sqrt(m_c)

     - VERDICT: Does NOT directly generate v0-doubling

     - The SUM arises because monopoles contribute ADDITIVELY

     - CAVEAT: This is on R^3 x S^1, not R^4
     - Unsal's continuity conjecture connects to R^4 physics
     - VERDICT: RIGHT algebraic structure; decompactification needed
     - Rating: PROMISING, needs further work

     - |sum sqrt(m_i)|^2 = sum m_i + cross terms
     - Non-holomorphic (v0 is non-holomorphic — matches!)
     - Monopole-anti-monopole pairs in the Kahler potential
       naturally produce |v0|^2 terms
     - VERDICT: Could accommodate v0 constraint as Kahler correction
     - Rating: CONSISTENT, not predictive alone

     - Z_3 center symmetry matches Koide Z_3
     - sqrt(sigma) = 440 MeV, not numerically close to v0 quantities
     - No direct mechanism for sum(sqrt(m))
     - VERDICT: Symmetry connection only, no dynamical mechanism
     - Rating: SUGGESTIVE but WEAK
