    80‚Üítriples} with a massless member that grow into full triples upon 
    81‚Üísymmetry breaking.
    82‚Üí
    83‚ÜíSecond, \textbf{the full sBootstrap has three Koide tuples, not one}, 
    84‚Üíand the breaking structure must generate all three: a light sector 
    85‚Üí$(0,s,c) \to (-s,c,b)$ seen in both quarks and mesons, a lepton 
    86‚Üísector $(e\!\approx\!0, \mu, \tau)$, and a heavy sector $(c,b,t)$ 
    87‚Üíthat decouples from meson physics because the top quark does not 
    88‚Üíhadronize. The diquark partners of quarks, needed to complete the 
    89‚ÜíSUSY multiplets, fill the symmetric $\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ 
    90‚Üíof $SU(5)$ flavor---the representation produced naturally by the 
    91‚ÜíSUSY generator and by the $SO(32)$ decomposition. A kinematic 
    92‚Üítheorem shows that no $J=0$, color-$\bar{3}$ diquark can have 
    93‚Üísymmetric flavor---regardless of orbital angular momentum---so the 
    94‚ÜísBootstrap diquark cannot be a $qq$ composite but must be an 
    95‚Üíalgebraically or string-theoretically generated object.

    97‚Üí%======================================================================
    98‚Üí\section{The Koide Formula as Energy Balance}
    99‚Üí\label{sec:koide_algebra}
   100‚Üí%======================================================================
   101‚Üí
   102‚Üí\subsection{Charges, not masses}
   103‚Üí
   104‚ÜíThe fundamental variable is the signed mass charge $\zch = z_0 + z_i 
   105‚Üí\in \mathbb{R}$, with the physical mass $m = \zch^2 \geq 0$.
   106‚Üí
   107‚Üí\subsection{Derivation of $Q = 2/3$}
   108‚Üí
   109‚ÜíFor three charges $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ with $z_0$ the mean 
   110‚Üí($\sum z_k = 0$):
   111‚Üí\begin{equation}
   112‚ÜíQ = \frac{\sum \zch_k^2}{(\sum \zch_k)^2} 
   113‚Üí= \frac{3z_0^2 + \sum z_k^2}{9z_0^2}
   114‚Üí= \frac{1}{3} + \frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{3z_0^2}\,.
   115‚Üí\end{equation}
   116‚ÜíTherefore:
   117‚Üí\begin{equation}
   118‚Üí\boxed{Q = \frac{2}{3} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad 
   119‚Üí\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2\,.}
   120‚Üí\end{equation}
   121‚Üí
   122‚ÜíThe two conditions are:
   123‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   124‚Üí\item \textbf{Zero-sum perturbations:} $z_1 + z_2 + z_3 = 0$ 
   125‚Üí(definition of $z_0$ as the mean charge).
   126‚Üí\item \textbf{Energy balance:} The mean energy of the perturbations 
   127‚Üíequals the energy of the unperturbed charge: 
   128‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
   129‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   130‚Üí
   131‚ÜíCondition~2 is the physics. It says the splitting scale matches the 
   132‚Üíbase scale---the coefficient of variation equals unity: 
   133‚Üí$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$. This is neither perturbative 
   134‚Üí($\sigma \ll \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1/3$, all equal) nor 
   135‚Üídominant ($\sigma \gg \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1$, one mass 
   136‚Üídominates). The Koide value $2/3$ is the equipartition point.
   137‚Üí
   138‚Üí\subsection{Why one mass can vanish}
   139‚Üí
   140‚ÜíIf $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$, a perturbation $z_k$ can reach 
   141‚Üí$|z_k| \sim z_0$, making $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k \approx 0$. A vanishing 
   142‚Üímass is the \emph{natural extreme} of energy balance, not fine-tuning.
   143‚Üí
   144‚Üí%======================================================================
   145‚Üí\section{Seed Triples: The $(0, a, b)$ Structure}
   146‚Üí\label{sec:seeds}
   147‚Üí%======================================================================
   148‚Üí
   149‚Üí\subsection{Analytical condition}
   150‚Üí
   151‚ÜíFor a triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ with one massless member, the 
   152‚ÜíKoide ratio becomes:
   153‚Üí\begin{equation}
   154‚ÜíQ = \frac{\zch_a^2 + \zch_b^2}{(\zch_a + \zch_b)^2}\,.
   155‚Üí\end{equation}
   156‚ÜíSetting $Q = 2/3$ and writing $r = \zch_a/\zch_b$ (with $r < 1$):
   157‚Üí\begin{equation}
   158‚Üí3(r^2 + 1) = 2(r + 1)^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad r^2 - 4r + 1 = 0\,,
   159‚Üí\end{equation}
   160‚Üígiving:
   161‚Üí\begin{equation}
   162‚Üí\boxed{r = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.}
   163‚Üí\end{equation}
   164‚Üí
   165‚ÜíEquivalently, in mass space: 
   166‚Üí$m_{\text{light}}/m_{\text{heavy}} = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 = 7 - 4\sqrt{3} 
   167‚Üí\approx 0.07180$.
   168‚Üí
   169‚ÜíThis is a \emph{universal} prediction: any Koide triple with one 
   170‚Üímassless member has the same charge ratio between the two massive 
   171‚Üímembers, regardless of their absolute scale.
   172‚Üí
   173‚Üí\subsection{The quark seed: $(0, s, c)$}
   174‚Üí
   175‚ÜíThe strange and charm quark charges give:\footnote{Throughout 
   176‚Üíthis paper, quark masses follow PDG conventions: 
   177‚Üí$m_s(2\GeV) = 93.4\MeV$, $m_c(m_c) = 1.27\GeV$, 
   178‚Üí$m_b(m_b) = 4.18\GeV$, and the top pole mass 
   179‚Üí$m_t = 172.76\GeV$. This is a mixed convention: the light 
   180‚Üíquark is evaluated at 2 GeV while heavier quarks are 
   181‚Üíevaluated at their own scale. Since QCD mass ratios are 
   182‚ÜíRG invariants (the anomalous dimension $\gamma_m$ is 
   183‚Üíflavor-universal at leading order), the Koide ratio $Q$ is 
   184‚Üíscheme-independent. However, the seed ratio 
   185‚Üí$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ as computed here uses masses at 
   186‚Üí\emph{different} scales, giving $0.271$; at a common scale 
   187‚Üíthe ratio is $\sim 0.29$, further from $2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.268$. 
   188‚ÜíThe meson seed, using unambiguous physical (pole) masses, 
   189‚Üíis free of this scheme issue and provides the cleaner test.}
   190‚Üí\begin{equation}
   191‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_s}}{\sqrt{m_c}} = \frac{9.67}{35.64} = 0.2712\,, 
   192‚Üí\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 1.2\%\,.
   193‚Üí\end{equation}
   194‚Üí
   195‚ÜíThis triple has $Q = 0.6644$, deviating from $2/3$ by only $0.35\%$. 
   196‚ÜíIts vacuum energy is $v_0^2 = (\zch_s + \zch_c)^2/9 \approx 228\MeV$.
   197‚Üí
   198‚ÜíA historical note: Harari, Haut, and Weyers \cite{Harari} observed 
   199‚Üía quark mass ratio pattern that, in the language of the present paper, 
   200‚Üícorresponds to an approximate seed triple---$(0, d, s)$---at a time 
   201‚Üíwhen the up quark mass was 
   202‚Üístill thought to be possibly zero \cite{Harari}. With current masses, 
   203‚Üí$\sqrt{m_d}/\sqrt{m_s} = 0.224$, deviating from $2 - \sqrt{3}$ by 
   204‚Üí$16\%$---too large for a good Koide triple. But if $m_s$ were 
   205‚Üí$\sim 150\MeV$ and $m_d \sim 7\MeV$ (values common in that era), the 
   206‚Üíratio improves considerably. The seed idea was already present, ahead 
   207‚Üíof its time.
   208‚Üí
   209‚Üí\subsection{The meson seed: $(0, \pi, D_s)$}
   210‚Üí
   211‚ÜíThe pion and $D_s$ meson charges give:
   212‚Üí\begin{equation}
   213‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_\pi}}{\sqrt{m_{D_s}}} = \frac{11.81}{44.37} = 0.2663\,, 
   214‚Üí\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 0.6\%\,.
   215‚Üí\end{equation}
   216‚Üí
   217‚ÜíThis gives $Q = 0.6679$, deviating by $0.18\%$. It is the 
   218‚Üí\textbf{meson counterpart} of the quark seed $(0, s, c)$---both 
   219‚Üíinvolve the strange-charm sector. Indeed, $D_s = c\bar{s}$ is the 
   220‚Üímeson that directly contains the $s$ and $c$ quarks.
   221‚Üí
   222‚Üí\subsection{The lepton triple as an ``almost-seed''}
   223‚Üí
   224‚ÜíThe charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ has $\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$, 
   225‚Üíwhich is small but nonzero. If $m_e$ were zero, we would need 
   226‚Üí$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.2679$; the actual value 
   227‚Üíis $0.2439$, deviating by $9\%$. So the leptons are \emph{not} an 
   228‚Üíexact seed---the electron, while very light, is not massless. But the 
   229‚Üítriple is clearly the same family: the near-critical Koide phase puts 
   230‚Üíone member close to zero.
   231‚Üí
   232‚Üí\subsection{Vacuum energy coincidence}
   233‚Üí
   234‚ÜíThe $v_0^2$ values of the seed triples cluster remarkably:
   235‚Üí
   236‚Üí\begin{center}
   237‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcc}
   238‚Üí\toprule
   239‚ÜíTriple & $v_0^2$ (MeV) & $v_0$ ($\MeV^{1/2}$) \\
   240‚Üí\midrule
   241‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 & 17.72 \\
   242‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 351 & 18.73 \\
   243‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ & 228 & 15.10 \\
   244‚Üí\bottomrule
   245‚Üí\end{tabular}
   246‚Üí\end{center}
   247‚Üí
   248‚ÜíThe lepton and meson seed triples share a vacuum energy around 
   249‚Üí310--350~MeV, squarely in the QCD scale range. This is the 
   250‚ÜísBootstrap connection made quantitative: \textbf{the lepton triple 
   251‚Üíand the meson seed triple operate at the same scale.}

   257‚Üí
   258‚Üí\subsection{From seed to full triple}
   259‚Üí
   260‚ÜíThe seed triples grow into full triples upon symmetry breaking:
   261‚Üí
   262‚Üí\begin{center}
   263‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   264‚Üí\toprule
   265‚ÜíSector & Seed triple & Full triple & $v_0^2$ (full) \\
   266‚Üí\midrule
   267‚ÜíQuarks & $(0, s, c)$ & $(-s, c, b)$ & 913 MeV \\
   268‚ÜíMesons & $(0, \pi, D_s)$ & $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 1230 MeV \\
   269‚ÜíLeptons & $(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & $(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 MeV \\
   270‚Üí\bottomrule

   272‚Üí\end{center}
   273‚Üí
   274‚ÜíIn each case:
   275‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   276‚Üí\item The seed has a massless (or near-massless) member, two massive 
   277‚Üímembers in the ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$, and $Q \approx 2/3$.
   278‚Üí\item The full triple has \textbf{no} massless member: the zero has 
   279‚Üíbeen lifted (to $m_e$, or the sign-flipped strange/pion mass). The 
   280‚Üí$b$ quark and $B$ meson are always present as flavor degrees of 
   281‚Üífreedom; what the breaking does is give them their \emph{distinctive 
   282‚Üímasses}---the specific values that complete the Koide triple. Before 
   283‚Üíbreaking, the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$); 
   284‚Üíafter, it acquires $\zch_b = 64.65\MeV^{1/2}$, the value selected 
   285‚Üíby the energy balance with $c$ and the sign-flipped $s$. The bloom 
   286‚Üíis not particle creation but \emph{mass generation}.
   287‚Üí\end{itemize}
   288‚Üí
   289‚ÜíThe ``tale of two tuples'' is this: \textbf{each sector presents 
   290‚Üífirst as a seed with a massless member, then ``blooms'' into a 
   291‚Üífull triple when SUSY breaks.} The massless member is the necessary 
   292‚Üíinitial condition; the breaking lifts it and generates the full spectrum.

   323‚Üí$\vec{z} = (z_1, z_2, z_3)$ constrained to the plane $\sum z_k = 0$ 
   324‚Üíwith length $|\vec{z}|^2 = 3z_0^2$. The angle between seed and

   349‚Üí%======================================================================
   350‚Üí\section{The Meson Koide Triple: $(-\pi, D_s, B)$}
   351‚Üí\label{sec:mesonkoide}
   352‚Üí%======================================================================
   353‚Üí
   354‚ÜíWith the pion charge negative, the triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ satisfies:
   355‚Üí\begin{equation}
   356‚ÜíQ(-\pi, D_s, B) = 
   357‚Üí\frac{m_\pi + m_{D_s} + m_B}{(-\sqrt{m_\pi} + \sqrt{m_{D_s}} + \sqrt{m_B})^2}
   358‚Üí= 0.6674\,,
   359‚Üí\end{equation}
   360‚Üídeviating from $2/3$ by $0.10\%$---the second most precise Koide 
   361‚Üítriple known. The energy balance:
   362‚Üí\begin{equation}
   363‚Üí\frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{z_0^2} = 1.002\,.
   364‚Üí\end{equation}
   365‚Üí
   366‚ÜíFor comparison, the ``standard'' all-positive quark triples $(d,s,b)$ 
   367‚Üíand $(u,c,t)$ deviate from $Q = 2/3$ by 9.7\% and 27\% respectively 
   368‚Üíat $\overline{\text{MS}}$ masses. \textbf{The meson triple is more 
   369‚Üíprecise than any quark triple} except $(c,b,t)$.
   370‚Üí
   371‚ÜíThe pion's negative charge has physical meaning: as a pseudo-Goldstone 
   372‚Üíboson, the pion would be massless ($\zch = 0$) if chiral symmetry were 
   373‚Üíexact. It sits just below zero ($\zch_\pi = -11.81$), approaching zero 
   374‚Üífrom the negative side as $m_q \to 0$. The sign of $\zch$ encodes 
   375‚ÜíGoldstone nature: pseudo-Goldstone bosons sit on the negative side 
   376‚Üíof the critical point, having crossed through zero from positive 
   377‚Üíterritory during chiral symmetry breaking.

   379‚Üí%======================================================================
   380‚Üí\section{The Third Tuple: $(c, b, t)$}
   381‚Üí\label{sec:cbt}
   382‚Üí%======================================================================
   383‚Üí
   384‚Üí\subsection{A tuple without mesons}
   385‚Üí
   386‚ÜíThe triple $(c, b, t)$ with all positive signs gives $Q = 0.6695$, 
   387‚Üídeviating by $0.42\%$. Its vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ 
   388‚Üíis far above the QCD scale.
   389‚Üí
   390‚ÜíThis triple has \textbf{no meson counterpart}: the top quark decays 
   391‚Üíweakly ($t \to Wb$, lifetime $\sim 5 \times 10^{-25}$~s) before the 
   392‚Üístrong interaction can bind it ($\tau_{\text{QCD}} \sim 1/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} 
   393‚Üí\sim 10^{-24}$~s). No $T$-meson ($t\bar{q}$) exists. The meson matrix 
   394‚Üí$M^i{}_j$ extends only to $b$-flavored states; the top sector lives 
   395‚Üípurely at the quark level.
   396‚Üí
   397‚Üí\subsection{Diquarks and the top}
   398‚Üí
   399‚ÜíIn the sBootstrap, diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ are the scalar partners of 
   400‚Üíantiquarks $\bar{q}_k$, with the baryon acting as the SUSY generator 
   401‚Üí\cite{CattoGursey, Miyazawa}. For five active flavors 
   402‚Üí$(u, d, s, c, b)$, the diquarks form a representation of $SU(5)_f$.
   403‚Üí
   404‚ÜíThe top diquarks $[tq]$ are kinematic objects only---they do not 
   405‚Üíhadronize. Yet their masses should follow the same pattern as other 
   406‚Üídiquarks: \textbf{there is no dynamical reason for top diquark masses 
   407‚Üíto differ from the others}. The $(c,b,t)$ Koide triple governs the 
   408‚Üíquark masses that enter the diquark sector, and its energy balance 
   409‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ constrains the possible diquark 
   410‚Üíspectrum even though the top diquarks are never observed as bound 
   411‚Üístates.
   412‚Üí
   413‚Üí\subsection{The $(c,b,t)$ triple has no seed}
   414‚Üí
   415‚ÜíUnlike the $(0,s,c)$ and $(0,\pi,D_s)$ seeds, there is no obvious 
   416‚Üí$(0, x, y)$ seed for the $(c,b,t)$ triple. No pair of quarks in this 
   417‚Üísector satisfies $\sqrt{m_{\text{light}}}/\sqrt{m_{\text{heavy}}} 
   418‚Üí\approx 2 - \sqrt{3}$: one has $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_t} = 0.086$ 
   419‚Üí(too small) and $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_b} = 0.551$ (too large).
   420‚Üí
   421‚ÜíThis means $(c,b,t)$ is \emph{not} a ``bloomed seed'' in the same 
   422‚Üísense as $(-s,c,b)$. It may represent a fundamentally different 
   423‚Üístructure---a triple that was never seeded by a massless member but 
   424‚Üíinstead emerged fully formed at a higher breaking scale, perhaps 
   425‚Üíconnected to electroweak symmetry breaking rather than to chiral 
   426‚Üísymmetry breaking.
   427‚Üí
   428‚Üí%======================================================================
   429‚Üí\section{The Diquark Sector and SU(5) Flavor}
   430‚Üí\label{sec:diquarks}
   431‚Üí%======================================================================
   432‚Üí
   433‚Üí\subsection{Counting partners}
   434‚Üí
   435‚ÜíIn the full sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005}, the scalar partners of 
   436‚Üíthe Standard Model fermions include both mesons ($q\bar{q}$) and 
   437‚Üídiquarks ($qq$). For five active flavors, the meson matrix gives 
   438‚Üí$5 \times 5 = 25$ scalars. The diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ form a 
   439‚Üírepresentation of $SU(5)_f$:
   440‚Üí\begin{equation}
   441‚Üí\mathbf{5} \otimes \mathbf{5} = \overline{\mathbf{10}}_A 
   442‚Üí\oplus \mathbf{15}_S\,.
   443‚Üí\end{equation}
   444‚Üí
   445‚ÜíThe symmetric $\mathbf{15}_S$ gives 15 diquarks including 
   446‚Üísame-flavor states $[uu]$, $[dd]$, \ldots, while the antisymmetric 
   447‚Üí$\overline{\mathbf{10}}_A$ gives 10 diquarks with $i \neq j$ only.
   448‚Üí
   449‚Üí\subsection{The symmetric representation}
   450‚Üí\label{subsec:sym15}
   451‚Üí
   452‚ÜíThe sBootstrap SUSY generator acts asymmetrically on the two 
   453‚Üífermion sectors. When it acts on a \emph{lepton}, it produces a 
   454‚Üímeson: $Q \cdot \ell \sim q\bar{q}$. Since the quark and antiquark 
   455‚Üíare distinguishable, the meson lies in the full 
   456‚Üí$\mathbf{5} \otimes \bar{\mathbf{5}} = \mathbf{24} + \mathbf{1}$. 
   457‚ÜíThere is no Pauli constraint, and the meson spectrum is perfectly 
   458‚Üíreproduced.
   459‚Üí
   460‚ÜíWhen it acts on a \emph{quark}, it produces a diquark: 
   461‚Üí$Q \cdot q_i \sim q_i q_j$. The bosonic SUSY generator applied to 
   462‚Üía single flavor index $i$ naturally produces the \textbf{symmetric} 
   463‚Üíproduct---the $\mathbf{15}_S$ representation, not the antisymmetric 
   464‚Üí$\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. This is the representation identified 
   465‚Üíin the $SO(32)$ decomposition \cite{Rivero2005}: the adjoint of 
   466‚Üí$SO(32)$ under $SU(5)_f$ decomposes to include the 
   467‚Üí$\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ (diquarks) alongside the 
   468‚Üí$\mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ (mesons).
   469‚Üí
   470‚ÜíThe 15 includes 5 same-flavor states ($[uu]$, $[dd]$, $[ss]$, 
   471‚Üí$[cc]$, $[bb]$) absent from the $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. Among 
   472‚Üíthese are exotic $+4/3$-charged diquarks such as $[uu]$. The cost: 
   473‚Üían additional chiral $+4/3$ quark per generation, as identified 
   474‚Üíin \cite{Rivero2005}. Whether these exotic states appear in nature 
   475‚Üías resonances, or whether they decouple at some high scale, is an 
   476‚Üíopen question.
   477‚Üí
   478‚Üí\subsection{The Pauli theorem for scalar diquarks}
   479‚Üí\label{subsec:pauli}
   480‚Üí
   481‚ÜíThe requirement of the symmetric $\mathbf{15}$ conflicts with 
   482‚ÜíFermi statistics---not merely for $L=0$ diquarks, but for 
   483‚Üí\emph{all} scalar diquarks in the color $\bar{3}$ channel, 
   484‚Üíregardless of internal orbital structure.
   485‚Üí
   486‚Üí\medskip
   487‚Üí\noindent\textbf{Theorem.}
   488‚Üí\textit{For a spin-0 ($J=0$) diquark in the color $\bar{3}$ 
   489‚Üí(antisymmetric) representation, the flavor wave function is 
   490‚Üíantisymmetric for \textbf{all} values of the orbital angular 
   491‚Üímomentum $L$ and spin $S$.}
   492‚Üí
   493‚Üí\medskip
   494‚Üí\noindent\textit{Proof.}
   495‚Üí$J = 0$ requires $L = S$ (since $|L - S| \leq J \leq L + S$). 
   496‚ÜíThe exchange symmetry of the spin-orbital wave function is 
   497‚Üí$(-1)^{L+S+1} = (-1)^{2L+1} = -1$ for all $L$. Combined with 
   498‚Üíthe color $\bar{3}$ factor $(-1)$, the non-flavor product is 
   499‚Üí$(-1)(-1) = +1$ (symmetric). Total antisymmetry then requires 
   500‚Üíflavor to be antisymmetric: $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. \hfill$\square$
   501‚Üí
   502‚Üí\medskip
   503‚ÜíThis result is purely kinematic---independent of dynamics, 
   504‚Üíbinding mechanisms, or spatial extent. No wave function, however 
   505‚Üíextended, can produce a symmetric-flavor scalar diquark in color 
   506‚Üí$\bar{3}$ while respecting Fermi statistics. The key cases:
   507‚Üí
   508‚Üí\begin{center}
   509‚Üí\begin{tabular}{ccccc}
   510‚Üí\toprule
   511‚Üí$L$ & $S$ & Spin-orbital $(-1)^{2L+1}$ & $\times$ Color & Flavor \\
   512‚Üí\midrule
   513‚Üí0 & 0 & $-1$ & $\times\,(-1) = +1$ & must be A \\
   514‚Üí1 & 1 & $-1$ & $\times\,(-1) = +1$ & must be A \\
   515‚Üí2 & 2 & $-1$ & $\times\,(-1) = +1$ & must be A \\
   516‚Üí\bottomrule
   517‚Üí\end{tabular}
   518‚Üí\end{center}
   519‚Üí
   520‚ÜíThe $L=1$, $S=1$ row closes the ``P-wave diquark'' escape route 
   521‚Üíthat might otherwise seem available.
   522‚Üí
   523‚ÜíThe theorem has a sharp consequence: \textbf{the sBootstrap 
   524‚Üídiquark in the $\mathbf{15}$ cannot be a $qq$ composite.} It 
   525‚Üímust be either:
   526‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   527‚Üí\item An algebraically generated object---the image of the 
   528‚ÜíMiyazawa supercharge \cite{Miyazawa, CattoGursey} acting on 
   529‚Üían antiquark, whose symmetry is determined by the superalgebra 
   530‚Üírather than by the statistics of constituent quarks; or
   531‚Üí\item A fundamental string-theoretic object, as in the $SO(32)$ 
   532‚Üídecomposition \cite{Rivero2005}, where the $\mathbf{15}$ 
   533‚Üíarises from the adjoint representation of the gauge group and 
   534‚Üíthe open-string diquark is not built from point-like quarks.
   535‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   536‚Üí
   537‚ÜíThe contrast with the meson sector is instructive. When the 
   538‚ÜíSUSY generator acts on a lepton, it produces a meson 
   539‚Üí($q\bar{q}$): the quark and antiquark are distinguishable, 
   540‚Üíno Pauli constraint applies, and the meson spectrum is 
   541‚Üíreproduced without tension. When it acts on a quark, it 
   542‚Üíproduces a diquark in the ``wrong'' representation---and the 
   543‚Üítheorem shows this cannot be fixed by any internal structure 
   544‚Üíof a $qq$ pair. The asymmetry between the two SUSY directions 
   545‚Üíis absolute: mesons work as composites; diquarks do not. 
   546‚ÜíThis is not a weakness of the sBootstrap but a 
   547‚Üí\emph{prediction}: the diquark sector requires new physics 
   548‚Üíbeyond the $qq$ picture.
   549‚Üí
   550‚Üí\subsection{The SO(32) connection}
   551‚Üí
   552‚ÜíThe sBootstrap counting matches a decomposition of the $SO(32)$ gauge 
   553‚Üígroup familiar from Type~I/heterotic string theory. Under 
   554‚Üí$SO(32) \supset SU(5) \times \ldots$, the 496-dimensional adjoint 
   555‚Üídecomposes to include the 
   556‚Üí$\mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ (mesons/sleptons) and the 
   557‚Üí$\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ (diquarks/squarks).
   558‚Üí
   559‚ÜíThis is suggestive: the same group structure that gives anomaly-free 
   560‚Üístring compactifications produces the symmetric diquark 
   561‚Üírepresentation demanded by the SUSY generator. The $SO(32)$ embedding 
   562‚Üímay provide the UV completion in which the compositeness of diquarks 
   563‚Üí(and hence the resolution of the Pauli sign) is made explicit: in 
   564‚Üíthe string picture, diquarks are open-string states whose symmetry 
   565‚Üíis determined by the gauge group, not by point-particle statistics.
   566‚Üí
   567‚Üí%======================================================================
   568‚Üí\section{SUSY Breaking: From Seeds to Spectrum}
   569‚Üí\label{sec:breaking}
   570‚Üí%======================================================================
   571‚Üí
   572‚ÜíThe SUSY-breaking story begins with the foundational observation 
   573‚Üí\eqref{eq:pimu}: $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2$. This is the 
   574‚ÜíVolkov--Akulov mass formula for a pseudo-Goldstino---the muon---whose 
   575‚Üísuperpartner---the pion---is a pseudo-Goldstone boson. The SUSY-breaking 
   576‚Üíscale is $f_\pi \approx 92\MeV$, and the breaking mechanism is chiral: 
   577‚Üí$\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   578‚Üísimultaneously. The Volkov--Akulov realization is the \emph{physical 
   579‚Üístarting point}; what follows in this section is the SQCD machinery 
   580‚Üíthat makes it precise.
   581‚Üí
   582‚ÜíThe dynamics must accomplish three things: generate the seed-to-bloom 
   583‚Üítransition for each tuple, preserve the energy-balance condition 
   584‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ through the breaking, and produce 
   585‚Üíthe correct splitting between meson and lepton masses at scale $f_\pi$.
   586‚Üí
   587‚Üí\subsection{The Seiberg framework}
   588‚Üí
   589‚ÜíThe natural arena is $\mathcal{N}=1$ SQCD with gauge group $SU(3)_c$ 
   590‚Üíand $N_f$ flavors of chiral superfields $Q^i$, $\bar{Q}_j$ in the 
   591‚Üífundamental and antifundamental representations. For $N_f = N_c = 3$ 
   592‚Üí(the case relevant to light QCD), Seiberg's exact results 
   593‚Üí\cite{Seiberg1995} establish that the theory confines, with the 
   594‚Üílow-energy degrees of freedom being the composite meson superfield
   595‚Üí\begin{equation}
   596‚ÜíM^i{}_j = Q^i \cdot \bar{Q}_j\,,
   597‚Üí\end{equation}
   598‚Üía $3\times 3$ matrix of chiral superfields, together with baryons 
   599‚Üí$B = \epsilon_{ijk}Q^iQ^jQ^k$ and $\bar{B}$. These satisfy the 
   600‚Üíquantum-modified constraint
   601‚Üí\begin{equation}
   602‚Üí\label{eq:seiberg}
   603‚Üí\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^{2N_c}\,,
   604‚Üí\end{equation}
   605‚Üíwhere $\Lambda$ is the dynamical scale.
   606‚Üí
   607‚ÜíEach entry $M^i{}_j$ is a chiral superfield containing a complex 
   608‚Üíscalar (the meson) and a Weyl fermion (the lepton, in the sBootstrap 
   609‚Üíidentification). The scalar and fermion are degenerate in the SUSY 
   610‚Üílimit; SUSY breaking splits them.
   611‚Üí
   612‚Üí\subsection{The three chiral superfields of each seed}
   613‚Üí
   614‚ÜíThe seed triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ maps naturally onto three 
   615‚Üíchiral superfields with the following mass structure before breaking:
   616‚Üí
   617‚Üí\begin{center}
   618‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   619‚Üí\toprule
   620‚ÜíSuperfield & Mass & Role \\
   621‚Üí\midrule
   622‚Üí$\Phi_0$ & $m_0 = 0$ & Massless: flat direction \\
   623‚Üí$\Phi_a$ & $m_a = \zch_a^2$ & Light massive member \\
   624‚Üí$\Phi_b$ & $m_b = \zch_b^2$ & Heavy massive member \\
   625‚Üí\bottomrule
   626‚Üí\end{tabular}
   627‚Üí\end{center}
   628‚Üí
   629‚ÜíThe ratio $\zch_a/\zch_b = 2 - \sqrt{3}$ is enforced by the 
   630‚ÜíKoide energy-balance condition applied to the seed.
   631‚Üí
   632‚ÜíIn the Seiberg description, these three superfields are entries of 
   633‚Üíthe meson matrix $M^i{}_j$. For the $(0, s, c)$ seed, the relevant 
   634‚Üíentries involve the strange and charm quarks; $\Phi_0$ is the entry 
   635‚Üíwhose quark-mass combination vanishes in the seed limit (i.e., the 
   636‚Üídirection in flavor space where $m_i + m_j \to 0$ through GOR).
   637‚Üí
   638‚Üí\subsection{F-term breaking: the O'Raifeartaigh mechanism}
   639‚Üí\label{subsec:fterm}
   640‚Üí
   641‚ÜíThe VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} tells us \emph{that} SUSY is broken at 
   642‚Üíscale $f_\pi$. To understand \emph{how}, we need the microscopic 
   643‚Üímechanism. The O'Raifeartaigh model \cite{ORaifeartaigh} is the simplest 
   644‚Üírealization of spontaneous $F$-term SUSY breaking. With three chiral 
   645‚Üísuperfields, the superpotential
   646‚Üí\begin{equation}
   647‚ÜíW = f\,\Phi_0 + m\,\Phi_1\Phi_2 + g\,\Phi_0\Phi_1^2
   648‚Üí\end{equation}
   649‚Üígives $F$-terms:
   650‚Üí\begin{align}
   651‚ÜíF_{\Phi_0}^* &= f + g\,\phi_1^2\,, \\
   652‚ÜíF_{\Phi_1}^* &= m\,\phi_2 + 2g\,\phi_0\phi_1\,, \\
   653‚ÜíF_{\Phi_2}^* &= m\,\phi_1\,.
   654‚Üí\end{align}
   655‚ÜíIf $f \neq 0$ and $m \neq 0$, then $F_{\Phi_2}^* = 0$ requires 
   656‚Üí$\phi_1 = 0$, but then $F_{\Phi_0}^* = f \neq 0$: SUSY is 
   657‚Üí\textbf{necessarily broken}. The key features are:
   658‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   659‚Üí\item A massless field ($\Phi_0$) exists at tree level---it is the 
   660‚Üípseudo-modulus.
   661‚Üí\item The breaking is triggered because $F_{\Phi_0}$ cannot vanish.
   662‚Üí\item After including loop corrections, $\Phi_0$ acquires a mass 
   663‚Üí(the Coleman--Weinberg potential lifts the flat direction).
   664‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   665‚Üí
   666‚ÜíThe structural parallel with the seed triples is:
   667‚Üí
   668‚Üí\begin{center}
   669‚Üí\begin{tabular}{llll}
   670‚Üí\toprule
   671‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh & Flavor & Seed & After breaking \\
   672‚Üí\midrule
   673‚Üí$\Phi_0$ (massless) & $b$ / $B$ & $\zch = 0$ (flat direction) & $\zch_b = 64.65$ (mass generated) \\
   674‚Üí$\Phi_1$ (light) & $s$ / $\pi$ & $\zch_s = 9.67$ & $\zch \to -9.67$ (sign-flipped) \\
   675‚Üí$\Phi_2$ (heavy) & $c$ / $D_s$ & $\zch_c = 35.64$ & $\zch_c \approx 35.64$ (shifted) \\
   676‚Üí\midrule
   677‚Üí & & $f = 0$ (SUSY) & $f = f_\pi$ (broken) \\
   678‚Üí\bottomrule
   679‚Üí\end{tabular}
   680‚Üí\end{center}
   681‚Üí
   682‚ÜíThis parallel is \emph{structural}, not quantitative: the minimal 
   683‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh model gives $\Phi_1$ and $\Phi_2$ the same mass 
   684‚Üíparameter $m$, whereas the seed triple has a mass ratio 
   685‚Üí$m_s/m_c = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 \approx 0.07$---far from degenerate. 
   686‚ÜíThe hierarchy between the two massive members must come from 
   687‚Üíadditional dynamics beyond the minimal O'Raifeartaigh superpotential. 
   688‚ÜíWhat the O'Raifeartaigh structure \emph{does} provide is the essential 
   689‚Üífeature: a massless flat direction ($\Phi_0 = $ the $b$-direction) 
   690‚Üíwhose $F$-term is forced nonzero. The $b$ quark and $B$ meson are 
   691‚Üíalways present as flavor degrees of freedom; the seed configuration 
   692‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ means the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction before 
   693‚Üíbreaking. The bloom is not particle creation but mass generation: 
   694‚Üíbreaking lifts $\Phi_0$ from zero to $\zch_b \neq 0$, simultaneously 
   695‚Üíflipping the sign of $\zch_s$, so that the same three flavors 
   696‚Üírearrange from $(0, s, c)$ into $(-s, c, b)$.
   697‚Üí
   698‚ÜíA striking difference from standard O'Raifeartaigh: the 
   699‚Üípseudo-modulus $\Phi_0$ normally acquires the \emph{smallest} mass 
   700‚Üíin the spectrum (a loop-suppressed Coleman--Weinberg mass, 
   701‚Üíparametrically $\sim g^2 f/16\pi^2 m$). In the sBootstrap bloom, 
   702‚Üíthe $b$-direction acquires the \emph{largest} mass 
   703‚Üí($m_b \gg m_s, m_c$). The bloom is far more dramatic than a 
   704‚Üíperturbative CW lifting---it is a nonperturbative, strong-dynamics 
   705‚Üíeffect in which confinement generates a mass of order $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2/m_q$, 
   706‚Üínot $g^2/(16\pi^2)$. This inversion is a signature of the 
   707‚Üídynamical (SQCD) realization versus the perturbative 
   708‚Üí(superpotential) one.
   709‚Üí
   710‚ÜíThe identification $f = \langle F_{\Phi_0}\rangle \sim f_\pi$ is the 
   711‚ÜísBootstrap's central claim: the SUSY-breaking $F$-term is the chiral 
   712‚Üícondensate parameter.
   713‚Üí
   714‚ÜíIn SQCD, this is realized dynamically rather than through a 
   715‚Üísuperpotential: the chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ acts as the 
   716‚Üínonzero $F$-term, the impossibility of restoring chiral symmetry 
   717‚Üíin the QCD vacuum is the dynamical analogue of $f \neq 0$, and the 
   718‚Üípion---the would-be Goldstone boson that acquires mass from explicit 
   719‚Üíchiral breaking---plays the role of $\Phi_0$ whose mass is lifted 
   720‚Üíby loop corrections (here, by quark masses).
   721‚Üí
   722‚Üí\subsection{The Goldstino direction and the Volkov--Akulov realization}
   723‚Üí\label{subsec:va}
   724‚Üí
   725‚ÜíWhen SUSY is spontaneously broken, a massless fermion appears: the 
   726‚ÜíGoldstino, the fermionic analogue of the Goldstone boson. Its decay 
   727‚Üíconstant $f_{\text{VA}}$ sets the SUSY-breaking scale through the 
   728‚ÜíVolkov--Akulov Lagrangian \cite{VolkovAkulov1973}:
   729‚Üí\begin{equation}
   730‚Üí\mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}} = -f_{\text{VA}}^2 \det\!\left(
   731‚Üí\delta_\mu^\nu + \frac{i}{f_{\text{VA}}^2}\,\bar{\lambda}\bar{\sigma}^\nu
   732‚Üí\partial_\mu\lambda\right),
   733‚Üí\end{equation}
   734‚Üíwhere $\lambda$ is the Goldstino field. At low energies, the Goldstino 
   735‚Üícouples derivatively with strength $\sim 1/f_{\text{VA}}^2$, exactly 
   736‚Üías the pion couples with strength $\sim 1/f_\pi^2$ in the chiral 
   737‚ÜíLagrangian.
   738‚Üí
   739‚ÜíThe sBootstrap identification is:
   740‚Üí\begin{equation}
   741‚Üí\label{eq:va_id}
   742‚Üíf_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi\,, \qquad 
   743‚Üí\text{Goldstino} = \text{muon (partner of }\pi\text{)}\,.
   744‚Üí\end{equation}
   745‚ÜíMore precisely, the muon is a \emph{pseudo-Goldstino}: in the 
   746‚Üílimit $m_q \to 0$ where the pion becomes exactly massless (a true 
   747‚ÜíGoldstone boson), its fermionic partner also becomes massless (a 
   748‚Üítrue Goldstino). The finite quark masses explicitly break both 
   749‚Üíchiral symmetry (giving $m_\pi \neq 0$) and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   750‚Üí(giving $m_\mu \neq m_\pi$), with the splitting controlled by 
   751‚Üí$f_\pi$:
   752‚Üí\begin{equation}
   753‚Üím_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2\,.
   754‚Üí\end{equation}
   755‚Üí
   756‚ÜíThis is the Volkov--Akulov relation for the pseudo-Goldstino: the 
   757‚Üímass splitting between the scalar (pion) and its fermionic partner 
   758‚Üí(muon) is set by the SUSY-breaking scale squared.
   759‚Üí
   760‚ÜíThe VA mechanism is not a separate breaking from the F-term: it is 
   761‚Üíthe \emph{low-energy, composite-level description} of the same 
   762‚Üíbreaking. At the microscopic level, $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ 
   763‚Üíin the meson superfield; at the composite level, this manifests 
   764‚Üías the Goldstino coupling with $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$. These 
   765‚Üítwo quantities---the Goldstino decay constant and the pion decay 
   766‚Üíconstant---are \emph{identified} in the sBootstrap; this is the 
   767‚Üícentral claim, not a derivation.
   768‚Üí
   769‚ÜíThe identification should not be confused with the standard relation 
   770‚Üíbetween the $F$-term VEV and the chiral condensate. In standard 
   771‚ÜíSQCD, $\langle F\rangle \sim |\qqbar|/f_\pi \sim (250\MeV)^3/92\MeV 
   772‚Üí\approx 170{,}000\MeV^2$, while $f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2$---a 
   773‚Üífactor of $\sim 20$ discrepancy. The GOR relation 
   774‚Üí$m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$ connects these through the 
   775‚Üísmall quark masses, but does not equate them. The sBootstrap 
   776‚Üíidentification $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is therefore a 
   777‚Üí\emph{low-energy effective} statement about the composite sector, 
   778‚Üínot a microscopic identity. It says: the scale at which SUSY 
   779‚Üíappears broken in the meson--lepton spectrum is $f_\pi$, regardless 
   780‚Üíof the microscopic $F$-term that produces this breaking.
   781‚Üí
   782‚ÜíThe VA realization gives more than a mass formula---it organizes the 
   783‚Üíentire breaking narrative. In the chiral limit ($m_q \to 0$), both 
   784‚Üípion and muon are exactly massless: the pion as the Goldstone boson 
   785‚Üíof broken chiral symmetry, the muon as the Goldstino of broken 
   786‚ÜísBootstrap SUSY. This is the \emph{exact seed}: the $B$-meson 
   787‚Üídirection sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$), and the 
   788‚Üí$\pi$--$\mu$ pair is massless. Turning on quark masses ($m_q \neq 0$) 
   789‚Üídoes three things simultaneously:
   790‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   791‚Üí\item Gives the pion its GOR mass (lifting the flat direction for $\pi$).
   792‚Üí\item Gives the muon its mass (the pseudo-Goldstino acquires mass 
   793‚Üíwhen the explicit breaking parameter $m_q$ is nonzero).
   794‚Üí\item Generates the $B$-meson mass (the flat direction for $\Phi_0$ 
   795‚Üíis lifted by the Coleman--Weinberg potential, i.e., by the same 
   796‚Üíquark masses that feed the GOR formula).
   797‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   798‚ÜíThe splitting between (1) and (2) is $f_\pi^2$---the VA relation. 
   799‚ÜíThe seed-to-bloom transition of \S\ref{sec:two_tuples} is the same 
   800‚Üíphysics seen from the charge-space perspective: $m_q \neq 0$ lifts 
   801‚Üíthe zero, generates the $b$/$B$ mass, flips the sign of $\zch_s$ 
   802‚Üíand $\zch_\pi$, and produces the full triple. The VA equation 
   803‚Üí\eqref{eq:pimu} is not one fact among many; it is the generating 
   804‚Üírelation from which the bloom follows.
   805‚Üí
   806‚ÜíA note on quantum numbers: the Goldstino of spontaneously broken 
   807‚Üíspacetime SUSY is a neutral fermion (the fermionic component of the 
   808‚Üísuperfield whose $F$-term breaks SUSY). The muon carries electric 
   809‚Üícharge and lepton number. The sBootstrap SUSY, however, is not a 
   810‚Üíspacetime symmetry but an emergent hadronic symmetry---a 
   811‚ÜíMiyazawa-type superalgebra \cite{Miyazawa} acting on composite 
   812‚Üístates. In this context, the ``Goldstino'' need not carry the 
   813‚Üíquantum numbers of a spacetime supercharge; it carries the quantum 
   814‚Üínumbers appropriate to the broken hadronic generator. The muon's 
   815‚Üícharge is inherited from the pion multiplet it partners.
   816‚Üí
   817‚Üí\subsection{D-term breaking: electromagnetic splittings}
   818‚Üí\label{subsec:dterm}
   819‚Üí
   820‚ÜíThe unbroken $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ gauge symmetry allows a 
   821‚ÜíFayet--Iliopoulos (FI) term \cite{FayetIliopoulos}:
   822‚Üí\begin{equation}
   823‚Üí\mathcal{L}_{\text{FI}} = \xi\,D_{\text{em}}\,,
   824‚Üí\end{equation}
   825‚Üíwhich shifts scalar masses by $\delta m^2 = Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi$, 
   826‚Üíwhere $Q_{\text{em}}$ is the electric charge. This is the 
   827‚Üísupersymmetric version of the electromagnetic mass difference.
   828‚Üí
   829‚ÜíIn QCD without SUSY, the electromagnetic mass splitting of the 
   830‚Üípion is given by the Das--Guralnik--Mathur--Low--Young (DGMLY) 
   831‚Üíformula \cite{DGMLY1967}:
   832‚Üí\begin{equation}
   833‚Üím_{\pi^\pm}^2 - m_{\pi^0}^2 = \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,
   834‚Üí\frac{m_\rho^2}{f_\pi^2}\,f_\pi^2 
   835‚Üí\approx (35.5\MeV)^2\,,
   836‚Üí\end{equation}
   837‚Üíwhich in the SUSY framework becomes a D-term contribution:
   838‚Üí\begin{equation}
   839‚Üí\xi \sim \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,m_\rho^2 
   840‚Üí\sim (35\MeV)^2\,.
   841‚Üí\end{equation}
   842‚Üí
   843‚ÜíIn charge space, the D-term shifts $\zch$ by an amount proportional 
   844‚Üíto $Q_{\text{em}}$:
   845‚Üí\begin{equation}
   846‚Üí\zch \to \zch + \delta\zch\,, \qquad 
   847‚Üí\delta\zch \approx \frac{Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi}{2\zch}\,.
   848‚Üí\end{equation}
   849‚ÜíFor the pion ($Q_{\text{em}} = \pm 1$, $\zch \approx 11.8$): 
   850‚Üí$\delta\zch \approx 0.2\MeV^{1/2}$, matching the observed 
   851‚Üí$\zch_{\pi^\pm} - \zch_{\pi^0} = 11.82 - 11.62 = 0.20\MeV^{1/2}$.
   852‚Üí
   853‚ÜíThe D-term effect is subleading to the F-term: 
   854‚Üí$\xi \sim (35\MeV)^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 \sim (92\MeV)^2$. It 
   855‚Üíoperates \emph{within} each supermultiplet entry, splitting charged 
   856‚Üífrom neutral mesons, while the F-term splits bosons from fermions 
   857‚Üíacross the supermultiplet.
   858‚Üí
   859‚ÜíWitten's inequality \cite{Witten1983} guarantees 
   860‚Üí$m_{\pi^\pm} > m_{\pi^0}$ in QCD with electromagnetism---the 
   861‚Üícharged pion is always heavier. In the SUSY framework this 
   862‚Üíbecomes the statement that the D-term contribution is positive 
   863‚Üífor $Q_{\text{em}} \neq 0$, consistent with the FI sign.
   864‚Üí
   865‚Üí\subsection{Mapping the breaking to the tuples}
   866‚Üí\label{subsec:mapping}
   867‚Üí
   868‚ÜíThe tuple structure maps onto a layered breaking with two independent 
   869‚Üímechanisms (F-term and D-term) plus a separate heavy-sector origin. 
   870‚ÜíThe Volkov--Akulov realization of \S\ref{subsec:va} is not a third 
   871‚Üímechanism: it is the low-energy, composite-level description of the 
   872‚Üísame F-term breaking. The VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} \emph{sets the 
   873‚Üíscale} ($f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$); the O'Raifeartaigh/SQCD dynamics 
   874‚Üíof \S\ref{subsec:fterm} \emph{provides the mechanism} 
   875‚Üí($\langle F\rangle \neq 0$).
   876‚Üí
   877‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 1: F-term/VA (chiral condensate, scale $\sim f_\pi$).}
   878‚ÜíThe chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry 
   879‚Üíand the sBootstrap SUSY. This is the primary breaking, affecting 
   880‚Üíthe light sector. The seed triples $(0, s, c)$ and $(0, \pi, D_s)$ 
   881‚Üíare the pre-breaking configurations; the massless member is the flat 
   882‚Üídirection whose $F$-term is forced nonzero by confinement. After 
   883‚Üíbreaking:
   884‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   885‚Üí\item The zero member is lifted and acquires negative charge 
   886‚Üí($\zch < 0$), becoming the sign-flipped member of the full triple 
   887‚Üí($-s$, $-\pi$).
   888‚Üí\item The full triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ emerge, 
   889‚Üíincorporating the $b$-quark and $B$-meson sectors.
   890‚Üí\item The lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the fermionic shadow: 
   891‚Üíthe leptons acquire masses through the same condensate, with the 
   892‚Üímuon as pseudo-Goldstino.
   893‚Üí\end{itemize}
   894‚Üí
   895‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 2: D-term (electromagnetism, scale $\sim \alpha_{\text{em}} m_\rho$).}
   896‚ÜíThe $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI term generates electromagnetic mass 
   897‚Üísplittings within each multiplet. This is a perturbative correction 
   898‚Üíon top of the F-term breaking, affecting only charged particles. 
   899‚ÜíIt does not create new Koide triples but shifts existing ones by 
   900‚Üí$O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$.
   901‚Üí
   902‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 3: The heavy sector (scale $\sim v_0^{(cbt)}$).}
   903‚ÜíThe $(c,b,t)$ triple at $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ operates far 
   904‚Üíabove the chiral scale. It has no seed (no massless member), no 
   905‚Üímeson shadow (top does not hadronize), and its breaking may be 
   906‚Üíconnected to a different sector of the theory---possibly the 
   907‚Üíelectroweak Higgs mechanism.
   908‚Üí
   909‚ÜíThis layered structure involves three distinct scales, each with 
   910‚Üídifferent physical meaning:
   911‚Üí\begin{align}
   912‚Üí\text{F-term (boson--fermion splitting):} &\quad 
   913‚Üíf_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \quad f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2\,; \\
   914‚Üí\text{D-term (EM fine structure):} &\quad 
   915‚Üí\xi^{1/2} \approx 35\MeV\,, \quad \xi \approx 1{,}200\MeV^2\,; \\
   916‚Üí\text{Heavy sector (Koide scale):} &\quad 
   917‚Üív_0^{(cbt)} \approx 172\MeV^{1/2}\,, \quad (v_0^{(cbt)})^2 \approx 29{,}600\MeV\,.
   918‚Üí\end{align}
   919‚ÜíComparing the squared scales: $f_\pi^2 \gg \xi$ (F-term dominates 
   920‚Üíover D-term by a factor $\sim 7$, consistent with the D-term being 
   921‚Üía perturbative $O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$ correction). The heavy-sector 
   922‚Üíscale $(v_0^{(cbt)})^2$ carries dimensions of $\MeV$ (not $\MeV^2$) 
   923‚Üíbecause $v_0$ is a charge ($\MeV^{1/2}$), not an energy; direct 
   924‚Üícomparison with $f_\pi$ requires specifying which physical quantity 
   925‚Üíis being compared.
   926‚Üí
   927‚Üí\subsection{The O'Raifeartaigh structure in SQCD}
   928‚Üí
   929‚ÜíIn Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, the quantum constraint 
   930‚Üí\eqref{eq:seiberg} provides a natural setting for the 
   931‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh mechanism. The constraint $\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ 
   932‚Üímeans the moduli space has a specific geometry: not all field directions 
   933‚Üíare flat. When quark masses are added via a tree-level superpotential 
   934‚Üí$W_{\text{tree}} = m_i M^i{}_i$ (sum over flavors), the $F$-terms become:
   935‚Üí\begin{equation}
   936‚ÜíF_{M^i{}_j} = m_i\,\delta^i_j + \Lambda^6\,(\text{constraint terms})\,.
   937‚Üí\end{equation}
   938‚Üí
   939‚ÜíFor the seed triple $(0, s, c)$, the ``$0$'' member corresponds to a 
   940‚Üídirection in the meson matrix where the tree-level mass vanishes---the 
   941‚Üíentry involving the lightest quark combination. In the exact chiral 
   942‚Üílimit ($m_u = m_d = 0$), this entry is truly massless, and the 
   943‚Üíconstraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} forces $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ for 
   944‚Üíthat entry. This is the dynamical O'Raifeartaigh mechanism: 
   945‚Üíconfinement (encoded in $\Lambda^6$) plays the role of the parameter 
   946‚Üí$f$ in the superpotential, and the impossibility of setting all 
   947‚Üí$F$-terms to zero is a consequence of the strong dynamics rather 
   948‚Üíthan a choice of superpotential.
   949‚Üí
   950‚ÜíThe ISS mechanism (Intriligator--Seiberg--Shih \cite{ISS2006}) 
   951‚Üíestablishes metastable SUSY-breaking vacua for $N_c < N_f < 3N_c/2$, 
   952‚Üíwhere the rank condition in the magnetic dual prevents all $F$-terms 
   953‚Üífrom vanishing. The sBootstrap case $N_f = N_c = 3$ is strictly 
   954‚Üí\emph{below} the ISS window---at $N_f = N_c$ the low-energy 
   955‚Üídescription is qualitatively different, governed by the 
   956‚Üíquantum-modified constraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} on the moduli space 
   957‚Üírather than by a free magnetic dual. However, the physical QCD has 
   958‚Üí$N_f = 3$ light flavors and $N_f = 4$--$5$ if charm and bottom are 
   959‚Üíincluded at their respective thresholds, which does fall within the 
   960‚ÜíISS window for $N_c = 3$. The sBootstrap's multi-scale structure---with 
   961‚Üíthe seed triples involving $s$, $c$, and $b$ quarks at different 
   962‚Üíscales---may require treating the effective $N_f$ as 
   963‚Üíscale-dependent, entering the ISS regime above the strange threshold.
   964‚Üí
   965‚Üí\subsection{How the three supermultiplets join}
   966‚Üí
   967‚ÜíThe seed-to-bloom transition generates three separate Koide triples. 
   968‚ÜíBut the full sBootstrap requires these triples to be \emph{embedded 
   969‚Üíin a single meson matrix}. The $3\times 3$ light-flavor matrix is:
   970‚Üí
   971‚Üí\begin{equation}
   972‚ÜíM^{(3)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   973‚Üíu\bar{u} & u\bar{d} & u\bar{s} \\
   974‚Üíd\bar{u} & d\bar{d} & d\bar{s} \\
   975‚Üís\bar{u} & s\bar{d} & s\bar{s}
   976‚Üí\end{pmatrix}
   977‚Üí\;\longleftrightarrow\;
   978‚Üí\begin{pmatrix}
   979‚Üí\pi^0/\eta & \pi^+ & K^+ \\
   980‚Üí\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 \\
   981‚ÜíK^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta'
   982‚Üí\end{pmatrix}.
   983‚Üí\end{equation}
   984‚Üí
   985‚ÜíWhen charm and bottom are included, the matrix extends to $5\times 5$. 
   986‚ÜíWe display it schematically, with the members of the meson Koide 
   987‚Üítriple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ marked by boxes:
   988‚Üí
   989‚Üí\begin{equation}
   990‚ÜíM^{(5)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   991‚Üí\pi^0/\eta & \boxed{\pi^+} & K^+ & D^0 & \boxed{B^+} \\
   992‚Üí\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 & D^- & B^0 \\
   993‚ÜíK^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta' & \boxed{D_s^-} & B_s \\
   994‚Üí\bar{D}^0 & D^+ & D_s^+ & \eta_c & B_c \\
   995‚ÜíB^- & \bar{B}^0 & \bar{B}_s & \bar{B}_c & \eta_b
   996‚Üí\end{pmatrix},
   997‚Üí\end{equation}
   998‚Üíwhere rows are indexed by $(u, d, s, c, b)$ and columns by 
   999‚Üí$(\bar{u}, \bar{d}, \bar{s}, \bar{c}, \bar{b})$.
  1000‚Üí
  1001‚ÜíThe three members of the meson Koide triple occupy different 
  1002‚Üíoff-diagonal blocks of this matrix: $\pi^+ = u\bar{d}$ is in the 
  1003‚Üílight $(u,d)$ block, $D_s^- = s\bar{c}$ bridges the light-charm 
  1004‚Üísector, and $B^+ = u\bar{b}$ bridges the light-bottom sector. They 
  1005‚Üído not form a ``slice'' in any simple linear-algebraic sense---they 
  1006‚Üíare scattered entries connected by the Koide condition on their 
  1007‚Üí\emph{masses}, not by their positions in the matrix. The meson seed 
  1008‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ drops the $B^+$ and replaces it with zero (the 
  1009‚Üíentry that would involve the top quark, absent from the matrix).
  1010‚Üí
  1011‚ÜíThis scattered structure is the central puzzle: the Koide triples 
  1012‚Üíare \emph{mass relations} among matrix entries, not submatrices. 
  1013‚ÜíThe question is whether the Seiberg constraint 
  1014‚Üí$\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ (appropriately generalized to 
  1015‚Üí$5 \times 5$), combined with the SUSY-breaking dynamics, produces 
  1016‚Üícorrelations among these scattered entries that enforce the 
  1017‚Üíenergy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
  1018‚Üí
  1019‚Üí\subsection{The energy balance as a fixed point}
  1020‚Üí\label{subsec:fixedpoint}
  1021‚ÜíOne possibility is that the energy-balance condition 
  1022‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a \emph{dynamical attractor}: 
  1023‚Üíany set of three masses evolving under the SUSY-breaking dynamics 
  1024‚Üíflows toward the Koide surface $Q = 2/3$, as a fixed point of the 
  1025‚ÜíRG flow for the soft SUSY-breaking parameters. This is speculative, 
  1026‚Üíbut the observation that the condition holds for fundamental fermions 
  1027‚Üí(leptons, quarks) \emph{and} composite bosons (mesons) across very 
  1028‚Üídifferent scales lends it plausibility. A concrete test would be to 
  1029‚Üíshow that the one-loop soft-mass RG equations in $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
  1030‚ÜíSQCD with appropriate boundary conditions flow toward 
  1031‚Üí$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$; this calculation has not yet been 
  1032‚Üíperformed.
  1033‚Üí
  1034‚Üí\subsection{The exact seed and what breaks it}
  1035‚Üí\label{subsec:exactseed}
  1036‚Üí
  1037‚ÜíThe seed configuration $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ requires \emph{two} 
  1038‚Üíindependent conditions:
  1039‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
  1040‚Üí\item One member at $\zch = 0$ exactly (the flat direction).
  1041‚Üí\item The ratio of the massive members equals $2 - \sqrt{3}$:
  1042‚Üí\begin{equation}
  1043‚Üí\label{eq:seedratio}
  1044‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_a}}{\sqrt{m_b}} = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.
  1045‚Üí\end{equation}
  1046‚Üí\end{enumerate}
  1047‚ÜíThese are logically distinct. The chiral limit ($m_u = m_d = 0$, 
  1048‚Üí$\qqbar \neq 0$) enforces condition (1) for the meson sector: the 
  1049‚Üípion is an exact Goldstone boson, $m_\pi = 0$, and in the 
  1050‚Üípicture of \S\ref{subsec:fterm} the $B$-meson direction sits at 
  1051‚Üíthe flat direction before acquiring mass. But the chiral limit says 
  1052‚Üí\emph{nothing} about condition (2)---it does not constrain 
  1053‚Üí$\sqrt{m_{D_s}}/\sqrt{m_B}$ or $\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ 
  1054‚Üíto equal $2 - \sqrt{3}$.
  1055‚Üí
  1056‚ÜíThe physical deviations from the exact ratio are small but 
  1057‚Üímeasurable:
  1058‚Üí
  1059‚Üí\begin{center}
  1060‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccc}
  1061‚Üí\toprule
  1062‚ÜíSeed & Exact ratio & Physical ratio & Deviation \\
  1063‚Üí\midrule
  1064‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1065‚Üí$\sqrt{m_\pi}/\sqrt{m_{D_s}} = 0.2663$ & $-0.6\%$ \\
  1066‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1067‚Üí$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c} = 0.2712$ & $+1.2\%$ \\
  1068‚Üí$(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1069‚Üí$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 0.2439$ & $-9.0\%$ \\
  1070‚Üí\bottomrule
  1071‚Üí\end{tabular}
  1072‚Üí\end{center}
  1073‚Üí
  1074‚ÜíThe meson seed is closest to the exact ratio (0.6\% deviation). 
  1075‚ÜíThe quark seed deviates by 1.2\%. A natural thought is that QCD 
  1076‚Üírunning might shift the quark ratio: perhaps at some scale $\mu^*$ 
  1077‚Üíthe ratio $\sqrt{m_s(\mu^*)}/\sqrt{m_c(\mu^*)}$ hits $2 - \sqrt{3}$ 
  1078‚Üíexactly. But this is \emph{not possible within QCD alone}. At leading 
  1079‚Üíorder, all quark masses run with the same anomalous dimension 
  1080‚Üí$\gamma_m$:
  1081‚Üí\begin{equation}
  1082‚Üím_i(\mu) = m_i(\mu_0)\left(\frac{\alpha_s(\mu)}{\alpha_s(\mu_0)}\right)^{\gamma_m/\beta_0},
  1083‚Üí\end{equation}
  1084‚Üíso the ratio $m_s(\mu)/m_c(\mu)$---and therefore 
  1085‚Üí$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$---is an RG invariant of QCD. Higher-order 
  1086‚Üícorrections break this invariance, but only at the $O(\alpha_s^2)$ 
  1087‚Üílevel, far too small to account for 1.2\%.
  1088‚Üí
  1089‚ÜíThe quark seed deviation is therefore \emph{intrinsic}: the 
  1090‚Üí$\overline{\text{MS}}$ mass ratio $m_s/m_c$ is a fixed number 
  1091‚Üí(at any QCD scale), and it misses the Koide target by 1.2\%. 
  1092‚ÜíThree possible explanations present themselves:
  1093‚Üí
  1094‚Üí\paragraph{Electroweak running.} Above the electroweak scale, 
  1095‚Üíquark masses are controlled by Yukawa couplings $y_i$ to the 
  1096‚ÜíHiggs, which \emph{do} run differently for different flavors: 
  1097‚Üíthe $\beta$-functions for $y_s$ and $y_c$ involve different 
  1098‚Üíelectroweak quantum numbers ($SU(2)_L$ doublet structure, 
  1099‚Üíhypercharge). The ratio $y_s/y_c$ evolves with scale, and 
  1100‚Üíthere may exist a scale $\mu^*$ above the electroweak 
  1101‚Üíthreshold where $\sqrt{y_s(\mu^*)}/\sqrt{y_c(\mu^*)} = 2 - \sqrt{3}$. 
  1102‚ÜíThis would identify the ``seed scale'' as an electroweak-sector 
  1103‚Üíphenomenon, not a QCD one---consistent with the sBootstrap's 
  1104‚Üíidentification of the breaking with $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ 
  1105‚Üírather than $SU(3)_c$.
  1106‚Üí
  1107‚Üí\paragraph{Charge running.} Even if mass ratios are QCD-invariant, 
  1108‚Üíthe Koide decomposition $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ may have its own 
  1109‚Üíflow. The vacuum energy $v_0 = z_0^2$ sets the common scale, 
  1110‚Üíand the fluctuations $z_k$ encode the deviations. If the 
  1111‚Üíenergy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a 
  1112‚Üídynamical attractor (as suggested in \S\ref{subsec:fixedpoint}), 
  1113‚Üíthe $z_k$ may evolve under a flow in which the \emph{ratio} 
  1114‚Üí$z_a/z_b$ approaches $2 - \sqrt{3}$ at the fixed point while 
  1115‚Üíthe individual masses (and their QCD-invariant ratios) remain 
  1116‚Üíunchanged. In this picture, the 1.2\% deviation measures how 
  1117‚Üífar the quark sector is from the attractor.
  1118‚Üí
  1119‚Üí\paragraph{Meson vs.\ quark deviations.} The meson deviation 
  1120‚Üí(0.6\%) is smaller than the quark deviation (1.2\%), and for 
  1121‚Üía different reason: meson masses are \emph{not} simple functions 
  1122‚Üíof quark mass ratios. The pion mass is given by the GOR 
  1123‚Üírelation $m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$; the $D_s$ 
  1124‚Üímass involves heavy-quark effective theory; and both receive 
  1125‚Üíchiral perturbation theory corrections. These nonperturbative 
  1126‚ÜíQCD effects are the dominant source of the meson seed deviation, 
  1127‚Üíand are in principle calculable at NLO in ChPT.
  1128‚Üí
  1129‚Üí\paragraph{A remarkable feature of the VA relation.} The 
  1130‚Üífoundational equation $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2$ contains 
  1131‚Üíno QCD coupling constant: no $\alpha_s$, no $N_c$, no 
  1132‚Üí$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$. It is a pure mass relation among three 
  1133‚Üíexperimentally measured quantities---two masses and a decay 
  1134‚Üíconstant---holding to 2.2\%. In the sBootstrap, this is natural: 
  1135‚Üíthe relation is a SUSY mass splitting, set by the breaking 
  1136‚Üíscale $f_\pi$, and SUSY mass relations do not involve the gauge 
  1137‚Üícoupling of the confining theory. Standard QCD \emph{explains} 
  1138‚Üí$f_\pi$ in terms of $\qqbar$ and $\alpha_s$; the sBootstrap 
  1139‚Üí\emph{uses} $f_\pi$ as the fundamental parameter, with QCD 
  1140‚Üíproviding the dynamical origin but not appearing in the mass 
  1141‚Üíformula. The absence of QCD factors in \eqref{eq:pimu} is a 
  1142‚Üíhint that the relevant symmetry is not $SU(3)_c$ but the 
  1143‚Üíhadronic superalgebra that connects pions to leptons.
  1144‚Üí
  1145‚ÜíThe lepton ``seed'' has the worst ratio match ($-9\%$), which 
  1146‚Üíis consistent with the observation that the lepton triple is 
  1147‚Üí\emph{not} a true seed: $m_e \neq 0$, and the electron was never 
  1148‚Üíat the flat direction. The leptons are an ``almost-seed'' whose 
  1149‚Üíexcellent $Q = 0.66666$ arises not from the seed structure but 
  1150‚Üífrom the smallness of $m_e$, which makes the two conditions 
  1151‚Üíapproximately degenerate. For the leptons, condition (1) is 
  1152‚Üíapproximate rather than exact, and condition (2) is correspondingly 
  1153‚Üírelaxed.
  1154‚Üí
  1155‚Üí\subsection{What the $(c,b,t)$ triple tells us}
  1156‚Üí
  1157‚ÜíThe $(c,b,t)$ triple ($Q = 0.6695$, $v_0^2 = 29.6\GeV$) has 
  1158‚Üíneither a seed nor a meson shadow. It operates at a scale far 
  1159‚Üíabove $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, suggesting its origin is different:
  1160‚Üí
  1161‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
  1162‚Üí\item It may be governed by the electroweak Higgs mechanism rather 
  1163‚Üíthan the chiral condensate. The top Yukawa $y_t \approx 1$ is 
  1164‚Üí``natural'' in the sense that $m_t \approx v_{\text{EW}}/\sqrt{2}$; 
  1165‚Üíthe Koide condition on $(c,b,t)$ would then constrain the Yukawa 
  1166‚Üícoupling matrix.
  1167‚Üí\item It may represent a Volkov--Akulov realization at the 
  1168‚Üíelectroweak scale: if there is a second SUSY breaking at 
  1169‚Üí$f' \sim v_{\text{EW}}$, the $(c,b,t)$ triple would be its 
  1170‚Üíanalog of the $(\pi, \mu)$ system, but with $f'$ replacing $f_\pi$.
  1171‚Üí\item Or it may be a purely kinematic accident---the quark masses 
  1172‚Üíhappen to satisfy the Koide condition without a dynamical reason. 
  1173‚ÜíThe 0.42\% precision makes this unlikely but not impossible.
  1174‚Üí\end{enumerate}
  1175‚Üí
  1176‚ÜíInterpretation (1) is most natural within the sBootstrap: the 
  1177‚Üí$(c,b,t)$ triple involves quarks whose masses are dominated by 
  1178‚ÜíYukawa couplings to the Higgs, not by the chiral condensate, and the 
  1179‚ÜíKoide condition would then be a constraint on the Yukawa matrix 
  1180‚Üírather than on the SQCD dynamics. The chiral sector and the 
  1181‚Üíelectroweak sector would each independently produce a Koide-type 
  1182‚Üíenergy balance, with the charm and bottom quarks serving as the 
  1183‚Üíbridge between the two.
  1184‚Üí
  1185‚ÜíIf interpretation (2) is also invoked, the sBootstrap has two breaking 
  1186‚Üíscales:
  1187‚Üí\begin{equation}
  1188‚Üíf_{\text{chiral}} = f_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \qquad
  1189‚Üíf_{\text{EW}} \sim v_{\text{EW}} \approx 246\GeV\,,
  1190‚Üí\end{equation}
  1191‚Üíand the full spectrum requires both. The light sector (leptons, 
  1192‚Üílight mesons) is governed by $f_\pi$; the heavy sector (top, and 
  1193‚Üíby extension charm and bottom through the Koide network) is 
  1194‚Üígoverned by $v_{\text{EW}}$. The charm and bottom quarks, 
  1195‚Üíappearing in both the $(0,s,c)/(-s,c,b)$ light-sector triples 
  1196‚Üíand the $(c,b,t)$ heavy-sector triple, are the ``bridges'' 
  1197‚Üíbetween the two scales.
  1198‚Üí

  1200‚Üí\section{The Electron Mass and the Lepton Seed}
  1201‚Üí\label{sec:electron}
  1202‚Üí%======================================================================
  1203‚Üí
  1204‚ÜíThe charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the ``almost-seed'': 
  1205‚Üí$\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$ is small but nonzero. The electron's 
  1206‚Üícharge is near zero because of cancellation in $\zch_e = z_0 + z_e$:
  1207‚Üí\begin{equation}
  1208‚Üív_0 = 17.716, \quad z_e = -17.001, \quad 
  1209‚Üí\zch_e = 17.716 - 17.001 = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}\,.
  1210‚Üí\end{equation}
  1211‚ÜíA 96\% cancellation. The mass $m_e = 0.715^2 = 0.511\MeV$ is the 
  1212‚Üísquare of something already small.
  1213‚Üí
  1214‚ÜíThe near-vanishing of $m_e$ is a structural property of the Koide 
  1215‚Üíembedding, not fine-tuning: the energy balance allows perturbations 
  1216‚Üías large as $z_0$. Every Koide triple has one member with 
  1217‚Üí$|\zch|/v_0$ small:
  1218‚Üí
  1219‚Üí\begin{center}
  1220‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccl}
  1221‚Üí\toprule
  1222‚ÜíTriple & Lightest & $|\zch|/v_0$ & Character \\
  1223‚Üí\midrule
  1224‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & $e$ & 0.040 & Almost-seed \\
  1225‚Üí$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & $\pi$ (neg.) & 0.337 & Full triple \\
  1226‚Üí$(c, b, t)$ & $c$ & 0.236 & Full triple \\
  1227‚Üí$(-s, c, b)$ & $s$ (neg.) & 0.320 & Full triple \\
  1228‚Üí\bottomrule
  1229‚Üí\end{tabular}
  1230‚Üí\end{center}
  1231‚Üí
  1232‚ÜíThe electron is by far the most extreme case, consistent with the 
  1233‚Üílepton triple being nearest to its seed.
  1234‚Üí
  1235‚Üí%======================================================================
  1236‚Üí\section{Symmetry Restoration}
  1237‚Üí\label{sec:restoration}
  1238‚Üí%======================================================================
  1239‚Üí
  1240‚ÜíThe broken spectrum reassembles as symmetry-breaking effects are 
  1241‚Üíremoved:
  1242‚Üí
  1243‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
  1244‚Üí\item \textbf{Turn off QED:} Electromagnetic splittings vanish 
  1245‚Üí($\pi^\pm = \pi^0$, $K^\pm = K^0$). D-term effects disappear.
  1246‚Üí\item \textbf{Set $m_u = m_d$:} Exact isospin.
  1247‚Üí\item \textbf{Set $m_s = m_d$:} $SU(3)_V$ restored, 
  1248‚Üí$\pi = K = \eta$. Koide splitting $v_1 \to 0$; lepton partners 
  1249‚Üídegenerate.
  1250‚Üí\item \textbf{Send $m_q \to 0$:} Chiral limit, all meson charges 
  1251‚Üí$\to 0$. If sBootstrap SUSY restores, all light lepton masses 
  1252‚Üívanish too. This is the seed configuration: $(0, 0, 0)$.
  1253‚Üí\end{enumerate}
  1254‚Üí
  1255‚ÜíThe seed triples are the ``penultimate'' stage: one member has 
  1256‚Üíreached zero, but the other two retain the $2 - \sqrt{3}$ ratio. 
  1257‚ÜíFull triples are the final broken state. The lepton triple, 
  1258‚Üítrapped between seed and bloom, reveals the hierarchy: 
  1259‚Üíchiral breaking $\to$ seed $\to$ bloom $\to$ full spectrum.
  1260‚Üí
  1261‚ÜíThe direction of the flow also matters: approaching the chiral 
  1262‚Üílimit, the pion charge rises from $\zch = -11.81$ toward zero 
  1263‚Üífrom below. It crosses zero exactly in the chiral limit. This 
  1264‚Üímeans the pion was ``born negative''---its charge sign is set by 
  1265‚Üíthe direction of chiral symmetry breaking, and the seed 
  1266‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ is the configuration at the moment of crossing.
  1267‚Üí
  1268‚Üí%======================================================================
  1269‚Üí\section{Nature Abhors Chirality}
  1270‚Üí\label{sec:chirality}
  1271‚Üí%======================================================================
  1272‚Üí
  1273‚ÜíA pattern across the Standard Model: every chiral structure is 
  1274‚Üíeliminated in the infrared.
  1275‚Üí
  1276‚Üí\begin{center}
  1277‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
  1278‚Üí\toprule
  1279‚ÜíUV structure & IR survivor & Mechanism \\
  1280‚Üí\midrule
  1281‚Üí$SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ & $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ & Higgs \\
  1282‚Üí$SU(N_f)_L \times SU(N_f)_R$ & $SU(N_f)_V$ & $\qqbar$ condensate \\
  1283‚Üí$U(1)_A$ & nothing & Anomaly \\
  1284‚Üí$\bar{\theta}$-vacuum & $|\bar{\theta}| < 10^{-10}$ & Unknown \\
  1285‚ÜíChiral color? & $SU(3)_C$ & Axigluon mass \\
  1286‚Üí\bottomrule
  1287‚Üí\end{tabular}
  1288‚Üí\end{center}
  1289‚Üí
  1290‚ÜíThe sBootstrap adds: superspace chirality (boson vs.\ fermion 
  1291‚Üídistinction within a multiplet) is broken by the same condensate 
  1292‚Üíthat breaks flavor chirality, with $f_{\text{SUSY}} = f_\pi$. The 
  1293‚Üíenergy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ may be 
  1294‚Üírelated: the breaking is ``as large as the background 
  1295‚Üíallows''---maximal, not perturbative.

  1297‚Üí%======================================================================
  1298‚Üí\section{Open Problems}
  1299‚Üí\label{sec:open}
  1300‚Üí%======================================================================
  1301‚Üí
  1302‚Üí\subsection{The diquark sector}
  1303‚ÜíThe Pauli theorem (\S\ref{subsec:pauli}) proves that no $J=0$, 
  1304‚Üícolor-$\bar{3}$ diquark can have symmetric flavor, closing all 
  1305‚Üífield-theoretic escape routes ($L=0$, P-wave, or higher). The 
  1306‚ÜísBootstrap's $\mathbf{15}$ diquark must therefore be non-composite: 
  1307‚Üíeither an algebraic image of the Miyazawa supercharge or a 
  1308‚Üífundamental $SO(32)$ string state. The exotic $+4/3$-charged 
  1309‚Üídiquarks predicted by the $\mathbf{15}$ are a testable consequence.
  1310‚Üí
  1311‚Üí\subsection{What determines the Koide phase?}
  1312‚ÜíThe energy balance constrains but does not uniquely fix $\delta$. 
  1313‚ÜíThe value $\delta_\ell \approx 2/9$ for leptons must come from 
  1314‚Üídynamics. Is it a fixed point?
  1315‚Üí
  1316‚Üí\subsection{Connecting the three tuples}
  1317‚ÜíThe light sector (seed $\to$ bloom, scale $\sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$) 
  1318‚Üíand heavy sector ($c,b,t$, scale $\sim v_{\text{EW}}$) must connect. 
  1319‚ÜíThe charm and bottom quarks appear in both, providing the links.
  1320‚Üí
  1321‚Üí\subsection{The baryon sector}
  1322‚ÜíIn Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, baryons are part of the 
  1323‚Üílow-energy spectrum. Their SUSY partners are ``baryonic fermions'' 
  1324‚Üíwith no known candidates among observed particles.
  1325‚Üí
  1326‚Üí\subsection{The strong CP problem}
  1327‚ÜíThe principle ``Nature abhors chirality'' predicts $\bar{\theta} = 0$ 
  1328‚Üíbut provides no mechanism.
  1329‚Üí
  1330‚Üí\subsection{The kaon--muon puzzle}
  1331‚ÜíThe SUSY splitting $m_K^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx (482\MeV)^2$ is much 
  1332‚Üílarger than $f_K^2 \approx (110\MeV)^2$, showing that the breaking 
  1333‚Üíis strongly flavor-dependent.
  1334‚Üí
  1335‚Üí%======================================================================
  1336‚Üí\section{Conclusions}
  1337‚Üí\label{sec:conclusions}
  1338‚Üí%======================================================================
  1339‚Üí
  1340‚ÜíThree principal results:
  1341‚Üí
  1342‚Üí\paragraph{1.\ The Koide formula is energy equipartition.}
  1343‚Üí$Q = 2/3$ is $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$: perturbation energies 
  1344‚Üímatch the vacuum energy. The signed-charge framework resolves the 
  1345‚Üíelectron mass puzzle (near-cancellation, not fine-tuning) and reveals 
  1346‚Üíthat mesons satisfy Koide with $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to $0.1\%$.
  1347‚Üí
  1348‚Üí\paragraph{2.\ Seed triples set the stage for SUSY breaking.}
  1349‚ÜíThe quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ share the 
  1350‚Üíuniversal ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$ and operate at $v_0^2 \sim 
  1351‚Üí230$--$350\MeV$, close to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 = 314\MeV$. 
  1352‚ÜíThe massless member is the necessary condition for F-term 
  1353‚Üí(O'Raifeartaigh) SUSY breaking. Upon breaking, seeds bloom into 
  1354‚Üífull triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi,D_s,B)$. The lepton triple 
  1355‚Üíis the ``almost-seed'' that has barely bloomed.
  1356‚Üí
  1357‚Üí\paragraph{3.\ Two breaking mechanisms plus a heavy-sector origin.}
  1358‚ÜíF-term breaking (via the massless seed member) generates the light 
  1359‚Üísector; the Volkov--Akulov relation $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is its 
  1360‚Üílow-energy realization, not a separate mechanism. D-term breaking 
  1361‚Üí($U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI parameter) provides electromagnetic 
  1362‚Üísplittings as a perturbative correction. The heavy triple 
  1363‚Üí$(c,b,t)$ stands apart: no seed, no meson shadow, scale far above 
  1364‚Üí$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, possibly connected to electroweak breaking.
  1365‚Üí
  1366‚ÜíThe diquark sector, required to complete the SUSY multiplet count, 
  1367‚Üífills the symmetric $\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ of 
  1368‚Üí$SU(5)_f$---the representation produced naturally by the SUSY 
  1369‚Üígenerator acting on quarks and by the $SO(32)$ decomposition. A 
  1370‚Üíkinematic theorem (\S\ref{subsec:pauli}) shows that \emph{no} 
  1371‚Üí$J=0$ color-$\bar{3}$ diquark can have symmetric flavor, regardless 
  1372‚Üíof orbital angular momentum: the sBootstrap diquark in the 
  1373‚Üí$\mathbf{15}$ cannot be a $qq$ composite. It must be an 
  1374‚Üíalgebraically generated object (Miyazawa supercharge) or a 
  1375‚Üífundamental string-theoretic state ($SO(32)$ adjoint).
  1376‚Üí
  1377‚ÜíThe organizing principle is that each sector of the spectrum begins 
  1378‚Üíwith a massless member (a seed), which is the necessary condition 
  1379‚Üífor spontaneous SUSY breaking. The breaking lifts the zero, 
  1380‚Üígenerates the full triple, and the energy-balance condition 
  1381‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is preserved as a fixed point 
  1382‚Üíof the dynamics. The entire charged fermion and pseudoscalar meson 
  1383‚Üíspectrum is then determined by a small number of parameters: the 
  1384‚Üívacuum charges $v_0$ of the three tuples, the Koide phases 
  1385‚Üí$\delta$ that control the bloom, and the SUSY-breaking scale 
  1386‚Üí$f_\pi$ that separates bosons from fermions.
