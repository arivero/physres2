    29→\begin{abstract}
    30→We reformulate the sBootstrap in terms of signed mass charges 
    31→$\zch = z_0 + z_i$ with $m = \zch^2$, and show that the Koide ratio 
    32→$Q = 2/3$ is the algebraic consequence of two conditions: 
    33→$\sum z_i = 0$ and $\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$ (perturbation 
    34→energies average to the vacuum energy). Allowing negative charges, 
    35→we find that pseudoscalar mesons satisfy a Koide triple 
    36→$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to 0.1\%, and identify \emph{seed triples} with 
    37→one massless member---$(0, s, c)$ for quarks, $(0, \pi, D_s)$ for 
    38→mesons---as the pre-breaking configurations from which the full 
    39→spectrum emerges. These seed triples share the same analytical 
    40→structure (charge ratio $2-\sqrt{3}$) and, strikingly, the meson 
    41→seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ has vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 351\MeV$, 
    42→close to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 \approx 314\MeV$. A third 
    43→tuple $(c,b,t)$ stands apart, having no meson counterpart because 
    44→top diquarks do not hadronize. We discuss the diquark sector---which 
    45→must fill the antisymmetric representation of $SU(5)$ flavor rather 
    46→than the symmetric, requiring additional dynamical input---and outline 
    47→how SUSY breaking proceeds through two mechanisms---F-term (chiral 
    48→condensate, with Volkov--Akulov as its low-energy realization) and 
    49→D-term (electromagnetic Fayet--Iliopoulos)---plus a separate 
    50→heavy-sector origin for the $(c,b,t)$ triple.
    51→\end{abstract}

    55→%======================================================================
    56→\section{Introduction}
    57→\label{sec:intro}
    58→%======================================================================
    59→
    60→The sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005,Rivero2012} proposes that the Standard 
    61→Model's pseudoscalar mesons and leptons fill common $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
    62→supermultiplets. Its foundational relation is
    63→\begin{equation}
    64→\label{eq:pimu}
    65→m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2\,,
    66→\end{equation}
    67→identifying the pion decay constant $f_\pi \approx 92.2\MeV$ as the 
    68→SUSY-breaking order parameter. Numerically, $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = 
    69→8{,}316\MeV^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 = 8{,}501\MeV^2$, a $2.2\%$ 
    70→discrepancy---comparable to the precision of the quark Koide triples 
    71→discussed below. In this paper we develop two themes 
    72→that change the structure of the program.
    73→
    74→First, \textbf{mass is the square of a signed charge}: $m = \zch^2$ 
    75→where $\zch = z_0 + z_i$ can be negative. The Koide ratio $Q = 2/3$ 
    76→is then not numerology but the energy-balance condition 
    77→$\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$. With signed charges, pseudoscalar 
    78→mesons satisfy Koide, and the spectrum organizes into \emph{seed 
    79→triples} with a massless member that grow into full triples upon 
    80→symmetry breaking.
    81→
    82→Second, \textbf{the full sBootstrap has three Koide tuples, not one}, 
    83→and the breaking structure must generate all three: a light sector 
    84→$(0,s,c) \to (-s,c,b)$ seen in both quarks and mesons, a lepton 
    85→sector $(e\!\approx\!0, \mu, \tau)$, and a heavy sector $(c,b,t)$ 
    86→that decouples from meson physics because the top quark does not 
    87→hadronize. The diquark partners of quarks, needed to complete the 
    88→SUSY multiplets, fill the symmetric $\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ 
    89→of $SU(5)$ flavor---the representation produced naturally by the 
    90→SUSY generator and by the $SO(32)$ decomposition. The ``wrong'' 
    91→Pauli sign of the symmetric diquark is a compositeness indicator: 
    92→the diquark is an extended object, not a point particle.

    94→%======================================================================
    95→\section{The Koide Formula as Energy Balance}
    96→\label{sec:koide_algebra}
    97→%======================================================================
    98→
    99→\subsection{Charges, not masses}
   100→
   101→The fundamental variable is the signed mass charge $\zch = z_0 + z_i 
   102→\in \mathbb{R}$, with the physical mass $m = \zch^2 \geq 0$.
   103→
   104→\subsection{Derivation of $Q = 2/3$}
   105→
   106→For three charges $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ with $z_0$ the mean 
   107→($\sum z_k = 0$):
   108→\begin{equation}
   109→Q = \frac{\sum \zch_k^2}{(\sum \zch_k)^2} 
   110→= \frac{3z_0^2 + \sum z_k^2}{9z_0^2}
   111→= \frac{1}{3} + \frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{3z_0^2}\,.
   112→\end{equation}
   113→Therefore:
   114→\begin{equation}
   115→\boxed{Q = \frac{2}{3} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad 
   116→\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2\,.}
   117→\end{equation}
   118→
   119→The two conditions are:
   120→\begin{enumerate}
   121→\item \textbf{Zero-sum perturbations:} $z_1 + z_2 + z_3 = 0$ 
   122→(definition of $z_0$ as the mean charge).
   123→\item \textbf{Energy balance:} The mean energy of the perturbations 
   124→equals the energy of the unperturbed charge: 
   125→$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
   126→\end{enumerate}
   127→
   128→Condition~2 is the physics. It says the splitting scale matches the 
   129→base scale---the coefficient of variation equals unity: 
   130→$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$. This is neither perturbative 
   131→($\sigma \ll \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1/3$, all equal) nor 
   132→dominant ($\sigma \gg \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1$, one mass 
   133→dominates). The Koide value $2/3$ is the equipartition point.
   134→
   135→\subsection{Why one mass can vanish}
   136→
   137→If $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$, a perturbation $z_k$ can reach 
   138→$|z_k| \sim z_0$, making $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k \approx 0$. A vanishing 
   139→mass is the \emph{natural extreme} of energy balance, not fine-tuning.
   140→
   141→%======================================================================
   142→\section{Seed Triples: The $(0, a, b)$ Structure}
   143→\label{sec:seeds}
   144→%======================================================================
   145→
   146→\subsection{Analytical condition}
   147→
   148→For a triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ with one massless member, the 
   149→Koide ratio becomes:
   150→\begin{equation}
   151→Q = \frac{\zch_a^2 + \zch_b^2}{(\zch_a + \zch_b)^2}\,.
   152→\end{equation}
   153→Setting $Q = 2/3$ and writing $r = \zch_a/\zch_b$ (with $r < 1$):
   154→\begin{equation}
   155→3(r^2 + 1) = 2(r + 1)^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad r^2 - 4r + 1 = 0\,,
   156→\end{equation}
   157→giving:
   158→\begin{equation}
   159→\boxed{r = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.}
   160→\end{equation}
   161→
   162→Equivalently, in mass space: 
   163→$m_{\text{light}}/m_{\text{heavy}} = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 = 7 - 4\sqrt{3} 
   164→\approx 0.07180$.
   165→
   166→This is a \emph{universal} prediction: any Koide triple with one 
   167→massless member has the same charge ratio between the two massive 
   168→members, regardless of their absolute scale.
   169→
   170→\subsection{The quark seed: $(0, s, c)$}
   171→
   172→The strange and charm quark charges give:\footnote{Throughout 
   173→this paper, quark masses follow PDG conventions: 
   174→$m_s(2\GeV) = 93.4\MeV$, $m_c(m_c) = 1.27\GeV$, 
   175→$m_b(m_b) = 4.18\GeV$, and the top pole mass 
   176→$m_t = 172.76\GeV$. This is a mixed convention: the light 
   177→quark is evaluated at 2 GeV while heavier quarks are 
   178→evaluated at their own scale. Since QCD mass ratios are 
   179→RG invariants (the anomalous dimension $\gamma_m$ is 
   180→flavor-universal at leading order), the Koide ratio $Q$ is 
   181→scheme-independent. However, the seed ratio 
   182→$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ as computed here uses masses at 
   183→\emph{different} scales, giving $0.271$; at a common scale 
   184→the ratio is $\sim 0.29$, further from $2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.268$. 
   185→The meson seed, using unambiguous physical (pole) masses, 
   186→is free of this scheme issue and provides the cleaner test.}
   187→\begin{equation}
   188→\frac{\sqrt{m_s}}{\sqrt{m_c}} = \frac{9.67}{35.64} = 0.2712\,, 
   189→\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 1.2\%\,.
   190→\end{equation}
   191→
   192→This triple has $Q = 0.6644$, deviating from $2/3$ by only $0.35\%$. 
   193→Its vacuum energy is $v_0^2 = (\zch_s + \zch_c)^2/9 \approx 228\MeV$.
   194→
   195→A historical note: Harari, Haut, and Weyers \cite{Harari} observed 
   196→a quark mass ratio pattern that, in the language of the present paper, 
   197→corresponds to an approximate seed triple---$(0, d, s)$---at a time 
   198→when the up quark mass was 
   199→still thought to be possibly zero \cite{Harari}. With current masses, 
   200→$\sqrt{m_d}/\sqrt{m_s} = 0.224$, deviating from $2 - \sqrt{3}$ by 
   201→$16\%$---too large for a good Koide triple. But if $m_s$ were 
   202→$\sim 150\MeV$ and $m_d \sim 7\MeV$ (values common in that era), the 
   203→ratio improves considerably. The seed idea was already present, ahead 
   204→of its time.
   205→
   206→\subsection{The meson seed: $(0, \pi, D_s)$}
   207→
   208→The pion and $D_s$ meson charges give:
   209→\begin{equation}
   210→\frac{\sqrt{m_\pi}}{\sqrt{m_{D_s}}} = \frac{11.81}{44.37} = 0.2663\,, 
   211→\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 0.6\%\,.
   212→\end{equation}
   213→
   214→This gives $Q = 0.6679$, deviating by $0.18\%$. It is the 
   215→\textbf{meson counterpart} of the quark seed $(0, s, c)$---both 
   216→involve the strange-charm sector. Indeed, $D_s = c\bar{s}$ is the 
   217→meson that directly contains the $s$ and $c$ quarks.
   218→
   219→\subsection{The lepton triple as an ``almost-seed''}
   220→
   221→The charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ has $\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$, 
   222→which is small but nonzero. If $m_e$ were zero, we would need 
   223→$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.2679$; the actual value 
   224→is $0.2439$, deviating by $9\%$. So the leptons are \emph{not} an 
   225→exact seed---the electron, while very light, is not massless. But the 
   226→triple is clearly the same family: the near-critical Koide phase puts 
   227→one member close to zero.
   228→
   229→\subsection{Vacuum energy coincidence}
   230→
   231→The $v_0^2$ values of the seed triples cluster remarkably:
   232→
   233→\begin{center}
   234→\begin{tabular}{lcc}
   235→\toprule
   236→Triple & $v_0^2$ (MeV) & $v_0$ ($\MeV^{1/2}$) \\
   237→\midrule
   238→$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 & 17.72 \\
   239→$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 351 & 18.73 \\
   240→$(0, s, c)$ & 228 & 15.10 \\
   241→\bottomrule
   242→\end{tabular}
   243→\end{center}
   244→
   245→The lepton and meson seed triples share a vacuum energy around 
   246→310--350~MeV, squarely in the QCD scale range. This is the 
   247→sBootstrap connection made quantitative: \textbf{the lepton triple 
   248→and the meson seed triple operate at the same scale.}

   250→%======================================================================
   251→\section{A Tale of Two Tuples}
   252→\label{sec:two_tuples}
   253→%======================================================================
   254→
   255→\subsection{From seed to full triple}
   256→
   257→The seed triples grow into full triples upon symmetry breaking:
   258→
   259→\begin{center}
   260→\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   261→\toprule
   262→Sector & Seed triple & Full triple & $v_0^2$ (full) \\
   263→\midrule
   264→Quarks & $(0, s, c)$ & $(-s, c, b)$ & 913 MeV \\
   265→Mesons & $(0, \pi, D_s)$ & $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 1230 MeV \\
   266→Leptons & $(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & $(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 MeV \\
   267→\bottomrule
   268→\end{tabular}
   269→\end{center}
   270→
   271→In each case:
   272→\begin{itemize}
   273→\item The seed has a massless (or near-massless) member, two massive 
   274→members in the ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$, and $Q \approx 2/3$.
   275→\item The full triple has \textbf{no} massless member: the zero has 
   276→been lifted (to $m_e$, or the sign-flipped strange/pion mass). The 
   277→$b$ quark and $B$ meson are always present as flavor degrees of 
   278→freedom; what the breaking does is give them their \emph{distinctive 
   279→masses}---the specific values that complete the Koide triple. Before 
   280→breaking, the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$); 
   281→after, it acquires $\zch_b = 64.65\MeV^{1/2}$, the value selected 
   282→by the energy balance with $c$ and the sign-flipped $s$. The bloom 
   283→is not particle creation but \emph{mass generation}.
   284→\end{itemize}
   285→
   286→The ``tale of two tuples'' is this: \textbf{each sector presents 
   287→first as a seed with a massless member, then ``blooms'' into a 
   288→full triple when SUSY breaks.} The massless member is the necessary 
   289→initial condition; the breaking lifts it and generates the full spectrum.
   290→
   291→\subsection{Precision summary}
   292→
   293→\begin{center}
   294→\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   295→\toprule
   296→Triple & $Q$ & Dev.\ from $2/3$ & $v_0^2$ (MeV) \\
   297→\midrule
   298→\multicolumn{4}{c}{\textit{Seed triples}} \\
   299→$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 0.6679 & 0.18\% & 351 \\
   300→$(0, s, c)$ & 0.6644 & 0.35\% & 228 \\
   301→$(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.6667 & 0.001\% & 314 \\
   302→\midrule
   303→\multicolumn{4}{c}{\textit{Full triples}} \\
   304→$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.66666 & 0.001\% & 314 \\
   305→$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 0.6674 & 0.10\% & 1230 \\
   306→$(-s, c, b)$ & 0.6750 & 1.24\% & 913 \\
   307→$(c, b, t)$ & 0.6695 & 0.42\% & 29\,580 \\
   308→\bottomrule
   309→\end{tabular}
   310→\end{center}
   311→
   312→The lepton triple is unique: seed and full triple are nearly 
   313→identical, because the electron mass is small enough to serve as 
   314→the ``zero'' while being nonzero enough to make $Q$ extremely 
   315→precise.
   316→
   317→\subsection{Orthogonality of seed and full triples}
   318→
   319→Each Koide triple defines a perturbation vector 
   320→$\vec{z} = (z_1, z_2, z_3)$ constrained to the plane $\sum z_k = 0$ 
   321→with length $|\vec{z}|^2 = 3z_0^2$. The angle between seed and 
   322→full triples measures how much the breaking rotates the spectrum 
   323→in charge space:
   324→
   325→\begin{center}
   326→\begin{tabular}{lc}
   327→\toprule
   328→Pair of triples & Angle \\
   329→\midrule
   330→$(0,s,c)$ vs.\ $(-s,c,b)$ & $22^\circ$ \\
   331→$(0,\pi,D_s)$ vs.\ $(-\pi,D_s,B)$ & $26^\circ$ \\
   332→$(e,\mu,\tau)$ vs.\ $(0,\mu,\tau)$ & $0.8^\circ$ \\
   333→$(0,s,c)$ vs.\ $(0,\pi,D_s)$ & $0.3^\circ$ \\
   334→\bottomrule
   335→\end{tabular}
   336→\end{center}
   337→
   338→Two results are notable. First, the quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson 
   339→seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ are \textbf{nearly parallel} ($0.3^\circ$): the 
   340→meson seed is the composite image of the quark seed, with the GOR 
   341→map preserving direction while changing scale. Second, the lepton 
   342→triple is essentially its own seed ($0.8^\circ$)---the bloom has 
   343→barely begun.

   346→%======================================================================
   347→\section{The Meson Koide Triple: $(-\pi, D_s, B)$}
   348→\label{sec:mesonkoide}
   349→%======================================================================
   350→
   351→With the pion charge negative, the triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ satisfies:
   352→\begin{equation}
   353→Q(-\pi, D_s, B) = 
   354→\frac{m_\pi + m_{D_s} + m_B}{(-\sqrt{m_\pi} + \sqrt{m_{D_s}} + \sqrt{m_B})^2}
   355→= 0.6674\,,
   356→\end{equation}
   357→deviating from $2/3$ by $0.10\%$---the second most precise Koide 
   358→triple known. The energy balance:
   359→\begin{equation}
   360→\frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{z_0^2} = 1.002\,.
   361→\end{equation}
   362→
   363→For comparison, the ``standard'' all-positive quark triples $(d,s,b)$ 
   364→and $(u,c,t)$ deviate from $Q = 2/3$ by 9.7\% and 27\% respectively 
   365→at $\overline{\text{MS}}$ masses. \textbf{The meson triple is more 
   366→precise than any quark triple} except $(c,b,t)$.
   367→
   368→The pion's negative charge has physical meaning: as a pseudo-Goldstone 
   369→boson, the pion would be massless ($\zch = 0$) if chiral symmetry were 
   370→exact. It sits just below zero ($\zch_\pi = -11.81$), approaching zero 
   371→from the negative side as $m_q \to 0$. The sign of $\zch$ encodes 
   372→Goldstone nature: pseudo-Goldstone bosons sit on the negative side 
   373→of the critical point, having crossed through zero from positive 
   374→territory during chiral symmetry breaking.

   376→%======================================================================
   377→\section{The Third Tuple: $(c, b, t)$}
   378→\label{sec:cbt}
   379→%======================================================================
   380→
   381→\subsection{A tuple without mesons}
   382→
   383→The triple $(c, b, t)$ with all positive signs gives $Q = 0.6695$, 
   384→deviating by $0.42\%$. Its vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ 
   385→is far above the QCD scale.
   386→
   387→This triple has \textbf{no meson counterpart}: the top quark decays 
   388→weakly ($t \to Wb$, lifetime $\sim 5 \times 10^{-25}$~s) before the 
   389→strong interaction can bind it ($\tau_{\text{QCD}} \sim 1/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} 
   390→\sim 10^{-24}$~s). No $T$-meson ($t\bar{q}$) exists. The meson matrix 
   391→$M^i{}_j$ extends only to $b$-flavored states; the top sector lives 
   392→purely at the quark level.
   393→
   394→\subsection{Diquarks and the top}
   395→
   396→In the sBootstrap, diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ are the scalar partners of 
   397→antiquarks $\bar{q}_k$, with the baryon acting as the SUSY generator 
   398→\cite{CattoGursey, Miyazawa}. For five active flavors 
   399→$(u, d, s, c, b)$, the diquarks form a representation of $SU(5)_f$.
   400→
   401→The top diquarks $[tq]$ are kinematic objects only---they do not 
   402→hadronize. Yet their masses should follow the same pattern as other 
   403→diquarks: \textbf{there is no dynamical reason for top diquark masses 
   404→to differ from the others}. The $(c,b,t)$ Koide triple governs the 
   405→quark masses that enter the diquark sector, and its energy balance 
   406→$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ constrains the possible diquark 
   407→spectrum even though the top diquarks are never observed as bound 
   408→states.
   409→
   410→\subsection{The $(c,b,t)$ triple has no seed}
   411→
   412→Unlike the $(0,s,c)$ and $(0,\pi,D_s)$ seeds, there is no obvious 
   413→$(0, x, y)$ seed for the $(c,b,t)$ triple. No pair of quarks in this 
   414→sector satisfies $\sqrt{m_{\text{light}}}/\sqrt{m_{\text{heavy}}} 
   415→\approx 2 - \sqrt{3}$: one has $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_t} = 0.086$ 
   416→(too small) and $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_b} = 0.551$ (too large).
   417→
   418→This means $(c,b,t)$ is \emph{not} a ``bloomed seed'' in the same 
   419→sense as $(-s,c,b)$. It may represent a fundamentally different 
   420→structure---a triple that was never seeded by a massless member but 
   421→instead emerged fully formed at a higher breaking scale, perhaps 
   422→connected to electroweak symmetry breaking rather than to chiral 
   423→symmetry breaking.

   425→%======================================================================
   426→\section{The Diquark Sector and SU(5) Flavor}
   427→\label{sec:diquarks}
   428→%======================================================================
   429→
   430→\subsection{Counting partners}
   431→
   432→In the full sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005}, the scalar partners of 
   433→the Standard Model fermions include both mesons ($q\bar{q}$) and 
   434→diquarks ($qq$). For five active flavors, the meson matrix gives 
   435→$5 \times 5 = 25$ scalars. The diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ form a 
   436→representation of $SU(5)_f$:
   437→\begin{equation}
   438→\mathbf{5} \otimes \mathbf{5} = \overline{\mathbf{10}}_A 
   439→\oplus \mathbf{15}_S\,.
   440→\end{equation}
   441→
   442→The symmetric $\mathbf{15}_S$ gives 15 diquarks including 
   443→same-flavor states $[uu]$, $[dd]$, \ldots, while the antisymmetric 
   444→$\overline{\mathbf{10}}_A$ gives 10 diquarks with $i \neq j$ only.
   445→
   446→\subsection{The symmetric representation}
   447→\label{subsec:sym15}
   448→
   449→The sBootstrap SUSY generator acts asymmetrically on the two 
   450→fermion sectors. When it acts on a \emph{lepton}, it produces a 
   451→meson: $Q \cdot \ell \sim q\bar{q}$. Since the quark and antiquark 
   452→are distinguishable, the meson lies in the full 
   453→$\mathbf{5} \otimes \bar{\mathbf{5}} = \mathbf{24} + \mathbf{1}$. 
   454→There is no Pauli constraint, and the meson spectrum is perfectly 
   455→reproduced.
   456→
   457→When it acts on a \emph{quark}, it produces a diquark: 
   458→$Q \cdot q_i \sim q_i q_j$. The bosonic SUSY generator applied to 
   459→a single flavor index $i$ naturally produces the \textbf{symmetric} 
   460→product---the $\mathbf{15}_S$ representation, not the antisymmetric 
   461→$\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. This is the representation identified 
   462→in the $SO(32)$ decomposition \cite{Rivero2005}: the adjoint of 
   463→$SO(32)$ under $SU(5)_f$ decomposes to include the 
   464→$\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ (diquarks) alongside the 
   465→$\mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ (mesons).
   466→
   467→The 15 includes 5 same-flavor states ($[uu]$, $[dd]$, $[ss]$, 
   468→$[cc]$, $[bb]$) absent from the $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. Among 
   469→these are exotic $+4/3$-charged diquarks such as $[uu]$. The cost: 
   470→an additional chiral $+4/3$ quark per generation, as identified 
   471→in \cite{Rivero2005}. Whether these exotic states appear in nature 
   472→as resonances, or whether they decouple at some high scale, is an 
   473→open question.
   474→
   475→\subsection{The Pauli sign as a compositeness indicator}
   476→\label{subsec:pauli}
   477→
   478→The requirement of the symmetric $\mathbf{15}$ conflicts with a 
   479→naive application of Fermi statistics. For spin-0, $L=0$ diquarks 
   480→in the color $\bar{3}$ (antisymmetric) channel:
   481→
   482→\begin{center}
   483→\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   484→\toprule
   485→Property & Symmetry & Factor & \\
   486→\midrule
   487→Color $\bar{3}$ & Antisymmetric & $-1$ & \\
   488→Spin 0 & Antisymmetric & $-1$ & \\
   489→Spatial $L=0$ & Symmetric & $+1$ & \\
   490→\midrule
   491→Non-flavor product & Symmetric & $+1$ & \\
   492→For overall A: & & & flavor must be \textbf{A} $= \overline{\mathbf{10}}$ \\
   493→\bottomrule
   494→\end{tabular}
   495→\end{center}
   496→
   497→Pauli statistics for point-like diquarks requires 
   498→\emph{antisymmetric} flavor---the $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$. 
   499→The sBootstrap SUSY generator and the $SO(32)$ embedding both 
   500→demand the \emph{symmetric} $\mathbf{15}$. This is a genuine 
   501→conflict for point particles.
   502→
   503→The resolution lies in the contrast between the two SUSY 
   504→directions. When the generator acts on leptons, it produces 
   505→\emph{mesons}---and these work perfectly: $q\bar{q}$ involves 
   506→distinguishable constituents, no Pauli constraint applies, and 
   507→the meson spectrum is reproduced without tension. When the 
   508→generator acts on quarks, it produces \emph{diquarks} in the 
   509→``wrong'' representation. The wrong Pauli sign is not a bug but 
   510→a \emph{compositeness indicator}: it tells us that the diquark 
   511→is an extended object, not a point particle. The two quarks inside 
   512→the diquark are spatially separated, so the $L=0$ assumption 
   513→that enters the Pauli argument breaks down. The diquark is a 
   514→bound state whose internal structure defeats the point-particle 
   515→statistics, much as the deuteron (two fermions, spin 1) exists 
   516→in an $L \neq 0$ state that accommodates both its spin and its 
   517→antisymmetry.
   518→
   519→This asymmetry between the lepton $\to$ meson and quark $\to$ 
   520→diquark directions is a central structural feature of the 
   521→sBootstrap: the hadronic SUSY maps cleanly onto the meson sector 
   522→(where $q\bar{q}$ pairs are ``free'') but encounters resistance 
   523→in the diquark sector (where $qq$ pairs must negotiate Fermi 
   524→statistics). The resistance manifests as spatial extension---the 
   525→diquark is necessarily a composite object with internal orbital 
   526→structure, not a simple scalar.

   534→$\mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ (mesons/sleptons) and the

   537→This is suggestive: the same group structure that gives anomaly-free

   546→\section{SUSY Breaking: From Seeds to Spectrum}
   547→\label{sec:breaking}
   548→%======================================================================
   549→
   550→The SUSY-breaking story begins with the foundational observation 
   551→\eqref{eq:pimu}: $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2$. This is the 
   552→Volkov--Akulov mass formula for a pseudo-Goldstino---the muon---whose 
   553→superpartner---the pion---is a pseudo-Goldstone boson. The SUSY-breaking 
   554→scale is $f_\pi \approx 92\MeV$, and the breaking mechanism is chiral: 
   555→$\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   556→simultaneously. The Volkov--Akulov realization is the \emph{physical 
   557→starting point}; what follows in this section is the SQCD machinery 
   558→that makes it precise.
   559→
   560→The dynamics must accomplish three things: generate the seed-to-bloom 
   561→transition for each tuple, preserve the energy-balance condition 
   562→$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ through the breaking, and produce 
   563→the correct splitting between meson and lepton masses at scale $f_\pi$.
   564→
   565→\subsection{The Seiberg framework}
   566→
   567→The natural arena is $\mathcal{N}=1$ SQCD with gauge group $SU(3)_c$ 
   568→and $N_f$ flavors of chiral superfields $Q^i$, $\bar{Q}_j$ in the 
   569→fundamental and antifundamental representations. For $N_f = N_c = 3$ 
   570→(the case relevant to light QCD), Seiberg's exact results 
   571→\cite{Seiberg1995} establish that the theory confines, with the 
   572→low-energy degrees of freedom being the composite meson superfield
   573→\begin{equation}
   574→M^i{}_j = Q^i \cdot \bar{Q}_j\,,
   575→\end{equation}
   576→a $3\times 3$ matrix of chiral superfields, together with baryons 
   577→$B = \epsilon_{ijk}Q^iQ^jQ^k$ and $\bar{B}$. These satisfy the 
   578→quantum-modified constraint
   579→\begin{equation}
   580→\label{eq:seiberg}
   581→\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^{2N_c}\,,
   582→\end{equation}
   583→where $\Lambda$ is the dynamical scale.
   584→
   585→Each entry $M^i{}_j$ is a chiral superfield containing a complex 
   586→scalar (the meson) and a Weyl fermion (the lepton, in the sBootstrap 
   587→identification). The scalar and fermion are degenerate in the SUSY 
   588→limit; SUSY breaking splits them.
   589→
   590→\subsection{The three chiral superfields of each seed}
   591→
   592→The seed triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ maps naturally onto three 
   593→chiral superfields with the following mass structure before breaking:
   594→
   595→\begin{center}
   596→\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   597→\toprule
   598→Superfield & Mass & Role \\
   599→\midrule
   600→$\Phi_0$ & $m_0 = 0$ & Massless: flat direction \\
   601→$\Phi_a$ & $m_a = \zch_a^2$ & Light massive member \\
   602→$\Phi_b$ & $m_b = \zch_b^2$ & Heavy massive member \\
   603→\bottomrule
   604→\end{tabular}
   605→\end{center}
   606→
   607→The ratio $\zch_a/\zch_b = 2 - \sqrt{3}$ is enforced by the 
   608→Koide energy-balance condition applied to the seed.
   609→
   610→In the Seiberg description, these three superfields are entries of 
   611→the meson matrix $M^i{}_j$. For the $(0, s, c)$ seed, the relevant 
   612→entries involve the strange and charm quarks; $\Phi_0$ is the entry 
   613→whose quark-mass combination vanishes in the seed limit (i.e., the 
   614→direction in flavor space where $m_i + m_j \to 0$ through GOR).

   616→\subsection{F-term breaking: the O'Raifeartaigh mechanism}
   617→\label{subsec:fterm}
   618→
   619→The VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} tells us \emph{that} SUSY is broken at 
   620→scale $f_\pi$. To understand \emph{how}, we need the microscopic 
   621→mechanism. The O'Raifeartaigh model \cite{ORaifeartaigh} is the simplest 
   622→realization of spontaneous $F$-term SUSY breaking. With three chiral 
   623→superfields, the superpotential
   624→\begin{equation}
   625→W = f\,\Phi_0 + m\,\Phi_1\Phi_2 + g\,\Phi_0\Phi_1^2
   626→\end{equation}
   627→gives $F$-terms:
   628→\begin{align}
   629→F_{\Phi_0}^* &= f + g\,\phi_1^2\,, \\
   630→F_{\Phi_1}^* &= m\,\phi_2 + 2g\,\phi_0\phi_1\,, \\
   631→F_{\Phi_2}^* &= m\,\phi_1\,.
   632→\end{align}
   633→If $f \neq 0$ and $m \neq 0$, then $F_{\Phi_2}^* = 0$ requires 
   634→$\phi_1 = 0$, but then $F_{\Phi_0}^* = f \neq 0$: SUSY is 
   635→\textbf{necessarily broken}. The key features are:
   636→\begin{enumerate}
   637→\item A massless field ($\Phi_0$) exists at tree level---it is the 
   638→pseudo-modulus.
   639→\item The breaking is triggered because $F_{\Phi_0}$ cannot vanish.
   640→\item After including loop corrections, $\Phi_0$ acquires a mass 
   641→(the Coleman--Weinberg potential lifts the flat direction).
   642→\end{enumerate}
   643→
   644→The structural parallel with the seed triples is:
   645→
   646→\begin{center}
   647→\begin{tabular}{llll}
   648→\toprule
   649→O'Raifeartaigh & Flavor & Seed & After breaking \\
   650→\midrule
   651→$\Phi_0$ (massless) & $b$ / $B$ & $\zch = 0$ (flat direction) & $\zch_b = 64.65$ (mass generated) \\
   652→$\Phi_1$ (light) & $s$ / $\pi$ & $\zch_s = 9.67$ & $\zch \to -9.67$ (sign-flipped) \\
   653→$\Phi_2$ (heavy) & $c$ / $D_s$ & $\zch_c = 35.64$ & $\zch_c \approx 35.64$ (shifted) \\
   654→\midrule
   655→ & & $f = 0$ (SUSY) & $f = f_\pi$ (broken) \\
   656→\bottomrule
   657→\end{tabular}
   658→\end{center}
   659→
   660→This parallel is \emph{structural}, not quantitative: the minimal 
   661→O'Raifeartaigh model gives $\Phi_1$ and $\Phi_2$ the same mass 
   662→parameter $m$, whereas the seed triple has a mass ratio 
   663→$m_s/m_c = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 \approx 0.07$---far from degenerate. 
   664→The hierarchy between the two massive members must come from 
   665→additional dynamics beyond the minimal O'Raifeartaigh superpotential. 
   666→What the O'Raifeartaigh structure \emph{does} provide is the essential 
   667→feature: a massless flat direction ($\Phi_0 = $ the $b$-direction) 
   668→whose $F$-term is forced nonzero. The $b$ quark and $B$ meson are 
   669→always present as flavor degrees of freedom; the seed configuration 
   670→$(0, s, c)$ means the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction before 
   671→breaking. The bloom is not particle creation but mass generation: 
   672→breaking lifts $\Phi_0$ from zero to $\zch_b \neq 0$, simultaneously 
   673→flipping the sign of $\zch_s$, so that the same three flavors 
   674→rearrange from $(0, s, c)$ into $(-s, c, b)$.
   675→
   676→A striking difference from standard O'Raifeartaigh: the 
   677→pseudo-modulus $\Phi_0$ normally acquires the \emph{smallest} mass 
   678→in the spectrum (a loop-suppressed Coleman--Weinberg mass, 
   679→parametrically $\sim g^2 f/16\pi^2 m$). In the sBootstrap bloom, 
   680→the $b$-direction acquires the \emph{largest} mass 
   681→($m_b \gg m_s, m_c$). The bloom is far more dramatic than a 
   682→perturbative CW lifting---it is a nonperturbative, strong-dynamics 
   683→effect in which confinement generates a mass of order $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2/m_q$, 
   684→not $g^2/(16\pi^2)$. This inversion is a signature of the 
   685→dynamical (SQCD) realization versus the perturbative 
   686→(superpotential) one.
   687→
   688→The identification $f = \langle F_{\Phi_0}\rangle \sim f_\pi$ is the 
   689→sBootstrap's central claim: the SUSY-breaking $F$-term is the chiral 
   690→condensate parameter.
   691→
   692→In SQCD, this is realized dynamically rather than through a 
   693→superpotential: the chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ acts as the 
   694→nonzero $F$-term, the impossibility of restoring chiral symmetry 
   695→in the QCD vacuum is the dynamical analogue of $f \neq 0$, and the 
   696→pion---the would-be Goldstone boson that acquires mass from explicit 
   697→chiral breaking---plays the role of $\Phi_0$ whose mass is lifted 
   698→by loop corrections (here, by quark masses).

   700→\subsection{The Goldstino direction and the Volkov--Akulov realization}
   701→\label{subsec:va}
   702→
   703→When SUSY is spontaneously broken, a massless fermion appears: the 
   704→Goldstino, the fermionic analogue of the Goldstone boson. Its decay 
   705→constant $f_{\text{VA}}$ sets the SUSY-breaking scale through the 
   706→Volkov--Akulov Lagrangian \cite{VolkovAkulov1973}:
   707→\begin{equation}
   708→\mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}} = -f_{\text{VA}}^2 \det\!\left(
   709→\delta_\mu^\nu + \frac{i}{f_{\text{VA}}^2}\,\bar{\lambda}\bar{\sigma}^\nu
   710→\partial_\mu\lambda\right),
   711→\end{equation}
   712→where $\lambda$ is the Goldstino field. At low energies, the Goldstino 
   713→couples derivatively with strength $\sim 1/f_{\text{VA}}^2$, exactly 
   714→as the pion couples with strength $\sim 1/f_\pi^2$ in the chiral 
   715→Lagrangian.
   716→
   717→The sBootstrap identification is:
   718→\begin{equation}
   719→\label{eq:va_id}
   720→f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi\,, \qquad 
   721→\text{Goldstino} = \text{muon (partner of }\pi\text{)}\,.
   722→\end{equation}
   723→More precisely, the muon is a \emph{pseudo-Goldstino}: in the 
   724→limit $m_q \to 0$ where the pion becomes exactly massless (a true 
   725→Goldstone boson), its fermionic partner also becomes massless (a 
   726→true Goldstino). The finite quark masses explicitly break both 
   727→chiral symmetry (giving $m_\pi \neq 0$) and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   728→(giving $m_\mu \neq m_\pi$), with the splitting controlled by 
   729→$f_\pi$:
   730→\begin{equation}
   731→m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2\,.
   732→\end{equation}
   733→
   734→This is the Volkov--Akulov relation for the pseudo-Goldstino: the 
   735→mass splitting between the scalar (pion) and its fermionic partner 
   736→(muon) is set by the SUSY-breaking scale squared.
   737→
   738→The VA mechanism is not a separate breaking from the F-term: it is 
   739→the \emph{low-energy, composite-level description} of the same 
   740→breaking. At the microscopic level, $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ 
   741→in the meson superfield; at the composite level, this manifests 
   742→as the Goldstino coupling with $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$. These 
   743→two quantities---the Goldstino decay constant and the pion decay 
   744→constant---are \emph{identified} in the sBootstrap; this is the 
   745→central claim, not a derivation.
   746→
   747→The identification should not be confused with the standard relation 
   748→between the $F$-term VEV and the chiral condensate. In standard 
   749→SQCD, $\langle F\rangle \sim |\qqbar|/f_\pi \sim (250\MeV)^3/92\MeV 
   750→\approx 170{,}000\MeV^2$, while $f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2$---a 
   751→factor of $\sim 20$ discrepancy. The GOR relation 
   752→$m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$ connects these through the 
   753→small quark masses, but does not equate them. The sBootstrap 
   754→identification $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is therefore a 
   755→\emph{low-energy effective} statement about the composite sector, 
   756→not a microscopic identity. It says: the scale at which SUSY 
   757→appears broken in the meson--lepton spectrum is $f_\pi$, regardless 
   758→of the microscopic $F$-term that produces this breaking.
   759→
   760→The VA realization gives more than a mass formula---it organizes the 
   761→entire breaking narrative. In the chiral limit ($m_q \to 0$), both 
   762→pion and muon are exactly massless: the pion as the Goldstone boson 
   763→of broken chiral symmetry, the muon as the Goldstino of broken 
   764→sBootstrap SUSY. This is the \emph{exact seed}: the $B$-meson 
   765→direction sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$), and the 
   766→$\pi$--$\mu$ pair is massless. Turning on quark masses ($m_q \neq 0$) 
   767→does three things simultaneously:
   768→\begin{enumerate}
   769→\item Gives the pion its GOR mass (lifting the flat direction for $\pi$).
   770→\item Gives the muon its mass (the pseudo-Goldstino acquires mass 
   771→when the explicit breaking parameter $m_q$ is nonzero).
   772→\item Generates the $B$-meson mass (the flat direction for $\Phi_0$ 
   773→is lifted by the Coleman--Weinberg potential, i.e., by the same 
   774→quark masses that feed the GOR formula).
   775→\end{enumerate}
   776→The splitting between (1) and (2) is $f_\pi^2$---the VA relation. 
   777→The seed-to-bloom transition of \S\ref{sec:two_tuples} is the same 
   778→physics seen from the charge-space perspective: $m_q \neq 0$ lifts 
   779→the zero, generates the $b$/$B$ mass, flips the sign of $\zch_s$ 
   780→and $\zch_\pi$, and produces the full triple. The VA equation 
   781→\eqref{eq:pimu} is not one fact among many; it is the generating 
   782→relation from which the bloom follows.
   783→
   784→A note on quantum numbers: the Goldstino of spontaneously broken 
   785→spacetime SUSY is a neutral fermion (the fermionic component of the 
   786→superfield whose $F$-term breaks SUSY). The muon carries electric 
   787→charge and lepton number. The sBootstrap SUSY, however, is not a 
   788→spacetime symmetry but an emergent hadronic symmetry---a 
   789→Miyazawa-type superalgebra \cite{Miyazawa} acting on composite 
   790→states. In this context, the ``Goldstino'' need not carry the 
   791→quantum numbers of a spacetime supercharge; it carries the quantum 
   792→numbers appropriate to the broken hadronic generator. The muon's 
   793→charge is inherited from the pion multiplet it partners.

   795→\subsection{D-term breaking: electromagnetic splittings}
   796→\label{subsec:dterm}
   797→
   798→The unbroken $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ gauge symmetry allows a 
   799→Fayet--Iliopoulos (FI) term \cite{FayetIliopoulos}:
   800→\begin{equation}
   801→\mathcal{L}_{\text{FI}} = \xi\,D_{\text{em}}\,,
   802→\end{equation}
   803→which shifts scalar masses by $\delta m^2 = Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi$, 
   804→where $Q_{\text{em}}$ is the electric charge. This is the 
   805→supersymmetric version of the electromagnetic mass difference.
   806→
   807→In QCD without SUSY, the electromagnetic mass splitting of the 
   808→pion is given by the Das--Guralnik--Mathur--Low--Young (DGMLY) 
   809→formula \cite{DGMLY1967}:
   810→\begin{equation}
   811→m_{\pi^\pm}^2 - m_{\pi^0}^2 = \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,
   812→\frac{m_\rho^2}{f_\pi^2}\,f_\pi^2 
   813→\approx (35.5\MeV)^2\,,
   814→\end{equation}
   815→which in the SUSY framework becomes a D-term contribution:
   816→\begin{equation}
   817→\xi \sim \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,m_\rho^2 
   818→\sim (35\MeV)^2\,.
   819→\end{equation}
   820→
   821→In charge space, the D-term shifts $\zch$ by an amount proportional 
   822→to $Q_{\text{em}}$:
   823→\begin{equation}
   824→\zch \to \zch + \delta\zch\,, \qquad 
   825→\delta\zch \approx \frac{Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi}{2\zch}\,.
   826→\end{equation}
   827→For the pion ($Q_{\text{em}} = \pm 1$, $\zch \approx 11.8$): 
   828→$\delta\zch \approx 0.2\MeV^{1/2}$, matching the observed 
   829→$\zch_{\pi^\pm} - \zch_{\pi^0} = 11.82 - 11.62 = 0.20\MeV^{1/2}$.
   830→
   831→The D-term effect is subleading to the F-term: 
   832→$\xi \sim (35\MeV)^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 \sim (92\MeV)^2$. It 
   833→operates \emph{within} each supermultiplet entry, splitting charged 
   834→from neutral mesons, while the F-term splits bosons from fermions 
   835→across the supermultiplet.
   836→
   837→Witten's inequality \cite{Witten1983} guarantees 
   838→$m_{\pi^\pm} > m_{\pi^0}$ in QCD with electromagnetism---the 
   839→charged pion is always heavier. In the SUSY framework this 
   840→becomes the statement that the D-term contribution is positive 
   841→for $Q_{\text{em}} \neq 0$, consistent with the FI sign.
   842→
   843→\subsection{Mapping the breaking to the tuples}
   844→\label{subsec:mapping}
   845→
   846→The tuple structure maps onto a layered breaking with two independent 
   847→mechanisms (F-term and D-term) plus a separate heavy-sector origin. 
   848→The Volkov--Akulov realization of \S\ref{subsec:va} is not a third 
   849→mechanism: it is the low-energy, composite-level description of the 
   850→same F-term breaking. The VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} \emph{sets the 
   851→scale} ($f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$); the O'Raifeartaigh/SQCD dynamics 
   852→of \S\ref{subsec:fterm} \emph{provides the mechanism} 
   853→($\langle F\rangle \neq 0$).
   854→
   855→\paragraph{Layer 1: F-term/VA (chiral condensate, scale $\sim f_\pi$).}
   856→The chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry 
   857→and the sBootstrap SUSY. This is the primary breaking, affecting 
   858→the light sector. The seed triples $(0, s, c)$ and $(0, \pi, D_s)$ 
   859→are the pre-breaking configurations; the massless member is the flat 
   860→direction whose $F$-term is forced nonzero by confinement. After 
   861→breaking:
   862→\begin{itemize}
   863→\item The zero member is lifted and acquires negative charge 
   864→($\zch < 0$), becoming the sign-flipped member of the full triple 
   865→($-s$, $-\pi$).
   866→\item The full triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ emerge, 
   867→incorporating the $b$-quark and $B$-meson sectors.
   868→\item The lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the fermionic shadow: 
   869→the leptons acquire masses through the same condensate, with the 
   870→muon as pseudo-Goldstino.
   871→\end{itemize}
   872→
   873→\paragraph{Layer 2: D-term (electromagnetism, scale $\sim \alpha_{\text{em}} m_\rho$).}
   874→The $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI term generates electromagnetic mass 
   875→splittings within each multiplet. This is a perturbative correction 
   876→on top of the F-term breaking, affecting only charged particles. 
   877→It does not create new Koide triples but shifts existing ones by 
   878→$O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$.
   879→
   880→\paragraph{Layer 3: The heavy sector (scale $\sim v_0^{(cbt)}$).}
   881→The $(c,b,t)$ triple at $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ operates far 
   882→above the chiral scale. It has no seed (no massless member), no 
   883→meson shadow (top does not hadronize), and its breaking may be 
   884→connected to a different sector of the theory---possibly the 
   885→electroweak Higgs mechanism.
   886→
   887→This layered structure involves three distinct scales, each with 
   888→different physical meaning:
   889→\begin{align}
   890→\text{F-term (boson--fermion splitting):} &\quad 
   891→f_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \quad f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2\,; \\
   892→\text{D-term (EM fine structure):} &\quad 
   893→\xi^{1/2} \approx 35\MeV\,, \quad \xi \approx 1{,}200\MeV^2\,; \\
   894→\text{Heavy sector (Koide scale):} &\quad 
   895→v_0^{(cbt)} \approx 172\MeV^{1/2}\,, \quad (v_0^{(cbt)})^2 \approx 29{,}600\MeV\,.
   896→\end{align}
   897→Comparing the squared scales: $f_\pi^2 \gg \xi$ (F-term dominates 
   898→over D-term by a factor $\sim 7$, consistent with the D-term being 
   899→a perturbative $O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$ correction). The heavy-sector 
   900→scale $(v_0^{(cbt)})^2$ carries dimensions of $\MeV$ (not $\MeV^2$) 
   901→because $v_0$ is a charge ($\MeV^{1/2}$), not an energy; direct 
   902→comparison with $f_\pi$ requires specifying which physical quantity 
   903→is being compared.
   904→
   905→\subsection{The O'Raifeartaigh structure in SQCD}
   906→
   907→In Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, the quantum constraint 
   908→\eqref{eq:seiberg} provides a natural setting for the 
   909→O'Raifeartaigh mechanism. The constraint $\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ 
   910→means the moduli space has a specific geometry: not all field directions 
   911→are flat. When quark masses are added via a tree-level superpotential 
   912→$W_{\text{tree}} = m_i M^i{}_i$ (sum over flavors), the $F$-terms become:
   913→\begin{equation}
   914→F_{M^i{}_j} = m_i\,\delta^i_j + \Lambda^6\,(\text{constraint terms})\,.
   915→\end{equation}
   916→
   917→For the seed triple $(0, s, c)$, the ``$0$'' member corresponds to a 
   918→direction in the meson matrix where the tree-level mass vanishes---the 
   919→entry involving the lightest quark combination. In the exact chiral 
   920→limit ($m_u = m_d = 0$), this entry is truly massless, and the 
   921→constraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} forces $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ for 
   922→that entry. This is the dynamical O'Raifeartaigh mechanism: 
   923→confinement (encoded in $\Lambda^6$) plays the role of the parameter 
   924→$f$ in the superpotential, and the impossibility of setting all 
   925→$F$-terms to zero is a consequence of the strong dynamics rather 
   926→than a choice of superpotential.
   927→
   928→The ISS mechanism (Intriligator--Seiberg--Shih \cite{ISS2006}) 
   929→establishes metastable SUSY-breaking vacua for $N_c < N_f < 3N_c/2$, 
   930→where the rank condition in the magnetic dual prevents all $F$-terms 
   931→from vanishing. The sBootstrap case $N_f = N_c = 3$ is strictly 
   932→\emph{below} the ISS window---at $N_f = N_c$ the low-energy 
   933→description is qualitatively different, governed by the 
   934→quantum-modified constraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} on the moduli space 
   935→rather than by a free magnetic dual. However, the physical QCD has 
   936→$N_f = 3$ light flavors and $N_f = 4$--$5$ if charm and bottom are 
   937→included at their respective thresholds, which does fall within the 
   938→ISS window for $N_c = 3$. The sBootstrap's multi-scale structure---with 
   939→the seed triples involving $s$, $c$, and $b$ quarks at different 
   940→scales---may require treating the effective $N_f$ as 
   941→scale-dependent, entering the ISS regime above the strange threshold.
   942→
   943→\subsection{How the three supermultiplets join}
   944→
   945→The seed-to-bloom transition generates three separate Koide triples. 
   946→But the full sBootstrap requires these triples to be \emph{embedded 
   947→in a single meson matrix}. The $3\times 3$ light-flavor matrix is:
   948→
   949→\begin{equation}
   950→M^{(3)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   951→u\bar{u} & u\bar{d} & u\bar{s} \\
   952→d\bar{u} & d\bar{d} & d\bar{s} \\
   953→s\bar{u} & s\bar{d} & s\bar{s}
   954→\end{pmatrix}
   955→\;\longleftrightarrow\;
   956→\begin{pmatrix}
   957→\pi^0/\eta & \pi^+ & K^+ \\
   958→\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 \\
   959→K^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta'
   960→\end{pmatrix}.
   961→\end{equation}
   962→
   963→When charm and bottom are included, the matrix extends to $5\times 5$. 
   964→We display it schematically, with the members of the meson Koide 
   965→triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ marked by boxes:
   966→
   967→\begin{equation}
   968→M^{(5)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   969→\pi^0/\eta & \boxed{\pi^+} & K^+ & D^0 & \boxed{B^+} \\
   970→\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 & D^- & B^0 \\
   971→K^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta' & \boxed{D_s^-} & B_s \\
   972→\bar{D}^0 & D^+ & D_s^+ & \eta_c & B_c \\
   973→B^- & \bar{B}^0 & \bar{B}_s & \bar{B}_c & \eta_b
   974→\end{pmatrix},
   975→\end{equation}
   976→where rows are indexed by $(u, d, s, c, b)$ and columns by 
   977→$(\bar{u}, \bar{d}, \bar{s}, \bar{c}, \bar{b})$.
   978→
   979→The three members of the meson Koide triple occupy different 
   980→off-diagonal blocks of this matrix: $\pi^+ = u\bar{d}$ is in the 
   981→light $(u,d)$ block, $D_s^- = s\bar{c}$ bridges the light-charm 
   982→sector, and $B^+ = u\bar{b}$ bridges the light-bottom sector. They 
   983→do not form a ``slice'' in any simple linear-algebraic sense---they 
   984→are scattered entries connected by the Koide condition on their 
   985→\emph{masses}, not by their positions in the matrix. The meson seed 
   986→$(0, \pi, D_s)$ drops the $B^+$ and replaces it with zero (the 
   987→entry that would involve the top quark, absent from the matrix).
   988→
   989→This scattered structure is the central puzzle: the Koide triples 
   990→are \emph{mass relations} among matrix entries, not submatrices. 
   991→The question is whether the Seiberg constraint 
   992→$\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ (appropriately generalized to 
   993→$5 \times 5$), combined with the SUSY-breaking dynamics, produces 
   994→correlations among these scattered entries that enforce the 
   995→energy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
   996→
   997→\subsection{The energy balance as a fixed point}
   998→\label{subsec:fixedpoint}
   999→One possibility is that the energy-balance condition 
  1000→$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a \emph{dynamical attractor}: 
  1001→any set of three masses evolving under the SUSY-breaking dynamics 
  1002→flows toward the Koide surface $Q = 2/3$, as a fixed point of the 
  1003→RG flow for the soft SUSY-breaking parameters. This is speculative, 
  1004→but the observation that the condition holds for fundamental fermions 
  1005→(leptons, quarks) \emph{and} composite bosons (mesons) across very 
  1006→different scales lends it plausibility. A concrete test would be to 
  1007→show that the one-loop soft-mass RG equations in $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
  1008→SQCD with appropriate boundary conditions flow toward 
  1009→$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$; this calculation has not yet been 
  1010→performed.
  1011→
  1012→\subsection{The exact seed and what breaks it}
  1013→\label{subsec:exactseed}
  1014→
  1015→The seed configuration $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ requires \emph{two} 
  1016→independent conditions:
  1017→\begin{enumerate}
  1018→\item One member at $\zch = 0$ exactly (the flat direction).
  1019→\item The ratio of the massive members equals $2 - \sqrt{3}$:
  1020→\begin{equation}
  1021→\label{eq:seedratio}
  1022→\frac{\sqrt{m_a}}{\sqrt{m_b}} = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.
  1023→\end{equation}
  1024→\end{enumerate}
  1025→These are logically distinct. The chiral limit ($m_u = m_d = 0$, 
  1026→$\qqbar \neq 0$) enforces condition (1) for the meson sector: the 
  1027→pion is an exact Goldstone boson, $m_\pi = 0$, and in the 
  1028→picture of \S\ref{subsec:fterm} the $B$-meson direction sits at 
  1029→the flat direction before acquiring mass. But the chiral limit says 
  1030→\emph{nothing} about condition (2)---it does not constrain 
  1031→$\sqrt{m_{D_s}}/\sqrt{m_B}$ or $\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ 
  1032→to equal $2 - \sqrt{3}$.
  1033→
  1034→The physical deviations from the exact ratio are small but 
  1035→measurable:
  1036→
  1037→\begin{center}
  1038→\begin{tabular}{lccc}
  1039→\toprule
  1040→Seed & Exact ratio & Physical ratio & Deviation \\
  1041→\midrule
  1042→$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1043→$\sqrt{m_\pi}/\sqrt{m_{D_s}} = 0.2663$ & $-0.6\%$ \\
  1044→$(0, s, c)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1045→$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c} = 0.2712$ & $+1.2\%$ \\
  1046→$(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1047→$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 0.2439$ & $-9.0\%$ \\
  1048→\bottomrule
  1049→\end{tabular}
  1050→\end{center}
  1051→
  1052→The meson seed is closest to the exact ratio (0.6\% deviation). 
  1053→The quark seed deviates by 1.2\%. A natural thought is that QCD 
  1054→running might shift the quark ratio: perhaps at some scale $\mu^*$ 
  1055→the ratio $\sqrt{m_s(\mu^*)}/\sqrt{m_c(\mu^*)}$ hits $2 - \sqrt{3}$ 
  1056→exactly. But this is \emph{not possible within QCD alone}. At leading 
  1057→order, all quark masses run with the same anomalous dimension 
  1058→$\gamma_m$:
  1059→\begin{equation}
  1060→m_i(\mu) = m_i(\mu_0)\left(\frac{\alpha_s(\mu)}{\alpha_s(\mu_0)}\right)^{\gamma_m/\beta_0},
  1061→\end{equation}
  1062→so the ratio $m_s(\mu)/m_c(\mu)$---and therefore 
  1063→$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$---is an RG invariant of QCD. Higher-order 
  1064→corrections break this invariance, but only at the $O(\alpha_s^2)$ 
  1065→level, far too small to account for 1.2\%.
  1066→
  1067→The quark seed deviation is therefore \emph{intrinsic}: the 
  1068→$\overline{\text{MS}}$ mass ratio $m_s/m_c$ is a fixed number 
  1069→(at any QCD scale), and it misses the Koide target by 1.2\%. 
  1070→Three possible explanations present themselves:
  1071→
  1072→\paragraph{Electroweak running.} Above the electroweak scale, 
  1073→quark masses are controlled by Yukawa couplings $y_i$ to the 
  1074→Higgs, which \emph{do} run differently for different flavors: 
  1075→the $\beta$-functions for $y_s$ and $y_c$ involve different 
  1076→electroweak quantum numbers ($SU(2)_L$ doublet structure, 
  1077→hypercharge). The ratio $y_s/y_c$ evolves with scale, and 
  1078→there may exist a scale $\mu^*$ above the electroweak 
  1079→threshold where $\sqrt{y_s(\mu^*)}/\sqrt{y_c(\mu^*)} = 2 - \sqrt{3}$. 
  1080→This would identify the ``seed scale'' as an electroweak-sector 
  1081→phenomenon, not a QCD one---consistent with the sBootstrap's 
  1082→identification of the breaking with $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ 
  1083→rather than $SU(3)_c$.
  1084→
  1085→\paragraph{Charge running.} Even if mass ratios are QCD-invariant, 
  1086→the Koide decomposition $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ may have its own 
  1087→flow. The vacuum energy $v_0 = z_0^2$ sets the common scale, 
  1088→and the fluctuations $z_k$ encode the deviations. If the 
  1089→energy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a 
  1090→dynamical attractor (as suggested in \S\ref{subsec:fixedpoint}), 
  1091→the $z_k$ may evolve under a flow in which the \emph{ratio} 
  1092→$z_a/z_b$ approaches $2 - \sqrt{3}$ at the fixed point while 
  1093→the individual masses (and their QCD-invariant ratios) remain 
  1094→unchanged. In this picture, the 1.2\% deviation measures how 
  1095→far the quark sector is from the attractor.
  1096→
  1097→\paragraph{Meson vs.\ quark deviations.} The meson deviation 
  1098→(0.6\%) is smaller than the quark deviation (1.2\%), and for 
  1099→a different reason: meson masses are \emph{not} simple functions 
  1100→of quark mass ratios. The pion mass is given by the GOR 
  1101→relation $m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$; the $D_s$ 
  1102→mass involves heavy-quark effective theory; and both receive 
  1103→chiral perturbation theory corrections. These nonperturbative 
  1104→QCD effects are the dominant source of the meson seed deviation, 
  1105→and are in principle calculable at NLO in ChPT.
  1106→
  1107→\paragraph{A remarkable feature of the VA relation.} The 
  1108→foundational equation $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2$ contains 
  1109→no QCD coupling constant: no $\alpha_s$, no $N_c$, no 
  1110→$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$. It is a pure mass relation among three 
  1111→experimentally measured quantities---two masses and a decay 
  1112→constant---holding to 2.2\%. In the sBootstrap, this is natural: 
  1113→the relation is a SUSY mass splitting, set by the breaking 
  1114→scale $f_\pi$, and SUSY mass relations do not involve the gauge 
  1115→coupling of the confining theory. Standard QCD \emph{explains} 
  1116→$f_\pi$ in terms of $\qqbar$ and $\alpha_s$; the sBootstrap 
  1117→\emph{uses} $f_\pi$ as the fundamental parameter, with QCD 
  1118→providing the dynamical origin but not appearing in the mass 
  1119→formula. The absence of QCD factors in \eqref{eq:pimu} is a 
  1120→hint that the relevant symmetry is not $SU(3)_c$ but the 
  1121→hadronic superalgebra that connects pions to leptons.
  1122→
  1123→The lepton ``seed'' has the worst ratio match ($-9\%$), which 
  1124→is consistent with the observation that the lepton triple is 
  1125→\emph{not} a true seed: $m_e \neq 0$, and the electron was never 
  1126→at the flat direction. The leptons are an ``almost-seed'' whose 
  1127→excellent $Q = 0.66666$ arises not from the seed structure but 
  1128→from the smallness of $m_e$, which makes the two conditions 
  1129→approximately degenerate. For the leptons, condition (1) is 
  1130→approximate rather than exact, and condition (2) is correspondingly 
  1131→relaxed.
  1132→
  1133→\subsection{What the $(c,b,t)$ triple tells us}
  1134→
  1135→The $(c,b,t)$ triple ($Q = 0.6695$, $v_0^2 = 29.6\GeV$) has 
  1136→neither a seed nor a meson shadow. It operates at a scale far 
  1137→above $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, suggesting its origin is different:
  1138→
  1139→\begin{enumerate}
  1140→\item It may be governed by the electroweak Higgs mechanism rather 
  1141→than the chiral condensate. The top Yukawa $y_t \approx 1$ is 
  1142→``natural'' in the sense that $m_t \approx v_{\text{EW}}/\sqrt{2}$; 
  1143→the Koide condition on $(c,b,t)$ would then constrain the Yukawa 
  1144→coupling matrix.
  1145→\item It may represent a Volkov--Akulov realization at the 
  1146→electroweak scale: if there is a second SUSY breaking at 
  1147→$f' \sim v_{\text{EW}}$, the $(c,b,t)$ triple would be its 
  1148→analog of the $(\pi, \mu)$ system, but with $f'$ replacing $f_\pi$.
  1149→\item Or it may be a purely kinematic accident---the quark masses 
  1150→happen to satisfy the Koide condition without a dynamical reason. 
  1151→The 0.42\% precision makes this unlikely but not impossible.
  1152→\end{enumerate}
  1153→
  1154→Interpretation (1) is most natural within the sBootstrap: the 
  1155→$(c,b,t)$ triple involves quarks whose masses are dominated by 
  1156→Yukawa couplings to the Higgs, not by the chiral condensate, and the 
  1157→Koide condition would then be a constraint on the Yukawa matrix 
  1158→rather than on the SQCD dynamics. The chiral sector and the 
  1159→electroweak sector would each independently produce a Koide-type 
  1160→energy balance, with the charm and bottom quarks serving as the 
  1161→bridge between the two.
  1162→
  1163→If interpretation (2) is also invoked, the sBootstrap has two breaking 
  1164→scales:
  1165→\begin{equation}
  1166→f_{\text{chiral}} = f_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \qquad
  1167→f_{\text{EW}} \sim v_{\text{EW}} \approx 246\GeV\,,
  1168→\end{equation}
  1169→and the full spectrum requires both. The light sector (leptons, 
  1170→light mesons) is governed by $f_\pi$; the heavy sector (top, and 
  1171→by extension charm and bottom through the Koide network) is 
  1172→governed by $v_{\text{EW}}$. The charm and bottom quarks, 
  1173→appearing in both the $(0,s,c)/(-s,c,b)$ light-sector triples 
  1174→and the $(c,b,t)$ heavy-sector triple, are the ``bridges'' 
  1175→between the two scales.

  1223→($\pi^\pm = \pi^0$, $K^\pm = K^0$). D-term effects disappear.

  1225→\item \textbf{Set $m_s = m_d$:} $SU(3)_V$ restored, 
  1226→$\pi = K = \eta$. Koide splitting $v_1 \to 0$; lepton partners 
  1227→degenerate.

  1229→$\to 0$. If sBootstrap SUSY restores, all light lepton masses 
  1230→vanish too. This is the seed configuration: $(0, 0, 0)$.
  1231→\end{enumerate}

  1234→reached zero, but the other two retain the $2 - \sqrt{3}$ ratio. 
  1235→Full triples are the final broken state. The lepton triple, 
  1236→trapped between seed and bloom, reveals the hierarchy: 
  1237→chiral breaking $\to$ seed $\to$ bloom $\to$ full spectrum.

  1242→means the pion was ``born negative''---its charge sign is set by 
  1243→the direction of chiral symmetry breaking, and the seed 
  1244→$(0, \pi, D_s)$ is the configuration at the moment of crossing.

  1314→%======================================================================
  1315→\section{Conclusions}
  1316→\label{sec:conclusions}
  1317→%======================================================================
  1318→
  1319→Three principal results:
  1320→
  1321→\paragraph{1.\ The Koide formula is energy equipartition.}
  1322→$Q = 2/3$ is $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$: perturbation energies 
  1323→match the vacuum energy. The signed-charge framework resolves the 
  1324→electron mass puzzle (near-cancellation, not fine-tuning) and reveals 
  1325→that mesons satisfy Koide with $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to $0.1\%$.
  1326→
  1327→\paragraph{2.\ Seed triples set the stage for SUSY breaking.}
  1328→The quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ share the 
  1329→universal ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$ and operate at $v_0^2 \sim 
  1330→230$--$350\MeV$, close to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 = 314\MeV$. 
  1331→The massless member is the necessary condition for F-term 
  1332→(O'Raifeartaigh) SUSY breaking. Upon breaking, seeds bloom into 
  1333→full triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi,D_s,B)$. The lepton triple 
  1334→is the ``almost-seed'' that has barely bloomed.
  1335→
  1336→\paragraph{3.\ Two breaking mechanisms plus a heavy-sector origin.}
  1337→F-term breaking (via the massless seed member) generates the light 
  1338→sector; the Volkov--Akulov relation $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is its 
  1339→low-energy realization, not a separate mechanism. D-term breaking 
  1340→($U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI parameter) provides electromagnetic 
  1341→splittings as a perturbative correction. The heavy triple 
  1342→$(c,b,t)$ stands apart: no seed, no meson shadow, scale far above 
  1343→$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, possibly connected to electroweak breaking.
  1344→
  1345→The diquark sector, required to complete the SUSY multiplet count, 
  1346→fills the symmetric $\mathbf{15} + \overline{\mathbf{15}}$ of 
  1347→$SU(5)_f$---the representation produced naturally by the SUSY 
  1348→generator acting on quarks and by the $SO(32)$ decomposition. The 
  1349→conflict with Pauli statistics for point particles is a 
  1350→compositeness indicator: the diquark is an extended object whose 
  1351→internal spatial structure defeats the point-particle statistics 
  1352→argument.
  1353→
  1354→The organizing principle is that each sector of the spectrum begins 
  1355→with a massless member (a seed), which is the necessary condition 
  1356→for spontaneous SUSY breaking. The breaking lifts the zero, 
  1357→generates the full triple, and the energy-balance condition 
  1358→$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is preserved as a fixed point 
  1359→of the dynamics. The entire charged fermion and pseudoscalar meson
