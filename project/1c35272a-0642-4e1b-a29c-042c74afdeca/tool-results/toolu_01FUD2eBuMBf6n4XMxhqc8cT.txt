    19‚Üí    19‚Üí\title{A Tale of Two Tuples: Seed Triples, Signed Charges, \\
    20‚Üí    20‚Üíand SUSY Breaking in the sBootstrap}

    29‚Üí    29‚Üí\begin{abstract}
    30‚Üí    30‚ÜíWe reformulate the sBootstrap in terms of signed mass charges 
    31‚Üí    31‚Üí$\zch = z_0 + z_i$ with $m = \zch^2$, and show that the Koide ratio 
    32‚Üí    32‚Üí$Q = 2/3$ is the algebraic consequence of two conditions: 
    33‚Üí    33‚Üí$\sum z_i = 0$ and $\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$ (perturbation 
    34‚Üí    34‚Üíenergies average to the vacuum energy). Allowing negative charges, 
    35‚Üí    35‚Üíwe find that pseudoscalar mesons satisfy a Koide triple 
    36‚Üí    36‚Üí$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to 0.1\%, and identify \emph{seed triples} with 
    37‚Üí    37‚Üíone massless member---$(0, s, c)$ for quarks, $(0, \pi, D_s)$ for 
    38‚Üí    38‚Üímesons---as the pre-breaking configurations from which the full 
    39‚Üí    39‚Üíspectrum emerges. These seed triples share the same analytical 
    40‚Üí    40‚Üístructure (charge ratio $2-\sqrt{3}$) and, strikingly, the meson 
    41‚Üí    41‚Üíseed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ has vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 351\MeV$, 
    42‚Üí    42‚Üíclose to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 \approx 314\MeV$. A third 
    43‚Üí    43‚Üítuple $(c,b,t)$ stands apart, having no meson counterpart because 
    44‚Üí    44‚Üítop diquarks do not hadronize. We discuss the diquark sector---which 
    45‚Üí    45‚Üímust fill the antisymmetric representation of $SU(5)$ flavor rather 
    46‚Üí    46‚Üíthan the symmetric, requiring additional dynamical input---and outline 
    47‚Üí    47‚Üíhow the three types of SUSY breaking (F-term, D-term, nonlinear 
    48‚Üí    48‚ÜíVolkov--Akulov) may map onto the three-tuple structure.
    49‚Üí    49‚Üí\end{abstract}

    54‚Üí    54‚Üí\section{Introduction}
    55‚Üí    55‚Üí\label{sec:intro}
    56‚Üí    56‚Üí%======================================================================
    57‚Üí    57‚Üí
    58‚Üí    58‚ÜíThe sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005,Rivero2012} proposes that the Standard 
    59‚Üí    59‚ÜíModel's pseudoscalar mesons and leptons fill common $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
    60‚Üí    60‚Üísupermultiplets. Its foundational relation is
    61‚Üí    61‚Üí\begin{equation}
    62‚Üí    62‚Üí\label{eq:pimu}
    63‚Üí    63‚Üím_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2\,,
    64‚Üí    64‚Üí\end{equation}
    65‚Üí    65‚Üíidentifying the pion decay constant $f_\pi \approx 92.2\MeV$ as the 
    66‚Üí    66‚ÜíSUSY-breaking order parameter. In this paper we develop two themes 
    67‚Üí    67‚Üíthat change the structure of the program.
    68‚Üí    68‚Üí
    69‚Üí    69‚ÜíFirst, \textbf{mass is the square of a signed charge}: $m = \zch^2$ 
    70‚Üí    70‚Üíwhere $\zch = z_0 + z_i$ can be negative. The Koide ratio $Q = 2/3$ 
    71‚Üí    71‚Üíis then not numerology but the energy-balance condition 
    72‚Üí    72‚Üí$\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$. With signed charges, pseudoscalar 
    73‚Üí    73‚Üímesons satisfy Koide, and the spectrum organizes into \emph{seed 
    74‚Üí    74‚Üítriples} with a massless member that grow into full triples upon 
    75‚Üí    75‚Üísymmetry breaking.
    76‚Üí    76‚Üí
    77‚Üí    77‚ÜíSecond, \textbf{the full sBootstrap has three Koide tuples, not one}, 
    78‚Üí    78‚Üíand the breaking structure must generate all three: a light sector 
    79‚Üí    79‚Üí$(0,s,c) \to (-s,c,b)$ seen in both quarks and mesons, a lepton 
    80‚Üí    80‚Üísector $(e\!\approx\!0, \mu, \tau)$, and a heavy sector $(c,b,t)$ 
    81‚Üí    81‚Üíthat decouples from meson physics because the top quark does not 
    82‚Üí    82‚Üíhadronize. The diquark partners of quarks, needed to complete the 
    83‚Üí    83‚ÜíSUSY multiplets, must fill the antisymmetric $\overline{10}$ of 
    84‚Üí    84‚Üí$SU(5)$ flavor rather than the symmetric 15, requiring extra 
    85‚Üí    85‚Üídynamical input from the breaking sector.
    86‚Üí    86‚Üí
    87‚Üí    87‚Üí%======================================================================
    88‚Üí    88‚Üí\section{The Koide Formula as Energy Balance}
    89‚Üí    89‚Üí\label{sec:koide_algebra}
    90‚Üí    90‚Üí%======================================================================
    91‚Üí    91‚Üí
    92‚Üí    92‚Üí\subsection{Charges, not masses}
    93‚Üí    93‚Üí
    94‚Üí    94‚ÜíThe fundamental variable is the signed mass charge $\zch = z_0 + z_i 
    95‚Üí    95‚Üí\in \mathbb{R}$, with the physical mass $m = \zch^2 \geq 0$.
    96‚Üí    96‚Üí
    97‚Üí    97‚Üí\subsection{Derivation of $Q = 2/3$}
    98‚Üí    98‚Üí
    99‚Üí    99‚ÜíFor three charges $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ with $z_0$ the mean 
   100‚Üí   100‚Üí($\sum z_k = 0$):
   101‚Üí   101‚Üí\begin{equation}
   102‚Üí   102‚ÜíQ = \frac{\sum \zch_k^2}{(\sum \zch_k)^2} 
   103‚Üí   103‚Üí= \frac{3z_0^2 + \sum z_k^2}{9z_0^2}
   104‚Üí   104‚Üí= \frac{1}{3} + \frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{3z_0^2}\,.
   105‚Üí   105‚Üí\end{equation}
   106‚Üí   106‚ÜíTherefore:
   107‚Üí   107‚Üí\begin{equation}
   108‚Üí   108‚Üí\boxed{Q = \frac{2}{3} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad 
   109‚Üí   109‚Üí\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2\,.}
   110‚Üí   110‚Üí\end{equation}
   111‚Üí   111‚Üí
   112‚Üí   112‚ÜíThe two conditions are:
   113‚Üí   113‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   114‚Üí   114‚Üí\item \textbf{Zero-sum perturbations:} $z_1 + z_2 + z_3 = 0$ 
   115‚Üí   115‚Üí(definition of $z_0$ as the mean charge).
   116‚Üí   116‚Üí\item \textbf{Energy balance:} The mean energy of the perturbations 
   117‚Üí   117‚Üíequals the energy of the unperturbed charge: 
   118‚Üí   118‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
   119‚Üí   119‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   120‚Üí   120‚Üí
   121‚Üí   121‚ÜíCondition~2 is the physics. It says the splitting scale matches the 
   122‚Üí   122‚Üíbase scale---the coefficient of variation equals unity: 
   123‚Üí   123‚Üí$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$. This is neither perturbative 
   124‚Üí   124‚Üí($\sigma \ll \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1/3$, all equal) nor 
   125‚Üí   125‚Üídominant ($\sigma \gg \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1$, one mass 
   126‚Üí   126‚Üídominates). The Koide value $2/3$ is the equipartition point.
   127‚Üí   127‚Üí
   128‚Üí   128‚Üí\subsection{Why one mass can vanish}
   129‚Üí   129‚Üí
   130‚Üí   130‚ÜíIf $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$, a perturbation $z_k$ can reach 
   131‚Üí   131‚Üí$|z_k| \sim z_0$, making $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k \approx 0$. A vanishing 
   132‚Üí   132‚Üímass is the \emph{natural extreme} of energy balance, not fine-tuning.
   133‚Üí   133‚Üí
   134‚Üí   134‚Üí%======================================================================
   135‚Üí   135‚Üí\section{Seed Triples: The $(0, a, b)$ Structure}
   136‚Üí   136‚Üí\label{sec:seeds}
   137‚Üí   137‚Üí%======================================================================
   138‚Üí   138‚Üí
   139‚Üí   139‚Üí\subsection{Analytical condition}
   140‚Üí   140‚Üí
   141‚Üí   141‚ÜíFor a triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ with one massless member, the 
   142‚Üí   142‚ÜíKoide ratio becomes:
   143‚Üí   143‚Üí\begin{equation}
   144‚Üí   144‚ÜíQ = \frac{\zch_a^2 + \zch_b^2}{(\zch_a + \zch_b)^2}\,.
   145‚Üí   145‚Üí\end{equation}
   146‚Üí   146‚ÜíSetting $Q = 2/3$ and writing $r = \zch_a/\zch_b$ (with $r < 1$):
   147‚Üí   147‚Üí\begin{equation}
   148‚Üí   148‚Üí3(r^2 + 1) = 2(r + 1)^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad r^2 - 4r + 1 = 0\,,
   149‚Üí   149‚Üí\end{equation}
   150‚Üí   150‚Üígiving:
   151‚Üí   151‚Üí\begin{equation}
   152‚Üí   152‚Üí\boxed{r = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.}
   153‚Üí   153‚Üí\end{equation}
   154‚Üí   154‚Üí
   155‚Üí   155‚ÜíEquivalently, in mass space: 
   156‚Üí   156‚Üí$m_{\text{light}}/m_{\text{heavy}} = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 = 7 - 4\sqrt{3} 
   157‚Üí   157‚Üí\approx 0.07180$.
   158‚Üí   158‚Üí
   159‚Üí   159‚ÜíThis is a \emph{universal} prediction: any Koide triple with one 
   160‚Üí   160‚Üímassless member has the same charge ratio between the two massive 
   161‚Üí   161‚Üímembers, regardless of their absolute scale.
   162‚Üí   162‚Üí
   163‚Üí   163‚Üí\subsection{The quark seed: $(0, s, c)$}
   164‚Üí   164‚Üí
   165‚Üí   165‚ÜíThe strange and charm quark charges give:
   166‚Üí   166‚Üí\begin{equation}
   167‚Üí   167‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_s}}{\sqrt{m_c}} = \frac{9.67}{35.64} = 0.2712\,, 
   168‚Üí   168‚Üí\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 1.2\%\,.
   169‚Üí   169‚Üí\end{equation}
   170‚Üí   170‚Üí
   171‚Üí   171‚ÜíThis triple has $Q = 0.6644$, deviating from $2/3$ by only $0.35\%$. 
   172‚Üí   172‚ÜíIts vacuum energy is $v_0^2 = (\zch_s + \zch_c)^2/9 \approx 228\MeV$.
   173‚Üí   173‚Üí
   174‚Üí   174‚ÜíA historical note: Harari and a collaborator observed that $(0, d, s)$ 
   175‚Üí   175‚Üíwas approximately a Koide triple, at a time when the up quark mass was 
   176‚Üí   176‚Üístill thought to be possibly zero \cite{Harari}. With current masses, 
   177‚Üí   177‚Üí$\sqrt{m_d}/\sqrt{m_s} = 0.224$, deviating from $2 - \sqrt{3}$ by 
   178‚Üí   178‚Üí$16\%$---too large for a good Koide triple. But if $m_s$ were 
   179‚Üí   179‚Üí$\sim 150\MeV$ and $m_d \sim 7\MeV$ (values common in that era), the 
   180‚Üí   180‚Üíratio improves considerably. The seed idea was already present, ahead 
   181‚Üí   181‚Üíof its time.
   182‚Üí   182‚Üí
   183‚Üí   183‚Üí\subsection{The meson seed: $(0, \pi, D_s)$}
   184‚Üí   184‚Üí
   185‚Üí   185‚ÜíThe pion and $D_s$ meson charges give:
   186‚Üí   186‚Üí\begin{equation}
   187‚Üí   187‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_\pi}}{\sqrt{m_{D_s}}} = \frac{11.81}{44.37} = 0.2663\,, 
   188‚Üí   188‚Üí\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 0.6\%\,.
   189‚Üí   189‚Üí\end{equation}
   190‚Üí   190‚Üí
   191‚Üí   191‚ÜíThis gives $Q = 0.6679$, deviating by $0.18\%$. It is the 
   192‚Üí   192‚Üí\textbf{meson counterpart} of the quark seed $(0, s, c)$---both 
   193‚Üí   193‚Üíinvolve the strange-charm sector. Indeed, $D_s = c\bar{s}$ is the 
   194‚Üí   194‚Üímeson that directly contains the $s$ and $c$ quarks.
   195‚Üí   195‚Üí
   196‚Üí   196‚Üí\subsection{The lepton triple as an ``almost-seed''}
   197‚Üí   197‚Üí
   198‚Üí   198‚ÜíThe charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ has $\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$, 
   199‚Üí   199‚Üíwhich is small but nonzero. If $m_e$ were zero, we would need 
   200‚Üí   200‚Üí$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.2679$; the actual value 
   201‚Üí   201‚Üíis $0.2439$, deviating by $9\%$. So the leptons are \emph{not} an 
   202‚Üí   202‚Üíexact seed---the electron, while very light, is not massless. But the 
   203‚Üí   203‚Üítriple is clearly the same family: the near-critical Koide phase puts 
   204‚Üí   204‚Üíone member close to zero.
   205‚Üí   205‚Üí
   206‚Üí   206‚Üí\subsection{Vacuum energy coincidence}
   207‚Üí   207‚Üí
   208‚Üí   208‚ÜíThe $v_0^2$ values of the seed triples cluster remarkably:
   209‚Üí   209‚Üí
   210‚Üí   210‚Üí\begin{center}
   211‚Üí   211‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcc}
   212‚Üí   212‚Üí\toprule
   213‚Üí   213‚ÜíTriple & $v_0^2$ (MeV) & $v_0$ ($\MeV^{1/2}$) \\
   214‚Üí   214‚Üí\midrule
   215‚Üí   215‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 & 17.72 \\
   216‚Üí   216‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 351 & 18.73 \\
   217‚Üí   217‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ & 228 & 15.10 \\
   218‚Üí   218‚Üí\bottomrule
   219‚Üí   219‚Üí\end{tabular}
   220‚Üí   220‚Üí\end{center}
   221‚Üí   221‚Üí
   222‚Üí   222‚ÜíThe lepton and meson seed triples share a vacuum energy around 
   223‚Üí   223‚Üí310--350~MeV, squarely in the QCD scale range. This is the 
   224‚Üí   224‚ÜísBootstrap connection made quantitative: \textbf{the lepton triple 
   225‚Üí   225‚Üíand the meson seed triple operate at the same scale.}
   226‚Üí   226‚Üí
   227‚Üí   227‚Üí%======================================================================
   228‚Üí   228‚Üí\section{A Tale of Two Tuples}
   229‚Üí   229‚Üí\label{sec:two_tuples}
   230‚Üí   230‚Üí%======================================================================
   231‚Üí   231‚Üí
   232‚Üí   232‚Üí\subsection{From seed to full triple}
   233‚Üí   233‚Üí
   234‚Üí   234‚ÜíThe seed triples grow into full triples upon symmetry breaking:
   235‚Üí   235‚Üí
   236‚Üí   236‚Üí\begin{center}
   237‚Üí   237‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   238‚Üí   238‚Üí\toprule
   239‚Üí   239‚ÜíSector & Seed triple & Full triple & $v_0^2$ (full) \\
   240‚Üí   240‚Üí\midrule
   241‚Üí   241‚ÜíQuarks & $(0, s, c)$ & $(-s, c, b)$ & 913 MeV \\
   242‚Üí   242‚ÜíMesons & $(0, \pi, D_s)$ & $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 1230 MeV \\
   243‚Üí   243‚ÜíLeptons & $(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & $(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 MeV \\
   244‚Üí   244‚Üí\bottomrule
   245‚Üí   245‚Üí\end{tabular}
   246‚Üí   246‚Üí\end{center}
   247‚Üí   247‚Üí
   248‚Üí   248‚ÜíIn each case:
   249‚Üí   249‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   250‚Üí   250‚Üí\item The seed has a massless (or near-massless) member, two massive 
   251‚Üí   251‚Üímembers in the ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$, and $Q \approx 2/3$.
   252‚Üí   252‚Üí\item The full triple has \textbf{no} massless member: the zero has 
   253‚Üí   253‚Üíbeen lifted (to $m_e$, or the sign-flipped strange/pion mass), and a 
   254‚Üí   254‚Üínew heavy member ($b$ quark, $B$ meson) has appeared. The full triple 
   255‚Üí   255‚Üíalso satisfies $Q \approx 2/3$ but at a larger scale $v_0$.
   256‚Üí   256‚Üí\end{itemize}
   257‚Üí   257‚Üí
   258‚Üí   258‚ÜíThe ``tale of two tuples'' is this: \textbf{each sector presents 
   259‚Üí   259‚Üífirst as a seed with a massless member, then ``blooms'' into a 
   260‚Üí   260‚Üífull triple when SUSY breaks.} The massless member is the necessary 
   261‚Üí   261‚Üíinitial condition; the breaking lifts it and generates the full spectrum.
   262‚Üí   262‚Üí
   263‚Üí   263‚Üí\subsection{Precision summary}
   264‚Üí   264‚Üí
   265‚Üí   265‚Üí\begin{center}
   266‚Üí   266‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   267‚Üí   267‚Üí\toprule
   268‚Üí   268‚ÜíTriple & $Q$ & Dev.\ from $2/3$ & $v_0^2$ (MeV) \\
   269‚Üí   269‚Üí\midrule
   270‚Üí   270‚Üí\multicolumn{4}{c}{\textit{Seed triples}} \\
   271‚Üí   271‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 0.6679 & 0.18\% & 351 \\
   272‚Üí   272‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ & 0.6644 & 0.35\% & 228 \\
   273‚Üí   273‚Üí$(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.6667 & 0.001\% & 314 \\
   274‚Üí   274‚Üí\midrule
   275‚Üí   275‚Üí\multicolumn{4}{c}{\textit{Full triples}} \\
   276‚Üí   276‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.66666 & 0.001\% & 314 \\
   277‚Üí   277‚Üí$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 0.6674 & 0.10\% & 1230 \\
   278‚Üí   278‚Üí$(-s, c, b)$ & 0.6750 & 1.24\% & 913 \\
   279‚Üí   279‚Üí$(c, b, t)$ & 0.6695 & 0.42\% & 22\,770 \\
   280‚Üí   280‚Üí\bottomrule
   281‚Üí   281‚Üí\end{tabular}
   282‚Üí   282‚Üí\end{center}
   283‚Üí   283‚Üí
   284‚Üí   284‚ÜíThe lepton triple is unique: seed and full triple are nearly 
   285‚Üí   285‚Üíidentical, because the electron mass is small enough to serve as 
   286‚Üí   286‚Üíthe ``zero'' while being nonzero enough to make $Q$ extremely 
   287‚Üí   287‚Üíprecise.
   288‚Üí   288‚Üí
   289‚Üí   289‚Üí\subsection{Orthogonality of seed and full triples}
   290‚Üí   290‚Üí
   291‚Üí   291‚ÜíEach Koide triple defines a perturbation vector 
   292‚Üí   292‚Üí$\vec{z} = (z_1, z_2, z_3)$ constrained to the plane $\sum z_k = 0$ 
   293‚Üí   293‚Üíwith length $|\vec{z}|^2 = 3z_0^2$. The angle between seed and 
   294‚Üí   294‚Üífull triples measures how much the breaking rotates the spectrum 
   295‚Üí   295‚Üíin charge space:
   296‚Üí   296‚Üí
   297‚Üí   297‚Üí\begin{center}
   298‚Üí   298‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lc}
   299‚Üí   299‚Üí\toprule
   300‚Üí   300‚ÜíPair of triples & Angle \\
   301‚Üí   301‚Üí\midrule
   302‚Üí   302‚Üí$(0,s,c)$ vs.\ $(-s,c,b)$ & $22^\circ$ \\
   303‚Üí   303‚Üí$(0,\pi,D_s)$ vs.\ $(-\pi,D_s,B)$ & $26^\circ$ \\
   304‚Üí   304‚Üí$(e,\mu,\tau)$ vs.\ $(0,\mu,\tau)$ & $0.8^\circ$ \\
   305‚Üí   305‚Üí$(0,s,c)$ vs.\ $(0,\pi,D_s)$ & $0.3^\circ$ \\
   306‚Üí   306‚Üí\bottomrule
   307‚Üí   307‚Üí\end{tabular}
   308‚Üí   308‚Üí\end{center}
   309‚Üí   309‚Üí
   310‚Üí   310‚ÜíTwo results are notable. First, the quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson 
   311‚Üí   311‚Üíseed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ are \textbf{nearly parallel} ($0.3^\circ$): the 
   312‚Üí   312‚Üímeson seed is the composite image of the quark seed, with the GOR 
   313‚Üí   313‚Üímap preserving direction while changing scale. Second, the lepton 
   314‚Üí   314‚Üítriple is essentially its own seed ($0.8^\circ$)---the bloom has 
   315‚Üí   315‚Üíbarely begun.
   316‚Üí   316‚Üí
   317‚Üí   317‚Üí%======================================================================
   318‚Üí   318‚Üí\section{The Third Tuple: $(c, b, t)$}
   319‚Üí   319‚Üí\label{sec:cbt}
   320‚Üí   320‚Üí%======================================================================
   321‚Üí   321‚Üí
   322‚Üí   322‚Üí\subsection{A tuple without mesons}
   323‚Üí   323‚Üí
   324‚Üí   324‚ÜíThe triple $(c, b, t)$ with all positive signs gives $Q = 0.6695$, 
   325‚Üí   325‚Üídeviating by $0.42\%$. Its vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 22.8\GeV$ 
   326‚Üí   326‚Üíis far above the QCD scale.
   327‚Üí   327‚Üí
   328‚Üí   328‚ÜíThis triple has \textbf{no meson counterpart}: the top quark decays 
   329‚Üí   329‚Üíweakly ($t \to Wb$, lifetime $\sim 5 \times 10^{-25}$~s) before the 
   330‚Üí   330‚Üístrong interaction can bind it ($\tau_{\text{QCD}} \sim 1/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} 
   331‚Üí   331‚Üí\sim 10^{-24}$~s). No $T$-meson ($t\bar{q}$) exists. The meson matrix 
   332‚Üí   332‚Üí$M^i{}_j$ extends only to $b$-flavored states; the top sector lives 
   333‚Üí   333‚Üípurely at the quark level.
   334‚Üí   334‚Üí
   335‚Üí   335‚Üí\subsection{Diquarks and the top}
   336‚Üí   336‚Üí
   337‚Üí   337‚ÜíIn the sBootstrap, diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ are the scalar partners of 
   338‚Üí   338‚Üíantiquarks $\bar{q}_k$, with the baryon acting as the SUSY generator 
   339‚Üí   339‚Üí\cite{CattoGursey, Miyazawa}. For five active flavors 
   340‚Üí   340‚Üí$(u, d, s, c, b)$, the diquarks form a representation of $SU(5)_f$.
   341‚Üí   341‚Üí
   342‚Üí   342‚ÜíThe top diquarks $[tq]$ are kinematic objects only---they do not 
   343‚Üí   343‚Üíhadronize. Yet their masses should follow the same pattern as other 
   344‚Üí   344‚Üídiquarks: \textbf{there is no dynamical reason for top diquark masses 
   345‚Üí   345‚Üíto differ from the others}. The $(c,b,t)$ Koide triple governs the 
   346‚Üí   346‚Üíquark masses that enter the diquark sector, and its energy balance 
   347‚Üí   347‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ constrains the possible diquark 
   348‚Üí   348‚Üíspectrum even though the top diquarks are never observed as bound 
   349‚Üí   349‚Üístates.
   350‚Üí   350‚Üí
   351‚Üí   351‚Üí\subsection{The $(c,b,t)$ triple has no seed}
   352‚Üí   352‚Üí
   353‚Üí   353‚ÜíUnlike the $(0,s,c)$ and $(0,\pi,D_s)$ seeds, there is no obvious 
   354‚Üí   354‚Üí$(0, x, y)$ seed for the $(c,b,t)$ triple. No pair of quarks in this 
   355‚Üí   355‚Üísector satisfies $\sqrt{m_{\text{light}}}/\sqrt{m_{\text{heavy}}} 
   356‚Üí   356‚Üí\approx 2 - \sqrt{3}$: one has $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_t} = 0.086$ 
   357‚Üí   357‚Üí(too small) and $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_b} = 0.551$ (too large).
   358‚Üí   358‚Üí
   359‚Üí   359‚ÜíThis means $(c,b,t)$ is \emph{not} a ``bloomed seed'' in the same 
   360‚Üí   360‚Üísense as $(-s,c,b)$. It may represent a fundamentally different 
   361‚Üí   361‚Üístructure---a triple that was never seeded by a massless member but 
   362‚Üí   362‚Üíinstead emerged fully formed at a higher breaking scale, perhaps 
   363‚Üí   363‚Üíconnected to electroweak symmetry breaking rather than to chiral 
   364‚Üí   364‚Üísymmetry breaking.
   365‚Üí   365‚Üí
   366‚Üí   366‚Üí%======================================================================
   367‚Üí   367‚Üí\section{The Diquark Sector and SU(5) Flavor}
   368‚Üí   368‚Üí\label{sec:diquarks}
   369‚Üí   369‚Üí%======================================================================
   370‚Üí   370‚Üí
   371‚Üí   371‚Üí\subsection{Counting partners}
   372‚Üí   372‚Üí
   373‚Üí   373‚ÜíIn the full sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005}, the scalar partners of 
   374‚Üí   374‚Üíthe Standard Model fermions include both mesons ($q\bar{q}$) and 
   375‚Üí   375‚Üídiquarks ($qq$). For five active flavors, the meson matrix gives 
   376‚Üí   376‚Üí$5 \times 5 = 25$ scalars. The diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ form a 
   377‚Üí   377‚Üírepresentation of $SU(5)_f$:
   378‚Üí   378‚Üí\begin{equation}
   379‚Üí   379‚Üí\mathbf{5} \otimes \mathbf{5} = \overline{\mathbf{10}}_A 
   380‚Üí   380‚Üí\oplus \mathbf{15}_S\,.
   381‚Üí   381‚Üí\end{equation}
   382‚Üí   382‚Üí
   383‚Üí   383‚ÜíThe symmetric $\mathbf{15}_S$ gives 15 diquarks including 
   384‚Üí   384‚Üísame-flavor states $[uu]$, $[dd]$, \ldots, while the antisymmetric 
   385‚Üí   385‚Üí$\overline{\mathbf{10}}_A$ gives 10 diquarks with $i \neq j$ only.
   386‚Üí   386‚Üí
   387‚Üí   387‚Üí\subsection{Why antisymmetric?}
   388‚Üí   388‚Üí
   389‚Üí   389‚ÜíFor the sBootstrap to produce the exact count of scalar partners 
   390‚Üí   390‚Üímatching the Standard Model fermion content, the diquarks must 
   391‚Üí   391‚Üítransform in the \textbf{antisymmetric} $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ 
   392‚Üí   392‚Üí\cite{Rivero2005}. The argument:
   393‚Üí   393‚Üí
   394‚Üí   394‚ÜíThe 25 mesons account for the slepton and ``internal'' squark 
   395‚Üí   395‚Üísectors. The remaining squarks must come from diquarks. The 
   396‚Üí   396‚Üí$\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ provides exactly 10 diquarks: $[ud]$, 
   397‚Üí   397‚Üí$[us]$, $[uc]$, $[ub]$, $[ds]$, $[dc]$, $[db]$, $[sc]$, $[sb]$, 
   398‚Üí   398‚Üí$[cb]$. With their antiparticles, this gives 20 scalar states. The 
   399‚Üí   399‚Üítotal $25 + 10 = 35$ (or $25 + 20 = 45$ counting antiparticles 
   400‚Üí   400‚Üíseparately) matches the scalar content of the Supersymmetric 
   401‚Üí   401‚ÜíStandard Model.
   402‚Üí   402‚Üí
   403‚Üí   403‚ÜíThe symmetric $\mathbf{15}$ would give 15 diquarks, overcounting 
   404‚Üí   404‚Üíby 5 (the same-flavor states). Moreover, the exotic $+4/3$ charged 
   405‚Üí   405‚Üídiquarks $[uu]$, $[uc]$, $[cc]$ have no lepton partner among known 
   406‚Üí   406‚Üíparticles---a fatal obstacle.
   407‚Üí   407‚Üí
   408‚Üí   408‚Üí\subsection{The Pauli problem}
   409‚Üí   409‚Üí
   410‚Üí   410‚ÜíThe antisymmetric flavor requirement conflicts with a naive 
   411‚Üí   411‚Üíapplication of Fermi statistics. For spin-0, $L=0$ diquarks in 
   412‚Üí   412‚Üíthe color $\bar{3}$ (antisymmetric) channel:
   413‚Üí   413‚Üí
   414‚Üí   414‚Üí\begin{center}
   415‚Üí   415‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   416‚Üí   416‚Üí\toprule
   417‚Üí   417‚ÜíProperty & Symmetry & Wavefunction \\
   418‚Üí   418‚Üí\midrule
   419‚Üí   419‚ÜíColor $\bar{3}$ & Antisymmetric & $\epsilon_{abc} q^b q^c$ \\
   420‚Üí   420‚ÜíSpin 0 & Antisymmetric & $(\uparrow\downarrow - \downarrow\uparrow)/\sqrt{2}$ \\
   421‚Üí   421‚ÜíSpatial $L=0$ & Symmetric & $\psi(r)$ \\
   422‚Üí   422‚Üí\midrule
   423‚Üí   423‚ÜíOverall & Must be A & $A \times A \times S = S$ \quad$(\neq A!)$ \\
   424‚Üí   424‚Üí\bottomrule
   425‚Üí   425‚Üí\end{tabular}
   426‚Üí   426‚Üí\end{center}
   427‚Üí   427‚Üí
   428‚Üí   428‚ÜíThe product is symmetric---\textbf{flavor must also be symmetric} 
   429‚Üí   429‚Üíto make the overall wavefunction antisymmetric. But the sBootstrap 
   430‚Üí   430‚Üírequires antisymmetric flavor.
   431‚Üí   431‚Üí
   432‚Üí   432‚ÜíThis is one of the deepest puzzles of the program. Possible 
   433‚Üí   433‚Üíresolutions:
   434‚Üí   434‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   435‚Üí   435‚Üí\item \textbf{$L = 1$ diquarks:} Adding orbital angular momentum 
   436‚Üí   436‚Üíintroduces spatial antisymmetry. Then color(A) $\times$ spin(A) 
   437‚Üí   437‚Üí$\times$ space(A) $\times$ flavor = overall A requires 
   438‚Üí   438‚Üíflavor(S)---still wrong. But $L=1$ with spin-1 gives spin(S), 
   439‚Üí   439‚Üíand then color(A) $\times$ spin(S) $\times$ space(A) $\times$ 
   440‚Üí   440‚Üíflavor(?) = A requires flavor(A). This works but requires both 
   441‚Üí   441‚Üí$L=1$ and spin-1 diquarks, departing from the simplest scalar 
   442‚Üí   442‚Üídiquark picture.
   443‚Üí   443‚Üí\item \textbf{Dynamical projection:} A binding mechanism that 
   444‚Üí   444‚Üíeffectively projects onto the flavor-antisymmetric channel, perhaps 
   445‚Üí   445‚Üíthrough instanton effects or a condensate in the breaking sector.
   446‚Üí   446‚Üí\item \textbf{The SO(32) route:} In the string-theoretic embedding 
   447‚Üí   447‚Üí\cite{Rivero2005}, the $SO(32)$ spinor representation automatically 
   448‚Üí   448‚Üíproduces the correct antisymmetric structure. The Pauli constraint 
   449‚Üí   449‚Üíis an artifact of treating diquarks as simple two-quark states; in 
   450‚Üí   450‚Üíthe string picture, they are fundamental objects of the open-string 
   451‚Üí   451‚Üísector and the symmetry is determined by the gauge group, not by 
   452‚Üí   452‚Üíquark statistics.
   453‚Üí   453‚Üí\end{itemize}
   454‚Üí   454‚Üí
   455‚Üí   455‚Üí\subsection{The SO(32) connection}
   456‚Üí   456‚Üí
   457‚Üí   457‚ÜíThe sBootstrap counting matches a decomposition of the $SO(32)$ gauge 
   458‚Üí   458‚Üígroup familiar from Type~I/heterotic string theory. Under 
   459‚Üí   459‚Üí$SO(32) \supset SU(5) \times \ldots$, the adjoint or spinor 
   460‚Üí   460‚Üírepresentations produce exactly the right combination of mesons 
   461‚Üí   461‚Üí(in $\mathbf{5} \otimes \bar{\mathbf{5}}$) and antisymmetric 
   462‚Üí   462‚Üídiquarks (in $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$). The 496-dimensional adjoint 
   463‚Üí   463‚Üíof $SO(32)$ decomposes to include the 24 of $SU(5)$ (gauge bosons), 
   464‚Üí   464‚Üíthe $\mathbf{5} \otimes \bar{\mathbf{5}} = \mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ 
   465‚Üí   465‚Üí(mesons/sleptons), and the $\overline{\mathbf{10}} + \mathbf{10}$ 
   466‚Üí   466‚Üí(diquarks/squarks).
   467‚Üí   467‚Üí
   468‚Üí   468‚ÜíThis is suggestive: the same structure that gives anomaly-free string 
   469‚Üí   469‚Üícompactifications also gives the correct sBootstrap particle count 
   470‚Üí   470‚Üíwith the required antisymmetric diquarks. Whether this is a 
   471‚Üí   471‚Üícoincidence or a hint of UV completion is an open question.
   472‚Üí   472‚Üí
   473‚Üí   473‚Üí%======================================================================
   474‚Üí   474‚Üí\section{The Meson Koide Triple: $(-\pi, D_s, B)$}
   475‚Üí   475‚Üí\label{sec:mesonkoide}
   476‚Üí   476‚Üí%======================================================================
   477‚Üí   477‚Üí
   478‚Üí   478‚ÜíWith the pion charge negative, the triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ satisfies:
   479‚Üí   479‚Üí\begin{equation}
   480‚Üí   480‚ÜíQ(-\pi, D_s, B) = 
   481‚Üí   481‚Üí\frac{m_\pi + m_{D_s} + m_B}{(-\sqrt{m_\pi} + \sqrt{m_{D_s}} + \sqrt{m_B})^2}
   482‚Üí   482‚Üí= 0.6674\,,
   483‚Üí   483‚Üí\end{equation}
   484‚Üí   484‚Üídeviating from $2/3$ by $0.10\%$---the second most precise Koide 
   485‚Üí   485‚Üítriple known. The energy balance:
   486‚Üí   486‚Üí\begin{equation}
   487‚Üí   487‚Üí\frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{z_0^2} = 1.002\,.
   488‚Üí   488‚Üí\end{equation}
   489‚Üí   489‚Üí
   490‚Üí   490‚ÜíFor comparison, the ``standard'' all-positive quark triples $(d,s,b)$ 
   491‚Üí   491‚Üíand $(u,c,t)$ deviate from $Q = 2/3$ by 9.7\% and 27\% respectively 
   492‚Üí   492‚Üíat $\overline{\text{MS}}$ masses. \textbf{The meson triple is more 
   493‚Üí   493‚Üíprecise than any quark triple} except $(c,b,t)$.
   494‚Üí   494‚Üí
   495‚Üí   495‚ÜíThe pion's negative charge has physical meaning: as a pseudo-Goldstone 
   496‚Üí   496‚Üíboson, the pion would be massless ($\zch = 0$) if chiral symmetry were 
   497‚Üí   497‚Üíexact. It sits just below zero ($\zch_\pi = -11.81$), approaching zero 
   498‚Üí   498‚Üífrom the negative side as $m_q \to 0$. The sign of $\zch$ encodes 
   499‚Üí   499‚ÜíGoldstone nature: pseudo-Goldstone bosons sit on the negative side 
   500‚Üí   500‚Üíof the critical point, having crossed through zero from positive 
   501‚Üí   501‚Üíterritory during chiral symmetry breaking.
   502‚Üí   502‚Üí
   503‚Üí   503‚Üí%======================================================================
   504‚Üí   504‚Üí\section{SUSY Breaking: From Seeds to Spectrum}
   505‚Üí   505‚Üí\label{sec:breaking}
   506‚Üí   506‚Üí%======================================================================
   507‚Üí   507‚Üí
   508‚Üí   508‚ÜíThe SUSY-breaking dynamics must accomplish three things simultaneously:
   509‚Üí   509‚Üígenerate the seed-to-bloom transition for each tuple, preserve the 
   510‚Üí   510‚Üíenergy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ through the 
   511‚Üí   511‚Üíbreaking, and produce the correct splitting between meson and lepton 
   512‚Üí   512‚Üímasses at scale $f_\pi$. In this section we develop the framework in 
   513‚Üí   513‚Üídetail, connecting the three standard SUSY-breaking mechanisms to the 
   514‚Üí   514‚Üíthree-tuple structure.
   515‚Üí   515‚Üí
   516‚Üí   516‚Üí\subsection{The Seiberg framework}
   517‚Üí   517‚Üí
   518‚Üí   518‚ÜíThe natural arena is $\mathcal{N}=1$ SQCD with gauge group $SU(3)_c$ 
   519‚Üí   519‚Üíand $N_f$ flavors of chiral superfields $Q^i$, $\bar{Q}_j$ in the 
   520‚Üí   520‚Üífundamental and antifundamental representations. For $N_f = N_c = 3$ 
   521‚Üí   521‚Üí(the case relevant to light QCD), Seiberg's exact results 
   522‚Üí   522‚Üí\cite{Seiberg1995} establish that the theory confines, with the 
   523‚Üí   523‚Üílow-energy degrees of freedom being the composite meson superfield
   524‚Üí   524‚Üí\begin{equation}
   525‚Üí   525‚ÜíM^i{}_j = Q^i \cdot \bar{Q}_j\,,
   526‚Üí   526‚Üí\end{equation}
   527‚Üí   527‚Üía $3\times 3$ matrix of chiral superfields, together with baryons 
   528‚Üí   528‚Üí$B = \epsilon_{ijk}Q^iQ^jQ^k$ and $\bar{B}$. These satisfy the 
   529‚Üí   529‚Üíquantum-modified constraint
   530‚Üí   530‚Üí\begin{equation}
   531‚Üí   531‚Üí\label{eq:seiberg}
   532‚Üí   532‚Üí\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^{2N_c}\,,
   533‚Üí   533‚Üí\end{equation}
   534‚Üí   534‚Üíwhere $\Lambda$ is the dynamical scale.
   535‚Üí   535‚Üí
   536‚Üí   536‚ÜíEach entry $M^i{}_j$ is a chiral superfield containing a complex 
   537‚Üí   537‚Üíscalar (the meson) and a Weyl fermion (the lepton, in the sBootstrap 
   538‚Üí   538‚Üíidentification). The scalar and fermion are degenerate in the SUSY 
   539‚Üí   539‚Üílimit; SUSY breaking splits them.
   540‚Üí   540‚Üí
   541‚Üí   541‚Üí\subsection{The three chiral superfields of each seed}
   542‚Üí   542‚Üí
   543‚Üí   543‚ÜíThe seed triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ maps naturally onto three 
   544‚Üí   544‚Üíchiral superfields with the following mass structure before breaking:
   545‚Üí   545‚Üí
   546‚Üí   546‚Üí\begin{center}
   547‚Üí   547‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   548‚Üí   548‚Üí\toprule
   549‚Üí   549‚ÜíSuperfield & Mass & Role \\
   550‚Üí   550‚Üí\midrule
   551‚Üí   551‚Üí$\Phi_0$ & $m_0 = 0$ & Massless: flat direction \\
   552‚Üí   552‚Üí$\Phi_a$ & $m_a = \zch_a^2$ & Light massive member \\
   553‚Üí   553‚Üí$\Phi_b$ & $m_b = \zch_b^2$ & Heavy massive member \\
   554‚Üí   554‚Üí\bottomrule
   555‚Üí   555‚Üí\end{tabular}
   556‚Üí   556‚Üí\end{center}
   557‚Üí   557‚Üí
   558‚Üí   558‚ÜíThe ratio $\zch_a/\zch_b = 2 - \sqrt{3}$ is enforced by the 
   559‚Üí   559‚ÜíKoide energy-balance condition applied to the seed.
   560‚Üí   560‚Üí
   561‚Üí   561‚ÜíIn the Seiberg description, these three superfields are entries of 
   562‚Üí   562‚Üíthe meson matrix $M^i{}_j$. For the $(0, s, c)$ seed, the relevant 
   563‚Üí   563‚Üíentries involve the strange and charm quarks; $\Phi_0$ is the entry 
   564‚Üí   564‚Üíwhose quark-mass combination vanishes in the seed limit (i.e., the 
   565‚Üí   565‚Üídirection in flavor space where $m_i + m_j \to 0$ through GOR).
   566‚Üí   566‚Üí
   567‚Üí   567‚Üí\subsection{F-term breaking: the O'Raifeartaigh mechanism}
   568‚Üí   568‚Üí\label{subsec:fterm}
   569‚Üí   569‚Üí
   570‚Üí   570‚ÜíThe O'Raifeartaigh model \cite{ORaifeartaigh} is the simplest 
   571‚Üí   571‚Üírealization of spontaneous $F$-term SUSY breaking. With three chiral 
   572‚Üí   572‚Üísuperfields, the superpotential
   573‚Üí   573‚Üí\begin{equation}
   574‚Üí   574‚ÜíW = f\,\Phi_0 + m\,\Phi_1\Phi_2 + g\,\Phi_0\Phi_1^2
   575‚Üí   575‚Üí\end{equation}
   576‚Üí   576‚Üígives $F$-terms:
   577‚Üí   577‚Üí\begin{align}
   578‚Üí   578‚ÜíF_{\Phi_0}^* &= f + g\,\phi_1^2\,, \\
   579‚Üí   579‚ÜíF_{\Phi_1}^* &= m\,\phi_2 + 2g\,\phi_0\phi_1\,, \\
   580‚Üí   580‚ÜíF_{\Phi_2}^* &= m\,\phi_1\,.
   581‚Üí   581‚Üí\end{align}
   582‚Üí   582‚ÜíIf $f \neq 0$ and $m \neq 0$, then $F_{\Phi_2}^* = 0$ requires 
   583‚Üí   583‚Üí$\phi_1 = 0$, but then $F_{\Phi_0}^* = f \neq 0$: SUSY is 
   584‚Üí   584‚Üí\textbf{necessarily broken}. The key features are:
   585‚Üí   585‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   586‚Üí   586‚Üí\item A massless field ($\Phi_0$) exists at tree level---it is the 
   587‚Üí   587‚Üípseudo-modulus.
   588‚Üí   588‚Üí\item The breaking is triggered because $F_{\Phi_0}$ cannot vanish.
   589‚Üí   589‚Üí\item After including loop corrections, $\Phi_0$ acquires a mass 
   590‚Üí   590‚Üí(the Coleman--Weinberg potential lifts the flat direction).
   591‚Üí   591‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   592‚Üí   592‚Üí
   593‚Üí   593‚ÜíThe structural parallel with the seed triples is precise:
   594‚Üí   594‚Üí
   595‚Üí   595‚Üí\begin{center}
   596‚Üí   596‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lll}
   597‚Üí   597‚Üí\toprule
   598‚Üí   598‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh & Seed triple & After breaking \\
   599‚Üí   599‚Üí\midrule
   600‚Üí   600‚Üí$\Phi_0$ (massless) & 0 member & Lifted to $\zch < 0$ \\
   601‚Üí   601‚Üí$\Phi_1$ (mass $m$) & $\zch_a$ (light) & Shifted \\
   602‚Üí   602‚Üí$\Phi_2$ (mass $m$) & $\zch_b$ (heavy) & Shifted \\
   603‚Üí   603‚Üí\midrule
   604‚Üí   604‚Üí$f = \langle F\rangle$ & --- & $f_\pi$ \\
   605‚Üí   605‚Üí\bottomrule
   606‚Üí   606‚Üí\end{tabular}
   607‚Üí   607‚Üí\end{center}
   608‚Üí   608‚Üí
   609‚Üí   609‚ÜíThe identification $f = \langle F_{\Phi_0}\rangle \sim f_\pi$ is the 
   610‚Üí   610‚ÜísBootstrap's central claim: the SUSY-breaking $F$-term is the chiral 
   611‚Üí   611‚Üícondensate parameter.
   612‚Üí   612‚Üí
   613‚Üí   613‚ÜíIn SQCD, this is realized dynamically rather than through a 
   614‚Üí   614‚Üísuperpotential: the chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ acts as the 
   615‚Üí   615‚Üínonzero $F$-term, the impossibility of restoring chiral symmetry 
   616‚Üí   616‚Üíin the QCD vacuum is the dynamical analogue of $f \neq 0$, and the 
   617‚Üí   617‚Üípion---the would-be Goldstone boson that acquires mass from explicit 
   618‚Üí   618‚Üíchiral breaking---plays the role of $\Phi_0$ whose mass is lifted 
   619‚Üí   619‚Üíby loop corrections (here, by quark masses).
   620‚Üí   620‚Üí
   621‚Üí   621‚Üí\subsection{The Goldstino direction and the Volkov--Akulov realization}
   622‚Üí   622‚Üí\label{subsec:va}
   623‚Üí   623‚Üí
   624‚Üí   624‚ÜíWhen SUSY is spontaneously broken, a massless fermion appears: the 
   625‚Üí   625‚ÜíGoldstino, the fermionic analogue of the Goldstone boson. Its decay 
   626‚Üí   626‚Üíconstant $f_{\text{VA}}$ sets the SUSY-breaking scale through the 
   627‚Üí   627‚ÜíVolkov--Akulov Lagrangian \cite{VolkovAkulov1973}:
   628‚Üí   628‚Üí\begin{equation}
   629‚Üí   629‚Üí\mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}} = -f_{\text{VA}}^2 \det\!\left(
   630‚Üí   630‚Üí\delta_\mu^\nu + \frac{i}{f_{\text{VA}}^2}\,\bar{\lambda}\bar{\sigma}^\nu
   631‚Üí   631‚Üí\partial_\mu\lambda\right),
   632‚Üí   632‚Üí\end{equation}
   633‚Üí   633‚Üíwhere $\lambda$ is the Goldstino field. At low energies, the Goldstino 
   634‚Üí   634‚Üícouples derivatively with strength $\sim 1/f_{\text{VA}}^2$, exactly 
   635‚Üí   635‚Üías the pion couples with strength $\sim 1/f_\pi^2$ in the chiral 
   636‚Üí   636‚ÜíLagrangian.
   637‚Üí   637‚Üí
   638‚Üí   638‚ÜíThe sBootstrap identification is:
   639‚Üí   639‚Üí\begin{equation}
   640‚Üí   640‚Üí\label{eq:va_id}
   641‚Üí   641‚Üíf_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi\,, \qquad 
   642‚Üí   642‚Üí\text{Goldstino} = \text{muon (partner of }\pi\text{)}\,.
   643‚Üí   643‚Üí\end{equation}
   644‚Üí   644‚ÜíMore precisely, the muon is a \emph{pseudo-Goldstino}: in the 
   645‚Üí   645‚Üílimit $m_q \to 0$ where the pion becomes exactly massless (a true 
   646‚Üí   646‚ÜíGoldstone boson), its fermionic partner also becomes massless (a 
   647‚Üí   647‚Üítrue Goldstino). The finite quark masses explicitly break both 
   648‚Üí   648‚Üíchiral symmetry (giving $m_\pi \neq 0$) and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   649‚Üí   649‚Üí(giving $m_\mu \neq m_\pi$), with the splitting controlled by 
   650‚Üí   650‚Üí$f_\pi$:
   651‚Üí   651‚Üí\begin{equation}
   652‚Üí   652‚Üím_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2\,.
   653‚Üí   653‚Üí\end{equation}
   654‚Üí   654‚Üí
   655‚Üí   655‚ÜíThis is the Volkov--Akulov relation for the pseudo-Goldstino: the 
   656‚Üí   656‚Üímass splitting between the scalar (pion) and its fermionic partner 
   657‚Üí   657‚Üí(muon) is set by the SUSY-breaking scale squared.
   658‚Üí   658‚Üí
   659‚Üí   659‚ÜíThe VA mechanism is not a separate breaking from the F-term: it is 
   660‚Üí   660‚Üíthe \emph{low-energy, composite-level description} of the same 
   661‚Üí   661‚Üíbreaking. At the microscopic level, $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ 
   662‚Üí   662‚Üíin the meson superfield; at the composite level, this manifests 
   663‚Üí   663‚Üías the Goldstino coupling with $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$. The two 
   664‚Üí   664‚Üídescriptions are related by:
   665‚Üí   665‚Üí\begin{equation}
   666‚Üí   666‚Üíf_{\text{VA}}^2 = \langle F\rangle = \frac{|\qqbar|}{f_\pi}\,,
   667‚Üí   667‚Üí\end{equation}
   668‚Üí   668‚Üíconsistent with the GOR relation $m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = 
   669‚Üí   669‚Üí-(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$.
   670‚Üí   670‚Üí
   671‚Üí   671‚Üí\subsection{D-term breaking: electromagnetic splittings}
   672‚Üí   672‚Üí\label{subsec:dterm}
   673‚Üí   673‚Üí
   674‚Üí   674‚ÜíThe unbroken $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ gauge symmetry allows a 
   675‚Üí   675‚ÜíFayet--Iliopoulos (FI) term \cite{FayetIliopoulos}:
   676‚Üí   676‚Üí\begin{equation}
   677‚Üí   677‚Üí\mathcal{L}_{\text{FI}} = \xi\,D_{\text{em}}\,,
   678‚Üí   678‚Üí\end{equation}
   679‚Üí   679‚Üíwhich shifts scalar masses by $\delta m^2 = Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi$, 
   680‚Üí   680‚Üíwhere $Q_{\text{em}}$ is the electric charge. This is the 
   681‚Üí   681‚Üísupersymmetric version of the electromagnetic mass difference.
   682‚Üí   682‚Üí
   683‚Üí   683‚ÜíIn QCD without SUSY, the electromagnetic mass splitting of the 
   684‚Üí   684‚Üípion is given by the Das--Guralnik--Mathur--Low--Young (DGMLY) 
   685‚Üí   685‚Üíformula \cite{DGMLY1967}:
   686‚Üí   686‚Üí\begin{equation}
   687‚Üí   687‚Üím_{\pi^\pm}^2 - m_{\pi^0}^2 = \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,
   688‚Üí   688‚Üí\frac{m_\rho^2}{f_\pi^2}\,f_\pi^2 
   689‚Üí   689‚Üí\approx (35.5\MeV)^2\,,
   690‚Üí   690‚Üí\end{equation}
   691‚Üí   691‚Üíwhich in the SUSY framework becomes a D-term contribution:
   692‚Üí   692‚Üí\begin{equation}
   693‚Üí   693‚Üí\xi \sim \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,m_\rho^2 
   694‚Üí   694‚Üí\sim (35\MeV)^2\,.
   695‚Üí   695‚Üí\end{equation}
   696‚Üí   696‚Üí
   697‚Üí   697‚ÜíIn charge space, the D-term shifts $\zch$ by an amount proportional 
   698‚Üí   698‚Üíto $Q_{\text{em}}$:
   699‚Üí   699‚Üí\begin{equation}
   700‚Üí   700‚Üí\zch \to \zch + \delta\zch\,, \qquad 
   701‚Üí   701‚Üí\delta\zch \approx \frac{Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi}{2\zch}\,.
   702‚Üí   702‚Üí\end{equation}
   703‚Üí   703‚ÜíFor the pion ($Q_{\text{em}} = \pm 1$, $\zch \approx 11.8$): 
   704‚Üí   704‚Üí$\delta\zch \approx 0.2\MeV^{1/2}$, matching the observed 
   705‚Üí   705‚Üí$\zch_{\pi^\pm} - \zch_{\pi^0} = 11.82 - 11.62 = 0.20\MeV^{1/2}$.
   706‚Üí   706‚Üí
   707‚Üí   707‚ÜíThe D-term effect is subleading to the F-term: 
   708‚Üí   708‚Üí$\xi \sim (35\MeV)^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 \sim (92\MeV)^2$. It 
   709‚Üí   709‚Üíoperates \emph{within} each supermultiplet entry, splitting charged 
   710‚Üí   710‚Üífrom neutral mesons, while the F-term splits bosons from fermions 
   711‚Üí   711‚Üíacross the supermultiplet.
   712‚Üí   712‚Üí
   713‚Üí   713‚ÜíWitten's inequality \cite{Witten1983} guarantees 
   714‚Üí   714‚Üí$m_{\pi^\pm} > m_{\pi^0}$ in QCD with electromagnetism---the 
   715‚Üí   715‚Üícharged pion is always heavier. In the SUSY framework this 
   716‚Üí   716‚Üíbecomes the statement that the D-term contribution is positive 
   717‚Üí   717‚Üífor $Q_{\text{em}} \neq 0$, consistent with the FI sign.
   718‚Üí   718‚Üí
   719‚Üí   719‚Üí\subsection{Mapping the three mechanisms to the three tuples}
   720‚Üí   720‚Üí\label{subsec:mapping}
   721‚Üí   721‚Üí
   722‚Üí   722‚ÜíThe three-tuple structure suggests a layered breaking:
   723‚Üí   723‚Üí
   724‚Üí   724‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 1: F-term (chiral condensate, scale $\sim f_\pi$).}
   725‚Üí   725‚ÜíThe chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry 
   726‚Üí   726‚Üíand the sBootstrap SUSY. This is the primary breaking, affecting 
   727‚Üí   727‚Üíthe light sector. The seed triples $(0, s, c)$ and $(0, \pi, D_s)$ 
   728‚Üí   728‚Üíare the pre-breaking configurations; the massless member is the flat 
   729‚Üí   729‚Üídirection whose $F$-term is forced nonzero by confinement. After 
   730‚Üí   730‚Üíbreaking:
   731‚Üí   731‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   732‚Üí   732‚Üí\item The zero member is lifted and acquires negative charge 
   733‚Üí   733‚Üí($\zch < 0$), becoming the sign-flipped member of the full triple 
   734‚Üí   734‚Üí($-s$, $-\pi$).
   735‚Üí   735‚Üí\item The full triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ emerge, 
   736‚Üí   736‚Üíincorporating the $b$-quark and $B$-meson sectors.
   737‚Üí   737‚Üí\item The lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the fermionic shadow: 
   738‚Üí   738‚Üíthe leptons acquire masses through the same condensate, with the 
   739‚Üí   739‚Üímuon as pseudo-Goldstino.
   740‚Üí   740‚Üí\end{itemize}
   741‚Üí   741‚Üí
   742‚Üí   742‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 2: D-term (electromagnetism, scale $\sim \alpha_{\text{em}} m_\rho$).}
   743‚Üí   743‚ÜíThe $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI term generates electromagnetic mass 
   744‚Üí   744‚Üísplittings within each multiplet. This is a perturbative correction 
   745‚Üí   745‚Üíon top of the F-term breaking, affecting only charged particles. 
   746‚Üí   746‚ÜíIt does not create new Koide triples but shifts existing ones by 
   747‚Üí   747‚Üí$O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$.
   748‚Üí   748‚Üí
   749‚Üí   749‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 3: The heavy sector (scale $\sim v_0^{(cbt)}$).}
   750‚Üí   750‚ÜíThe $(c,b,t)$ triple at $v_0^2 \approx 22.8\GeV$ operates far 
   751‚Üí   751‚Üíabove the chiral scale. It has no seed (no massless member), no 
   752‚Üí   752‚Üímeson shadow (top does not hadronize), and its breaking may be 
   753‚Üí   753‚Üíconnected to a different sector of the theory---possibly the 
   754‚Üí   754‚Üíelectroweak Higgs mechanism.
   755‚Üí   755‚Üí
   756‚Üí   756‚ÜíThis layered structure has a natural hierarchy:
   757‚Üí   757‚Üí\begin{equation}
   758‚Üí   758‚Üí\underbrace{f_\pi \sim 92\MeV}_{\text{F-term, chiral}} 
   759‚Üí   759‚Üí\;\ll\; 
   760‚Üí   760‚Üí\underbrace{\xi^{1/2} \sim 35\MeV}_{\text{D-term, EM}} 
   761‚Üí   761‚Üí\;\ll\; 
   762‚Üí   762‚Üí\underbrace{v_0^{(cbt)} \sim 151\MeV^{1/2}}_{\text{heavy sector}}\,,
   763‚Üí   763‚Üí\end{equation}
   764‚Üí   764‚Üíwhere the scales refer to different physical quantities (the 
   765‚Üí   765‚Üícomparison is schematic). The F-term dominates the boson--fermion 
   766‚Üí   766‚Üísplitting; the D-term provides electromagnetic fine structure; the 
   767‚Üí   767‚Üíheavy sector is governed by its own dynamics.
   768‚Üí   768‚Üí
   769‚Üí   769‚Üí\subsection{The O'Raifeartaigh structure in SQCD}
   770‚Üí   770‚Üí
   771‚Üí   771‚ÜíIn Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, the quantum constraint 
   772‚Üí   772‚Üí\eqref{eq:seiberg} provides a natural setting for the 
   773‚Üí   773‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh mechanism. The constraint $\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ 
   774‚Üí   774‚Üímeans the moduli space has a specific geometry: not all field directions 
   775‚Üí   775‚Üíare flat. When quark masses are added via a tree-level superpotential 
   776‚Üí   776‚Üí$W_{\text{tree}} = m_i M^i{}_i$ (sum over flavors), the $F$-terms become:
   777‚Üí   777‚Üí\begin{equation}
   778‚Üí   778‚ÜíF_{M^i{}_j} = m_i\,\delta^i_j + \Lambda^6\,(\text{constraint terms})\,.
   779‚Üí   779‚Üí\end{equation}
   780‚Üí   780‚Üí
   781‚Üí   781‚ÜíFor the seed triple $(0, s, c)$, the ``$0$'' member corresponds to a 
   782‚Üí   782‚Üídirection in the meson matrix where the tree-level mass vanishes---the 
   783‚Üí   783‚Üíentry involving the lightest quark combination. In the exact chiral 
   784‚Üí   784‚Üílimit ($m_u = m_d = 0$), this entry is truly massless, and the 
   785‚Üí   785‚Üíconstraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} forces $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ for 
   786‚Üí   786‚Üíthat entry. This is the dynamical O'Raifeartaigh mechanism: 
   787‚Üí   787‚Üíconfinement (encoded in $\Lambda^6$) plays the role of the parameter 
   788‚Üí   788‚Üí$f$ in the superpotential, and the impossibility of setting all 
   789‚Üí   789‚Üí$F$-terms to zero is a consequence of the strong dynamics rather 
   790‚Üí   790‚Üíthan a choice of superpotential.
   791‚Üí   791‚Üí
   792‚Üí   792‚ÜíThe ISS mechanism (Intriligator--Seiberg--Shih \cite{ISS2006}) makes 
   793‚Üí   793‚Üíthis precise: for $N_f$ slightly above $N_c$, SQCD has metastable 
   794‚Üí   794‚ÜíSUSY-breaking vacua where the rank condition prevents all $F$-terms 
   795‚Üí   795‚Üífrom vanishing. The sBootstrap case $N_f = N_c = 3$ is at the 
   796‚Üí   796‚Üíboundary of the ISS window, suggesting that the SUSY breaking may 
   797‚Üí   797‚Üíbe long-lived but not absolutely stable---consistent with the 
   798‚Üí   798‚Üíapproximate nature of the Koide relations.
   799‚Üí   799‚Üí
   800‚Üí   800‚Üí\subsection{How the three supermultiplets join}
   801‚Üí   801‚Üí
   802‚Üí   802‚ÜíThe seed-to-bloom transition generates three separate Koide triples. 
   803‚Üí   803‚ÜíBut the full sBootstrap requires these triples to be \emph{embedded 
   804‚Üí   804‚Üíin a single $3\times 3$ meson matrix}. The entries of $M^i{}_j$ are:
   805‚Üí   805‚Üí
   806‚Üí   806‚Üí\begin{equation}
   807‚Üí   807‚ÜíM = \begin{pmatrix}
   808‚Üí   808‚Üíu\bar{u} & u\bar{d} & u\bar{s} \\
   809‚Üí   809‚Üíd\bar{u} & d\bar{d} & d\bar{s} \\
   810‚Üí   810‚Üís\bar{u} & s\bar{d} & s\bar{s}
   811‚Üí   811‚Üí\end{pmatrix}
   812‚Üí   812‚Üí\;\longleftrightarrow\;
   813‚Üí   813‚Üí\begin{pmatrix}
   814‚Üí   814‚Üí\pi^0/\eta & \pi^+ & K^+ \\
   815‚Üí   815‚Üí\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 \\
   816‚Üí   816‚ÜíK^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta'
   817‚Üí   817‚Üí\end{pmatrix}.
   818‚Üí   818‚Üí\end{equation}
   819‚Üí   819‚Üí
   820‚Üí   820‚ÜíWhen heavier quarks ($c$, $b$) are included, the matrix extends to 
   821‚Üí   821‚Üí$5\times 5$ (or effectively $5\times 5$ minus the top row/column). 
   822‚Üí   822‚ÜíThe Koide triples are specific \emph{slices} through this matrix:
   823‚Üí   823‚Üí
   824‚Üí   824‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   825‚Üí   825‚Üí\item The lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ corresponds to the 
   826‚Üí   826‚Üífermionic components of specific matrix entries.
   827‚Üí   827‚Üí\item The meson seed $(0, \pi, D_s)$ involves the $(u\bar{d})$, 
   828‚Üí   828‚Üí$(c\bar{s})$ entries and a zero.
   829‚Üí   829‚Üí\item The full meson triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ involves 
   830‚Üí   830‚Üí$(u\bar{d})$, $(c\bar{s})$, $(u\bar{b})$.
   831‚Üí   831‚Üí\end{itemize}
   832‚Üí   832‚Üí
   833‚Üí   833‚ÜíThe joining of the three supermultiplets is the central unsolved 
   834‚Üí   834‚Üíproblem: how does a single $5\times 5$ (or $3\times 3$) meson 
   835‚Üí   835‚Üímatrix, subject to the Seiberg constraint, produce \emph{three} 
   836‚Üí   836‚ÜíKoide triples with different $v_0$ values, each satisfying 
   837‚Üí   837‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$?
   838‚Üí   838‚Üí
   839‚Üí   839‚Üí\subsection{The energy balance as a fixed point}
   840‚Üí   840‚Üí
   841‚Üí   841‚ÜíOne possibility is that the energy-balance condition 
   842‚Üí   842‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a \emph{dynamical attractor}: 
   843‚Üí   843‚Üíany set of three masses evolving under the SUSY-breaking dynamics 
   844‚Üí   844‚Üíflows toward the Koide surface $Q = 2/3$. In the renormalization 
   845‚Üí   845‚Üígroup language, the Koide condition would be a fixed point of the 
   846‚Üí   846‚ÜíRG flow for the soft SUSY-breaking parameters.
   847‚Üí   847‚Üí
   848‚Üí   848‚ÜíThis is plausible if the breaking dynamics has an extremum (minimum 
   849‚Üí   849‚Üíof effective potential, or maximum of entropy) at the equipartition 
   850‚Üí   850‚Üípoint $\sigma = \mu$. The condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ 
   851‚Üí   851‚Üísays the variance of the charges equals their squared mean---this 
   852‚Üí   852‚Üíis the maximum-entropy distribution for three positive-definite 
   853‚Üí   853‚Üíquantities with a given mean, subject to a log-normal prior. If 
   854‚Üí   854‚Üíthe SUSY-breaking effective potential favors ``democratic'' 
   855‚Üí   855‚Üídistributions of mass among the multiplet members, the Koide 
   856‚Üí   856‚Üícondition would emerge as a thermodynamic consequence.
   857‚Üí   857‚Üí
   858‚Üí   858‚ÜíThe fact that the condition holds both for fundamental fermions 
   859‚Üí   859‚Üí(leptons, quarks) and composite bosons (mesons) supports the 
   860‚Üí   860‚Üíattractor interpretation: it is not specific to one sector but 
   861‚Üí   861‚Üíoperates universally wherever three related masses coexist.
   862‚Üí   862‚Üí
   863‚Üí   863‚Üí\subsection{What the $(c,b,t)$ triple tells us}
   864‚Üí   864‚Üí
   865‚Üí   865‚ÜíThe $(c,b,t)$ triple ($Q = 0.6695$, $v_0^2 = 22.8\GeV$) has 
   866‚Üí   866‚Üíneither a seed nor a meson shadow. It operates at a scale far 
   867‚Üí   867‚Üíabove $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, suggesting its origin is different:
   868‚Üí   868‚Üí
   869‚Üí   869‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   870‚Üí   870‚Üí\item It may be governed by the electroweak Higgs mechanism rather 
   871‚Üí   871‚Üíthan the chiral condensate. The top Yukawa $y_t \approx 1$ is 
   872‚Üí   872‚Üí``natural'' in the sense that $m_t \approx v_{\text{EW}}/\sqrt{2}$; 
   873‚Üí   873‚Üíthe Koide condition on $(c,b,t)$ would then constrain the Yukawa 
   874‚Üí   874‚Üícoupling matrix.
   875‚Üí   875‚Üí\item It may represent a Volkov--Akulov realization at the 
   876‚Üí   876‚Üíelectroweak scale: if there is a second SUSY breaking at 
   877‚Üí   877‚Üí$f' \sim v_{\text{EW}}$, the $(c,b,t)$ triple would be its 
   878‚Üí   878‚Üíanalog of the $(\pi, \mu)$ system, but with $f'$ replacing $f_\pi$.
   879‚Üí   879‚Üí\item Or it may be a purely kinematic accident---the quark masses 
   880‚Üí   880‚Üíhappen to satisfy the Koide condition without a dynamical reason. 
   881‚Üí   881‚ÜíThe 0.42\% precision makes this unlikely but not impossible.
   882‚Üí   882‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   883‚Üí   883‚Üí
   884‚Üí   884‚ÜíIf interpretation (2) is correct, the sBootstrap has two breaking 
   885‚Üí   885‚Üíscales:
   886‚Üí   886‚Üí\begin{equation}
   887‚Üí   887‚Üíf_{\text{chiral}} = f_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \qquad
   888‚Üí   888‚Üíf_{\text{EW}} \sim v_{\text{EW}} \approx 246\GeV\,,
   889‚Üí   889‚Üí\end{equation}
   890‚Üí   890‚Üíand the full spectrum requires both. The light sector (leptons, 
   891‚Üí   891‚Üílight mesons) is governed by $f_\pi$; the heavy sector (top, and 
   892‚Üí   892‚Üíby extension charm and bottom through the Koide network) is 
   893‚Üí   893‚Üígoverned by $v_{\text{EW}}$. The charm and bottom quarks, 
   894‚Üí   894‚Üíappearing in both the $(0,s,c)/(-s,c,b)$ light-sector triples 
   895‚Üí   895‚Üíand the $(c,b,t)$ heavy-sector triple, are the ``bridges'' 
   896‚Üí   896‚Üíbetween the two scales.
   897‚Üí   897‚Üí
   898‚Üí   898‚Üí%======================================================================
   899‚Üí   899‚Üí\section{The Electron Mass and the Lepton Seed}
   900‚Üí   900‚Üí\label{sec:electron}
   901‚Üí   901‚Üí%======================================================================
   902‚Üí   902‚Üí
   903‚Üí   903‚ÜíThe charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the ``almost-seed'': 
   904‚Üí   904‚Üí$\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$ is small but nonzero. The electron's 
   905‚Üí   905‚Üícharge is near zero because of cancellation in $\zch_e = z_0 + z_e$:
   906‚Üí   906‚Üí\begin{equation}
   907‚Üí   907‚Üív_0 = 17.72, \quad z_e = -17.00, \quad 
   908‚Üí   908‚Üí\zch_e = 17.72 - 17.00 = 0.72\MeV^{1/2}\,.
   909‚Üí   909‚Üí\end{equation}
   910‚Üí   910‚ÜíA 96\% cancellation. The mass $m_e = 0.72^2 = 0.52\MeV$ is the 
   911‚Üí   911‚Üísquare of something already small.
   912‚Üí   912‚Üí
   913‚Üí   913‚ÜíThe near-vanishing of $m_e$ is a structural property of the Koide 
   914‚Üí   914‚Üíembedding, not fine-tuning: the energy balance allows perturbations 
   915‚Üí   915‚Üías large as $z_0$. Every Koide triple has one member with 
   916‚Üí   916‚Üí$|\zch|/v_0$ small:
   917‚Üí   917‚Üí
   918‚Üí   918‚Üí\begin{center}
   919‚Üí   919‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccl}
   920‚Üí   920‚Üí\toprule
   921‚Üí   921‚ÜíTriple & Lightest & $|\zch|/v_0$ & Character \\
   922‚Üí   922‚Üí\midrule
   923‚Üí   923‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & $e$ & 0.040 & Almost-seed \\
   924‚Üí   924‚Üí$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & $\pi$ (neg.) & 0.337 & Full triple \\
   925‚Üí   925‚Üí$(c, b, t)$ & $c$ & 0.236 & Full triple \\
   926‚Üí   926‚Üí$(-s, c, b)$ & $s$ (neg.) & 0.320 & Full triple \\
   927‚Üí   927‚Üí\bottomrule
   928‚Üí   928‚Üí\end{tabular}
   929‚Üí   929‚Üí\end{center}
   930‚Üí   930‚Üí
   931‚Üí   931‚ÜíThe electron is by far the most extreme case, consistent with the 
   932‚Üí   932‚Üílepton triple being nearest to its seed.
   933‚Üí   933‚Üí
   934‚Üí   934‚Üí%======================================================================
   935‚Üí   935‚Üí\section{Symmetry Restoration}
   936‚Üí   936‚Üí\label{sec:restoration}
   937‚Üí   937‚Üí%======================================================================
   938‚Üí   938‚Üí
   939‚Üí   939‚ÜíThe broken spectrum reassembles as symmetry-breaking effects are 
   940‚Üí   940‚Üíremoved:
   941‚Üí   941‚Üí
   942‚Üí   942‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   943‚Üí   943‚Üí\item \textbf{Turn off QED:} Electromagnetic splittings vanish 
   944‚Üí   944‚Üí($\pi^\pm = \pi^0$, $K^\pm = K^0$). D-term effects disappear.
   945‚Üí   945‚Üí\item \textbf{Set $m_u = m_d$:} Exact isospin.
   946‚Üí   946‚Üí\item \textbf{Set $m_s = m_d$:} $SU(3)_V$ restored, 
   947‚Üí   947‚Üí$\pi = K = \eta$. Koide splitting $v_1 \to 0$; lepton partners 
   948‚Üí   948‚Üídegenerate.
   949‚Üí   949‚Üí\item \textbf{Send $m_q \to 0$:} Chiral limit, all meson charges 
   950‚Üí   950‚Üí$\to 0$. If sBootstrap SUSY restores, all light lepton masses 
   951‚Üí   951‚Üívanish too. This is the seed configuration: $(0, 0, 0)$.
   952‚Üí   952‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   953‚Üí   953‚Üí
   954‚Üí   954‚ÜíThe seed triples are the ``penultimate'' stage: one member has 
   955‚Üí   955‚Üíreached zero, but the other two retain the $2 - \sqrt{3}$ ratio. 
   956‚Üí   956‚ÜíFull triples are the final broken state. The lepton triple, 
   957‚Üí   957‚Üítrapped between seed and bloom, reveals the hierarchy: 
   958‚Üí   958‚Üíchiral breaking $\to$ seed $\to$ bloom $\to$ full spectrum.
   959‚Üí   959‚Üí
   960‚Üí   960‚ÜíThe direction of the flow also matters: approaching the chiral 
   961‚Üí   961‚Üílimit, the pion charge rises from $\zch = -11.81$ toward zero 
   962‚Üí   962‚Üífrom below. It crosses zero exactly in the chiral limit. This 
   963‚Üí   963‚Üímeans the pion was ``born negative''---its charge sign is set by 
   964‚Üí   964‚Üíthe direction of chiral symmetry breaking, and the seed 
   965‚Üí   965‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ is the configuration at the moment of crossing.
   966‚Üí   966‚Üí
   967‚Üí   967‚Üí%======================================================================
   968‚Üí   968‚Üí\section{Nature Abhors Chirality}
   969‚Üí   969‚Üí\label{sec:chirality}
   970‚Üí   970‚Üí%======================================================================
   971‚Üí   971‚Üí
   972‚Üí   972‚ÜíA pattern across the Standard Model: every chiral structure is 
   973‚Üí   973‚Üíeliminated in the infrared.
   974‚Üí   974‚Üí
   975‚Üí   975‚Üí\begin{center}
   976‚Üí   976‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   977‚Üí   977‚Üí\toprule
   978‚Üí   978‚ÜíUV structure & IR survivor & Mechanism \\
   979‚Üí   979‚Üí\midrule
   980‚Üí   980‚Üí$SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ & $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ & Higgs \\
   981‚Üí   981‚Üí$SU(N_f)_L \times SU(N_f)_R$ & $SU(N_f)_V$ & $\qqbar$ condensate \\
   982‚Üí   982‚Üí$U(1)_A$ & nothing & Anomaly \\
   983‚Üí   983‚Üí$\bar{\theta}$-vacuum & $|\bar{\theta}| < 10^{-10}$ & Unknown \\
   984‚Üí   984‚ÜíChiral color? & $SU(3)_C$ & Axigluon mass \\
   985‚Üí   985‚Üí\bottomrule
   986‚Üí   986‚Üí\end{tabular}
   987‚Üí   987‚Üí\end{center}
   988‚Üí   988‚Üí
   989‚Üí   989‚ÜíThe sBootstrap adds: superspace chirality (boson vs.\ fermion 
   990‚Üí   990‚Üídistinction within a multiplet) is broken by the same condensate 
   991‚Üí   991‚Üíthat breaks flavor chirality, with $f_{\text{SUSY}} = f_\pi$. The 
   992‚Üí   992‚Üíenergy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ may be 
   993‚Üí   993‚Üírelated: the breaking is ``as large as the background 
   994‚Üí   994‚Üíallows''---maximal, not perturbative.
   995‚Üí   995‚Üí
   996‚Üí   996‚Üí%======================================================================
   997‚Üí   997‚Üí\section{Open Problems}
   998‚Üí   998‚Üí\label{sec:open}
   999‚Üí   999‚Üí%======================================================================
  1000‚Üí  1000‚Üí
  1001‚Üí  1001‚Üí\subsection{The diquark statistics problem}
  1002‚Üí  1002‚ÜíThe sBootstrap requires antisymmetric $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ 
  1003‚Üí  1003‚Üídiquarks; Pauli statistics for spin-0, $L=0$, color $\bar{3}$ 
  1004‚Üí  1004‚Üídiquarks requires symmetric flavor. Extra dynamical input---$L=1$ 
  1005‚Üí  1005‚Üíplus spin-1, instantons, or the $SO(32)$ embedding---is needed.
  1006‚Üí  1006‚Üí
  1007‚Üí  1007‚Üí\subsection{What determines the Koide phase?}
  1008‚Üí  1008‚ÜíThe energy balance constrains but does not uniquely fix $\delta$. 
  1009‚Üí  1009‚ÜíThe value $\delta_\ell \approx 2/9$ for leptons must come from 
  1010‚Üí  1010‚Üídynamics. Is it a fixed point?
  1011‚Üí  1011‚Üí
  1012‚Üí  1012‚Üí\subsection{Connecting the three tuples}
  1013‚Üí  1013‚ÜíThe light sector (seed $\to$ bloom, scale $\sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$) 
  1014‚Üí  1014‚Üíand heavy sector ($c,b,t$, scale $\sim v_{\text{EW}}$) must connect. 
  1015‚Üí  1015‚ÜíThe charm and bottom quarks appear in both, providing the links.
  1016‚Üí  1016‚Üí
  1017‚Üí  1017‚Üí\subsection{The baryon sector}
  1018‚Üí  1018‚ÜíIn Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, baryons are part of the 
  1019‚Üí  1019‚Üílow-energy spectrum. Their SUSY partners are ``baryonic fermions'' 
  1020‚Üí  1020‚Üíwith no known candidates among observed particles.
  1021‚Üí  1021‚Üí
  1022‚Üí  1022‚Üí\subsection{The strong CP problem}
  1023‚Üí  1023‚ÜíThe principle ``Nature abhors chirality'' predicts $\bar{\theta} = 0$ 
  1024‚Üí  1024‚Üíbut provides no mechanism.
  1025‚Üí  1025‚Üí
  1026‚Üí  1026‚Üí\subsection{The kaon--muon puzzle}
  1027‚Üí  1027‚ÜíThe SUSY splitting $m_K^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx (482\MeV)^2$ is much 
  1028‚Üí  1028‚Üílarger than $f_K^2 \approx (110\MeV)^2$, showing that the breaking 
  1029‚Üí  1029‚Üíis strongly flavor-dependent.
  1030‚Üí  1030‚Üí
  1031‚Üí  1031‚Üí%======================================================================
  1032‚Üí  1032‚Üí\section{Conclusions}
  1033‚Üí  1033‚Üí\label{sec:conclusions}
  1034‚Üí  1034‚Üí%======================================================================
  1035‚Üí  1035‚Üí
  1036‚Üí  1036‚ÜíThree principal results:
  1037‚Üí  1037‚Üí
  1038‚Üí  1038‚Üí\paragraph{1.\ The Koide formula is energy equipartition.}
  1039‚Üí  1039‚Üí$Q = 2/3$ is $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$: perturbation energies 
  1040‚Üí  1040‚Üímatch the vacuum energy. The signed-charge framework resolves the 
  1041‚Üí  1041‚Üíelectron mass puzzle (near-cancellation, not fine-tuning) and reveals 
  1042‚Üí  1042‚Üíthat mesons satisfy Koide with $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to $0.1\%$.
  1043‚Üí  1043‚Üí
  1044‚Üí  1044‚Üí\paragraph{2.\ Seed triples set the stage for SUSY breaking.}
  1045‚Üí  1045‚ÜíThe quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ share the 
  1046‚Üí  1046‚Üíuniversal ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$ and operate at $v_0^2 \sim 
  1047‚Üí  1047‚Üí230$--$350\MeV$, close to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 = 314\MeV$. 
  1048‚Üí  1048‚ÜíThe massless member is the necessary condition for F-term 
  1049‚Üí  1049‚Üí(O'Raifeartaigh) SUSY breaking. Upon breaking, seeds bloom into 
  1050‚Üí  1050‚Üífull triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi,D_s,B)$. The lepton triple 
  1051‚Üí  1051‚Üíis the ``almost-seed'' that has barely bloomed.
  1052‚Üí  1052‚Üí
  1053‚Üí  1053‚Üí\paragraph{3.\ Three tuples require three breaking structures.}
  1054‚Üí  1054‚ÜíF-term breaking (via the massless seed member) generates the light 
  1055‚Üí  1055‚Üísector. D-term breaking ($U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI parameter) provides 
  1056‚Üí  1056‚Üíelectromagnetic splittings. Volkov--Akulov nonlinear realization 
  1057‚Üí  1057‚Üísets the overall scale $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$. The heavy triple 
  1058‚Üí  1058‚Üí$(c,b,t)$ stands apart: no seed, no meson shadow, scale far above 
  1059‚Üí  1059‚Üí$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, possibly connected to electroweak breaking.
  1060‚Üí  1060‚Üí
  1061‚Üí  1061‚ÜíThe diquark sector, required to complete the SUSY multiplet count, 
  1062‚Üí  1062‚Üímust fill the antisymmetric $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ of $SU(5)_f$ 
  1063‚Üí  1063‚Üídespite the apparent conflict with Fermi statistics---a problem 
  1064‚Üí  1064‚Üíthat may ultimately require the $SO(32)$ embedding for its 
  1065‚Üí  1065‚Üíresolution.
  1066‚Üí  1066‚Üí
  1067‚Üí  1067‚ÜíThe organizing principle is that each sector of the spectrum begins 
  1068‚Üí  1068‚Üíwith a massless member (a seed), which is the necessary condition 
  1069‚Üí  1069‚Üífor spontaneous SUSY breaking. The breaking lifts the zero, 
  1070‚Üí  1070‚Üígenerates the full triple, and the energy-balance condition 
  1071‚Üí  1071‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is preserved as a fixed point 
  1072‚Üí  1072‚Üíof the dynamics. The entire charged fermion and pseudoscalar meson 
  1073‚Üí  1073‚Üíspectrum is then determined by a small number of parameters: the 
  1074‚Üí  1074‚Üívacuum charges $v_0$ of the three tuples, the Koide phases 
  1075‚Üí  1075‚Üí$\delta$ that control the bloom, and the SUSY-breaking scale 
  1076‚Üí  1076‚Üí$f_\pi$ that separates bosons from fermions.
