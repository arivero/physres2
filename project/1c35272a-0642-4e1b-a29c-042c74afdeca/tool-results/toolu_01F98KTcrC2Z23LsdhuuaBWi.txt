    29‚Üí\begin{abstract}
    30‚ÜíWe reformulate the sBootstrap in terms of signed mass charges 
    31‚Üí$\zch = z_0 + z_i$ with $m = \zch^2$, and show that the Koide ratio 
    32‚Üí$Q = 2/3$ is the algebraic consequence of two conditions: 
    33‚Üí$\sum z_i = 0$ and $\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$ (perturbation 
    34‚Üíenergies average to the vacuum energy). Allowing negative charges, 
    35‚Üíwe find that pseudoscalar mesons satisfy a Koide triple 
    36‚Üí$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to 0.1\%, and identify \emph{seed triples} with 
    37‚Üíone massless member---$(0, s, c)$ for quarks, $(0, \pi, D_s)$ for 
    38‚Üímesons---as the pre-breaking configurations from which the full 
    39‚Üíspectrum emerges. These seed triples share the same analytical 
    40‚Üístructure (charge ratio $2-\sqrt{3}$) and, strikingly, the meson 
    41‚Üíseed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ has vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 351\MeV$, 
    42‚Üíclose to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 \approx 314\MeV$. A third 
    43‚Üítuple $(c,b,t)$ stands apart, having no meson counterpart because 
    44‚Üítop diquarks do not hadronize. We discuss the diquark sector---which 
    45‚Üímust fill the antisymmetric representation of $SU(5)$ flavor rather 
    46‚Üíthan the symmetric, requiring additional dynamical input---and outline 
    47‚Üíhow SUSY breaking proceeds through two mechanisms---F-term (chiral 
    48‚Üícondensate, with Volkov--Akulov as its low-energy realization) and 
    49‚ÜíD-term (electromagnetic Fayet--Iliopoulos)---plus a separate 
    50‚Üíheavy-sector origin for the $(c,b,t)$ triple.
    51‚Üí\end{abstract}

    55‚Üí%======================================================================
    56‚Üí\section{Introduction}
    57‚Üí\label{sec:intro}
    58‚Üí%======================================================================
    59‚Üí
    60‚ÜíThe sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005,Rivero2012} proposes that the Standard 
    61‚ÜíModel's pseudoscalar mesons and leptons fill common $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
    62‚Üísupermultiplets. Its foundational relation is
    63‚Üí\begin{equation}
    64‚Üí\label{eq:pimu}
    65‚Üím_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2\,,
    66‚Üí\end{equation}
    67‚Üíidentifying the pion decay constant $f_\pi \approx 92.2\MeV$ as the 
    68‚ÜíSUSY-breaking order parameter. Numerically, $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = 
    69‚Üí8{,}316\MeV^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 = 8{,}501\MeV^2$, a $2.2\%$ 
    70‚Üídiscrepancy---comparable to the precision of the quark Koide triples 
    71‚Üídiscussed below. In this paper we develop two themes 
    72‚Üíthat change the structure of the program.
    73‚Üí
    74‚ÜíFirst, \textbf{mass is the square of a signed charge}: $m = \zch^2$ 
    75‚Üíwhere $\zch = z_0 + z_i$ can be negative. The Koide ratio $Q = 2/3$ 
    76‚Üíis then not numerology but the energy-balance condition 
    77‚Üí$\langle z_i^2\rangle = z_0^2$. With signed charges, pseudoscalar 
    78‚Üímesons satisfy Koide, and the spectrum organizes into \emph{seed 
    79‚Üítriples} with a massless member that grow into full triples upon 
    80‚Üísymmetry breaking.
    81‚Üí
    82‚ÜíSecond, \textbf{the full sBootstrap has three Koide tuples, not one}, 
    83‚Üíand the breaking structure must generate all three: a light sector 
    84‚Üí$(0,s,c) \to (-s,c,b)$ seen in both quarks and mesons, a lepton 
    85‚Üísector $(e\!\approx\!0, \mu, \tau)$, and a heavy sector $(c,b,t)$ 
    86‚Üíthat decouples from meson physics because the top quark does not 
    87‚Üíhadronize. The diquark partners of quarks, needed to complete the 
    88‚ÜíSUSY multiplets, must fill the antisymmetric $\overline{10}$ of 
    89‚Üí$SU(5)$ flavor rather than the symmetric 15, requiring extra 
    90‚Üídynamical input from the breaking sector.
    91‚Üí
    92‚Üí%======================================================================
    93‚Üí\section{The Koide Formula as Energy Balance}
    94‚Üí\label{sec:koide_algebra}
    95‚Üí%======================================================================
    96‚Üí
    97‚Üí\subsection{Charges, not masses}
    98‚Üí
    99‚ÜíThe fundamental variable is the signed mass charge $\zch = z_0 + z_i 
   100‚Üí\in \mathbb{R}$, with the physical mass $m = \zch^2 \geq 0$.
   101‚Üí
   102‚Üí\subsection{Derivation of $Q = 2/3$}
   103‚Üí
   104‚ÜíFor three charges $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k$ with $z_0$ the mean 
   105‚Üí($\sum z_k = 0$):
   106‚Üí\begin{equation}
   107‚ÜíQ = \frac{\sum \zch_k^2}{(\sum \zch_k)^2} 
   108‚Üí= \frac{3z_0^2 + \sum z_k^2}{9z_0^2}
   109‚Üí= \frac{1}{3} + \frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{3z_0^2}\,.
   110‚Üí\end{equation}
   111‚ÜíTherefore:
   112‚Üí\begin{equation}
   113‚Üí\boxed{Q = \frac{2}{3} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad 
   114‚Üí\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2\,.}
   115‚Üí\end{equation}
   116‚Üí
   117‚ÜíThe two conditions are:
   118‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   119‚Üí\item \textbf{Zero-sum perturbations:} $z_1 + z_2 + z_3 = 0$ 
   120‚Üí(definition of $z_0$ as the mean charge).
   121‚Üí\item \textbf{Energy balance:} The mean energy of the perturbations 
   122‚Üíequals the energy of the unperturbed charge: 
   123‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
   124‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   125‚Üí
   126‚ÜíCondition~2 is the physics. It says the splitting scale matches the 
   127‚Üíbase scale---the coefficient of variation equals unity: 
   128‚Üí$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$. This is neither perturbative 
   129‚Üí($\sigma \ll \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1/3$, all equal) nor 
   130‚Üídominant ($\sigma \gg \bar{\zch}$, giving $Q \to 1$, one mass 
   131‚Üídominates). The Koide value $2/3$ is the equipartition point.
   132‚Üí
   133‚Üí\subsection{Why one mass can vanish}
   134‚Üí
   135‚ÜíIf $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$, a perturbation $z_k$ can reach 
   136‚Üí$|z_k| \sim z_0$, making $\zch_k = z_0 + z_k \approx 0$. A vanishing 
   137‚Üímass is the \emph{natural extreme} of energy balance, not fine-tuning.
   138‚Üí
   139‚Üí%======================================================================
   140‚Üí\section{Seed Triples: The $(0, a, b)$ Structure}
   141‚Üí\label{sec:seeds}
   142‚Üí%======================================================================
   143‚Üí
   144‚Üí\subsection{Analytical condition}
   145‚Üí
   146‚ÜíFor a triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ with one massless member, the 
   147‚ÜíKoide ratio becomes:
   148‚Üí\begin{equation}
   149‚ÜíQ = \frac{\zch_a^2 + \zch_b^2}{(\zch_a + \zch_b)^2}\,.
   150‚Üí\end{equation}
   151‚ÜíSetting $Q = 2/3$ and writing $r = \zch_a/\zch_b$ (with $r < 1$):
   152‚Üí\begin{equation}
   153‚Üí3(r^2 + 1) = 2(r + 1)^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad r^2 - 4r + 1 = 0\,,
   154‚Üí\end{equation}
   155‚Üígiving:
   156‚Üí\begin{equation}
   157‚Üí\boxed{r = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.}
   158‚Üí\end{equation}
   159‚Üí
   160‚ÜíEquivalently, in mass space: 
   161‚Üí$m_{\text{light}}/m_{\text{heavy}} = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 = 7 - 4\sqrt{3} 
   162‚Üí\approx 0.07180$.
   163‚Üí
   164‚ÜíThis is a \emph{universal} prediction: any Koide triple with one 
   165‚Üímassless member has the same charge ratio between the two massive 
   166‚Üímembers, regardless of their absolute scale.
   167‚Üí
   168‚Üí\subsection{The quark seed: $(0, s, c)$}
   169‚Üí
   170‚ÜíThe strange and charm quark charges give:\footnote{Throughout 
   171‚Üíthis paper, quark masses are $\overline{\text{MS}}$ running masses 
   172‚Üíevaluated at their own scale: $m_s(2\GeV) = 93.4\MeV$, 
   173‚Üí$m_c(m_c) = 1.27\GeV$, $m_b(m_b) = 4.18\GeV$ (PDG 2024 central 
   174‚Üívalues). For the top quark, the pole mass $m_t = 172.76\GeV$ is 
   175‚Üíused because it gives the best Koide fit ($Q = 0.6695$); the 
   176‚Üí$\overline{\text{MS}}$ mass $\bar{m}_t(m_t) \approx 162.5\GeV$ 
   177‚Üígives $Q = 0.663$, significantly worse. This mixed convention 
   178‚Üíreflects the fact that QCD running is large for light quarks and 
   179‚Üínegligible for the top. Evaluating all masses at a common scale 
   180‚Üí(e.g., $\mu = m_Z$) would change the light-quark Koide ratios 
   181‚Üíby several percent, degrading the fit. Whether the ``natural'' 
   182‚Üíscale for each quark is its own mass (as assumed here) or a 
   183‚Üíuniversal scale is an open question tied to the seed-scale 
   184‚Üídiscussion in \S\ref{subsec:exactseed}.}
   185‚Üí\begin{equation}
   186‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_s}}{\sqrt{m_c}} = \frac{9.67}{35.64} = 0.2712\,, 
   187‚Üí\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 1.2\%\,.
   188‚Üí\end{equation}
   189‚Üí
   190‚ÜíThis triple has $Q = 0.6644$, deviating from $2/3$ by only $0.35\%$. 
   191‚ÜíIts vacuum energy is $v_0^2 = (\zch_s + \zch_c)^2/9 \approx 228\MeV$.
   192‚Üí
   193‚ÜíA historical note: Harari, Haut, and Weyers \cite{Harari} observed 
   194‚Üía quark mass ratio pattern that, in the language of the present paper, 
   195‚Üícorresponds to an approximate seed triple---$(0, d, s)$---at a time 
   196‚Üíwhen the up quark mass was 
   197‚Üístill thought to be possibly zero \cite{Harari}. With current masses, 
   198‚Üí$\sqrt{m_d}/\sqrt{m_s} = 0.224$, deviating from $2 - \sqrt{3}$ by 
   199‚Üí$16\%$---too large for a good Koide triple. But if $m_s$ were 
   200‚Üí$\sim 150\MeV$ and $m_d \sim 7\MeV$ (values common in that era), the 
   201‚Üíratio improves considerably. The seed idea was already present, ahead 
   202‚Üíof its time.
   203‚Üí
   204‚Üí\subsection{The meson seed: $(0, \pi, D_s)$}
   205‚Üí
   206‚ÜíThe pion and $D_s$ meson charges give:
   207‚Üí\begin{equation}
   208‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_\pi}}{\sqrt{m_{D_s}}} = \frac{11.81}{44.37} = 0.2663\,, 
   209‚Üí\qquad r_{\text{Koide}} = 0.2679\,, \qquad \text{deviation: } 0.6\%\,.
   210‚Üí\end{equation}
   211‚Üí
   212‚ÜíThis gives $Q = 0.6679$, deviating by $0.18\%$. It is the 
   213‚Üí\textbf{meson counterpart} of the quark seed $(0, s, c)$---both 
   214‚Üíinvolve the strange-charm sector. Indeed, $D_s = c\bar{s}$ is the 
   215‚Üímeson that directly contains the $s$ and $c$ quarks.
   216‚Üí
   217‚Üí\subsection{The lepton triple as an ``almost-seed''}
   218‚Üí
   219‚ÜíThe charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ has $\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$, 
   220‚Üíwhich is small but nonzero. If $m_e$ were zero, we would need 
   221‚Üí$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 2 - \sqrt{3} = 0.2679$; the actual value 
   222‚Üíis $0.2439$, deviating by $9\%$. So the leptons are \emph{not} an 
   223‚Üíexact seed---the electron, while very light, is not massless. But the 
   224‚Üítriple is clearly the same family: the near-critical Koide phase puts 
   225‚Üíone member close to zero.
   226‚Üí
   227‚Üí\subsection{Vacuum energy coincidence}
   228‚Üí
   229‚ÜíThe $v_0^2$ values of the seed triples cluster remarkably:
   230‚Üí
   231‚Üí\begin{center}
   232‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcc}
   233‚Üí\toprule
   234‚ÜíTriple & $v_0^2$ (MeV) & $v_0$ ($\MeV^{1/2}$) \\
   235‚Üí\midrule
   236‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 & 17.72 \\
   237‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 351 & 18.73 \\
   238‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ & 228 & 15.10 \\
   239‚Üí\bottomrule
   240‚Üí\end{tabular}
   241‚Üí\end{center}
   242‚Üí
   243‚ÜíThe lepton and meson seed triples share a vacuum energy around 
   244‚Üí310--350~MeV, squarely in the QCD scale range. This is the 
   245‚ÜísBootstrap connection made quantitative: \textbf{the lepton triple 
   246‚Üíand the meson seed triple operate at the same scale.}
   247‚Üí
   248‚Üí%======================================================================
   249‚Üí\section{A Tale of Two Tuples}
   250‚Üí\label{sec:two_tuples}
   251‚Üí%======================================================================
   252‚Üí
   253‚Üí\subsection{From seed to full triple}
   254‚Üí
   255‚ÜíThe seed triples grow into full triples upon symmetry breaking:
   256‚Üí
   257‚Üí\begin{center}
   258‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   259‚Üí\toprule
   260‚ÜíSector & Seed triple & Full triple & $v_0^2$ (full) \\
   261‚Üí\midrule
   262‚ÜíQuarks & $(0, s, c)$ & $(-s, c, b)$ & 913 MeV \\
   263‚ÜíMesons & $(0, \pi, D_s)$ & $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 1230 MeV \\
   264‚ÜíLeptons & $(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & $(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 314 MeV \\
   265‚Üí\bottomrule
   266‚Üí\end{tabular}
   267‚Üí\end{center}
   268‚Üí
   269‚ÜíIn each case:
   270‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   271‚Üí\item The seed has a massless (or near-massless) member, two massive 
   272‚Üímembers in the ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$, and $Q \approx 2/3$.
   273‚Üí\item The full triple has \textbf{no} massless member: the zero has 
   274‚Üíbeen lifted (to $m_e$, or the sign-flipped strange/pion mass). The 
   275‚Üí$b$ quark and $B$ meson are always present as flavor degrees of 
   276‚Üífreedom; what the breaking does is give them their \emph{distinctive 
   277‚Üímasses}---the specific values that complete the Koide triple. Before 
   278‚Üíbreaking, the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$); 
   279‚Üíafter, it acquires $\zch_b = 64.65\MeV^{1/2}$, the value selected 
   280‚Üíby the energy balance with $c$ and the sign-flipped $s$. The bloom 
   281‚Üíis not particle creation but \emph{mass generation}.
   282‚Üí\end{itemize}
   283‚Üí
   284‚ÜíThe ``tale of two tuples'' is this: \textbf{each sector presents 
   285‚Üífirst as a seed with a massless member, then ``blooms'' into a 
   286‚Üífull triple when SUSY breaks.} The massless member is the necessary 
   287‚Üíinitial condition; the breaking lifts it and generates the full spectrum.
   288‚Üí
   289‚Üí\subsection{Precision summary}
   290‚Üí
   291‚Üí\begin{center}
   292‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccc}
   293‚Üí\toprule
   294‚ÜíTriple & $Q$ & Dev.\ from $2/3$ & $v_0^2$ (MeV) \\
   295‚Üí\midrule
   296‚Üí\multicolumn{4}{c}{\textit{Seed triples}} \\
   297‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 0.6679 & 0.18\% & 351 \\
   298‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ & 0.6644 & 0.35\% & 228 \\
   299‚Üí$(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.6667 & 0.001\% & 314 \\
   300‚Üí\midrule
   301‚Üí\multicolumn{4}{c}{\textit{Full triples}} \\
   302‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.66666 & 0.001\% & 314 \\
   303‚Üí$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & 0.6674 & 0.10\% & 1230 \\
   304‚Üí$(-s, c, b)$ & 0.6750 & 1.24\% & 913 \\
   305‚Üí$(c, b, t)$ & 0.6695 & 0.42\% & 29\,580 \\
   306‚Üí\bottomrule
   307‚Üí\end{tabular}
   308‚Üí\end{center}
   309‚Üí
   310‚ÜíThe lepton triple is unique: seed and full triple are nearly 
   311‚Üíidentical, because the electron mass is small enough to serve as 
   312‚Üíthe ``zero'' while being nonzero enough to make $Q$ extremely 
   313‚Üíprecise.
   314‚Üí
   315‚Üí\subsection{Orthogonality of seed and full triples}
   316‚Üí
   317‚ÜíEach Koide triple defines a perturbation vector 
   318‚Üí$\vec{z} = (z_1, z_2, z_3)$ constrained to the plane $\sum z_k = 0$ 
   319‚Üíwith length $|\vec{z}|^2 = 3z_0^2$. The angle between seed and 
   320‚Üífull triples measures how much the breaking rotates the spectrum 
   321‚Üíin charge space:
   322‚Üí
   323‚Üí\begin{center}
   324‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lc}
   325‚Üí\toprule
   326‚ÜíPair of triples & Angle \\
   327‚Üí\midrule
   328‚Üí$(0,s,c)$ vs.\ $(-s,c,b)$ & $22^\circ$ \\
   329‚Üí$(0,\pi,D_s)$ vs.\ $(-\pi,D_s,B)$ & $26^\circ$ \\
   330‚Üí$(e,\mu,\tau)$ vs.\ $(0,\mu,\tau)$ & $0.8^\circ$ \\
   331‚Üí$(0,s,c)$ vs.\ $(0,\pi,D_s)$ & $0.3^\circ$ \\
   332‚Üí\bottomrule
   333‚Üí\end{tabular}
   334‚Üí\end{center}
   335‚Üí
   336‚ÜíTwo results are notable. First, the quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson 
   337‚Üíseed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ are \textbf{nearly parallel} ($0.3^\circ$): the 
   338‚Üímeson seed is the composite image of the quark seed, with the GOR 
   339‚Üímap preserving direction while changing scale. Second, the lepton 
   340‚Üítriple is essentially its own seed ($0.8^\circ$)---the bloom has 
   341‚Üíbarely begun.

   343‚Üí
   344‚Üí%======================================================================
   345‚Üí\section{The Meson Koide Triple: $(-\pi, D_s, B)$}
   346‚Üí\label{sec:mesonkoide}
   347‚Üí%======================================================================
   348‚Üí
   349‚ÜíWith the pion charge negative, the triple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ satisfies:
   350‚Üí\begin{equation}
   351‚ÜíQ(-\pi, D_s, B) = 
   352‚Üí\frac{m_\pi + m_{D_s} + m_B}{(-\sqrt{m_\pi} + \sqrt{m_{D_s}} + \sqrt{m_B})^2}
   353‚Üí= 0.6674\,,
   354‚Üí\end{equation}
   355‚Üídeviating from $2/3$ by $0.10\%$---the second most precise Koide 
   356‚Üítriple known. The energy balance:
   357‚Üí\begin{equation}
   358‚Üí\frac{\langle z_k^2\rangle}{z_0^2} = 1.002\,.
   359‚Üí\end{equation}
   360‚Üí
   361‚ÜíFor comparison, the ``standard'' all-positive quark triples $(d,s,b)$ 
   362‚Üíand $(u,c,t)$ deviate from $Q = 2/3$ by 9.7\% and 27\% respectively 
   363‚Üíat $\overline{\text{MS}}$ masses. \textbf{The meson triple is more 
   364‚Üíprecise than any quark triple} except $(c,b,t)$.
   365‚Üí
   366‚ÜíThe pion's negative charge has physical meaning: as a pseudo-Goldstone 
   367‚Üíboson, the pion would be massless ($\zch = 0$) if chiral symmetry were 
   368‚Üíexact. It sits just below zero ($\zch_\pi = -11.81$), approaching zero 
   369‚Üífrom the negative side as $m_q \to 0$. The sign of $\zch$ encodes 
   370‚ÜíGoldstone nature: pseudo-Goldstone bosons sit on the negative side 
   371‚Üíof the critical point, having crossed through zero from positive 
   372‚Üíterritory during chiral symmetry breaking.
   373‚Üí
   374‚Üí%======================================================================
   375‚Üí\section{The Third Tuple: $(c, b, t)$}
   376‚Üí\label{sec:cbt}
   377‚Üí%======================================================================
   378‚Üí
   379‚Üí\subsection{A tuple without mesons}
   380‚Üí
   381‚ÜíThe triple $(c, b, t)$ with all positive signs gives $Q = 0.6695$, 
   382‚Üídeviating by $0.42\%$. Its vacuum energy $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ 
   383‚Üíis far above the QCD scale.
   384‚Üí
   385‚ÜíThis triple has \textbf{no meson counterpart}: the top quark decays 
   386‚Üíweakly ($t \to Wb$, lifetime $\sim 5 \times 10^{-25}$~s) before the 
   387‚Üístrong interaction can bind it ($\tau_{\text{QCD}} \sim 1/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} 
   388‚Üí\sim 10^{-24}$~s). No $T$-meson ($t\bar{q}$) exists. The meson matrix 
   389‚Üí$M^i{}_j$ extends only to $b$-flavored states; the top sector lives 
   390‚Üípurely at the quark level.
   391‚Üí
   392‚Üí\subsection{Diquarks and the top}
   393‚Üí
   394‚ÜíIn the sBootstrap, diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ are the scalar partners of 
   395‚Üíantiquarks $\bar{q}_k$, with the baryon acting as the SUSY generator 
   396‚Üí\cite{CattoGursey, Miyazawa}. For five active flavors 
   397‚Üí$(u, d, s, c, b)$, the diquarks form a representation of $SU(5)_f$.
   398‚Üí
   399‚ÜíThe top diquarks $[tq]$ are kinematic objects only---they do not 
   400‚Üíhadronize. Yet their masses should follow the same pattern as other 
   401‚Üídiquarks: \textbf{there is no dynamical reason for top diquark masses 
   402‚Üíto differ from the others}. The $(c,b,t)$ Koide triple governs the 
   403‚Üíquark masses that enter the diquark sector, and its energy balance 
   404‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ constrains the possible diquark 
   405‚Üíspectrum even though the top diquarks are never observed as bound 
   406‚Üístates.
   407‚Üí
   408‚Üí\subsection{The $(c,b,t)$ triple has no seed}
   409‚Üí
   410‚ÜíUnlike the $(0,s,c)$ and $(0,\pi,D_s)$ seeds, there is no obvious 
   411‚Üí$(0, x, y)$ seed for the $(c,b,t)$ triple. No pair of quarks in this 
   412‚Üísector satisfies $\sqrt{m_{\text{light}}}/\sqrt{m_{\text{heavy}}} 
   413‚Üí\approx 2 - \sqrt{3}$: one has $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_t} = 0.086$ 
   414‚Üí(too small) and $\sqrt{m_c}/\sqrt{m_b} = 0.551$ (too large).
   415‚Üí
   416‚ÜíThis means $(c,b,t)$ is \emph{not} a ``bloomed seed'' in the same 
   417‚Üísense as $(-s,c,b)$. It may represent a fundamentally different 
   418‚Üístructure---a triple that was never seeded by a massless member but 
   419‚Üíinstead emerged fully formed at a higher breaking scale, perhaps 
   420‚Üíconnected to electroweak symmetry breaking rather than to chiral 
   421‚Üísymmetry breaking.
   422‚Üí
   423‚Üí%======================================================================
   424‚Üí\section{The Diquark Sector and SU(5) Flavor}
   425‚Üí\label{sec:diquarks}
   426‚Üí%======================================================================
   427‚Üí
   428‚Üí\subsection{Counting partners}
   429‚Üí
   430‚ÜíIn the full sBootstrap \cite{Rivero2005}, the scalar partners of 
   431‚Üíthe Standard Model fermions include both mesons ($q\bar{q}$) and 
   432‚Üídiquarks ($qq$). For five active flavors, the meson matrix gives 
   433‚Üí$5 \times 5 = 25$ scalars. The diquarks $[q_i q_j]$ form a 
   434‚Üírepresentation of $SU(5)_f$:
   435‚Üí\begin{equation}
   436‚Üí\mathbf{5} \otimes \mathbf{5} = \overline{\mathbf{10}}_A 
   437‚Üí\oplus \mathbf{15}_S\,.
   438‚Üí\end{equation}
   439‚Üí
   440‚ÜíThe symmetric $\mathbf{15}_S$ gives 15 diquarks including 
   441‚Üísame-flavor states $[uu]$, $[dd]$, \ldots, while the antisymmetric 
   442‚Üí$\overline{\mathbf{10}}_A$ gives 10 diquarks with $i \neq j$ only.
   443‚Üí
   444‚Üí\subsection{Why antisymmetric?}
   445‚Üí
   446‚ÜíFor the sBootstrap to produce the exact count of scalar partners 
   447‚Üímatching the Standard Model fermion content, the diquarks must 
   448‚Üítransform in the \textbf{antisymmetric} $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ 
   449‚Üí\cite{Rivero2005}. The argument:
   450‚Üí
   451‚ÜíThe 25 mesons account for the slepton and ``internal'' squark 
   452‚Üísectors. The remaining squarks must come from diquarks. The 
   453‚Üí$\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ provides exactly 10 diquarks: $[ud]$, 
   454‚Üí$[us]$, $[uc]$, $[ub]$, $[ds]$, $[dc]$, $[db]$, $[sc]$, $[sb]$, 
   455‚Üí$[cb]$. With their antiparticles, this gives 20 scalar states. The 
   456‚Üítotal $25 + 10 = 35$ (or $25 + 20 = 45$ counting antiparticles 
   457‚Üíseparately) matches the scalar content of the Supersymmetric 
   458‚ÜíStandard Model.
   459‚Üí
   460‚ÜíThe symmetric $\mathbf{15}$ would give 15 diquarks, overcounting 
   461‚Üíby 5 (the same-flavor states). Moreover, the exotic $+4/3$ charged 
   462‚Üídiquarks $[uu]$, $[uc]$, $[cc]$ have no lepton partner among known 
   463‚Üíparticles---a fatal obstacle.
   464‚Üí
   465‚Üí\subsection{The Pauli problem}
   466‚Üí
   467‚ÜíThe antisymmetric flavor requirement conflicts with a naive 
   468‚Üíapplication of Fermi statistics. For spin-0, $L=0$ diquarks in 
   469‚Üíthe color $\bar{3}$ (antisymmetric) channel:
   470‚Üí
   471‚Üí\begin{center}
   472‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   473‚Üí\toprule
   474‚ÜíProperty & Symmetry & Wavefunction \\
   475‚Üí\midrule
   476‚ÜíColor $\bar{3}$ & Antisymmetric & $\epsilon_{abc} q^b q^c$ \\
   477‚ÜíSpin 0 & Antisymmetric & $(\uparrow\downarrow - \downarrow\uparrow)/\sqrt{2}$ \\
   478‚ÜíSpatial $L=0$ & Symmetric & $\psi(r)$ \\
   479‚Üí\midrule
   480‚ÜíOverall & Must be A & $A \times A \times S = S$ \quad$(\neq A!)$ \\
   481‚Üí\bottomrule
   482‚Üí\end{tabular}
   483‚Üí\end{center}
   484‚Üí
   485‚ÜíThe product is symmetric---\textbf{flavor must also be symmetric} 
   486‚Üíto make the overall wavefunction antisymmetric. But the sBootstrap 
   487‚Üírequires antisymmetric flavor.
   488‚Üí
   489‚ÜíThis is one of the deepest puzzles of the program. Possible 
   490‚Üíresolutions:
   491‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   492‚Üí\item \textbf{$L = 1$ diquarks:} Adding orbital angular momentum 
   493‚Üíintroduces spatial antisymmetry. Then color(A) $\times$ spin(A) 
   494‚Üí$\times$ space(A) $\times$ flavor = overall A requires 
   495‚Üíflavor(S)---still wrong. But $L=1$ with spin-1 gives spin(S), 
   496‚Üíand then color(A) $\times$ spin(S) $\times$ space(A) $\times$ 
   497‚Üíflavor(?) = A requires flavor(A). This works but requires both 
   498‚Üí$L=1$ and spin-1 diquarks, departing from the simplest scalar 
   499‚Üídiquark picture.
   500‚Üí\item \textbf{Dynamical projection:} A binding mechanism that 
   501‚Üíeffectively projects onto the flavor-antisymmetric channel, perhaps 
   502‚Üíthrough instanton effects or a condensate in the breaking sector.
   503‚Üí\item \textbf{The SO(32) route:} In the string-theoretic embedding 
   504‚Üí\cite{Rivero2005}, the $SO(32)$ spinor representation automatically 
   505‚Üíproduces the correct antisymmetric structure. The Pauli constraint 
   506‚Üíis an artifact of treating diquarks as simple two-quark states; in 
   507‚Üíthe string picture, they are fundamental objects of the open-string 
   508‚Üísector and the symmetry is determined by the gauge group, not by 
   509‚Üíquark statistics.
   510‚Üí\end{itemize}
   511‚Üí
   512‚Üí\subsection{The SO(32) connection}
   513‚Üí
   514‚ÜíThe sBootstrap counting matches a decomposition of the $SO(32)$ gauge 
   515‚Üígroup familiar from Type~I/heterotic string theory. Under 
   516‚Üí$SO(32) \supset SU(5) \times \ldots$, the adjoint or spinor 
   517‚Üírepresentations produce exactly the right combination of mesons 
   518‚Üí(in $\mathbf{5} \otimes \bar{\mathbf{5}}$) and antisymmetric 
   519‚Üídiquarks (in $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$). The 496-dimensional adjoint 
   520‚Üíof $SO(32)$ decomposes to include the 24 of $SU(5)$ (gauge bosons), 
   521‚Üíthe $\mathbf{5} \otimes \bar{\mathbf{5}} = \mathbf{1} + \mathbf{24}$ 
   522‚Üí(mesons/sleptons), and the $\overline{\mathbf{10}} + \mathbf{10}$ 
   523‚Üí(diquarks/squarks).
   524‚Üí
   525‚ÜíThis is suggestive: the same structure that gives anomaly-free string 
   526‚Üícompactifications also gives the correct sBootstrap particle count 
   527‚Üíwith the required antisymmetric diquarks. Whether this is a 
   528‚Üícoincidence or a hint of UV completion is an open question.
   529‚Üí
   530‚Üí%======================================================================
   531‚Üí\section{SUSY Breaking: From Seeds to Spectrum}
   532‚Üí\label{sec:breaking}
   533‚Üí%======================================================================
   534‚Üí
   535‚ÜíThe SUSY-breaking story begins with the foundational observation 
   536‚Üí\eqref{eq:pimu}: $m_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx f_\pi^2$. This is the 
   537‚ÜíVolkov--Akulov mass formula for a pseudo-Goldstino---the muon---whose 
   538‚Üísuperpartner---the pion---is a pseudo-Goldstone boson. The SUSY-breaking 
   539‚Üíscale is $f_\pi \approx 92\MeV$, and the breaking mechanism is chiral: 
   540‚Üí$\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   541‚Üísimultaneously. The Volkov--Akulov realization is the \emph{physical 
   542‚Üístarting point}; what follows in this section is the SQCD machinery 
   543‚Üíthat makes it precise.
   544‚Üí
   545‚ÜíThe dynamics must accomplish three things: generate the seed-to-bloom 
   546‚Üítransition for each tuple, preserve the energy-balance condition 
   547‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ through the breaking, and produce 
   548‚Üíthe correct splitting between meson and lepton masses at scale $f_\pi$.
   549‚Üí
   550‚Üí\subsection{The Seiberg framework}
   551‚Üí
   552‚ÜíThe natural arena is $\mathcal{N}=1$ SQCD with gauge group $SU(3)_c$ 
   553‚Üíand $N_f$ flavors of chiral superfields $Q^i$, $\bar{Q}_j$ in the 
   554‚Üífundamental and antifundamental representations. For $N_f = N_c = 3$ 
   555‚Üí(the case relevant to light QCD), Seiberg's exact results 
   556‚Üí\cite{Seiberg1995} establish that the theory confines, with the 
   557‚Üílow-energy degrees of freedom being the composite meson superfield
   558‚Üí\begin{equation}
   559‚ÜíM^i{}_j = Q^i \cdot \bar{Q}_j\,,
   560‚Üí\end{equation}
   561‚Üía $3\times 3$ matrix of chiral superfields, together with baryons 
   562‚Üí$B = \epsilon_{ijk}Q^iQ^jQ^k$ and $\bar{B}$. These satisfy the 
   563‚Üíquantum-modified constraint
   564‚Üí\begin{equation}
   565‚Üí\label{eq:seiberg}
   566‚Üí\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^{2N_c}\,,
   567‚Üí\end{equation}
   568‚Üíwhere $\Lambda$ is the dynamical scale.
   569‚Üí
   570‚ÜíEach entry $M^i{}_j$ is a chiral superfield containing a complex 
   571‚Üíscalar (the meson) and a Weyl fermion (the lepton, in the sBootstrap 
   572‚Üíidentification). The scalar and fermion are degenerate in the SUSY 
   573‚Üílimit; SUSY breaking splits them.
   574‚Üí
   575‚Üí\subsection{The three chiral superfields of each seed}
   576‚Üí
   577‚ÜíThe seed triple $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ maps naturally onto three 
   578‚Üíchiral superfields with the following mass structure before breaking:
   579‚Üí
   580‚Üí\begin{center}
   581‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
   582‚Üí\toprule
   583‚ÜíSuperfield & Mass & Role \\
   584‚Üí\midrule
   585‚Üí$\Phi_0$ & $m_0 = 0$ & Massless: flat direction \\
   586‚Üí$\Phi_a$ & $m_a = \zch_a^2$ & Light massive member \\
   587‚Üí$\Phi_b$ & $m_b = \zch_b^2$ & Heavy massive member \\
   588‚Üí\bottomrule
   589‚Üí\end{tabular}
   590‚Üí\end{center}
   591‚Üí
   592‚ÜíThe ratio $\zch_a/\zch_b = 2 - \sqrt{3}$ is enforced by the 
   593‚ÜíKoide energy-balance condition applied to the seed.
   594‚Üí
   595‚ÜíIn the Seiberg description, these three superfields are entries of 
   596‚Üíthe meson matrix $M^i{}_j$. For the $(0, s, c)$ seed, the relevant 
   597‚Üíentries involve the strange and charm quarks; $\Phi_0$ is the entry 
   598‚Üíwhose quark-mass combination vanishes in the seed limit (i.e., the 
   599‚Üídirection in flavor space where $m_i + m_j \to 0$ through GOR).
   600‚Üí
   601‚Üí\subsection{F-term breaking: the O'Raifeartaigh mechanism}
   602‚Üí\label{subsec:fterm}
   603‚Üí
   604‚ÜíThe VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} tells us \emph{that} SUSY is broken at 
   605‚Üíscale $f_\pi$. To understand \emph{how}, we need the microscopic 
   606‚Üímechanism. The O'Raifeartaigh model \cite{ORaifeartaigh} is the simplest 
   607‚Üírealization of spontaneous $F$-term SUSY breaking. With three chiral 
   608‚Üísuperfields, the superpotential
   609‚Üí\begin{equation}
   610‚ÜíW = f\,\Phi_0 + m\,\Phi_1\Phi_2 + g\,\Phi_0\Phi_1^2
   611‚Üí\end{equation}
   612‚Üígives $F$-terms:
   613‚Üí\begin{align}
   614‚ÜíF_{\Phi_0}^* &= f + g\,\phi_1^2\,, \\
   615‚ÜíF_{\Phi_1}^* &= m\,\phi_2 + 2g\,\phi_0\phi_1\,, \\
   616‚ÜíF_{\Phi_2}^* &= m\,\phi_1\,.
   617‚Üí\end{align}
   618‚ÜíIf $f \neq 0$ and $m \neq 0$, then $F_{\Phi_2}^* = 0$ requires 
   619‚Üí$\phi_1 = 0$, but then $F_{\Phi_0}^* = f \neq 0$: SUSY is 
   620‚Üí\textbf{necessarily broken}. The key features are:
   621‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   622‚Üí\item A massless field ($\Phi_0$) exists at tree level---it is the 
   623‚Üípseudo-modulus.
   624‚Üí\item The breaking is triggered because $F_{\Phi_0}$ cannot vanish.
   625‚Üí\item After including loop corrections, $\Phi_0$ acquires a mass 
   626‚Üí(the Coleman--Weinberg potential lifts the flat direction).
   627‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   628‚Üí
   629‚ÜíThe structural parallel with the seed triples is:
   630‚Üí
   631‚Üí\begin{center}
   632‚Üí\begin{tabular}{llll}
   633‚Üí\toprule
   634‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh & Flavor & Seed & After breaking \\
   635‚Üí\midrule
   636‚Üí$\Phi_0$ (massless) & $b$ / $B$ & $\zch = 0$ (flat direction) & $\zch_b = 64.65$ (mass generated) \\
   637‚Üí$\Phi_1$ (light) & $s$ / $\pi$ & $\zch_s = 9.67$ & $\zch \to -9.67$ (sign-flipped) \\
   638‚Üí$\Phi_2$ (heavy) & $c$ / $D_s$ & $\zch_c = 35.64$ & $\zch_c \approx 35.64$ (shifted) \\
   639‚Üí\midrule
   640‚Üí & & $f = 0$ (SUSY) & $f = f_\pi$ (broken) \\
   641‚Üí\bottomrule
   642‚Üí\end{tabular}
   643‚Üí\end{center}
   644‚Üí
   645‚ÜíThis parallel is \emph{structural}, not quantitative: the minimal 
   646‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh model gives $\Phi_1$ and $\Phi_2$ the same mass 
   647‚Üíparameter $m$, whereas the seed triple has a mass ratio 
   648‚Üí$m_s/m_c = (2 - \sqrt{3})^2 \approx 0.07$---far from degenerate. 
   649‚ÜíThe hierarchy between the two massive members must come from 
   650‚Üíadditional dynamics beyond the minimal O'Raifeartaigh superpotential. 
   651‚ÜíWhat the O'Raifeartaigh structure \emph{does} provide is the essential 
   652‚Üífeature: a massless flat direction ($\Phi_0 = $ the $b$-direction) 
   653‚Üíwhose $F$-term is forced nonzero. The $b$ quark and $B$ meson are 
   654‚Üíalways present as flavor degrees of freedom; the seed configuration 
   655‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ means the $b$-direction sits at the flat direction before 
   656‚Üíbreaking. The bloom is not particle creation but mass generation: 
   657‚Üíbreaking lifts $\Phi_0$ from zero to $\zch_b \neq 0$, simultaneously 
   658‚Üíflipping the sign of $\zch_s$, so that the same three flavors 
   659‚Üírearrange from $(0, s, c)$ into $(-s, c, b)$.
   660‚Üí
   661‚ÜíThe identification $f = \langle F_{\Phi_0}\rangle \sim f_\pi$ is the 
   662‚ÜísBootstrap's central claim: the SUSY-breaking $F$-term is the chiral 
   663‚Üícondensate parameter.
   664‚Üí
   665‚ÜíIn SQCD, this is realized dynamically rather than through a 
   666‚Üísuperpotential: the chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ acts as the 
   667‚Üínonzero $F$-term, the impossibility of restoring chiral symmetry 
   668‚Üíin the QCD vacuum is the dynamical analogue of $f \neq 0$, and the 
   669‚Üípion---the would-be Goldstone boson that acquires mass from explicit 
   670‚Üíchiral breaking---plays the role of $\Phi_0$ whose mass is lifted 
   671‚Üíby loop corrections (here, by quark masses).
   672‚Üí
   673‚Üí\subsection{The Goldstino direction and the Volkov--Akulov realization}
   674‚Üí\label{subsec:va}
   675‚Üí
   676‚ÜíWhen SUSY is spontaneously broken, a massless fermion appears: the 
   677‚ÜíGoldstino, the fermionic analogue of the Goldstone boson. Its decay 
   678‚Üíconstant $f_{\text{VA}}$ sets the SUSY-breaking scale through the 
   679‚ÜíVolkov--Akulov Lagrangian \cite{VolkovAkulov1973}:
   680‚Üí\begin{equation}
   681‚Üí\mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}} = -f_{\text{VA}}^2 \det\!\left(
   682‚Üí\delta_\mu^\nu + \frac{i}{f_{\text{VA}}^2}\,\bar{\lambda}\bar{\sigma}^\nu
   683‚Üí\partial_\mu\lambda\right),
   684‚Üí\end{equation}
   685‚Üíwhere $\lambda$ is the Goldstino field. At low energies, the Goldstino 
   686‚Üícouples derivatively with strength $\sim 1/f_{\text{VA}}^2$, exactly 
   687‚Üías the pion couples with strength $\sim 1/f_\pi^2$ in the chiral 
   688‚ÜíLagrangian.
   689‚Üí
   690‚ÜíThe sBootstrap identification is:
   691‚Üí\begin{equation}
   692‚Üí\label{eq:va_id}
   693‚Üíf_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi\,, \qquad 
   694‚Üí\text{Goldstino} = \text{muon (partner of }\pi\text{)}\,.
   695‚Üí\end{equation}
   696‚ÜíMore precisely, the muon is a \emph{pseudo-Goldstino}: in the 
   697‚Üílimit $m_q \to 0$ where the pion becomes exactly massless (a true 
   698‚ÜíGoldstone boson), its fermionic partner also becomes massless (a 
   699‚Üítrue Goldstino). The finite quark masses explicitly break both 
   700‚Üíchiral symmetry (giving $m_\pi \neq 0$) and the sBootstrap SUSY 
   701‚Üí(giving $m_\mu \neq m_\pi$), with the splitting controlled by 
   702‚Üí$f_\pi$:
   703‚Üí\begin{equation}
   704‚Üím_\pi^2 - m_\mu^2 = f_\pi^2\,.
   705‚Üí\end{equation}
   706‚Üí
   707‚ÜíThis is the Volkov--Akulov relation for the pseudo-Goldstino: the 
   708‚Üímass splitting between the scalar (pion) and its fermionic partner 
   709‚Üí(muon) is set by the SUSY-breaking scale squared.
   710‚Üí
   711‚ÜíThe VA mechanism is not a separate breaking from the F-term: it is 
   712‚Üíthe \emph{low-energy, composite-level description} of the same 
   713‚Üíbreaking. At the microscopic level, $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ 
   714‚Üíin the meson superfield; at the composite level, this manifests 
   715‚Üías the Goldstino coupling with $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$. These 
   716‚Üítwo quantities---the Goldstino decay constant and the pion decay 
   717‚Üíconstant---are \emph{identified} in the sBootstrap; this is the 
   718‚Üícentral claim, not a derivation.
   719‚Üí
   720‚ÜíThe identification should not be confused with the standard relation 
   721‚Üíbetween the $F$-term VEV and the chiral condensate. In standard 
   722‚ÜíSQCD, $\langle F\rangle \sim |\qqbar|/f_\pi \sim (250\MeV)^3/92\MeV 
   723‚Üí\approx 170{,}000\MeV^2$, while $f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2$---a 
   724‚Üífactor of $\sim 20$ discrepancy. The GOR relation 
   725‚Üí$m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d)\qqbar$ connects these through the 
   726‚Üísmall quark masses, but does not equate them. The sBootstrap 
   727‚Üíidentification $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is therefore a 
   728‚Üí\emph{low-energy effective} statement about the composite sector, 
   729‚Üínot a microscopic identity. It says: the scale at which SUSY 
   730‚Üíappears broken in the meson--lepton spectrum is $f_\pi$, regardless 
   731‚Üíof the microscopic $F$-term that produces this breaking.
   732‚Üí
   733‚ÜíThe VA realization gives more than a mass formula---it organizes the 
   734‚Üíentire breaking narrative. In the chiral limit ($m_q \to 0$), both 
   735‚Üípion and muon are exactly massless: the pion as the Goldstone boson 
   736‚Üíof broken chiral symmetry, the muon as the Goldstino of broken 
   737‚ÜísBootstrap SUSY. This is the \emph{exact seed}: the $B$-meson 
   738‚Üídirection sits at the flat direction ($\zch = 0$), and the 
   739‚Üí$\pi$--$\mu$ pair is massless. Turning on quark masses ($m_q \neq 0$) 
   740‚Üídoes three things simultaneously:
   741‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   742‚Üí\item Gives the pion its GOR mass (lifting the flat direction for $\pi$).
   743‚Üí\item Gives the muon its mass (the pseudo-Goldstino acquires mass 
   744‚Üíwhen the explicit breaking parameter $m_q$ is nonzero).
   745‚Üí\item Generates the $B$-meson mass (the flat direction for $\Phi_0$ 
   746‚Üíis lifted by the Coleman--Weinberg potential, i.e., by the same 
   747‚Üíquark masses that feed the GOR formula).
   748‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   749‚ÜíThe splitting between (1) and (2) is $f_\pi^2$---the VA relation. 
   750‚ÜíThe seed-to-bloom transition of \S\ref{sec:two_tuples} is the same 
   751‚Üíphysics seen from the charge-space perspective: $m_q \neq 0$ lifts 
   752‚Üíthe zero, generates the $b$/$B$ mass, flips the sign of $\zch_s$ 
   753‚Üíand $\zch_\pi$, and produces the full triple. The VA equation 
   754‚Üí\eqref{eq:pimu} is not one fact among many; it is the generating 
   755‚Üírelation from which the bloom follows.
   756‚Üí
   757‚ÜíA note on quantum numbers: the Goldstino of spontaneously broken 
   758‚Üíspacetime SUSY is a neutral fermion (the fermionic component of the 
   759‚Üísuperfield whose $F$-term breaks SUSY). The muon carries electric 
   760‚Üícharge and lepton number. The sBootstrap SUSY, however, is not a 
   761‚Üíspacetime symmetry but an emergent hadronic symmetry---a 
   762‚ÜíMiyazawa-type superalgebra \cite{Miyazawa} acting on composite 
   763‚Üístates. In this context, the ``Goldstino'' need not carry the 
   764‚Üíquantum numbers of a spacetime supercharge; it carries the quantum 
   765‚Üínumbers appropriate to the broken hadronic generator. The muon's 
   766‚Üícharge is inherited from the pion multiplet it partners.
   767‚Üí
   768‚Üí\subsection{D-term breaking: electromagnetic splittings}
   769‚Üí\label{subsec:dterm}
   770‚Üí
   771‚ÜíThe unbroken $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ gauge symmetry allows a 
   772‚ÜíFayet--Iliopoulos (FI) term \cite{FayetIliopoulos}:
   773‚Üí\begin{equation}
   774‚Üí\mathcal{L}_{\text{FI}} = \xi\,D_{\text{em}}\,,
   775‚Üí\end{equation}
   776‚Üíwhich shifts scalar masses by $\delta m^2 = Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi$, 
   777‚Üíwhere $Q_{\text{em}}$ is the electric charge. This is the 
   778‚Üísupersymmetric version of the electromagnetic mass difference.
   779‚Üí
   780‚ÜíIn QCD without SUSY, the electromagnetic mass splitting of the 
   781‚Üípion is given by the Das--Guralnik--Mathur--Low--Young (DGMLY) 
   782‚Üíformula \cite{DGMLY1967}:
   783‚Üí\begin{equation}
   784‚Üím_{\pi^\pm}^2 - m_{\pi^0}^2 = \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,
   785‚Üí\frac{m_\rho^2}{f_\pi^2}\,f_\pi^2 
   786‚Üí\approx (35.5\MeV)^2\,,
   787‚Üí\end{equation}
   788‚Üíwhich in the SUSY framework becomes a D-term contribution:
   789‚Üí\begin{equation}
   790‚Üí\xi \sim \frac{3\alpha_{\text{em}}}{4\pi}\,m_\rho^2 
   791‚Üí\sim (35\MeV)^2\,.
   792‚Üí\end{equation}
   793‚Üí
   794‚ÜíIn charge space, the D-term shifts $\zch$ by an amount proportional 
   795‚Üíto $Q_{\text{em}}$:
   796‚Üí\begin{equation}
   797‚Üí\zch \to \zch + \delta\zch\,, \qquad 
   798‚Üí\delta\zch \approx \frac{Q_{\text{em}}\,\xi}{2\zch}\,.
   799‚Üí\end{equation}
   800‚ÜíFor the pion ($Q_{\text{em}} = \pm 1$, $\zch \approx 11.8$): 
   801‚Üí$\delta\zch \approx 0.2\MeV^{1/2}$, matching the observed 
   802‚Üí$\zch_{\pi^\pm} - \zch_{\pi^0} = 11.82 - 11.62 = 0.20\MeV^{1/2}$.
   803‚Üí
   804‚ÜíThe D-term effect is subleading to the F-term: 
   805‚Üí$\xi \sim (35\MeV)^2$ versus $f_\pi^2 \sim (92\MeV)^2$. It 
   806‚Üíoperates \emph{within} each supermultiplet entry, splitting charged 
   807‚Üífrom neutral mesons, while the F-term splits bosons from fermions 
   808‚Üíacross the supermultiplet.
   809‚Üí
   810‚ÜíWitten's inequality \cite{Witten1983} guarantees 
   811‚Üí$m_{\pi^\pm} > m_{\pi^0}$ in QCD with electromagnetism---the 
   812‚Üícharged pion is always heavier. In the SUSY framework this 
   813‚Üíbecomes the statement that the D-term contribution is positive 
   814‚Üífor $Q_{\text{em}} \neq 0$, consistent with the FI sign.
   815‚Üí
   816‚Üí\subsection{Mapping the breaking to the tuples}
   817‚Üí\label{subsec:mapping}
   818‚Üí
   819‚ÜíThe tuple structure maps onto a layered breaking with two independent 
   820‚Üímechanisms (F-term and D-term) plus a separate heavy-sector origin. 
   821‚ÜíThe Volkov--Akulov realization of \S\ref{subsec:va} is not a third 
   822‚Üímechanism: it is the low-energy, composite-level description of the 
   823‚Üísame F-term breaking. The VA relation \eqref{eq:pimu} \emph{sets the 
   824‚Üíscale} ($f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$); the O'Raifeartaigh/SQCD dynamics 
   825‚Üíof \S\ref{subsec:fterm} \emph{provides the mechanism} 
   826‚Üí($\langle F\rangle \neq 0$).
   827‚Üí
   828‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 1: F-term/VA (chiral condensate, scale $\sim f_\pi$).}
   829‚ÜíThe chiral condensate $\qqbar \neq 0$ breaks both chiral symmetry 
   830‚Üíand the sBootstrap SUSY. This is the primary breaking, affecting 
   831‚Üíthe light sector. The seed triples $(0, s, c)$ and $(0, \pi, D_s)$ 
   832‚Üíare the pre-breaking configurations; the massless member is the flat 
   833‚Üídirection whose $F$-term is forced nonzero by confinement. After 
   834‚Üíbreaking:
   835‚Üí\begin{itemize}
   836‚Üí\item The zero member is lifted and acquires negative charge 
   837‚Üí($\zch < 0$), becoming the sign-flipped member of the full triple 
   838‚Üí($-s$, $-\pi$).
   839‚Üí\item The full triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ emerge, 
   840‚Üíincorporating the $b$-quark and $B$-meson sectors.
   841‚Üí\item The lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the fermionic shadow: 
   842‚Üíthe leptons acquire masses through the same condensate, with the 
   843‚Üímuon as pseudo-Goldstino.
   844‚Üí\end{itemize}
   845‚Üí
   846‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 2: D-term (electromagnetism, scale $\sim \alpha_{\text{em}} m_\rho$).}
   847‚ÜíThe $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI term generates electromagnetic mass 
   848‚Üísplittings within each multiplet. This is a perturbative correction 
   849‚Üíon top of the F-term breaking, affecting only charged particles. 
   850‚ÜíIt does not create new Koide triples but shifts existing ones by 
   851‚Üí$O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$.
   852‚Üí
   853‚Üí\paragraph{Layer 3: The heavy sector (scale $\sim v_0^{(cbt)}$).}
   854‚ÜíThe $(c,b,t)$ triple at $v_0^2 \approx 29.6\GeV$ operates far 
   855‚Üíabove the chiral scale. It has no seed (no massless member), no 
   856‚Üímeson shadow (top does not hadronize), and its breaking may be 
   857‚Üíconnected to a different sector of the theory---possibly the 
   858‚Üíelectroweak Higgs mechanism.
   859‚Üí
   860‚ÜíThis layered structure involves three distinct scales, each with 
   861‚Üídifferent physical meaning:
   862‚Üí\begin{align}
   863‚Üí\text{F-term (boson--fermion splitting):} &\quad 
   864‚Üíf_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \quad f_\pi^2 \approx 8{,}500\MeV^2\,; \\
   865‚Üí\text{D-term (EM fine structure):} &\quad 
   866‚Üí\xi^{1/2} \approx 35\MeV\,, \quad \xi \approx 1{,}200\MeV^2\,; \\
   867‚Üí\text{Heavy sector (Koide scale):} &\quad 
   868‚Üív_0^{(cbt)} \approx 172\MeV^{1/2}\,, \quad (v_0^{(cbt)})^2 \approx 29{,}600\MeV\,.
   869‚Üí\end{align}
   870‚ÜíComparing the squared scales: $f_\pi^2 \gg \xi$ (F-term dominates 
   871‚Üíover D-term by a factor $\sim 7$, consistent with the D-term being 
   872‚Üía perturbative $O(\alpha_{\text{em}})$ correction). The heavy-sector 
   873‚Üíscale $(v_0^{(cbt)})^2$ carries dimensions of $\MeV$ (not $\MeV^2$) 
   874‚Üíbecause $v_0$ is a charge ($\MeV^{1/2}$), not an energy; direct 
   875‚Üícomparison with $f_\pi$ requires specifying which physical quantity 
   876‚Üíis being compared.
   877‚Üí
   878‚Üí\subsection{The O'Raifeartaigh structure in SQCD}
   879‚Üí
   880‚ÜíIn Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, the quantum constraint 
   881‚Üí\eqref{eq:seiberg} provides a natural setting for the 
   882‚ÜíO'Raifeartaigh mechanism. The constraint $\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ 
   883‚Üímeans the moduli space has a specific geometry: not all field directions 
   884‚Üíare flat. When quark masses are added via a tree-level superpotential 
   885‚Üí$W_{\text{tree}} = m_i M^i{}_i$ (sum over flavors), the $F$-terms become:
   886‚Üí\begin{equation}
   887‚ÜíF_{M^i{}_j} = m_i\,\delta^i_j + \Lambda^6\,(\text{constraint terms})\,.
   888‚Üí\end{equation}
   889‚Üí
   890‚ÜíFor the seed triple $(0, s, c)$, the ``$0$'' member corresponds to a 
   891‚Üídirection in the meson matrix where the tree-level mass vanishes---the 
   892‚Üíentry involving the lightest quark combination. In the exact chiral 
   893‚Üílimit ($m_u = m_d = 0$), this entry is truly massless, and the 
   894‚Üíconstraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} forces $\langle F\rangle \neq 0$ for 
   895‚Üíthat entry. This is the dynamical O'Raifeartaigh mechanism: 
   896‚Üíconfinement (encoded in $\Lambda^6$) plays the role of the parameter 
   897‚Üí$f$ in the superpotential, and the impossibility of setting all 
   898‚Üí$F$-terms to zero is a consequence of the strong dynamics rather 
   899‚Üíthan a choice of superpotential.
   900‚Üí
   901‚ÜíThe ISS mechanism (Intriligator--Seiberg--Shih \cite{ISS2006}) 
   902‚Üíestablishes metastable SUSY-breaking vacua for $N_c < N_f < 3N_c/2$, 
   903‚Üíwhere the rank condition in the magnetic dual prevents all $F$-terms 
   904‚Üífrom vanishing. The sBootstrap case $N_f = N_c = 3$ is strictly 
   905‚Üí\emph{below} the ISS window---at $N_f = N_c$ the low-energy 
   906‚Üídescription is qualitatively different, governed by the 
   907‚Üíquantum-modified constraint \eqref{eq:seiberg} on the moduli space 
   908‚Üírather than by a free magnetic dual. However, the physical QCD has 
   909‚Üí$N_f = 3$ light flavors and $N_f = 4$--$5$ if charm and bottom are 
   910‚Üíincluded at their respective thresholds, which does fall within the 
   911‚ÜíISS window for $N_c = 3$. The sBootstrap's multi-scale structure---with 
   912‚Üíthe seed triples involving $s$, $c$, and $b$ quarks at different 
   913‚Üíscales---may require treating the effective $N_f$ as 
   914‚Üíscale-dependent, entering the ISS regime above the strange threshold.
   915‚Üí
   916‚Üí\subsection{How the three supermultiplets join}
   917‚Üí
   918‚ÜíThe seed-to-bloom transition generates three separate Koide triples. 
   919‚ÜíBut the full sBootstrap requires these triples to be \emph{embedded 
   920‚Üíin a single meson matrix}. The $3\times 3$ light-flavor matrix is:
   921‚Üí
   922‚Üí\begin{equation}
   923‚ÜíM^{(3)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   924‚Üíu\bar{u} & u\bar{d} & u\bar{s} \\
   925‚Üíd\bar{u} & d\bar{d} & d\bar{s} \\
   926‚Üís\bar{u} & s\bar{d} & s\bar{s}
   927‚Üí\end{pmatrix}
   928‚Üí\;\longleftrightarrow\;
   929‚Üí\begin{pmatrix}
   930‚Üí\pi^0/\eta & \pi^+ & K^+ \\
   931‚Üí\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 \\
   932‚ÜíK^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta'
   933‚Üí\end{pmatrix}.
   934‚Üí\end{equation}
   935‚Üí
   936‚ÜíWhen charm and bottom are included, the matrix extends to $5\times 5$. 
   937‚ÜíWe display it schematically, with the members of the meson Koide 
   938‚Üítriple $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ marked by boxes:
   939‚Üí
   940‚Üí\begin{equation}
   941‚ÜíM^{(5)} = \begin{pmatrix}
   942‚Üí\pi^0/\eta & \boxed{\pi^+} & K^+ & D^0 & \boxed{B^+} \\
   943‚Üí\pi^- & \pi^0/\eta & K^0 & D^- & B^0 \\
   944‚ÜíK^- & \bar{K}^0 & \eta' & \boxed{D_s^-} & B_s \\
   945‚Üí\bar{D}^0 & D^+ & D_s^+ & \eta_c & B_c \\
   946‚ÜíB^- & \bar{B}^0 & \bar{B}_s & \bar{B}_c & \eta_b
   947‚Üí\end{pmatrix},
   948‚Üí\end{equation}
   949‚Üíwhere rows are indexed by $(u, d, s, c, b)$ and columns by 
   950‚Üí$(\bar{u}, \bar{d}, \bar{s}, \bar{c}, \bar{b})$.
   951‚Üí
   952‚ÜíThe three members of the meson Koide triple occupy different 
   953‚Üíoff-diagonal blocks of this matrix: $\pi^+ = u\bar{d}$ is in the 
   954‚Üílight $(u,d)$ block, $D_s^- = s\bar{c}$ bridges the light-charm 
   955‚Üísector, and $B^+ = u\bar{b}$ bridges the light-bottom sector. They 
   956‚Üído not form a ``slice'' in any simple linear-algebraic sense---they 
   957‚Üíare scattered entries connected by the Koide condition on their 
   958‚Üí\emph{masses}, not by their positions in the matrix. The meson seed 
   959‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ drops the $B^+$ and replaces it with zero (the 
   960‚Üíentry that would involve the top quark, absent from the matrix).
   961‚Üí
   962‚ÜíThis scattered structure is the central puzzle: the Koide triples 
   963‚Üíare \emph{mass relations} among matrix entries, not submatrices. 
   964‚ÜíThe question is whether the Seiberg constraint 
   965‚Üí$\det M - B\bar{B} = \Lambda^6$ (appropriately generalized to 
   966‚Üí$5 \times 5$), combined with the SUSY-breaking dynamics, produces 
   967‚Üícorrelations among these scattered entries that enforce the 
   968‚Üíenergy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$.
   969‚Üí
   970‚Üí\subsection{The energy balance as a fixed point}
   971‚Üí
   972‚ÜíOne possibility is that the energy-balance condition 
   973‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is a \emph{dynamical attractor}: 
   974‚Üíany set of three masses evolving under the SUSY-breaking dynamics 
   975‚Üíflows toward the Koide surface $Q = 2/3$, as a fixed point of the 
   976‚ÜíRG flow for the soft SUSY-breaking parameters. This is speculative, 
   977‚Üíbut the observation that the condition holds for fundamental fermions 
   978‚Üí(leptons, quarks) \emph{and} composite bosons (mesons) across very 
   979‚Üídifferent scales lends it plausibility. A concrete test would be to 
   980‚Üíshow that the one-loop soft-mass RG equations in $\mathcal{N}=1$ 
   981‚ÜíSQCD with appropriate boundary conditions flow toward 
   982‚Üí$\sigma(\zch)/\bar{\zch} = 1$; this calculation has not yet been 
   983‚Üíperformed.
   984‚Üí
   985‚Üí\subsection{The exact seed and what breaks it}
   986‚Üí\label{subsec:exactseed}
   987‚Üí
   988‚ÜíThe seed configuration $(0, \zch_a, \zch_b)$ requires \emph{two} 
   989‚Üíindependent conditions:
   990‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
   991‚Üí\item One member at $\zch = 0$ exactly (the flat direction).
   992‚Üí\item The ratio of the massive members equals $2 - \sqrt{3}$:
   993‚Üí\begin{equation}
   994‚Üí\label{eq:seedratio}
   995‚Üí\frac{\sqrt{m_a}}{\sqrt{m_b}} = 2 - \sqrt{3} \approx 0.26795\,.
   996‚Üí\end{equation}
   997‚Üí\end{enumerate}
   998‚ÜíThese are logically distinct. The chiral limit ($m_u = m_d = 0$, 
   999‚Üí$\qqbar \neq 0$) enforces condition (1) for the meson sector: the 
  1000‚Üípion is an exact Goldstone boson, $m_\pi = 0$, and in the 
  1001‚Üípicture of \S\ref{subsec:fterm} the $B$-meson direction sits at 
  1002‚Üíthe flat direction before acquiring mass. But the chiral limit says 
  1003‚Üí\emph{nothing} about condition (2)---it does not constrain 
  1004‚Üí$\sqrt{m_{D_s}}/\sqrt{m_B}$ or $\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ 
  1005‚Üíto equal $2 - \sqrt{3}$.
  1006‚Üí
  1007‚ÜíThe physical deviations from the exact ratio are small but 
  1008‚Üímeasurable:
  1009‚Üí
  1010‚Üí\begin{center}
  1011‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccc}
  1012‚Üí\toprule
  1013‚ÜíSeed & Exact ratio & Physical ratio & Deviation \\
  1014‚Üí\midrule
  1015‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1016‚Üí$\sqrt{m_\pi}/\sqrt{m_{D_s}} = 0.2663$ & $-0.6\%$ \\
  1017‚Üí$(0, s, c)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1018‚Üí$\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c} = 0.2712$ & $+1.2\%$ \\
  1019‚Üí$(0 \approx e, \mu, \tau)$ & 0.2680 & 
  1020‚Üí$\sqrt{m_\mu}/\sqrt{m_\tau} = 0.2439$ & $-9.0\%$ \\
  1021‚Üí\bottomrule
  1022‚Üí\end{tabular}
  1023‚Üí\end{center}
  1024‚Üí
  1025‚ÜíThe meson seed is closest to the exact ratio (0.6\% deviation). 
  1026‚ÜíThis is expected: the meson masses are the most ``infrared'' 
  1027‚Üíquantities, closest to the chiral dynamics that creates the seed. 
  1028‚ÜíThe quark seed deviates more (1.2\%) because the quark masses 
  1029‚Üí$m_s$ and $m_c$ are $\overline{\text{MS}}$ running masses, 
  1030‚Üíevaluated at $\mu = 2\GeV$. As the renormalization scale $\mu$ 
  1031‚Üívaries, the ratio $\sqrt{m_s(\mu)}/\sqrt{m_c(\mu)}$ changes 
  1032‚Üíbecause $m_s$ and $m_c$ run at different rates (different anomalous 
  1033‚Üídimensions in QCD). This raises a natural question:
  1034‚Üí
  1035‚Üí\emph{Does there exist a scale $\mu^*$ at which the ratio 
  1036‚Üí$\sqrt{m_s(\mu^*)}/\sqrt{m_c(\mu^*)}$ equals $2 - \sqrt{3}$ 
  1037‚Üíexactly?}
  1038‚Üí
  1039‚ÜíIf so, $\mu^*$ is the ``seed scale''---the energy at which the 
  1040‚Üíexact seed configuration is realized. Since $m_s$ runs faster 
  1041‚Üíthan $m_c$ (the anomalous dimension is larger for the lighter 
  1042‚Üíquark), the ratio $\sqrt{m_s}/\sqrt{m_c}$ decreases with 
  1043‚Üíincreasing $\mu$. At $\mu = 2\GeV$ the ratio is 0.2712 (above 
  1044‚Üíthe target 0.2680); it will reach 0.2680 at some 
  1045‚Üí$\mu^* > 2\GeV$, likely in the range 3--5 GeV. This is a 
  1046‚Üíconcrete, testable prediction.
  1047‚Üí
  1048‚ÜíThe lepton ``seed'' has the worst ratio match ($-9\%$), which 
  1049‚Üíis consistent with the observation that the lepton triple is 
  1050‚Üí\emph{not} a true seed: $m_e \neq 0$, and the electron was never 
  1051‚Üíat the flat direction. The leptons are an ``almost-seed'' whose 
  1052‚Üíexcellent $Q = 0.66666$ arises not from the seed structure but 
  1053‚Üífrom the smallness of $m_e$, which makes the two conditions 
  1054‚Üíapproximately degenerate. For the leptons, condition (1) is 
  1055‚Üíapproximate rather than exact, and condition (2) is correspondingly 
  1056‚Üírelaxed.
  1057‚Üí
  1058‚ÜíThe physical origin of the deviations from condition (2) is 
  1059‚ÜíQCD dynamics: running of quark masses, higher-order chiral 
  1060‚Üíperturbation theory corrections to meson masses, and 
  1061‚Üínonperturbative effects. In principle, these are calculable. 
  1062‚ÜíA next-to-leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculation 
  1063‚Üíof $m_{D_s}$ with the Koide constraint could determine 
  1064‚Üíwhether the 0.6\% meson deviation is consistent with known 
  1065‚Üíchiral corrections, or whether it requires new physics.
  1066‚Üí
  1067‚Üí\subsection{What the $(c,b,t)$ triple tells us}
  1068‚Üí
  1069‚ÜíThe $(c,b,t)$ triple ($Q = 0.6695$, $v_0^2 = 29.6\GeV$) has 
  1070‚Üíneither a seed nor a meson shadow. It operates at a scale far 
  1071‚Üíabove $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, suggesting its origin is different:
  1072‚Üí
  1073‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
  1074‚Üí\item It may be governed by the electroweak Higgs mechanism rather 
  1075‚Üíthan the chiral condensate. The top Yukawa $y_t \approx 1$ is 
  1076‚Üí``natural'' in the sense that $m_t \approx v_{\text{EW}}/\sqrt{2}$; 
  1077‚Üíthe Koide condition on $(c,b,t)$ would then constrain the Yukawa 
  1078‚Üícoupling matrix.
  1079‚Üí\item It may represent a Volkov--Akulov realization at the 
  1080‚Üíelectroweak scale: if there is a second SUSY breaking at 
  1081‚Üí$f' \sim v_{\text{EW}}$, the $(c,b,t)$ triple would be its 
  1082‚Üíanalog of the $(\pi, \mu)$ system, but with $f'$ replacing $f_\pi$.
  1083‚Üí\item Or it may be a purely kinematic accident---the quark masses 
  1084‚Üíhappen to satisfy the Koide condition without a dynamical reason. 
  1085‚ÜíThe 0.42\% precision makes this unlikely but not impossible.
  1086‚Üí\end{enumerate}
  1087‚Üí
  1088‚ÜíInterpretation (1) is most natural within the sBootstrap: the 
  1089‚Üí$(c,b,t)$ triple involves quarks whose masses are dominated by 
  1090‚ÜíYukawa couplings to the Higgs, not by the chiral condensate, and the 
  1091‚ÜíKoide condition would then be a constraint on the Yukawa matrix 
  1092‚Üírather than on the SQCD dynamics. The chiral sector and the 
  1093‚Üíelectroweak sector would each independently produce a Koide-type 
  1094‚Üíenergy balance, with the charm and bottom quarks serving as the 
  1095‚Üíbridge between the two.
  1096‚Üí
  1097‚ÜíIf interpretation (2) is also invoked, the sBootstrap has two breaking 
  1098‚Üíscales:
  1099‚Üí\begin{equation}
  1100‚Üíf_{\text{chiral}} = f_\pi \approx 92\MeV\,, \qquad
  1101‚Üíf_{\text{EW}} \sim v_{\text{EW}} \approx 246\GeV\,,
  1102‚Üí\end{equation}
  1103‚Üíand the full spectrum requires both. The light sector (leptons, 
  1104‚Üílight mesons) is governed by $f_\pi$; the heavy sector (top, and 
  1105‚Üíby extension charm and bottom through the Koide network) is 
  1106‚Üígoverned by $v_{\text{EW}}$. The charm and bottom quarks, 
  1107‚Üíappearing in both the $(0,s,c)/(-s,c,b)$ light-sector triples 
  1108‚Üíand the $(c,b,t)$ heavy-sector triple, are the ``bridges'' 
  1109‚Üíbetween the two scales.

  1113‚Üí\label{sec:electron}
  1114‚Üí%======================================================================
  1115‚Üí
  1116‚ÜíThe charged lepton triple $(e, \mu, \tau)$ is the ``almost-seed'': 
  1117‚Üí$\zch_e = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}$ is small but nonzero. The electron's 
  1118‚Üícharge is near zero because of cancellation in $\zch_e = z_0 + z_e$:
  1119‚Üí\begin{equation}
  1120‚Üív_0 = 17.716, \quad z_e = -17.001, \quad 
  1121‚Üí\zch_e = 17.716 - 17.001 = 0.715\MeV^{1/2}\,.
  1122‚Üí\end{equation}
  1123‚ÜíA 96\% cancellation. The mass $m_e = 0.715^2 = 0.511\MeV$ is the 
  1124‚Üísquare of something already small.
  1125‚Üí
  1126‚ÜíThe near-vanishing of $m_e$ is a structural property of the Koide 
  1127‚Üíembedding, not fine-tuning: the energy balance allows perturbations 
  1128‚Üías large as $z_0$. Every Koide triple has one member with 
  1129‚Üí$|\zch|/v_0$ small:
  1130‚Üí
  1131‚Üí\begin{center}
  1132‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lccl}
  1133‚Üí\toprule
  1134‚ÜíTriple & Lightest & $|\zch|/v_0$ & Character \\
  1135‚Üí\midrule
  1136‚Üí$(e, \mu, \tau)$ & $e$ & 0.040 & Almost-seed \\
  1137‚Üí$(-\pi, D_s, B)$ & $\pi$ (neg.) & 0.337 & Full triple \\
  1138‚Üí$(c, b, t)$ & $c$ & 0.236 & Full triple \\
  1139‚Üí$(-s, c, b)$ & $s$ (neg.) & 0.320 & Full triple \\
  1140‚Üí\bottomrule
  1141‚Üí\end{tabular}
  1142‚Üí\end{center}
  1143‚Üí
  1144‚ÜíThe electron is by far the most extreme case, consistent with the 
  1145‚Üílepton triple being nearest to its seed.
  1146‚Üí
  1147‚Üí%======================================================================
  1148‚Üí\section{Symmetry Restoration}
  1149‚Üí\label{sec:restoration}
  1150‚Üí%======================================================================
  1151‚Üí
  1152‚ÜíThe broken spectrum reassembles as symmetry-breaking effects are 
  1153‚Üíremoved:
  1154‚Üí
  1155‚Üí\begin{enumerate}
  1156‚Üí\item \textbf{Turn off QED:} Electromagnetic splittings vanish 
  1157‚Üí($\pi^\pm = \pi^0$, $K^\pm = K^0$). D-term effects disappear.
  1158‚Üí\item \textbf{Set $m_u = m_d$:} Exact isospin.
  1159‚Üí\item \textbf{Set $m_s = m_d$:} $SU(3)_V$ restored, 
  1160‚Üí$\pi = K = \eta$. Koide splitting $v_1 \to 0$; lepton partners 
  1161‚Üídegenerate.
  1162‚Üí\item \textbf{Send $m_q \to 0$:} Chiral limit, all meson charges 
  1163‚Üí$\to 0$. If sBootstrap SUSY restores, all light lepton masses 
  1164‚Üívanish too. This is the seed configuration: $(0, 0, 0)$.
  1165‚Üí\end{enumerate}
  1166‚Üí
  1167‚ÜíThe seed triples are the ``penultimate'' stage: one member has 
  1168‚Üíreached zero, but the other two retain the $2 - \sqrt{3}$ ratio. 
  1169‚ÜíFull triples are the final broken state. The lepton triple, 
  1170‚Üítrapped between seed and bloom, reveals the hierarchy: 
  1171‚Üíchiral breaking $\to$ seed $\to$ bloom $\to$ full spectrum.
  1172‚Üí
  1173‚ÜíThe direction of the flow also matters: approaching the chiral 
  1174‚Üílimit, the pion charge rises from $\zch = -11.81$ toward zero 
  1175‚Üífrom below. It crosses zero exactly in the chiral limit. This 
  1176‚Üímeans the pion was ``born negative''---its charge sign is set by 
  1177‚Üíthe direction of chiral symmetry breaking, and the seed 
  1178‚Üí$(0, \pi, D_s)$ is the configuration at the moment of crossing.
  1179‚Üí
  1180‚Üí%======================================================================
  1181‚Üí\section{Nature Abhors Chirality}
  1182‚Üí\label{sec:chirality}
  1183‚Üí%======================================================================
  1184‚Üí
  1185‚ÜíA pattern across the Standard Model: every chiral structure is 
  1186‚Üíeliminated in the infrared.
  1187‚Üí
  1188‚Üí\begin{center}
  1189‚Üí\begin{tabular}{lcl}
  1190‚Üí\toprule
  1191‚ÜíUV structure & IR survivor & Mechanism \\
  1192‚Üí\midrule
  1193‚Üí$SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ & $U(1)_{\text{em}}$ & Higgs \\
  1194‚Üí$SU(N_f)_L \times SU(N_f)_R$ & $SU(N_f)_V$ & $\qqbar$ condensate \\
  1195‚Üí$U(1)_A$ & nothing & Anomaly \\
  1196‚Üí$\bar{\theta}$-vacuum & $|\bar{\theta}| < 10^{-10}$ & Unknown \\
  1197‚ÜíChiral color? & $SU(3)_C$ & Axigluon mass \\
  1198‚Üí\bottomrule
  1199‚Üí\end{tabular}
  1200‚Üí\end{center}
  1201‚Üí
  1202‚ÜíThe sBootstrap adds: superspace chirality (boson vs.\ fermion 
  1203‚Üídistinction within a multiplet) is broken by the same condensate 
  1204‚Üíthat breaks flavor chirality, with $f_{\text{SUSY}} = f_\pi$. The 
  1205‚Üíenergy-balance condition $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ may be 
  1206‚Üírelated: the breaking is ``as large as the background 
  1207‚Üíallows''---maximal, not perturbative.
  1208‚Üí
  1209‚Üí%======================================================================
  1210‚Üí\section{Open Problems}
  1211‚Üí\label{sec:open}
  1212‚Üí%======================================================================
  1213‚Üí
  1214‚Üí\subsection{The diquark statistics problem}
  1215‚ÜíThe sBootstrap requires antisymmetric $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ 
  1216‚Üídiquarks; Pauli statistics for spin-0, $L=0$, color $\bar{3}$ 
  1217‚Üídiquarks requires symmetric flavor. Extra dynamical input---$L=1$ 
  1218‚Üíplus spin-1, instantons, or the $SO(32)$ embedding---is needed.
  1219‚Üí
  1220‚Üí\subsection{What determines the Koide phase?}
  1221‚ÜíThe energy balance constrains but does not uniquely fix $\delta$. 
  1222‚ÜíThe value $\delta_\ell \approx 2/9$ for leptons must come from 
  1223‚Üídynamics. Is it a fixed point?
  1224‚Üí
  1225‚Üí\subsection{Connecting the three tuples}
  1226‚ÜíThe light sector (seed $\to$ bloom, scale $\sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$) 
  1227‚Üíand heavy sector ($c,b,t$, scale $\sim v_{\text{EW}}$) must connect. 
  1228‚ÜíThe charm and bottom quarks appear in both, providing the links.
  1229‚Üí
  1230‚Üí\subsection{The baryon sector}
  1231‚ÜíIn Seiberg's SQCD with $N_f = N_c = 3$, baryons are part of the 
  1232‚Üílow-energy spectrum. Their SUSY partners are ``baryonic fermions'' 
  1233‚Üíwith no known candidates among observed particles.
  1234‚Üí
  1235‚Üí\subsection{The strong CP problem}
  1236‚ÜíThe principle ``Nature abhors chirality'' predicts $\bar{\theta} = 0$ 
  1237‚Üíbut provides no mechanism.
  1238‚Üí
  1239‚Üí\subsection{The kaon--muon puzzle}
  1240‚ÜíThe SUSY splitting $m_K^2 - m_\mu^2 \approx (482\MeV)^2$ is much 
  1241‚Üílarger than $f_K^2 \approx (110\MeV)^2$, showing that the breaking 
  1242‚Üíis strongly flavor-dependent.
  1243‚Üí
  1244‚Üí%======================================================================
  1245‚Üí\section{Conclusions}
  1246‚Üí\label{sec:conclusions}
  1247‚Üí%======================================================================
  1248‚Üí
  1249‚ÜíThree principal results:
  1250‚Üí
  1251‚Üí\paragraph{1.\ The Koide formula is energy equipartition.}
  1252‚Üí$Q = 2/3$ is $\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$: perturbation energies 
  1253‚Üímatch the vacuum energy. The signed-charge framework resolves the 
  1254‚Üíelectron mass puzzle (near-cancellation, not fine-tuning) and reveals 
  1255‚Üíthat mesons satisfy Koide with $(-\pi, D_s, B)$ to $0.1\%$.
  1256‚Üí
  1257‚Üí\paragraph{2.\ Seed triples set the stage for SUSY breaking.}
  1258‚ÜíThe quark seed $(0,s,c)$ and meson seed $(0,\pi,D_s)$ share the 
  1259‚Üíuniversal ratio $2 - \sqrt{3}$ and operate at $v_0^2 \sim 
  1260‚Üí230$--$350\MeV$, close to the lepton triple's $v_0^2 = 314\MeV$. 
  1261‚ÜíThe massless member is the necessary condition for F-term 
  1262‚Üí(O'Raifeartaigh) SUSY breaking. Upon breaking, seeds bloom into 
  1263‚Üífull triples $(-s,c,b)$ and $(-\pi,D_s,B)$. The lepton triple 
  1264‚Üíis the ``almost-seed'' that has barely bloomed.
  1265‚Üí
  1266‚Üí\paragraph{3.\ Two breaking mechanisms plus a heavy-sector origin.}
  1267‚ÜíF-term breaking (via the massless seed member) generates the light 
  1268‚Üísector; the Volkov--Akulov relation $f_{\text{VA}} = f_\pi$ is its 
  1269‚Üílow-energy realization, not a separate mechanism. D-term breaking 
  1270‚Üí($U(1)_{\text{em}}$ FI parameter) provides electromagnetic 
  1271‚Üísplittings as a perturbative correction. The heavy triple 
  1272‚Üí$(c,b,t)$ stands apart: no seed, no meson shadow, scale far above 
  1273‚Üí$\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$, possibly connected to electroweak breaking.
  1274‚Üí
  1275‚ÜíThe diquark sector, required to complete the SUSY multiplet count, 
  1276‚Üímust fill the antisymmetric $\overline{\mathbf{10}}$ of $SU(5)_f$ 
  1277‚Üídespite the apparent conflict with Fermi statistics---a problem 
  1278‚Üíthat may ultimately require the $SO(32)$ embedding for its 
  1279‚Üíresolution.
  1280‚Üí
  1281‚ÜíThe organizing principle is that each sector of the spectrum begins 
  1282‚Üíwith a massless member (a seed), which is the necessary condition 
  1283‚Üífor spontaneous SUSY breaking. The breaking lifts the zero, 
  1284‚Üígenerates the full triple, and the energy-balance condition 
  1285‚Üí$\langle z_k^2\rangle = z_0^2$ is preserved as a fixed point 
  1286‚Üíof the dynamics. The entire charged fermion and pseudoscalar meson 
  1287‚Üíspectrum is then determined by a small number of parameters: the 
  1288‚Üívacuum charges $v_0$ of the three tuples, the Koide phases 
  1289‚Üí$\delta$ that control the bloom, and the SUSY-breaking scale 
  1290‚Üí$f_\pi$ that separates bosons from fermions.
