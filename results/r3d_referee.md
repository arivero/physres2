# Adversarial Referee Report — Results

## Recommendation: MAJOR REVISION

## Key Criticisms

### 1. M_W and sin²θ_W are ONE fact, not two
R = 1 − x(1/2)/x(1) and sin²θ_W = 1 − M²_W/M²_Z are the same equation. Predicting M_W from M_Z via R is algebraically equivalent to the Weinberg angle match. **Must not present as independent predictions.**

### 2. Third Diophantine equation is ad hoc
The uniqueness of (N,r,s) = (3,3,2) depends entirely on eq 3 (r²+s²−1 = 4N). Without it, infinitely many solutions exist. Its physical justification (neutrino sector counting) is the least motivated of the three.

### 3. Casimir quartic is an ansatz, not a derivation
M⁴ − M²C₂ + C₁C₂ = 0 does not follow from standard Poincaré representation theory. Casimir invariants label irreps; they don't naturally combine into a quartic for mass. The equation is "Casimir-inspired", not "Casimir-derived."

### 4. Quark seed is scheme-dependent
√m_s/√m_c at a common renormalization scale moves AWAY from 2−√3. The "seed triple" prediction for quarks is scheme-dependent — a serious issue.

### 5. Second scaling failure weakens the pattern
M₁ = 3M₀ works once, fails once. This is "the definition of an underdetermined pattern." Does not undermine the first scaling as an observation but prevents elevation to a law.

### 6. 2.8σ is marginal
Sufficient for "hint/observation," not for "discovery." Publishable with appropriate caveats.
