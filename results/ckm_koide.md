# CKM Mixing and Koide Triples

## Key Finding: All Koide Triples Are Mixed
Exhaustive scan of all C(6,3) = 20 quark triples (u,d,s,c,b,t) with
all sign choices: EVERY triple close to Q = 2/3 mixes up-type and
down-type quarks. Pure down-type (d,s,b): Q = 0.73. Pure up-type
(u,c,t): Q = 0.85. Both far from 2/3.

The top 4 by Q proximity:
1. (c,b,t): Q = 0.6695, 2up+1dn
2. (-s,c,b): Q = 0.675, 1up+2dn

This is NOT just combinatorics (90% of triples are mixed by chance).
The 100% rate among Koide triples is a structural feature.

## Oakes Relation
tan(theta_C) = sqrt(m_d/m_s) = 0.224 vs V_us = 0.224 -- 97% match.
This connects the free parameters (m_u, m_d) to the CKM matrix.

## Parameter Counting
- Two overlapping Koide triples (-s,c,b) + (c,b,t): 2 constraints on 4 masses
- Seed (0,s,c): 3rd constraint
- v0-doubling: 4th constraint
- Leaves: m_u and m_d as free (connected to Cabibbo angle)

## Dual Koide (Seiberg Seesaw)
Q(1/m_d, 1/m_s, 1/m_b) = 0.665 (0.22% from 2/3)
The Seiberg vacuum has M_j ~ 1/m_j, so the dual Koide means
meson condensate VEVs form a near-Koide triple.
Only (d,s,b) shows this; next best (d,s,c) deviates 4.2%.

## Self-Dual Seed
The seed (0, m_s, m_c) sits at a self-dual point: Q(r) = Q(1/r)
where r = sqrt(m_s/m_c). The Koide condition in the electric
theory (quark masses) automatically implies the same condition
in the magnetic theory (meson VEVs).
