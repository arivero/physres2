# Orientifold Sign: Symmetric 15 Forced by Odd Dimension

## Result

For the T⁶/Z₃ Type I construction with Chan-Paton embedding γ_θ = diag(ω·1₅, ω²·1₅, 1₃, 1₃):

**The symmetric representation (15) is FORCED for the n=5 block. No discrete ambiguity exists.**

## Proof

The orientifold projection on two-index tensor states |ij⟩ + |ji⟩ (symmetric) or |ij⟩ − |ji⟩ (antisymmetric) depends on the sign in γ_Ω = ±γ_θᵀ γ_Ω.

For the Sp-type (antisymmetric) projection to be consistent, the block must admit a nondegenerate antisymmetric bilinear form. But a nondegenerate antisymmetric n×n matrix exists only for even n (since det(A) = det(Aᵀ) = det(−A) = (−1)ⁿ det(A), so for odd n, det(A) = 0).

Since n = 5 is odd:
- No nondegenerate antisymmetric form exists on a 5-dimensional space
- The Sp-type projection is inconsistent
- Only the SO-type (symmetric) projection survives
- The two-index representation is the **symmetric 15**, not the antisymmetric 10

## Implications

1. The string construction FORCES 15 of SU(5) — the same representation required by the sBootstrap
2. The 15 vs 10̄ conflict (Pauli statistics vs compositeness) is resolved: strings deliver 15 by construction
3. Same argument applies to the n=3 block: U(3) produces symmetric 6, not antisymmetric 3̄
4. This is a theorem of linear algebra, not a model-dependent choice
