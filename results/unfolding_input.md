## Summary

Staged symmetry breaking picture for the mass generation program.

### Stages:
- **Stage 0**: Full symmetry, all massless
- **Stage 1** (E ~ v_EW = 246 GeV): EWSB generates all Yukawa masses. (c,b,t) and (e,μ,τ) Koide triples active. Gen1 invisible to heavy Koide.
- **Stage 2** (E ~ Λ_QCD = 300 MeV): Chiral + sBootstrap SUSY breaking. Seeds (0,s,c) and (0,π,Dₛ) emerge. Gen2-dominated. Gen3↓ is flat direction.
- **Stage 3** (E ~ 5 MeV): Explicit chiral breaking. Bloom: Gen3↓ recruited. Sign flip. v₀-doubling.
- **Stage 4** (E ~ α_em): EM splittings. Perturbative shifts only.

### Key insight for CKM:
After Koide constraints, only m_u, m_d, m_s remain free. The Oakes relation tan θ_C ≈ √(m_d/m_s) connects m_d/m_s to the Cabibbo angle. This is the REMAINING FREEDOM, not a derived prediction.

### Key insight for (c,b,t):
(c,b,t) has no seed and no meson counterpart because it belongs to Stage 1 (EW/Yukawa). It is a Yukawa constraint, not a chiral one.

### Key insight for leptons:
Lepton Koide scale M₀ ≈ f²_π/27 bridges Stages 1 and 2. Lepton masses are Yukawa-generated but their scale is set by chiral dynamics.

### Implication for paper:
The paper should be organized around these stages. The CKM angle is NOT a prediction — it parametrizes the remaining freedom (m_d/m_s) after all Koide constraints are imposed. The number of free parameters is {m_s, m_u/m_d, θ_C} where only two of {m_u, m_d, θ_C} are independent via Oakes.
