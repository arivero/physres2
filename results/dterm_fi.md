# D-term Fayet-Iliopoulos Contributions

## Key Results

### Charge assignments (5×5 meson matrix)
- 13 neutral entries (Q=0)
- 6 with Q=+1 (ud̄, us̄, ub̄, cd̄, cs̄, cb̄)
- 6 with Q=-1 (conjugates)
- No fractional charges in meson sector

### FI parameter from π-μ splitting
- ξ = -80.34 MeV² (small, negative)
- 0.95% of f²_π = 8477 MeV²
- D-term partially cancels F-term for charged states

### Meson-lepton pairings (folding pattern)
- Row 3: μ ↔ π±, K± (works: Δm² ≈ f²_π)
- Row 2: τ ↔ D±, D_s± (fails: QCD dominates)
- Row 1: e ↔ B±, B_c± (fails badly: QCD dominates)

### Critical finding: Koide immunity to D-terms
- In N=1 SUSY, D-terms shift scalar masses but leave fermion masses untouched
- Koide triples are fermion mass conditions → naturally immune to D-term perturbations
- Even small Δm² = 80 MeV² additive shift would break Koide by 7.7%
- The fact that D-terms DON'T touch fermion masses is essential for Koide's survival

### Neutral sector
- m²_π0 / f²_π = 2.15, not 1.0
- √2 × f_π = 130.2 MeV vs m_π0 = 135.0 MeV (3.7% off)
- Pion mass includes explicit chiral breaking (GOR relation)

### Sensitivity analysis
- Koide fragile under additive m² shifts, robust under multiplicative rescaling
- At Δ = 80 MeV², Q drops from 0.6667 to 0.6154 (7.7% shift)
