# Geometric Connector — Results

## Verdict: NO CONNECTION

The Koide cone (Structure 1) and the Casimir eigenvalue equation (Structure 2) are **not related** in any deep geometric or algebraic sense.

## Evidence

### 1. Algebraic independence
- Koide cone lives in Q(√3) (matrix eigenvalues ±3)
- Casimir at s=1/2 produces √57 → R ∈ Q(√3, √19)
- √57 has no origin within the Koide geometry

### 2. No embedding
Triple (x(1/2), x(1), x(3/2)) = (0.568, 0.732, 0.820) does NOT satisfy the Koide cone equation. Verified numerically with and without sign choices.

### 3. Shared √3 is coincidental
- In the cone: √3 = √(dim ℝ³) from the all-ones vector normalization
- In the Casimir: √3 from √(C²+4C)|_{C=2} = √12 = 2√3, an arithmetic fact about s=1
- Different algebraic origins. For s=3/2, no √3 appears.

### 4. Involutions are generic
Both have Vieta-type reflections — but ALL quadratic forms do. This is the theory of orthogonal groups, not a specific connection.

### 5. Factor of 3 is not geometric
The Koide cone has continuous ℝ₊ scale symmetry. The factor M₁ = 3M₀ breaks this symmetry by hand. The S₃ permutation symmetry acts on angles, not scale. No geometric reason to single out 3.

### 6. Different symmetry groups
- Koide cone: O(2,1) ⋊ S₃
- Casimir family: ℤ₂ at each spin value, no global group relating spins

## Implication for the Paper

The Casimir/electroweak section and the Koide/mass-formula section describe **independent mathematical structures** that happen to involve the same physical particles. The paper should present them as separate observations, not as parts of a unified geometric framework. The dynamical link question remains open — and this analysis suggests it may be fundamentally absent at the algebraic level.
