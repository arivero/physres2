# CW Effective Potential: Precision Results

## Key finding
V_CW(t) is monotonically increasing for ALL y ∈ (0, 1/2). Minimum always at t=0.

## At t = √3, y = 1/4 (20 sig figs)
- V_CW  = 1.9941666048349150779
- V'_CW = 0.56784544251764801305  (positive → rising)
- V''   = -0.29861386702013537390 (negative → concave, decelerating)

## Taylor expansion around t=0 (y=1/4)
c₀ = 0.7390, c₂ = +1.3693, c₄ = -1.2794, c₆ = +1.3650
Series alternates from c₄ onward. Positive c₂ confirms V rises from t=0.

## CW mass at t=0
m²_CW = 2.739 × m²/(64π²) = 0.00434 m²  (y=1/4, g=1)
Parametrically suppressed by 1/(64π²).

## SUSY limit (y=0)
V_CW = 0 exactly (perfect boson-fermion cancellation).
STr[M²] = 0 for all t,y. STr[M⁴] = 2y² m⁴.

## Implication
One-loop CW pushes vacuum to t=0, not t=√3.
Stabilization at gv/m=√3 requires nonperturbative mechanism.
